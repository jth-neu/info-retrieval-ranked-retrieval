<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Framework Convention for the Protection of National Minorities</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg/300px-Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg.png" width="300" height="195" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg/450px-Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg/600px-Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="800" data-file-height="520" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Member_states_of_the_Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_on_National_Minorities.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Signatories which have not ratified in light green<br />Signatories which have ratified in dark green<br />Non-signatory members of the Council of Europe in white<br />Non-members of the Council of Europe in grey</div></div></div>
<p>The <b>Framework Convention for the Protection of National Minorities</b> (<b>FCNM</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Multilateral_treaty" title="Multilateral treaty">multilateral treaty</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> aimed at protecting the <a href="/wiki/Minority_rights" title="Minority rights">rights of minorities</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-P8_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P8-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It came into effect in 1998 and by 2009 it had been ratified by 39 member states.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_of_National_Minorities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Council of Europe first discussed according specific protection for national minorities in 1949, but it was not until 1990 that the Council of Europe made a firm commitment to protect these minority groups. Recommendation 1134 (1990) contained a list of principles which the Assembly considered necessary for this purpose. The Parliamentary Assembly did in the beginning call for adoption of a protocol to the <a href="/wiki/ECHR" class="mw-redirect" title="ECHR">ECHR</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Framework was signed on February 1995 by 22 member States of the <a href="/wiki/Council_of_Europe" title="Council of Europe">Council of Europe</a> and became active in 1998.<sup id="cite_ref-P8_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P8-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> By mid-2005, 43 member states had signed and 39 ratified it.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Aims_and_criticism">Aims and criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Framework_Convention_for_the_Protection_of_National_Minorities&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Aims and criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The broad aims of the convention are to ensure that the signatory states respect the rights of national minorities, undertaking to combat discrimination, promote equality, preserve and develop the culture and identity of national minorities, guarantee certain freedoms in relation to access to the media, <a href="/wiki/Minority_language" title="Minority language">minority languages</a> and education and encourage the participation of national minorities in public life. Article 25 of the Framework Convention binds the member states to submit a report to the Council of Europe containing "full information on the legislative and other measures taken to give effect to the principles set out in this framework Convention" (Council of Europe, 1994, 7).
</p><p>The convention has come under some criticism. First of all, not all member states of the Council of Europe have signed and ratified it. France and Turkey have done neither. Iceland, Belgium, Luxembourg and Greece have signed and have yet to ratify. Also, the provisions offer little new on already existing international treaties. Furthermore, they are hedged around with many phrases including 'as far as possible'.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> The convention does not define "national minority" and several countries set their own definition of the term when they ratified the treaty.<sup id="cite_ref-P8_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-P8-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> For example, the United Kingdom ratified the convention on the understanding that it would be applied with reference to "racial groups" within the meaning of Section 3(1) of the <a href="/wiki/Race_Relations_Act_1976" title="Race Relations Act 1976">Race Relations Act 1976</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Since this excluded the <a href="/wiki/Cornish_people" title="Cornish people">Cornish people</a>, this resulted in pressure, including from <a href="/wiki/Cornwall_Council" title="Cornwall Council">Cornwall Council</a>, for the UK Government to recognise the Cornish as a national minority.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In April 2014, it was announced by the <a href="/wiki/Chief_Secretary_to_the_Treasury" title="Chief Secretary to the Treasury">Chief Secretary to the Treasury</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danny_Alexander" title="Danny Alexander">Danny Alexander</a>, that the UK Government would recognise the Cornish as a national minority under the FCNM.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Overall however, Phillips (2002) has argued that because the FCNM is flexible it has allowed such a great number of states to ratify it so quickly. Therefore, it should not be considered a failure, but a start. Many authors agree with this arguing that it needs to be implemented in 'good faith' with the political will to support commitment to <a href="/wiki/Minority_rights" title="Minority rights">minority rights</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
