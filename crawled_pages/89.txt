<p><b>CBS Interactive Inc.</b> (formerly <b>CBS Digital Media Group</b>) is an American media company and is a division of the <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS Corporation</a>. It is an online content network for information and entertainment. Its websites cover <a href="/wiki/News" title="News">news</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports">sports</a>, <a href="/wiki/Entertainment" title="Entertainment">entertainment</a>, <a href="/wiki/Technology" title="Technology">technology</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Business" title="Business">business</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It is headed by <a href="/wiki/Jim_Lanzone" title="Jim Lanzone">Jim Lanzone</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>CBS Interactive coordinates with CBS Network Sales to bring together ad sales operations, as well as the corporation's television, sports, and news groups, who will continue to be involved in program development.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On May 30, 2007, CBS Interactive acquired <a href="/wiki/Last.fm" title="Last.fm">Last.fm</a> for £140 million (US$280 million).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 30, 2008, <a href="/wiki/CNET_Networks" class="mw-redirect" title="CNET Networks">CNET Networks</a> was acquired by CBS and the assets were merged into CBS Interactive, including <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a>, <a href="/wiki/TV.com" title="TV.com">TV.com</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Movietome" class="mw-redirect" title="Movietome">Movietome</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 15, 2012, it was announced that CBS Interactive acquired <a href="/wiki/Video_game" title="Video game">video game</a>-based website <a href="/wiki/Giant_Bomb" title="Giant Bomb">Giant Bomb</a> and <a href="/wiki/Comic_book" title="Comic book">comic book</a>-based website <a href="/wiki/Comic_Vine" class="mw-redirect" title="Comic Vine">Comic Vine</a> from <a href="/wiki/Whiskey_Media" title="Whiskey Media">Whiskey Media</a>, who sold off their other remaining websites to <a href="/wiki/BermanBraun" class="mw-redirect" title="BermanBraun">BermanBraun</a>. This occasion marked the return of <a href="/wiki/Video_game_journalism" title="Video game journalism">video game journalist</a> <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Gerstmann" title="Jeff Gerstmann">Jeff Gerstmann</a> to the CBS Interactive division of video game websites, which includes <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a> and <a href="/wiki/GameFAQs" title="GameFAQs">GameFAQs</a>, and has Gerstmann once again working directly with some of his former peers at GameSpot under the same building at the CBS Interactive headquarters.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On April 17, 2012, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Major_League_Gaming" title="Major League Gaming">Major League Gaming</a> and CBS Interactive would be entering a partnership alongside <a href="/wiki/TwitchTV" class="mw-redirect" title="TwitchTV">TwitchTV</a> to be the only exclusive online broadcaster of their Pro Circuit competitions, as well as for advertising representation.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Brands">Brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Entertainment">Entertainment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Entertainment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The CBS Interactive entertainment brands are CBS.com, the online home of <a href="/wiki/CBS" title="CBS">CBS</a>, an American network television channel and its OTT service, <a href="/wiki/CBS_All_Access" title="CBS All Access">CBS All Access</a>; TV Guide, the ultimate listings guide for any Service Provider; CHOW,  recipes, discussions, reviews, videos, and stories all related to <a href="/wiki/Food" title="Food">food</a>; <a href="/wiki/TV.com" title="TV.com">TV.com</a>, discussions, show guides, reviews, and videos all about <a href="/wiki/Television" title="Television">television</a>; <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregation" class="mw-redirect" title="Review aggregation">review aggregation</a> website for games, music, television and movies; <a href="/wiki/Comic_Vine" class="mw-redirect" title="Comic Vine">Comic Vine</a>, news, reviews, and forums about comics; and <a href="/wiki/GameRankings" title="GameRankings">GameRankings</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregation" class="mw-redirect" title="Review aggregation">review aggregation</a> website for games.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_games">Video games</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Video games">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CBS Interactive's video games brands are <a href="/wiki/GameFAQs" title="GameFAQs">GameFAQs</a>, a community driven website with guides and forums about <a href="/wiki/Video_games" class="mw-redirect" title="Video games">video games</a>; <a href="/wiki/GameSpot" title="GameSpot">GameSpot</a>, news, reviews, and guides about video games; <a href="/wiki/Giant_Bomb" title="Giant Bomb">Giant Bomb</a>, videos, podcasts, and reviews about video games; and <a href="/wiki/OnGamers" title="OnGamers">onGamers</a>, news analyses, and columns about the <a href="/wiki/ESports" title="ESports">eSports</a> scene.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Music">Music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CBS Interactive's music brands are <a href="/wiki/Last.fm" title="Last.fm">Last.fm</a>, the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Music_discovery_service&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Music discovery service (page does not exist)">music discovery service</a> and listening service; and <a href="/wiki/MetroLyrics" title="MetroLyrics">MetroLyrics</a>, lyrics, videos, and news for music.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="News">News</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: News">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CBS Interactive's news brands are CBSNews.com, the online home of <a href="/wiki/CBS_News" title="CBS News">CBS News</a>, the news division of <a href="/wiki/CBS_Corporation" title="CBS Corporation">CBS Corporation</a>; and <a href="/wiki/CBSN" title="CBSN">CBSN</a>, a 24-hour news channel based completely online and on smart devices, a division of CBS News.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CBS Interactive's sports brands are <a href="/wiki/CBSSports.com" title="CBSSports.com">CBSSports.com</a>, the online home of <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports" title="CBS Sports">CBS Sports</a>, with news, scores, video, and discussion about <a href="/wiki/Professional_sports" title="Professional sports">professional sports</a> along with the streaming sports network <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports_HQ" title="CBS Sports HQ">CBS Sports HQ</a>; CBSSports.com College Network, the online home of <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports_Network" title="CBS Sports Network">CBS Sports College Network</a> with news, scores, video, and discussion about <a href="/wiki/College_athletics" title="College athletics">collegiate sports</a>; CBS Sports Fantasy, a website featuring <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Fantasy sports">fantasy sports</a>; and <a href="/wiki/MaxPreps" title="MaxPreps">MaxPreps</a>, a website including information, recruiting, news, video, and discussion about <a href="/wiki/High_school_sports" class="mw-redirect" title="High school sports">high school sports</a>. <a href="/wiki/CBS_Sports" title="CBS Sports">CBS Sports</a> also manages an advance media division that hosts college and high school sports digital presence to both design the websites and manage the ads on each of those 250+ properties.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CBS Interactive's technology brands are <a href="/wiki/CNET" title="CNET">CNET</a>, an American media website that publishes reviews, news, articles, blogs, podcasts, and videos on technology and consumer electronics globally; CNET Content Solutions, content solutions related to technology and consumer electronics; <a href="/wiki/Download.com" title="Download.com">Download.com</a>, a website for free software; <a href="/wiki/TechRepublic" title="TechRepublic">TechRepublic</a>, news, information, resources, and forums for <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">IT</a> professionals; and <a href="/wiki/ZDNet" title="ZDNet">ZDNet</a>, technology news, reviews, and videos for technology and business professionals.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Former_brands">Former brands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=CBS_Interactive&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Former brands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/BNET" title="BNET">BNET</a>, founded in 2007, was an <a href="/wiki/Online_magazine" title="Online magazine">online magazine</a> dedicated to issues of <a href="/wiki/Business_management" class="mw-redirect" title="Business management">business management</a>.
