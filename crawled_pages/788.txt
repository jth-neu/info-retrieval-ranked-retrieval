<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Counties of the United Kingdom</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <b>counties of the United Kingdom</b> are subnational divisions of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, used for the purposes of administrative, geographical and political demarcation.  By the <a href="/wiki/Middle_Ages" title="Middle Ages">Middle Ages</a> <a href="/wiki/Counties" class="mw-redirect" title="Counties">counties</a> had become established as a unit of local government, at least in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> By the early 17th century, all of England, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a> had been separated into counties. The older term <i><a href="/wiki/Shire" title="Shire">shire</a></i> was historically equivalent to "county".  In Scotland <i>shire</i> was the only term used until after the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Union_1707" class="mw-redirect" title="Act of Union 1707">Act of Union 1707</a>.
</p><p>Since the early 19th century, counties have been adapted to meet new administrative and political requirements, and the word <i>county</i> (often with a qualifier) has been used in different senses for different purposes. In some areas of England and Wales, counties still perform the functions of modern local government. In other parts of the United Kingdom, especially within large metropolitan areas, they have been replaced with alternative <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a>, which are considered 'county level' authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-jones_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jones-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Today, these by and large have replaced the historic <a href="/wiki/County_corporate" title="County corporate">county corporate</a> entities granted self-governance with county government powers.
</p><p>Today, in addition to local government counties, every part of the United Kingdom lies within what is known as one of the historic counties; which have formed geographic and cultural units since the Middle Ages.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The names of these regions are often well known, even famous (e.g. <i>"The sheriff of Nottingham"</i>, a common county office in a historic county famed from its role in classic popular fiction) seen in older historic geographic, political, and literary (or today, in retrospective media productions) referents.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="England">England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Counties_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303569">@media all and (max-width:720px){.mw-parser-output .tmulti>.thumbinner{width:100%!important;max-width:none!important}.mw-parser-output .tmulti <div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_England" title="Counties of England">Counties of England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Traditional_counties_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="Traditional counties of England">Traditional counties of England</a></div>
<p>The whole of England is divided into 48 <a href="/wiki/Ceremonial_counties" class="mw-redirect" title="Ceremonial counties">ceremonial counties</a>, which are also known as geographic counties. Many of these counties have their basis in the 39 <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_England" title="Historic counties of England">historic counties</a> whose origins lie in antiquity,<sup id="cite_ref-hamton_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hamton-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  although some were established as recently as 1974.<sup id="cite_ref-maud_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-maud-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Outside <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Isles_of_Scilly" title="Isles of Scilly">Isles of Scilly</a>, England is also divided into 83 <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_and_non-metropolitan_counties_of_England" title="Metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties of England">metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties</a>. These correspond to areas used for the purposes of local government and may consist of a single district or be divided into several. As of April 2009, 27 such counties are divided into districts and have a <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county council</a>.
</p><p>Most ceremonial counties correspond to a metropolitan or non-metropolitan county of the same name, but often with reduced boundaries. The current arrangement is the result of incremental reform; from 1974 to 1996 the metropolitan and non-metropolitan counties corresponded directly with the ceremonial counties. From 1889 to 1974 areas with county councils were known as <a href="/wiki/Administrative_counties_of_England" title="Administrative counties of England">administrative counties</a> and ceremonial counties were defined separately.<sup id="cite_ref-kingdom_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kingdom-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Counties_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png/150px-Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png" width="150" height="128" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png/225px-Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png/300px-Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png 2x" data-file-width="1677" data-file-height="1426" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Northern_Ireland_-_Counties.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Counties of Northern Ireland</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main articles: <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Counties of Northern Ireland">Counties of Northern Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Traditional_counties_of_Ireland" class="mw-redirect" title="Traditional counties of Ireland">Traditional counties of Ireland</a></div>
<p>There are six counties in Northern Ireland. In order of landmass these are; <a href="/wiki/Tyrone" title="Tyrone">Tyrone</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Antrim" title="County Antrim">Antrim</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Down" title="County Down">Down</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Londonderry" title="County Londonderry">Londonderry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Fermanagh" title="Fermanagh">Fermanagh</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Armagh" title="County Armagh">Armagh</a>. A <a href="/wiki/Mnemonic" title="Mnemonic">mnemonic</a> to help remember these counties is 'FAT-LAD'.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>The six historic counties of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> are no longer in use for administrative purposes. Combined with the boroughs of <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a> and <a href="/wiki/Derry" title="Derry">Derry</a>, the counties do serve for organisational purposes within government, and often with private businesses and sporting clubs.
</p><p>The counties of Northern Ireland are all within the historic <a href="/wiki/Provinces_of_Ireland" title="Provinces of Ireland">province</a> of <a href="/wiki/Ulster" title="Ulster">Ulster</a>, which includes an additional three other counties from the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>: <a href="/wiki/County_Donegal" title="County Donegal">Donegal</a>, <a href="/wiki/County_Cavan" title="County Cavan">Cavan</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_Monaghan" title="County Monaghan">Monaghan</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Counties_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:ScotlandCountiesNumbered.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/ScotlandCountiesNumbered.png/220px-ScotlandCountiesNumbered.png" width="220" height="251" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/eb/ScotlandCountiesNumbered.png/330px-ScotlandCountiesNumbered.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/e/eb/ScotlandCountiesNumbered.png 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="457" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:ScotlandCountiesNumbered.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Scottish counties in 1975</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Shires_of_Scotland" title="Shires of Scotland">Shires of Scotland</a></div>
<p>In Scotland, there are 33 <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Scotland" title="Local government in Scotland">local government</a> counties, created under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1889" title="Local Government (Scotland) Act 1889">Local Government (Scotland) Act 1889</a>, they were abolished in 1975 under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_(Scotland)_Act_1973" title="Local Government (Scotland) Act 1973">Local Government (Scotland) Act 1973</a>, in favour of <a href="/wiki/Regions_and_districts_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Regions and districts of Scotland">regions and districts</a> and <a href="/wiki/Islands_council_areas_of_Scotland" title="Islands council areas of Scotland">islands council areas</a>. The regions and districts were themselves abolished in 1996, under the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_etc._(Scotland)_Act_1994" title="Local Government etc. (Scotland) Act 1994">Local Government etc. (Scotland) Act 1994</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> in favour of unitary <a href="/wiki/Council_areas_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Council areas of Scotland">Scottish council areas</a>. (The islands areas had been created as unitary council areas, and their boundaries were unaffected.)
</p><p>The 1889 legislation created <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a>, turned each civil county (with one exception) into a contiguous area (without separate fragments) and adjusted boundaries where <a href="/wiki/Civil_parish" title="Civil parish">civil parishes</a> straddled county boundaries, or had fragments in more than one county. The counties of <a href="/wiki/County_of_Ross" class="mw-redirect" title="County of Ross">Ross</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_of_Cromarty" class="mw-redirect" title="County of Cromarty">Cromarty</a> were merged to form <a href="/wiki/Ross_and_Cromarty" title="Ross and Cromarty">Ross and Cromarty</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One region and various districts, created in 1975, had areas similar to those of earlier counties, and various council areas, created in 1996, are also similar. Two of the three islands areas—<a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orkney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shetland" title="Shetland">Shetland</a>—have boundaries identical to those of earlier counties.
</p><p>Scotland has also <a href="/wiki/Registration_county" title="Registration county">registration counties</a>, which are in current use. The areas of Scotland that are appointed a <a href="/wiki/Lord-Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord-Lieutenant">Lord-Lieutenant</a> are called <a href="/wiki/Lieutenancy_areas_of_Scotland" title="Lieutenancy areas of Scotland">lieutenancy areas</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Wales">Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Counties_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:WalesTradNumbered.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/WalesTradNumbered.png/150px-WalesTradNumbered.png" width="150" height="187" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/WalesTradNumbered.png/225px-WalesTradNumbered.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2f/WalesTradNumbered.png/300px-WalesTradNumbered.png 2x" data-file-width="400" data-file-height="499" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:WalesTradNumbered.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Historic counties of Wales</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_Wales" title="Historic counties of Wales">Historic counties of Wales</a></div>
<p>The thirteen <a href="/wiki/Historic_counties_of_Wales" title="Historic counties of Wales">historic counties of Wales</a> were fixed by statute in 1535 (although counties such as <a href="/wiki/Pembrokeshire" title="Pembrokeshire">Pembrokeshire</a> date from 1138). The <a href="/wiki/History_of_local_government_in_Wales#Administrative_Counties" title="History of local government in Wales">Administrative Counties of Wales</a> created in 1889 were based on these. In 1974 a new system was created using vastly different entities. These were abolished in 1996 and since then Wales has been entirely divided into a system of <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities" class="mw-redirect" title="Unitary authorities">unitary authorities</a> (also known as <a href="/wiki/Principal_area" title="Principal area">principal areas</a>). Eleven of the 22 unitary authorities are legally "counties", and eleven are <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county boroughs</a>, although informally all are referred to as "counties". The areas of Wales that are appointed a <a href="/wiki/Lord-Lieutenant" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord-Lieutenant">Lord-Lieutenant</a> are the <a href="/wiki/Preserved_counties_of_Wales" title="Preserved counties of Wales">preserved counties of Wales</a>; which are combinations of principle areas chosen to approximate the counties constituted in 1974.
</p>
