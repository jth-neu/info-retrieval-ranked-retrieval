<p><b>Naver</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 네이버)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korean</a> online platform operated by <a href="/wiki/Naver_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Naver Corporation">Naver Corporation</a>. It debuted in 1999 as the first web portal in Korea to develop and use its own <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a>. It was also the world's first operator to introduce the comprehensive search feature, which compiles search results from various categories and presents them in a single page. Naver has since added a multitude of new services ranging from basic features such as e-mail and news to the world's first online Q&amp;A platform Knowledge iN. 
</p><p>As of September 2017, the search engine handled 74.7% of all web searches in South Korea and had 42 million enrolled users. More than 25 million Koreans have Naver as the start page on their default browser and the mobile application has 28 million daily visitors. Naver is also constantly referred to as 'the Google of South Korea'.<sup id="cite_ref-kt_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kt-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naver was created in June 1999 as the first South Korean portal website with a self-developed search engine. In August 2000, Naver began the 'comprehensive search' service, which allows users to get a variety of results from a search query on a single page, organized by type, including blogs, websites, images, cafes, etc. This was five years before Google launched a similar offer with its 'universal search' function.
</p><p>In the early days of Naver's operation, there was a relative dearth of webpages available in the Korean language. To fill this void, Naver became an early pioneer in <a href="/wiki/User-generated_content" title="User-generated content">user-generated content</a> through the creation of the 'Knowledge iN (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 네이버 지식인)' service in 2002. In Knowledge iN, users can pose questions on any subject and select among answers provided by other users, awarding points to the users who gives the best answers. Knowledge iN was launched three years before Yahoo! launched its similar 'Yahoo! Answers' service,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and now possesses a database of over 200 million answers.
</p><p>Over the years, Naver has continued to expand its services. It launched its Web comic service 'Webtoon' in 2004 and its personal Blog service in 2005. From 2005 to 2007, Naver expanded its multimedia search services, including music and video search as well as <a href="/wiki/Mobile_computing" title="Mobile computing">mobile</a> search.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Junior_Naver">Junior Naver</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Junior Naver">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Junior Naver</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 주니어 네이버), also known as <b>Juniver</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>:쥬니버), is a <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">portal</a> website for children considered similar to <a href="/wiki/Yahooligans" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahooligans">Yahooligans</a>. Junior Naver offers services such as avatars, educational content, quizzes, videos, Q&amp;A, and a homework helper.  Junior Naver utilizes a panel of experts and educators to filter out harmful content in order to ensure a safe internet environment for children. Ever since its competitors Daum Kids and Yahoo Kids have closed down, Junior Naver is the only children's portal site operating in Korea.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naver_Webtoon">Naver Webtoon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Naver Webtoon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Line_Webtoon" title="Line Webtoon">Line Webtoon</a></div>
<p><b>Naver Webtoon</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 네이버 웹툰) is a webcomic platform where users have free access to a variety of <a href="/wiki/Webtoons" class="mw-redirect" title="Webtoons">webtoons</a> created by professional artists. Users can also pay publishers to view comic books and genre fiction contents online. Naver has incorporated a 'Challenge' section which allows amateurs to post and promote their own works.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naver_Cafe">Naver Cafe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Naver Cafe">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Naver Cafe</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 네이버 카페) is a service that allows Naver users to create their own internet <a href="/wiki/Communities" class="mw-redirect" title="Communities">communities</a>. There were 10.5 million cafes running as of May 2017.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naver_Blog">Naver Blog</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Naver Blog">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Naver Blog</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>: 네이버 블로그) started with the name 'paper' in June 2003, and evolved to '<a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blog</a>' in October 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It had 23 million users as of April 2016.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naver_TV_Cast">Naver TV Cast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Naver TV Cast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_web_dramas_by_Naver_TV_Cast" title="List of web dramas by Naver TV Cast">List of web dramas by Naver TV Cast</a></div>
<p><b><a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://tvcast.naver.com/">Naver TV Cast</a></b> is a web broadcast network which mainly provides <a href="/wiki/Web_series" title="Web series">web dramas</a> distributed by Naver.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Knowledge_IN">Knowledge IN</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Knowledge IN">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Knowledge iN</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>:지식인), formerly <b>Knowledge Search</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>:지식검색), is an online Q&amp;A platform launched in October 2002.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The tool allows users to ask any question and to receive answers from other users. Knowledge iN was an early example of harnessing <a href="/wiki/User-generated_content" title="User-generated content">user-generated content</a> to expand the amount of information available on the web, particularly in the <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean language</a>. <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Horowitz" title="Bradley Horowitz">Bradley Horowitz</a>, former Vice President of Product Strategy at Yahoo!, has cited Knowledge iN as the inspiration for <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Answers" title="Yahoo! Answers">Yahoo! Answers</a>, which was launched three years after Naver introduced the original service.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Knowledge IN has several categories:
</p>
<ul><li>Knowledge Q&amp;A - allows general questions about anything, and has a lot of sub-categories within itself.
<ul><li>Education, Academy</li>
<li>Computer communication</li>
<li>Games</li>
<li>Entertainment, Art</li>
<li>Life (Everyday life)</li>
<li>Health</li>
<li>Society, Politics</li>
<li>Economy</li>
<li>Travel</li>
<li>Sports, Leisure</li>
<li>Shopping</li>
<li>Local Q&amp;A - allows people to ask for local information such as good restaurants, cheap stores, real estate, etc.</li>
<li>Agony Q&amp;A - for people seeking advice on relationships, love, career, and sex (including an anonymous function). While other categories usually provide factual answers, Agony Q&amp;A tends to return more opinionated responses.</li>
<li>Juniver Q&amp;A - questions for children.</li></ul></li>
<li>People - shows people who answer the most of questions in each area.</li>
<li>Best questions - shows questions that have generated the most traffic.</li>
<li>Open Dictionary - is a database of informative articles generated by users. Users can create an article alone, or they can also allow other users to contribute by creating a thread of articles on the same subject. Users can also add their own answers from Knowledge Q&amp;A to the Open Dictionary.</li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naver_Language_Dictionary">Naver Language Dictionary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Naver Language Dictionary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naver Dictionary supports 34 languages, including English, Korean, Chinese, Chinese characters, Japanese, Global Communication, Vietnamese, Uzbek, Indonesian, Thai, Arabic, Cambodian, Tamil, Mongolian, Hindi, Persian, Nepali, Swahili, French, German, Spanish, Russian, Italian, Latin, Portuguese, Turkish, Georgian, Albanian, Ukrainian, Romanian, Dutch, Swedish, Hungarian, Polish, and Czech as of May 2017. Handwriting recognition is supported in Japanese and Chinese. In 2016, Naver announced that it would invest 10 billion won for the development of language dictionaries.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naver_Encyclopedia">Naver Encyclopedia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Naver&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Naver Encyclopedia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Naver Encyclopedia consists of a professional database with over 3 million entries from many different <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedias" class="mw-redirect" title="Encyclopedias">encyclopedias</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> More than 1000 experts in physics, food, film, and other fields are producing 45 kinds of specialized content in the encyclopedia of Naver knowledge, and 50,000 headwords are accumulated.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The Naver Knowledge Encyclopedia also deals with more than 100 public institutions and has received over 900 kinds of DBs.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
