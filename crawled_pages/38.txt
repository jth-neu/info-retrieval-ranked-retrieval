<p><b>Vivisimo</b> was a privately held technology company in <a href="/wiki/Pittsburgh" title="Pittsburgh">Pittsburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania" title="Pennsylvania">Pennsylvania</a>, specialising in the development of computer <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engines</a>. The company was acquired<sup id="cite_ref-acq-anncmt_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acq-anncmt-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> in May 2012 and is now branded as <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.ibm.com/us-en/marketplace/content-analytics">IBM Watson Explorer</a>, a product of the <a href="/wiki/Watson_computer#IBM_Watson_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Watson computer">IBM Watson Group</a>. Vivisimo's public <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine" title="Web search engine">web search engine</a> <a href="/wiki/Clusty" class="mw-redirect" title="Clusty">Clusty</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Metasearch_engine" title="Metasearch engine">metasearch engine</a> with <a href="/wiki/Document_clustering" title="Document clustering">document clustering</a>; it was sold to Yippy, Inc. in 2010.
</p><p>Vivisimo specialized in <a href="/wiki/Federated_search" title="Federated search">federated search</a> and <a href="/wiki/Document_clustering" title="Document clustering">document clustering</a>. Clustering divides the results of a search for "cell" into groups including "biology", "battery", and "prison".
</p><p>Vivisimo software supported both structured and unstructured information.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivisimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Vivisimo was founded in 2000 by three computer science researchers at <a href="/wiki/Carnegie_Mellon_University" title="Carnegie Mellon University">Carnegie Mellon University</a>: Chris Palmer, Jerome Pesenti, and Raul Valdes-Perez. The name was taken from the Spanish superlative adjective <i>vivísimo</i> meaning "very lively" or "very clever."
</p><p>In October 2008, Vivisimo was awarded the contract to power the search portion of FirstGov.gov (now called <a href="/wiki/USA.gov" title="USA.gov">USA.gov</a>), the official web portal of the <a href="/wiki/United_States_federal_government" class="mw-redirect" title="United States federal government">United States federal government</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2012, <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> acquired Vivisimo to boost its <a href="/wiki/Big_Data" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Data">Big Data</a> Analytics capabilities .<sup id="cite_ref-acq-anncmt_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-acq-anncmt-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-V-IBM-00_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-V-IBM-00-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-V-IBM-01_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-V-IBM-01-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-V-IBM-02_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-V-IBM-02-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Vivisimo&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Velocity was sold as an installed or hosted application to enterprises, governments, and <a href="/wiki/OEM" class="mw-redirect" title="OEM">OEMs</a>. With Vivisimo providing additional professional services.
</p><p>Velocity's <a href="/wiki/Social_search" title="Social search">social search</a> features allowed users to contribute to organizational content by tagging, voting, annotating and sharing search results. The contributions are instantly indexed in new searches.
</p>
