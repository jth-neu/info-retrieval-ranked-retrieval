<p><b>Dogpile</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Metasearch_engine" title="Metasearch engine">metasearch engine</a> for information on the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a> that fetches results from <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yandex" title="Yandex">Yandex</a>, and includes results from several other popular <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engines</a>, including those from audio and video content providers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dogpile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dogpile began operation in November 1995. The site was created and developed by Aaron Flin and later acquired by <a href="/wiki/Infospace" class="mw-redirect" title="Infospace">Go2net</a> (which was in turn sold to <a href="/wiki/Infospace" class="mw-redirect" title="Infospace">Infospace</a>).
</p><p>From April 2001 to April 2007, Dogpile was primarily symbolized by a dog mascot. Named Arfie, the dog was featured in different themes from seasonal to holidays. From 2005 onwards, Dogpile added a link that gave the user a search query that corresponded with the theme the site was supporting.
</p><p>In April 2005, Dogpile (owned and operated by InfoSpace, Inc. at the time) collaborated with researchers from <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pittsburgh" title="University of Pittsburgh">University of Pittsburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Pennsylvania_State_University" title="Pennsylvania State University">Pennsylvania State University</a> to measure the overlap and ranking differences of leading Web search engines in order to gauge the benefits of using a metasearch engine to search the web. Results found that from 10,316 random user-defined queries from Google, Yahoo!, and <a href="/wiki/Ask_Jeeves" class="mw-redirect" title="Ask Jeeves">Ask Jeeves</a> only 3.2 percent of first page search results were the same across those search engines for a given query. Another study later that year using 12,570 random user-defined queries from Google, Yahoo!, MSN Search, and Ask Jeeves found that only 1.1 percent of first page search results were the same across those search engines for a given query.
</p><p>These studies showed that each search engine provides vastly different results. While users of the search engine may not recognize a problem, it was shown that they use ~3 search engines per month. Dogpile realized that searchers are not necessarily finding results they were looking for in one search engine and thus decided to redefine their existing metasearch engine to provide the best results.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Dogpile search engine earned the <a href="/wiki/J.D._Power_and_Associates" class="mw-redirect" title="J.D. Power and Associates">J.D. Power and Associates</a> award for best Residential Online Search Engine Service in both 2006<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Dogpile started a campaign in 2008 to use proceeds from site traffic to raise US$1 million for animals in need.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2010, Dogpile was ranked the 770th most popular website in the U.S., and 2,548th most popular in the world by <a href="/wiki/Alexa_Internet" title="Alexa Internet">Alexa</a>.  Quantcast estimated 2.0 million unique U.S. visitors a month, and Compete estimated 1,953,280.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2016, Blucora announced the sale of its Infospace business to OpenMail for $45 million in cash.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> OpenMail was later renamed System1.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dogpile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Dogpile features:<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Category Links: Links to help users focus their search on specific categories like News, Audio, etc.</li>
<li>Yellow Pages: Allows users to do a search using the Yellow Pages.</li>
<li>White Pages: Allows users to do a search using the White Pages. <i>(No longer available as of Feb 23, 2017)</i> <sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Web Search Box: The area where users enter the search term. Enter keyword(s) and hit the Search button to retrieve the results.</li>
<li>Search Button: The button pressed to search for results.</li>
<li>Preferences: Links to a page where users can set a variety of customized search preferences.</li>
<li>Spelling Correction: Offers suggested spellings for words that may be misspelled and automatically corrects commonly misspelled keywords.</li>
<li>Search Filter: Blocks potentially explicit content for multimedia searches in Moderate setting and for all searches when in Heavy setting.</li>
<li>Statistics Bar: Shows how many results were returned for the search term.</li>
<li>About Results: Find out about Dogpile's policies regarding sponsored and non-sponsored search results.</li>
<li>IntelliFind: Recommends additional content based on the original search term.</li>
<li>Are You Looking For?: Offers suggested spellings for words that may be misspelled and other search keywords that seem to be related to the original search term.</li>
<li>Recent Searches: Keeps track of the 15 most recent searches. The list resets when the browser is closed.</li>
<li>Favorite Fetches: Shows recent popular searches from other users</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Toolbar">Toolbar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dogpile&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Toolbar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Dogpile has also implemented a search toolbar that allows users an alternative way to search for web content on Internet Explorer or Firefox using Dogpile.com metasearch technology.
</p>
