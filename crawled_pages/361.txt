<p><b>Sogou, Inc.</b> is a public company<a href="/wiki/Sohu" title="Sohu">,</a> founded on 9 August 2010 by <a href="/wiki/Wang_Xiaochuan" title="Wang Xiaochuan">Wang Xiaochuan</a>. It is the owner and developer of <b>Sogou</b> (<a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh">搜狗</span>; <a href="/wiki/Pinyin" title="Pinyin">pinyin</a>&#58; <i><span lang="zh-Latn-pinyin">Sōugǒu</span></i>; literally&#58; "searching dog") <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sogou_Input" class="mw-redirect" title="Sogou Input">Sogou Input</a> and Sogou browser. According to iResearch statistics, Sogou accounted for 16.7% of the mobile search market share in China.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It is headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a>.  The offices of Sogou are located on the southeast corner of Tsinghua University.
</p><p>Sogou listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> on November 9, 2017, under the ticker “SOGO”.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The night before the IPO, <a href="/wiki/Tencent" title="Tencent">Tencent</a> owned <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2017-10-27/tencent-backed-sogou-aims-for-up-to-585-million-in-u-s-ipo">44%</a> of the Sogou while Sohu owned 38%. In the quarter prior to the IPO, Sogou reported 55% year over year revenue growth of 55% and 22% quarter over quarter growth.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_engine_and_web_applications">Search engine and web applications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Search engine and web applications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sogou search engine (Sogou.com) was launched on 3 August 2004. Sogou's web application products are designed to classify online information, such as music, picture, video clip, news, map and vertical information. As of 2017, it has an index of 10 billion pages.
</p><p>As of 2017, over 90% of the company's revenues are from search advertising.
</p><p>In October 2017, Sogou began <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.chinainternetwatch.com/22736/sogou-q3-2017/">testing</a> a general search engine within <a href="/wiki/WeChat" title="WeChat">WeChat</a>, which would allow all 902 million daily active users to access the internet within the app. Since 2013, Sogou has only been able to search and index contents of WeChat official accounts. On the first quarter conference call, 2018, Sogou provided an update indicating so far results in WeChat have been positive, resulting in a 30% improvement in click through rate. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sogou_Input_Method">Sogou Input Method</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Sogou Input Method">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sogou_Pinyin" title="Sogou Pinyin">Sogou Pinyin</a></div>
<p>Initially released in 2006, <a href="/wiki/Sogou_Pinyin" title="Sogou Pinyin">Sogou Pinyin</a> is the most popular Chinese input software in China. It makes use of its search engine techniques which are the analysis and categorization of the most popular words or phrases on the Internet. In 2007, Sogou won a patent infringement case against Google for copying their <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://technode.com/2017/09/15/sogou-vs-baidu-ip/">IME</a>.  Baidu is second with a 25.7% market share. IME data gives companies large amounts of keystroke history to improve search result accuracy. As of early 2018, the Sogou Input Method app is the third most downloaded app and commands a <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.xuehua.us/2018/06/30/sogou-doubles-down-on-ai-with-launch-of-real-time-devices/">70% marketshare</a>. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sogou_Browser">Sogou Browser</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sogou Browser">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Launched in December 2008, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou_browser&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Sogou browser (page does not exist)">Sogou browser</a> adopts a "dual-core" (<a href="/wiki/Google_Chrome" title="Google Chrome">Google Chrome</a>'s <a href="/wiki/WebKit" title="WebKit">WebKit</a> and <a href="/wiki/Internet_Explorer" title="Internet Explorer">Internet Explorer</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Trident_(layout_engine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Trident (layout engine)">Trident</a> <a href="/wiki/Browser_engine" title="Browser engine">layout engines</a>) techniques and it connects to the cloud to recognize malicious websites and software.
Several websites describe Sogou Explorer as malicious adware and recommend users not to install it, since user settings are changed and URLs are sent to servers in China, without user consent, violating user privacy. The software is reported as difficult to remove.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sogou_Voice_Recognition_Technology">Sogou Voice Recognition Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Sogou Voice Recognition Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sogou has been developing voice recognition technology since 2011. At the World Internet Conference in November 2016, CEO Wang Xiaochuan demonstrated a voice translation with both Chinese and English translation with up to 95% <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/business/3rdWuzhenWorldInternetConference/2016-11/19/content_27428750.htm">accuracy</a>.  There has been a rush to partner with manufacturers in connecting devices to the internet between competitors like Baidu. <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>-backed AI startup <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.techinasia.com/mobvoi-xiaomi-smart-tv-contract-claims">Mobvoi</a> had a deal fall through with <a href="/wiki/Xiaomi" title="Xiaomi">Xiaomi's</a> for their MiTV launch in March 2017. Xiaomi company representatives pointed to Sogou getting the business using their <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://gadgets.ndtv.com/tv/news/xiaomis-mi-tv-4a-series-launched-with-ai-based-speech-recognition-1672577">ASR</a> solution with accuracy up to 97%. Sogou is currently China's <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.xuehua.us/2018/06/30/sogou-doubles-down-on-ai-with-launch-of-real-time-devices/">largest search engine</a> by voice, as the country moves from desktop to mobile search. 
</p><p><b>Hardware Products</b>
</p><p>In 2017, Sogou through their Internet of Things subsidiary, sold over 1 million smartwatches in China. The GPS positioning smartwatch focuses on the child market.  
</p><p>On March 22, 2018, Sogou launched the Travel Translator and Travel Pen, which <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://thefirstworldhangzhou.com/News_743636.htm">sold out on the first day</a>. The company estimates it will exceed sales of their smartwatches. 
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sogou_Lip_Recognition_Technology">Sogou Lip Recognition Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sogou Lip Recognition Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 15 December 2017, Sogou debuted a lip <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.yicaiglobal.com/news/sogou-unveils-90-accurate-ai-lip-reading-system-aid-deaf-police">reading</a> program that can operate at up to 90% accuracy, beating out a similar program by Google.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Partnership_with_Microsoft_Bing">Partnership with Microsoft Bing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Partnership with Microsoft Bing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2016, Sogou partnered with <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> to use Bing to search for English results, then get the result translated back into <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://technode.com/2016/05/23/sogou-bing/">Chinese</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_Investments">Other Investments</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Other Investments">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2018, Sogou has expanded global presence of their other keyboard app, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://typany.com/about">Typany</a>, of which they own 100% of the company. To date, the app works with over 100 languages. The app goes with their strategy of dominating the voice recognition and language recognition market. 
</p><p>Along with Tencent, Sogou participated in round C and D in <a href="/wiki/Zhihu" title="Zhihu">Zhihu</a>.com. According to Sogou's IPO prospectus, their investment in Round C in 2015 was 4.8% of Zhihu. In 2014, Sogou launched Zhihu <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://technode.com/2016/05/23/sogou-bing/">search</a> and is also the default search engine on Zhihu. 
</p><p>In 2016, Sogou donated $30 million to <a href="/wiki/Tsinghua_University" title="Tsinghua University">Tsinghua University</a> in a joint partnership to build an Artificial Intelligence <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cs.tsinghua.edu.cn/publish/csen/4894/2017/20170414165140339237933/20170414165140339237933_.html">Institute</a>. It currently supports the Sogou Search Engine Technology Joint Laboratory at Tsinghua University. Located on the edge of the campus of Tsinghua University, Sogou strategically recruits and partners to train students from the most talented minds in China. Since 2015, Tsinghua has been <a href="/wiki/Tsinghua_University" title="Tsinghua University">ranked</a> as the best <a href="/wiki/Engineering" title="Engineering">engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> school in the world based on factors including total research output and performance. 
</p><p>According to the prospectus, Sogou owns 100% of Chengdu Easypay, a financial techology company specializing in micro loans. As of April 2018, Sogou is still awaiting Government approval to operate in the country. According to the company address, it is located on the 13th floor of Tencent's building in Chengdu in the High-tech zone. 
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Debut_on_NYSE">Debut on NYSE</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sogou&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Debut on NYSE">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 9, 2017, Sogou debuted on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and subsequently raised $585 million offering in a bid to accelerate the use of <a href="/wiki/Artificial_intelligence" title="Artificial intelligence">Artificial intelligence</a> in its business.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> According to the company, proceeds from the IPO will be used for research and development, sales and marketing efforts, and general corporate purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
