<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Permanent members of the United Nations Security Council</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>The <b> permanent members of the United Nations Security Council </b> (also known as the <b>Permanent Five</b>, <b>Big Five</b>, or <b>P5</b>) are the five states which the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" title="United Nations Charter">UN Charter</a> of 1945 grants a permanent seat on the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council" title="United Nations Security Council">UN Security Council.</a> 
</p>

<p>These countries were all <a href="/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II" title="Allies of World War II">allies in World War II</a>, which they won. They are also all <a href="/wiki/List_of_states_with_nuclear_weapons" title="List of states with nuclear weapons">nuclear weapons states</a>. A total of 15 <a href="/wiki/Member_states_of_the_United_Nations" title="Member states of the United Nations">UN member states</a> serve on the UNSC, the remainder of which are elected.  Only the five permanent members have the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_veto_power" title="United Nations Security Council veto power">power of veto</a>, which enables them to prevent the adoption of any "substantive" draft Council resolution, regardless of its level of international support.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:402px;"><a href="/wiki/File:1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png/400px-1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png" width="400" height="185" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png/600px-1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png/800px-1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png 2x" data-file-width="1357" data-file-height="628" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:1945_UNSC_P5_%2B_colonies.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The original permanent members of the United Nations Security Council in 1945 (dark blue) with their respective colonies and other holdings shown (pale blue).</div></div></div>
<p>At the UN's founding in 1945, the five permanent members of the Security Council were the <a href="/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_the_French_Republic" title="Provisional Government of the French Republic">French Republic</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%931949)" title="Republic of China (1912–1949)">Republic of China</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union" title="Soviet Union">Soviet Union</a>, the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>. There have been two seat changes since then, although not reflected in <a href="https://en.wikisource.org/wiki/Charter_of_the_United_Nations#Article_23" class="extiw" title="s:Charter of the United Nations">Article 23</a> of the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Charter" title="United Nations Charter">United Nations Charter</a> as it has not been accordingly amended:
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-23.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-23.jpg/300px-Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-23.jpg" width="300" height="155" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a7/Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-23.jpg/450px-Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-23.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a7/Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-23.jpg 2x" data-file-width="500" data-file-height="258" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Vladimir_Putin_at_the_Millennium_Summit_6-8_September_2000-23.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Leaders of the five permanent member states at a summit in 2000. Clockwise from front left: Chinese President <a href="/wiki/Jiang_Zemin" title="Jiang Zemin">Jiang Zemin</a>, U.S. President <a href="/wiki/Bill_Clinton" title="Bill Clinton">Bill Clinton</a>, UK Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Tony_Blair" title="Tony Blair">Tony Blair</a>, Russian President <a href="/wiki/Vladimir_Putin" title="Vladimir Putin">Vladimir Putin</a>, and French President <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Chirac" title="Jacques Chirac">Jacques Chirac</a>.</div></div></div>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/China_and_the_United_Nations" title="China and the United Nations">China's seat</a> was originally held by the <a href="/wiki/Nationalist_government" title="Nationalist government">Nationalist government</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_China_(1912%E2%80%931949)" title="Republic of China (1912–1949)">Republic of China</a>. However, it lost the <a href="/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War" title="Chinese Civil War">Chinese Civil War</a> and retreated to the island of <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a> in 1949. The <a href="/wiki/Communist_Party_of_China" title="Communist Party of China">Communist Party</a> won control of <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">mainland China</a> and established the <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">People's Republic of China</a>. In 1971, <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_Resolution_2758" title="United Nations General Assembly Resolution 2758">UN General Assembly Resolution 2758</a> recognized the Government of People's Republic of China as the legal representative of China in the UN, and gave it the seat on the Security Council that had been held by the Republic of China, which was expelled from the UN altogether. Both governments still officially <a href="/wiki/Political_status_of_Taiwan" title="Political status of Taiwan">claim one another's territory</a>. However, only 18 states continue to <a href="/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_Taiwan" title="Foreign relations of Taiwan">officially recognize the Republic of China's sovereignty</a>.</li>
<li>After the <a href="/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union" title="Dissolution of the Soviet Union">dissolution of the Soviet Union</a> in 1991, <a href="/wiki/Russia_and_the_United_Nations" title="Russia and the United Nations">Russia</a> was recognized as the <a href="/wiki/Succession_of_states" title="Succession of states">legal successor state</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Soviet_Union_and_the_United_Nations" title="Soviet Union and the United Nations">Soviet Union</a> and maintained the latter's position on the Security Council.</li></ul>
<p>Additionally, France reformed its provisional government into the <a href="/wiki/French_Fourth_Republic" title="French Fourth Republic">French Fourth Republic</a> in 1946 and later into the <a href="/wiki/French_Fifth_Republic" title="French Fifth Republic">French Fifth Republic</a> in 1958, both under the leadership of <a href="/wiki/Charles_de_Gaulle" title="Charles de Gaulle">Charles de Gaulle</a>. France maintained its seat as there was no change in its international status or recognition, although many of its <a href="/wiki/French_Community" title="French Community">overseas possessions</a> eventually became independent.
</p><p>The five permanent members of the Security Council were the victorious powers in <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">World War II</a> and have maintained the world's most powerful military forces ever since. They annually top the <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_military_expenditures" title="List of countries by military expenditures">list of countries with the highest military expenditures</a>; in 2011, they spent over <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>1 trillion combined on defense, accounting for over 60% of global military expenditures (the U.S. alone accounting for over 40%). They are also five of the world's <a href="/wiki/Arms_industry#World&#39;s_largest_arms_exporters" title="Arms industry">six largest arms exporters</a>, along with <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  and are the only nations officially recognized as "<a href="/wiki/List_of_states_with_nuclear_weapons" title="List of states with nuclear weapons">nuclear-weapon states</a>" under the <a href="/wiki/Treaty_on_the_Non-Proliferation_of_Nuclear_Weapons" title="Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons">Treaty on the Non-Proliferation of Nuclear Weapons</a> (NPT), though there are other states known or believed to be in possession of nuclear weapons.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Veto_power">Veto power</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Permanent_members_of_the_United_Nations_Security_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Veto power">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_veto_power" title="United Nations Security Council veto power">United Nations Security Council veto power</a></div>
<p>The "power of veto" refers to the <a href="/wiki/Veto" title="Veto">veto</a> power wielded solely by the permanent members, enabling them to prevent the adoption of any "substantive" draft Council resolution, regardless of the level of international support for the draft. The veto does not apply to procedural votes, which is significant in that the Security Council's permanent membership can vote against a "procedural" draft resolution, without necessarily blocking its adoption by the Council.
</p><p>The veto is exercised when any permanent member—the so-called "P5"—casts a "negative" vote on a "substantive" draft resolution. <a href="/wiki/Abstention" title="Abstention">Abstention</a> or absence from the vote by a permanent member does <i>not</i> prevent a draft resolution from being adopted.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Expansion">Expansion</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Permanent_members_of_the_United_Nations_Security_Council&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Expansion">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Reform_of_the_United_Nations_Security_Council" title="Reform of the United Nations Security Council">Reform of the United Nations Security Council</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:G4_Nations.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/G4_Nations.svg/250px-G4_Nations.svg.png" width="250" height="128" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/G4_Nations.svg/375px-G4_Nations.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bf/G4_Nations.svg/500px-G4_Nations.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="863" data-file-height="443" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:G4_Nations.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/G4_nations" title="G4 nations">G4 nations</a> that support one another's bids for permanent seats on the UN Security Council<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></div></div></div>
<p>There have been proposals suggesting the introduction of new permanent members. The candidates usually mentioned are <a href="/wiki/Brazil" title="Brazil">Brazil</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, <a href="/wiki/India" title="India">India</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>. They comprise the group of four countries known as the <a href="/wiki/G4_nations" title="G4 nations">G4 nations</a>, which mutually support one another's bids for permanent seats.
</p><p>This sort of reform has traditionally been opposed by the "<a href="/wiki/Uniting_for_Consensus" title="Uniting for Consensus">Uniting for Consensus</a>" group, which is composed primarily of nations that are regional rivals and economic competitors of the G4. The group is led by <a href="/wiki/Italy" title="Italy">Italy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Spain" title="Spain">Spain</a> (opposing Germany), <a href="/wiki/Mexico" title="Mexico">Mexico</a>, <a href="/wiki/Colombia" title="Colombia">Colombia</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> (opposing Brazil), <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> (opposing India), and <a href="/wiki/South_Korea" title="South Korea">South Korea</a> (opposing Japan), in addition to <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indonesia" title="Indonesia">Indonesia</a> and others.  Since 1992, Italy and other council members have instead proposed semi-permanent seats or expanding the number of temporary seats.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Most of the leading candidates for permanent membership are regularly elected onto the Security Council by their respective groups. Japan was elected for eleven two-year terms, Brazil for ten terms, and Germany for three terms. India has been elected to the council seven times in total, with the most recent successful bid being in 2010 after a gap of almost twenty years since 1991–92.
</p><p>In 2013, the P5 and G4 members of the UN Security Council accounted for eight of the <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_military_expenditures" title="List of countries by military expenditures">world's ten largest defense budgets</a>, according to the <a href="/wiki/Stockholm_International_Peace_Research_Institute" title="Stockholm International Peace Research Institute">Stockholm International Peace Research Institute</a> (SIPRI).
</p>
