<p><b>Nate</b> (<a href="/wiki/Hangul" title="Hangul">Hangul</a>&#58;&#160;<span lang="ko-Hang" title="Korean language text">네이트</span>) is a South Korean <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">web portal</a>, developed by <a href="/wiki/SK_Communications" title="SK Communications">SK Communications</a>. In 2003, Nate acquired <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> site <a href="/wiki/Cyworld" title="Cyworld">Cyworld</a>, and in 2004, it achieved first place in local page views with a total of 3.8 million, surpassing rival <a href="/wiki/Daum_Communications" class="mw-redirect" title="Daum Communications">Daum</a> for the first time.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="NateOn">NateOn</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Nate_(web_portal)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: NateOn">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>NateOn is an instant messaging client provided by Nate, which in 2005 first overtook <a href="/wiki/MSN_Messenger" class="mw-redirect" title="MSN Messenger">MSN Messenger</a> in terms of South Korean user numbers. During the 3rd week of May 2005, NateOn's South Korean users totalled 7.54 million, compared to 6.5 million for MSN Messenger.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
