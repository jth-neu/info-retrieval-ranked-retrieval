<p>The <b>Edinburgh Agreement</b> (full title: <i>Agreement between the United Kingdom Government and the Scottish Government on a referendum on independence for Scotland</i>) is the agreement between the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a> and the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Government">United Kingdom Government</a>, signed on 15 October 2012 at <a href="/wiki/St_Andrew%27s_House" title="St Andrew&#39;s House">St Andrew's House</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, on the terms for the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence_referendum,_2014" title="Scottish independence referendum, 2014">Scottish independence referendum, 2014</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Both governments agreed that the referendum should:
</p>
<ul><li>have a clear legal base</li>
<li>be legislated for by the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Parliament" title="Scottish Parliament">Scottish Parliament</a></li>
<li>be conducted so as to command the confidence of parliaments, government and people</li>
<li>deliver a fair test and decisive expression of the views of people in Scotland and a result that everyone will respect</li></ul>
<p>The governments agreed to promote an <a href="/wiki/Order_in_Council" title="Order in Council">Order in Council</a> under <a href="/wiki/Reserved_matters" class="mw-redirect" title="Reserved matters">Section 30</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Scotland_Act_1998" title="Scotland Act 1998">Scotland Act 1998</a> to allow a single-question referendum on <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence" title="Scottish independence">Scottish independence</a> to be held before the end of 2014 so to put beyond doubt that the Scottish Parliament can legislate for the referendum.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>The agreement was signed by <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a>, Prime Minister; <a href="/wiki/Michael_Moore_(Scottish_politician)" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael Moore (Scottish politician)">Michael Moore</a>, Secretary of State for Scotland; <a href="/wiki/Alex_Salmond" title="Alex Salmond">Alex Salmond</a>, First Minister; and <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Sturgeon" title="Nicola Sturgeon">Nicola Sturgeon</a>, Deputy First Minister.
</p><p>Whether the document was legally binding in theory is a matter of academic discussion.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>  In practice, an Order in Council was in fact approved on 12 February 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> granting constitutional legitimacy to the referendum held on 18 September 2014.
</p>
