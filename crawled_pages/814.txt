<p><b>Health in the United Kingdom</b> refers to the overall health of the population of the United Kingdom. <a href="/wiki/Smoking_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Smoking in the United Kingdom">Smoking</a>, alcohol consumption and <a href="/wiki/Obesity_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Obesity in the United Kingdom">obesity</a> in the United Kingdom are all above the <a href="/wiki/OECD" title="OECD">OECD</a> average.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_status">Health status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Health status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_expectancy">Life expectancy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Life expectancy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Life_expectancy_at_birth,_England_and_Wales,_1841_to_2011.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Life_expectancy_at_birth%2C_England_and_Wales%2C_1841_to_2011.jpg/300px-Life_expectancy_at_birth%2C_England_and_Wales%2C_1841_to_2011.jpg" width="300" height="222" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Life_expectancy_at_birth%2C_England_and_Wales%2C_1841_to_2011.jpg/450px-Life_expectancy_at_birth%2C_England_and_Wales%2C_1841_to_2011.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Life_expectancy_at_birth%2C_England_and_Wales%2C_1841_to_2011.jpg/600px-Life_expectancy_at_birth%2C_England_and_Wales%2C_1841_to_2011.jpg 2x" data-file-width="810" data-file-height="599" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Life_expectancy_at_birth,_England_and_Wales,_1841_to_2011.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Life expectancy at birth, England and Wales, 1841 to 2011.</div></div></div>
<p>In 2013 Life expectancy at birth for women was 83 years and for men 79 years.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Life expectancy in the UK is rising more slowly than in other comparable nations.  <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_government_austerity_programme#Health_and_social_care" title="United Kingdom government austerity programme">Austerity</a> may be a cause.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Underfunding of the <a href="/wiki/National_Health_Service" title="National Health Service">NHS</a> and <a href="/wiki/Social_care_in_England" title="Social care in England">Social care</a> are blamed.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  In 2018 life expectancy in the UK stopped increasing for the first time since 1982 when recording started.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Infant_mortality">Infant mortality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Infant mortality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:302px;"><a href="/wiki/File:First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg/300px-First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg" width="300" height="156" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg/450px-First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/15/First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg/600px-First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg 2x" data-file-width="825" data-file-height="429" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:First-World-Infant-Mortality-Trends.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The reduction in <a href="/wiki/Infant_mortality" title="Infant mortality">infant mortality</a> between 1960 and 2008 for the United Kingdom in comparison with France, Ireland, Sweden, Switzerland, and the United States. The overall trend has meant a large improvement in health inside the United Kingdom.</div></div></div>
<p>Infant mortality rates have been decreasing since the early 1900s, partially due to general improvements in healthcare and more recently because of improvements in midwifery and neonatal intensive care.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Obesity">Obesity</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Obesity">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Obesity_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Obesity in the United Kingdom">Obesity in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>The rising rates of <a href="/wiki/Childhood_obesity" title="Childhood obesity">childhood obesity</a> were described as a "national emergency" by Health Secretary <a href="/wiki/Jeremy_Hunt" title="Jeremy Hunt">Jeremy Hunt</a> in February 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>   28.1% of adults in the United Kingdom were recognised as clinically obese with a <a href="/wiki/Body_mass_index" title="Body mass index">Body Mass Index (BMI)</a> greater than 30 in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Smoking_rates">Smoking rates</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Smoking rates">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Smoking_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Smoking in the United Kingdom">Smoking in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>In 1974, 45% of the British population smoked. The smoking rate was down to 30% by the early-1990s, 21% by 2010, and 19.3% by 2013, the lowest level for eighty years.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, smoking rates in England had fallen to 16.9%.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cancer">Cancer</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Cancer">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Cancer_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Cancer in the United Kingdom">Cancer in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>There were 361,216 cancer diagnoses in 2014 in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Three quarters of cancers were related to smoking and drinking.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Mental_health">Mental health</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Mental health">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mental_health_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Mental health in the United Kingdom">Mental health in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>In 2014, the Adult Psychiatric Morbidity Survey reported that 17% of those surveyed in England met the criteria for a common mental disorder. About 37% of those were accessing mental health treatment. Those more severely affected were more likely to be accessing services.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In 2017 a survey found that 65% of Britons have experienced a mental health problem, with 26% having had a panic attack and 42% said they had suffered from depression.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Benefit cuts and sanctions "are having a toxic impact on mental health" according to the <a href="/wiki/UK_Council_for_Psychotherapy" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Council for Psychotherapy">UK Council for Psychotherapy</a>. Rates of severe anxiety and depression among unemployed people increased from 10.1% in June 2013 to 15.2% in March 2017.  In the general population the increase was from 3.4% to 4.1%.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Suicide">Suicide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Suicide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Suicide_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Suicide in the United Kingdom">Suicide in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>6,045; 5,608 and 5,675 people aged 15 and over died by suicide in from 2009-2011 respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-UK_suicide_rate_rises_&#39;significantly&#39;_in_2011_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-UK_suicide_rate_rises_&#39;significantly&#39;_in_2011-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-ONS_2006-2010_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ONS_2006-2010-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Drug-related_deaths">Drug-related deaths</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Drug-related deaths">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Drug-related_deaths_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Drug-related deaths in the United Kingdom">Drug-related deaths in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<h3><span id="HIV.2FAIDS"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="HIV/AIDS">HIV/AIDS</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: HIV/AIDS">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/HIV/AIDS_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="HIV/AIDS in the United Kingdom">HIV/AIDS in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>In 2015, the prevalence of <a href="/wiki/HIV/AIDS" title="HIV/AIDS">HIV</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> was estimated at 101,200 (0.16% of the population), 13% of whom are unaware of their infection.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Prevalence is highest amongst gay/bisexual men in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> with an estimated 1 in 7 living with HIV.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>An estimated 101,200 people are living with HIV in the UK. Of those, 69% are men and 31% were women.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Just under half of those living with HIV are gay or bisexual men.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> 1 in 7 gay or bisexual men in London are living with HIV, compared to 1 in 25 in the rest of the UK and less than 1 in 500 for the general population.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>6,095 people were newly diagnosed during 2015, a trend which has remained relatively constant since 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> An estimated 39% of diagnoses were late (likely to have been living with the virus for over three years).<sup id="cite_ref-:0_19-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disability">Disability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Disability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Disability_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Disability in the United Kingdom">Disability in the United Kingdom</a></div>
<p>In 2014 more than 11 million British people (excluding Northern Ireland) were reported to have a long term impairment or disability. The incidence rises with age. About 6% of children, 16% of working age adults and 45% of pensioners are reported as having a disability.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Vaccination">Vaccination</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Vaccination">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the United Kingdom, the purchase and distribution of vaccines is managed centrally, and recommended vaccines are provided for free by the NHS.<sup id="cite_ref-Freed2005_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Freed2005-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In the UK, no laws require vaccination of schoolchildren.<sup id="cite_ref-Freed2005_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Freed2005-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Health_dynamics">Health dynamics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Health dynamics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Socioeconomics">Socioeconomics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Health_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Socioeconomics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Black_Report" title="Black Report">Black Report</a>, published reluctantly by the Thatcher government in 1980, highlighted the relationship between <a href="/wiki/Socioeconomic_status" title="Socioeconomic status">socioeconomic status</a> and health outcomes. It demonstrated greater inequality of mortality between occupational classes I and V both in 1970-72 and 1959-63 than in 1949-53.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
