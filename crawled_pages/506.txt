<p>The <b>judiciary of the United Kingdom</b> are the separate judiciaries of the three legal systems in <a href="/wiki/England_and_Wales" title="England and Wales">England and Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>. However, the judges of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Special_Immigration_Appeals_Commission" title="Special Immigration Appeals Commission">Special Immigration Appeals Commission</a>, <a href="/wiki/Employment_Tribunal" class="mw-redirect" title="Employment Tribunal">Employment Tribunals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Employment_Appeal_Tribunal" title="Employment Appeal Tribunal">Employment Appeal Tribunal</a> and the <a href="/wiki/UK_tribunals_system" class="mw-redirect" title="UK tribunals system">UK tribunals system</a> do have a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>-wide jurisdiction.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Justices_of_the_Supreme_Court">Justices of the Supreme Court</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Judiciaries_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Justices of the Supreme Court">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The judges of the <a href="/wiki/Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Supreme Court of the United Kingdom</a> are known as Justices of the Supreme Court, and they are also <a href="/wiki/Privy_Council_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Privy Council of the United Kingdom">Privy Counsellors</a>. Justices of the Supreme Court are granted the courtesy title <i>Lord</i> or <i>Lady</i> for life.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Supreme Court is a relatively new Court being established in October 2009 following the Constitutional Reform Act of 2005. Formerly, the Highest Court of Appeal in the United Kingdom was the <a href="/wiki/Judicial_functions_of_the_House_of_Lords" title="Judicial functions of the House of Lords">House of Lords Appellate Committee</a> made up of <a href="/wiki/Lords_of_Appeal_in_Ordinary" title="Lords of Appeal in Ordinary">Lords of Appeal in Ordinary</a>, also known as Law Lords, which with other Lord Justices now form the Supreme Court. Such Law Lords were allowed to sit in the House of Lords and were members for life.
</p><p>The Supreme Court is headed by the <a href="/wiki/President_of_the_Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="President of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">President</a> and <a href="/wiki/Deputy_President_of_the_Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Deputy President of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Deputy President of the Supreme Court</a> and is composed of a further ten <a href="/wiki/Justice_of_the_Supreme_Court_of_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Justice of the Supreme Court of the United Kingdom">Justices of the Supreme Court</a>.
</p><p>The Justices do not wear any gowns or wigs in court, but on ceremonial occasions they wear black <a href="/wiki/Damask" title="Damask">damask</a> gowns with gold lace without a wig.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Tribunal_Judiciary">Tribunal Judiciary</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Judiciaries_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Tribunal Judiciary">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Tribunals_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="Tribunals in the United Kingdom">UK tribunal system</a> is part of the national system of <a href="/wiki/Administrative_justice" class="mw-redirect" title="Administrative justice">administrative justice</a> with tribunals classed as <a href="/wiki/Non-departmental_public_bodies" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-departmental public bodies">non-departmental public bodies</a> (NDPBs).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Though it has grown up on an <i>ad hoc</i> basis since the beginning of the twentieth century, from 2007 reforms were put in place to build a unified system with recognised judicial authority, routes of <a href="/wiki/Appeal" title="Appeal">appeal</a> and regulatory supervision, and recognised legally qualified members of tribunals as members of the judiciary who are guaranteed continued <a href="/wiki/Judicial_independence" title="Judicial independence">judicial independence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The UK tribunal system is headed by the <a href="/wiki/Senior_President_of_Tribunals" title="Senior President of Tribunals">Senior President of Tribunals</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ukpga2007c14_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ukpga2007c14-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
