<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Forestle</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Forestle</b> was an ecologically inspired <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> created by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Christian_Kroll&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Christian Kroll (page does not exist)">Christian Kroll</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wittenberg" title="Wittenberg">Wittenberg</a>, Germany, in 2008 and discontinued in 2011. Forestle supported rainforest conservation through donations of ad revenue and aimed to reduce <a href="/wiki/CO2_emissions" class="mw-redirect" title="CO2 emissions">CO2 emissions</a>. It is quite similar to the search engine Ecosia, which plants new trees with its ad revenue. Forestle was briefly associated with <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> before associating with <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Contributions_to_sustainability">Contributions to sustainability</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forestle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Contributions to sustainability">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Forestle saves 0.1 square meters (about 0.12 square yards) of <a href="/wiki/Rain_forest" class="mw-redirect" title="Rain forest">rain forest</a> per search event. It guarantees to donate 90% of its advertisement revenue to the <b>Adopt an Acre</b> program of is partner organization <b><a href="/wiki/The_Nature_Conservancy" title="The Nature Conservancy">The Nature Conservancy</a></b>. The Nature Conservancy uses these donations by Forestle to sustain rain forests. As of December 9, 2009, about 2,910,000 square meters of rain forest have been saved.<sup id="cite_ref-forestle-area-protected_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forestle-area-protected-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> As of November 20, 2010 about 9,250,000 square meters have been saved.
</p><p>A Forestle search is also essentially CO<sub>2</sub>-neutral, as Forestle.org offsets the carbon-dioxide emissions caused by electricity consumed by all Forestle servers, the network infrastructure as well as the computers of each user by purchasing an equivalent amount of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy" title="Renewable energy">renewable energy</a> certificates.<sup id="cite_ref-proofCO2_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-proofCO2-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The certificates are purchased from a part of the 10% of revenue left after conserving rain forest. This makes Forestle one of the few web search sites that are green certified.
</p><p>The number of search requests on Forestle.org continues to increase significantly:<sup id="cite_ref-monthly-report_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-monthly-report-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Within two months, it increased more than sixfold from about 4,000 per day on average in December 2008 to more than 24,000 per day in February 2009. The report about Forestle in a major German newspaper<sup id="cite_ref-FrankfurterRundschau_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FrankfurterRundschau-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> end of February 2009 transiently boosted the number of search events on Forestle.org within a week (3 March 2009) close to its all-time maximum.<sup id="cite_ref-Alexa_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Alexa-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> As of December 2009, the number of search events exceeds 200,000 per day.
</p><p>The degree of impact of Forestle.org and similar kinds of 'green' search engines is discussed; the (now removed) note on Forestle to not click on advertisements to 'help' achieving larger advertisement revenues was particularly criticized.<sup id="cite_ref-degreeOfImpact_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-degreeOfImpact-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forestle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The site pioneers a thumbnail <b>website preview</b> for all search results. Moreover, it offers a search with so-called <b>indicators</b>, for instance, one may directly search for '<a href="/wiki/Basic_Income" class="mw-redirect" title="Basic Income">Basic Income</a>' on Wikipedia (instead of the entire WWW) by typing 'Wikipedia::Basic Income'.<sup id="cite_ref-indicator_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-indicator-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The language chosen for indicator search is automatically associated, so a search on the US web site  <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://us.Forestle.org">http://us.Forestle.org</a> or on the British web site <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://uk.Forestle.org">http://uk.Forestle.org</a> leads to a search on English Wikipedia <a class="external free" href="http://en.wikipedia.org">http://en.wikipedia.org</a> and a search on the German web site <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://de.Forestle.org">http://de.Forestle.org</a> (or on the Austrian Website <a rel="nofollow" class="external free" href="http://at.Forestle.org">http://at.Forestle.org</a>)  leads a search on German Wikipedia <a class="external free" href="http://de.wikipedia.org">http://de.wikipedia.org</a>. Forestle also provides several browser plugins, can be added to iGoogle and is available in English and German (full versions) as well as in Spanish and Dutch (details partially in English)
.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Utopia_Award">Utopia Award</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forestle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Utopia Award">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On November 27, 2009, Forestle received the Utopia Award<sup id="cite_ref-forestleUtopia2009_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forestleUtopia2009-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> as an exemplary organisation enabling us to live more sustainably. The Jury emphasizes that Forestle "offers a simple and strong possibility to contribute to protect existing rain forest through the use of an everyday [...] service" and that "thereby Forestle unfolds a high effectiveness and sharpens the consumers' sense for the impact of consumer behavior".<sup id="cite_ref-forestleUtopia2009reason_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forestleUtopia2009reason-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_engine_partners">Search engine partners</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forestle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Search engine partners">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Forestle was associated to <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> until Google revoked the site's search functionality after four days due to a dispute over whether their terms of service were being broken.<sup id="cite_ref-google_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-google-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Forestle.org states that Google did not actually give reasons for stopping the association.<sup id="cite_ref-forestlevsgoogle_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-forestlevsgoogle-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> At the time, Forestle posted a message on their website stating that Google had contacted them and explained the reason for banning Forestle from using their Google Custom Search.<sup id="cite_ref-google_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-google-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The action by Google to not further support Forestle immediately drew international attention.<sup id="cite_ref-immediateattentionInquirer_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-immediateattentionInquirer-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-immediateattentionSoftPedia_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-immediateattentionSoftPedia-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Details about the conflict between Google and Forestle are debated.<sup id="cite_ref-discussion_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-discussion-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Forestle became associated with <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a> later.<sup id="cite_ref-thumbnail_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-thumbnail-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Forestle_Discontinued">Forestle Discontinued</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Forestle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Forestle Discontinued">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Forestle was discontinued and redirected to a similar search engine <a href="/wiki/Ecosia" title="Ecosia">Ecosia</a> on January 1 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
