<p><b>Sky UK</b> (formerly <b>British Sky Broadcasting Limited</b>, <b>BSkyB</b> and <b>Sky</b>) is a telecommunications company which serves the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>. Sky provides television and broadband Internet services, fixed line and mobile telephone services to consumers and businesses in the United Kingdom. It is the UK's largest
pay-TV broadcaster with 11 million customers as of 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It was the UK's most popular digital TV service until it was overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a> in April 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Its corporate headquarters are in <a href="/wiki/Isleworth" title="Isleworth">Isleworth</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Formed in November 1990 by the equal merger of <a href="/wiki/Sky_Television_plc" title="Sky Television plc">Sky Television</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Satellite_Broadcasting" title="British Satellite Broadcasting">British Satellite Broadcasting</a>, Sky became the UK's largest digital subscription television company. Following Sky's 2014 acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Sky_Italia" title="Sky Italia">Sky Italia</a> and a majority 90.04% interest in <a href="/wiki/Sky_Deutschland" title="Sky Deutschland">Sky Deutschland</a> in November 2014, its holding company British Sky Broadcasting Group plc changed its name to <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">Sky plc</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Sky_plc_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Sky_plc-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The UK subsidiary's name was changed from British Sky Broadcasting Limited to Sky UK Limited, and continues to trade as Sky.
</p><p>Sky UK Limited is a wholly owned subsidiary of <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">Sky plc</a>, with its current company directors being Andrew Griffith and Christopher Taylor.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Griffith acts as the Chief Financial Officer (CFO) and the Managing Director for the commercial businesses division.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<p>The present service can trace its heritage back to 1989, when BSkyB's predecessors <a href="/wiki/Sky_Television_plc" title="Sky Television plc">Sky Television plc</a> and <a href="/wiki/British_Satellite_Broadcasting" title="British Satellite Broadcasting">British Satellite Broadcasting</a> encrypted their respective film channels – <a href="/wiki/Sky_Movies" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky Movies">Sky Movies</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Movie_Channel_(UK)" title="The Movie Channel (UK)">The Movie Channel</a> which required viewers to get decoding equipment and a subscription to watch the channels. After the two companies merged, subscribers could get access to both channels, and later the sports channel <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a> also became encrypted.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Premier_League_football">Premier League football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Premier League football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports" title="Sky Sports">Sky Sports</a></div>
<p>In the autumn of 1991, talks were held for the broadcast rights for <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> for a five-year period, from the 1992 season.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> were the current rights holders, and fought hard to retain the new rights. ITV had increased its offer from £18m to £34m per year to keep control of the rights.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  BSkyB joined forces with the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> to make a counter bid. The <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> was given the highlights of most of the matches, while BSkyB paying £304m for the <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> rights, would give them a monopoly of all live matches, up to 60 per year from the 1992 season.
<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Murdoch described sport as a "battering ram" for pay-television, providing a strong customer base.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  A few weeks after the deal, ITV went to the High Court to get an injunction as it believed their bid details had been leaked before the decision was taken. <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a> also asked the Office of Fair Trading to investigate since it believed Rupert Murdoch's media empire via its newspapers had influenced the deal.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> A few days later neither action took effect, ITV believed BSkyB was telephoned and informed of its £262m bid, and <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> advised BSkyB to increase its counter bid.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>BSkyB retained the rights paying £670m 1997–2001 deal, but was challenged by On Digital<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> for the rights from 2001–2004, thus were forced to £1.1 billion which gave them 66 live games a year.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following a lengthy legal battle with the European Commission, which deemed the exclusivity of the rights to be against the interests of competition and the consumer, BSkyB's monopoly came to an end from the <a href="/wiki/2007%E2%80%9308_in_English_football" title="2007–08 in English football">2007–08 season</a>. In May 2006, the Irish broadcaster <a href="/wiki/Setanta_Sports" title="Setanta Sports">Setanta Sports</a> was awarded two of the six Premier League packages that the English FA offered to broadcasters. Sky picked up the remaining four for £1.3bn.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
In February 2015, Sky bid £4.2bn for a package of 120 premier league games across the three seasons from 2016. This represented an increase of 70% on the previous contract and was said to be £1bn more than the company had expected to pay. The move has been followed by staff cuts, increased subscription prices (including 9% in Sky's family package) and the dropping of the 3D channel.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky_Multichannels">Sky Multichannels</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Sky Multichannels">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sky_Multichannels" title="Sky Multichannels">Sky Multichannels</a></div>
<p>In September 1993, BSkyB launched <a href="/wiki/Sky_Multichannels" title="Sky Multichannels">Sky Multichannels</a> which was the present digital platform's analogue predecessor. Sky Multichannels was a subscription package that gave access not only to Sky's own channels but also those of third party broadcasters.
</p><p>The service started on 1 September 1993. It was based on an idea by then CEO <a href="/wiki/Sam_Chisholm" title="Sam Chisholm">Sam Chisholm</a> and chairman <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Murdoch" title="Rupert Murdoch">Rupert Murdoch</a> of converting the company business strategy to an entirely fee-based concept. The new package included four channels formerly available <a href="/wiki/Free-to-air" title="Free-to-air">free-to-air</a>, broadcasting on Astra's satellites, as well as introducing new channels.<sup id="cite_ref-fundinguniverse_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fundinguniverse-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The service continued until the closure of BSkyB's analogue service on 27 September 2001,<sup id="cite_ref-SkyAnalogueClosure_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SkyAnalogueClosure-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> due to the expansion of the Sky Digital platform after its launch three years ago. Some of the channels did broadcast either in the clear or soft encrypted (whereby a Videocrypt decoder was required to decode, without a subscription card) prior to their addition to the Sky Multichannels package.<sup id="cite_ref-VarietyChannels_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VarietyChannels-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-TVArkLeaflets_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TVArkLeaflets-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Within two months of the launch, BSkyB gained 400,000 new subscribers, with the majority taking at least one premium channel as well,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> which helped BSkyB reach 3.5 million households by mid-1994. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Grade" title="Michael Grade">Michael Grade</a> criticised the operations in front of the Select Committee on National Heritage, mainly for the lack of original programming on many of the new channels.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Launch_of_Sky_Digital">Launch of Sky Digital</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Launch of Sky Digital">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>BSkyB's digital service was officially launched on 1 October 1998 under the name Sky Digital, although small-scale tests were carried out before then. At this time the use of the Sky Digital brand made an important distinction between the new service and Sky's analogue services. Key selling points were the improvement in picture and sound quality, increased number of channels and an interactive service branded <a href="/wiki/Open...." title="Open....">Open....</a> now called <a href="/wiki/Sky_Active" title="Sky Active">Sky Active</a>, BSkyB competed with the <a href="/wiki/ITV_Digital" title="ITV Digital">ONdigital</a> (later <i>ITV Digital</i>) terrestrial offering and cable services. Within 30 days, over 100,000 digiboxes had been sold, which help bolstered BSkyB's decision to give away free digiboxes and minidishes from May 1999.
</p><p>In addition to most channels from the Sky Multichannels package, Sky Digital launched with several new channels that were exclusive to the digital offer.
</p><p>The switch-over from analogue to digital proceeded relatively quickly. In Q3 1998, there were 6 million 'multichannel' TV homes in the UK (i.e. homes that watch television other than the traditional analogue terrestrial), and over half of these homes watched television using BSkyB's analogue service. BSkyB's digital service surpassed the analogue service in terms of subscribers in late 1999.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By June 2000 the service had 3.6 million subscribers, which gave BSkyB 8.988 million subscribers across all platforms. This substantial growth reflected BSkyB’s 34% share of viewers in multi-channel homes (up from 13.4% from 1999).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>BSkyB's analogue service ended in October 2001, and the digital service would eventually be marketed as just 'Sky'.
</p><p>By June 2005, the number of digital subscribers increase to 7.8m, while it produced 38,375 hours of sport in 2005<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s">2010s</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sky's direct-to-home satellite service became available in 10 million homes in 2010, Europe's first pay-TV platform in to achieve that milestone. Confirming it had reached its target, the broadcaster said its reach into 36% of households in the UK represented an audience of more than 25m people. The target was first announced in August 2004, since then an additional 2.4m customers had subscribed to Sky's direct-to-home service. Media commentators had debated whether the figure could be reached as the growth in subscriber numbers elsewhere in Europe flattened.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <i><a href="/wiki/Daily_Mail" title="Daily Mail">Daily Mail</a></i> newspaper reported in 2012 that the UK government's benefits agency was checking claimants' "Sky TV bills to establish if a woman in receipt of benefits as a single mother is wrongly claiming to be living alone" – as, it claimed, subscription to sports channels would betray a man's presence in the household.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> In December, the UK’s parliament heard a claim that a subscription to Sky was ‘often damaging’, along with alcohol, tobacco and gambling. <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> MP <a href="/wiki/Alec_Shelbrooke" title="Alec Shelbrooke">Alec Shelbrooke</a> was proposing the payments of benefits and tax credits on a "Welfare Cash Card", in the style of the American <a href="/wiki/Supplemental_Nutrition_Assistance_Program" title="Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program">Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program</a>, that could be used to buy only "essentials".<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 1 March 2018, it was reported that Sky UK had concluded successful negotiations with <a href="/wiki/Netflix" title="Netflix">Netflix</a> to offer Sky subscribers with access to the international streaming service.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Comcast" title="Comcast">Comcast</a>, the largest cable TV provider in the USA, outbid <a href="/wiki/21st_Century_Fox" title="21st Century Fox">21st Century Fox</a> and its backer, <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">Disney</a>, on September 22, 2018 in an auction for control of  <a class="mw-selflink selflink">Sky UK</a>. Its shareholders have until October 11, 2018 to accept or reject the offer.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Digital_terrestrial_television">Digital terrestrial television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Digital terrestrial television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sky initially faced competition from the <a href="/wiki/ONdigital" class="mw-redirect" title="ONdigital">ONdigital</a> <a href="/wiki/Digital_terrestrial_television" title="Digital terrestrial television">digital terrestrial television</a> service (later renamed <a href="/wiki/ITV_Digital" title="ITV Digital">ITV Digital</a>). ITV Digital failed for numerous reasons, including, but not limited to numerous administrative and technical failures, nervous investors after a large down-turn in the advertising market and the <a href="/wiki/Dot_com_crash" class="mw-redirect" title="Dot com crash">dot com crash</a>, and Sky's aggressive marketing and domination of premium sporting rights.
</p><p>While Sky had been excluded from being a part of the ONdigital consortium, thereby making them a competitor by default, Sky was able to join ITV Digital's <a href="/wiki/Free-to-air" title="Free-to-air">free-to-air</a> replacement, <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a>, in which it holds an equal stake with the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>, <a href="/wiki/ITV_plc" title="ITV plc">ITV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arqiva" title="Arqiva">Arqiva</a>. Prior to October 2005, three Sky channels were available on this platform: <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky3" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky3">Sky Three</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_News" title="Sky Sports News">Sky Sports News</a>. Initially, Sky provided <a href="/wiki/Sky_Travel" title="Sky Travel">Sky Travel</a> to the service. However, this was replaced by <a href="/wiki/Sky3" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky3">Sky Three</a> on 31 October 2005, which was itself later re-branded as 'Pick TV' in 2011.
</p><p>On 8 February 2007, Sky announced its intention to replace its three free-to-air digital terrestrial channels with four subscription channels. It was proposed that these channels would offer a range of content from the Sky portfolio including sport (including English Premier League Football), films, entertainment and news.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>  The announcement came a day after <a href="/wiki/Setanta_Sports" title="Setanta Sports">Setanta Sports</a> confirmed that it would launch in March as a subscription service on the digital terrestrial platform, and on the same day that NTL's services re-branded as <a href="/wiki/Virgin_Media" title="Virgin Media">Virgin Media</a>. However, industry sources believe Sky will be forced to shelve plans to withdraw its channels from Freeview and replace them with subscription channels, due to possible lost advertising revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Video_on_demand">Video on demand</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Video on demand">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/On_Demand_(Sky)" title="On Demand (Sky)">On Demand (Sky)</a></div>
<p>Sky initially faced increased competition from telecommunications providers to deliver pay television services over existing telephone lines using <a href="/wiki/ADSL" class="mw-redirect" title="ADSL">ADSL</a>. Such providers are able to offer "triple-play" or "quad-play" packages combining landline telephone, broadband Internet, mobile telephone and pay television services.
</p><p>To compete with these providers, in October 2005, Sky bought the <a href="/wiki/Broadband" title="Broadband">broadband</a> <a href="/wiki/Internet_service_provider" title="Internet service provider">Internet service provider</a> <a href="/wiki/Easynet" title="Easynet">Easynet</a>  for £211&#160;million. This acquisition allowed Sky to start offering a Sky-branded broadband service as well as a "<a href="/wiki/Triple_play_(telecommunications)" title="Triple play (telecommunications)">triple play</a>" package combining satellite television, land-line telephone and Broadband service. Sky also offers some streaming live TV channels to a computer using Microsoft's <a href="/wiki/Silverlight" class="mw-redirect" title="Silverlight">Silverlight</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2012, Sky released an update to its <a href="/wiki/Sky_Anytime" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky Anytime">Sky Anytime</a> service. This update offers customers the chance to buy and rent films from the Sky Store.
</p><p>On 26 September 2012, Sky relaunched its "Anytime+" on-demand-via-broadband service as "On Demand" as the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>’s <a href="/wiki/IPlayer" class="mw-redirect" title="IPlayer">iPlayer</a> joined the line-up of channels offering catch-up TV on the company’s <a href="/wiki/Sky%2BHD" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky+HD">Sky+ HD</a> box – linked to a router, the signal from which was recorded before viewing. The BBC was making the preceding week’s programmes available alongside <a href="/wiki/ITV_(TV_network)" title="ITV (TV network)">ITV</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_4" title="Channel 4">Channel 4</a>’s <a href="/wiki/All_4" title="All 4">All 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Channel_5_(UK)" title="Channel 5 (UK)">Channel 5</a> and the partly BBC Worldwide-owned <a href="/wiki/UKTV" title="UKTV">UKTV</a>, as well as Sky’s own channels.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Game_consoles">Game consoles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Game consoles">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 29 May 2009, it was confirmed that <a href="/wiki/Sky_Go" title="Sky Go">Sky Go</a> would be made available on the <a href="/wiki/Xbox_360" title="Xbox 360">Xbox 360</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>  In November 2011 Sony Computer Entertainment struck a deal with Sky to bring some of its shows to the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_Store" title="PlayStation Store">PlayStation Store</a> Video Store. Users are able buy individual TV episodes in SD or HD.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> On 3 December 2014, Sky Go became available on the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a>, followed by the <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_3" title="PlayStation 3">PlayStation 3</a> on 29 January 2015.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (May 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky_Broadband">Sky Broadband</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Sky Broadband">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sky_Broadband" title="Sky Broadband">Sky Broadband</a></div>
<p>On 1 March 2013, it was announced that Sky would buy <a href="/wiki/O2_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="O2 (United Kingdom)">O2</a>'s and <a href="/wiki/Be_Un_Limited" title="Be Un Limited">Be</a>'s broadband services from <a href="/wiki/Telef%C3%B3nica" title="Telefónica">Telefónica</a> for £180 million up front plus another £20 million once customers have been transferred. Telefónica said the deal would allow it to concentrate on providing better mobile services, including rolling out 4G.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sky offers superfast broadband, Sky Fibre, using ADSL2+ technology and fibre-optic, which are provided by <a href="/wiki/BT_Group" title="BT Group">BT</a> <a href="/wiki/Openreach" title="Openreach">Openreach</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky_Mobile">Sky Mobile</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Sky Mobile">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 21 October 2016, it was announced that public pre-registration for Sky's new mobile network, Sky Mobile, would take place from 31 October 2016. The network will operate as a Full <a href="/wiki/Mobile_virtual_network_operator" title="Mobile virtual network operator">MVNO</a>, utilising the <a href="/wiki/O2_(UK)" title="O2 (UK)">O2</a> radio access network infrastructure.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
On 5 January 2017 Sky Mobile went live to the public across the UK. Coining itself as the Smarter Network, with tariffs mainly focused on data rather than traditional calls &amp; text, effectively saving consumers money in wasted unused minutes and texts. 1GB data costs £10.00 per month while 5GB costs £15.00 per month with 10GB data costing £20.00 per month. With all those data tariffs, the customer can choose from two different call &amp; text packages with the 'Pay as you use' costing 10p per minute of calls and 10p per text message sent or £10.00 per month for Unlimited Calls &amp; Texts. With the latter being free for new or existing Sky TV customers using the Sky Mobile network.
</p><p>As of 30 March 2017, Sky Mobile is offering handset deals. Products are available from manufacturers such as <a href="/wiki/Samsung" title="Samsung">Samsung</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a>, <a href="/wiki/LG_Corporation" title="LG Corporation">LG</a> and <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products">Products</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Products">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky_Digibox">Sky Digibox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Sky Digibox">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sky launched with a set top box known as the <a href="/wiki/Digibox" title="Digibox">Sky Digibox</a>, using the Slogans <i>"What do you want to watch?", "Entertainment your way"</i> and the current slogan <i>"Believe in Better"</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Believe_in_Better_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Believe_in_Better-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> This was followed by Sky+, a <a href="/wiki/Digital_video_recorder" title="Digital video recorder">digital video recorder</a> with an internal <a href="/wiki/Hard_drive" class="mw-redirect" title="Hard drive">hard drive</a> which allows viewers to 'pause live television' (by switching from a live feed to a paused real-time recording that can be restarted at any point) and schedule programs to record in the future.
</p><p>In later years the <a href="/wiki/Sky%2B" title="Sky+">Sky+</a> box and then the <a href="/wiki/Sky%2B_HD" title="Sky+ HD">Sky+ HD</a> box replaced the original <a href="/wiki/Digibox" title="Digibox">Digibox</a>. The first photos of a prototype Sky HD box began appearing in magazines in August 2005. Sky launched <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">HDTV</a> services in May 2006. All Sky+ HD boxes incorporate a version of Sky+ using a 300GB, 500GB, or 1TB hard drive (of which 160GB, 250GB or 500GB are available to the user) to accommodate the necessary extra data.
</p>
<h3><span id="Sky.2B"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky+">Sky+</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Sky+">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sky initially charged an additional subscription fee for using a Sky+ PVR with their service; waiving the charge for subscribers whose package included two or more premium channels. This changed as from 1 July 2007, and now customers that subscribe to any Sky package have Sky+ included at no extra charge. Customers that do not subscribe to Sky's channels can still pay a monthly fee to enable Sky+ functions.
</p><p>In January 2010 Sky discontinued the Sky+ Box, limited the standard Sky Box to Multiroom upgrade only and started to issue the Sky+ HD Box as standard, thus giving all new subscribers the functions of Sky+. In February 2011 Sky discontinued the non-HD variant of its Multiroom box, offering a smaller version of the SkyHD box without Sky+ functionality.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In September 2007, Sky launched a new TV advertising campaign targeting Sky+ at women. As of 31 March 2008, Sky had 3,393,000 Sky+ users.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Sky.2B_HD"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky+_HD">Sky+ HD</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Sky+ HD">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sky%2B_HD" title="Sky+ HD">Sky+ HD</a></div>
<p>Sky launched its <a href="/wiki/HDTV" class="mw-redirect" title="HDTV">HDTV</a> service, <a href="/wiki/Sky%2B_HD" title="Sky+ HD">Sky+ HD</a>, on 22 May 2006. Prior to its launch, Sky claimed that 40,000 people had registered to receive the HD service. In the week before the launch, rumours started to surface that Sky was having supply issues with its set top box (STB) from manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Thomson_SA" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomson SA">Thomson</a>. On Thursday 18 May 2006, and continuing through the weekend before launch, people were reporting that Sky had either cancelled or rescheduled its installation. Finally, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> reported that 17,000 customers had yet to receive the service due to failed deliveries.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup> On 31 March 2012, Sky announced the total number of homes with Sky+ HD was 4,222,000.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2012, Sky released an update to its Sky Anytime service. This update offers customers the chance to buy and rent films from the Sky Store. In June 2012, Sky launched a new EPG for Sky+ HD boxes. The update included a new modernised look and improved functionality. As of 1 October 2012, Sky Anytime was rebranded as Sky On Demand which included <a href="/wiki/ITV_Player" class="mw-redirect" title="ITV Player">ITV Player</a> and <a href="/wiki/Demand_5" class="mw-redirect" title="Demand 5">Demand 5</a>. <a href="/wiki/BBC_iPlayer" title="BBC iPlayer">BBC iPlayer</a> followed in late Autumn with 4oD which changed to <a href="/wiki/All_4" title="All 4">All 4</a> on 30 March 2015, launched in early 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky_3D">Sky 3D</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Sky 3D">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sky_3D" title="Sky 3D">Sky 3D</a></div>
<p>Sky began to broadcast programmes in <a href="/wiki/3D_television" title="3D television">3D</a> in April 2010. This included new 3D channels, including a <i>Sky Sports 3D</i> and <i>Sky Movies 3D</i>. Sky previously experimented with 3D broadcasting by broadcasting an <a href="/wiki/Arsenal_F.C." title="Arsenal F.C.">Arsenal</a> vs <a href="/wiki/Manchester_United_F.C." title="Manchester United F.C.">Manchester United</a> football game live in 3D in nine pubs situated throughout the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sky_Q">Sky Q</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Sky Q">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On 18 November 2015, Sky announced Sky Q, a range of products and services to be available in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The Sky Q range consists of three set top boxes (Sky Q 1TB, Sky Q 2TB and Sky Q Mini), a broadband router (Sky Q Hub) and mobile applications.
</p><p>The Sky Q set top boxes introduce a new user interface, <a href="/wiki/Hotspot_(Wi-Fi)" title="Hotspot (Wi-Fi)">Wi-Fi hotspot</a> functionality, <a href="/wiki/Power-line_communication" title="Power-line communication">Power-line</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bluetooth" title="Bluetooth">Bluetooth</a> connectivity and a new touch-sensitive remote control.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The Sky Q Mini set top boxes connect to the Sky Q set top boxes with a Wi-Fi or Power-line connection rather than receive their own satellite feeds. This allows all set top boxes in a household to share recordings and other media.
</p><p>Sky Q became available to order on 9 February 2016 and is currently the only range available to new customers.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=21" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Sky</b> (formerly marketed as <b>Sky Digital</b>) is the brand name for <a href="/wiki/Sky_plc" title="Sky plc">Sky plc</a>'s United Kingdom digital <a href="/wiki/Satellite_television" title="Satellite television">satellite television</a> and <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications" class="mw-redirect" title="Telecommunications">telecommunications</a> services. <h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcasting">Broadcasting</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=22" title="Edit section: Broadcasting">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Transmission">Transmission</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=23" title="Edit section: Transmission">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>When Sky Digital was launched in 1998 the new service used the <a href="/wiki/Astra_2A" title="Astra 2A">Astra 2A</a> satellite which was located at the 28.2°E orbital position, unlike the analogue service which was broadcast from <a href="/wiki/Astra_19.2%C2%B0E" title="Astra 19.2°E">19.2°E</a>. This was subsequently followed by more Astra satellites as well as <a href="/wiki/Eutelsat" title="Eutelsat">Eutelsat</a>'s Eurobird 1 (now <a href="/wiki/Eutelsat_33C" title="Eutelsat 33C">Eutelsat 33C</a>) at 28.5°E), enabled the company to launch a new all-digital service, Sky, with the potential to carry hundreds of television and radio channels.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> The old position was shared with broadcasters from several European countries, while the new position at 28.5°E came to be used almost exclusively for channels that broadcast to the United Kingdom.
</p><p>New Astra satellites joined the position in 2000 and 2001, and the number of channels available to customers increased accordingly. This trend continued with the launch of Eurobird 1 (now Eutelsat 33C) in 2001. Additionally, some channels occasionally received new numbering &#8212; However, in early 2006, the majority of channels received new numbering, with some receiving single digit changes, whilst others received new numbers entirely.
</p><p>It was the country's most popular digital TV service until it was overtaken by <a href="/wiki/Freeview_(UK)" title="Freeview (UK)">Freeview</a> in April 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-Freeview_digital_overtakes_Sky_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Freeview_digital_overtakes_Sky-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Sky is currently transmitted from the <a href="/wiki/Astra_(satellites)" class="mw-redirect" title="Astra (satellites)">Astra</a> satellites located at <a href="/wiki/Astra_28.2%C2%B0E" title="Astra 28.2°E">28.2° east</a> (<a href="/wiki/Astra_2A" title="Astra 2A">2A</a>/<a href="/wiki/Astra_2C" title="Astra 2C">2C</a>/<a href="/wiki/Astra_2E" title="Astra 2E">2E</a>/<a href="/wiki/Astra_2F" title="Astra 2F">2F</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Eutelsat" title="Eutelsat">Eutelsat</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Eutelsat_33C" title="Eutelsat 33C">Eutelsat 33C</a> satellite at 28.5°E.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Low-noise_block_converter">Low-noise block converter</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Low-noise block converter">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Provided is a universal <a href="/wiki/Ku_band" title="Ku band">Ku band</a> <a href="/wiki/Low-noise_block_converter" class="mw-redirect" title="Low-noise block converter">LNB</a> (9.75/10.600&#160;GHz) which is fitted at the end of the dish and pointed at the correct satellite constellation; most digital receivers will receive the free to air channels. Some broadcasts are free-to-air and unencrypted, some are encrypted but do not require a monthly subscription (known as free-to-view), some are encrypted and require a monthly subscription, and some are pay-per-view services. To view the encrypted content a <a href="/wiki/VideoGuard" title="VideoGuard">VideoGuard</a> UK equipped receiver (all of which are dedicated to the Sky service, and cannot be used to decrypt other services) needs to be used. Unofficial <a href="/wiki/Conditional-access_module" title="Conditional-access module">CAMs</a> are now available to view the service, although use of
them breaks the user's contract with Sky and invalidates the user's rights to use the card.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Standard_definition_broadcasts">Standard definition broadcasts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Standard definition broadcasts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sky's standard definition broadcasts are in <a href="/wiki/Digital_Video_Broadcasting" title="Digital Video Broadcasting">DVB</a>-compliant <a href="/wiki/MPEG-2" title="MPEG-2">MPEG-2</a>, with the <a href="/wiki/Sky_Cinema" title="Sky Cinema">Sky Cinema</a> and <a href="/wiki/Sky_Box_Office" title="Sky Box Office">Sky Box Office</a> channels including optional <a href="/wiki/Dolby_Digital" title="Dolby Digital">Dolby Digital</a> soundtracks for recent films, although these are only accessible with a Sky+ box. Sky+ HD material is broadcast using <a href="/wiki/MPEG-4" title="MPEG-4">MPEG-4</a> and most of the HD material uses the <a href="/wiki/DVB-S2" title="DVB-S2">DVB-S2</a> standard. Interactive services and 7-day <a href="/wiki/EPG" class="mw-redirect" title="EPG">EPG</a> use the proprietary OpenTV system, with set-top boxes including <a href="/wiki/Modem" title="Modem">modems</a> for a return path. <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a>, amongst other channels, provides a pseudo-<a href="/wiki/Video_on_demand" title="Video on demand">video on demand</a> interactive service by broadcasting looping video streams.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Digital_satellite_receivers">Digital satellite receivers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Digital satellite receivers">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Sky utilises the <a href="/wiki/VideoGuard" title="VideoGuard">VideoGuard</a> pay-TV scrambling system owned by <a href="/wiki/NDS_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="NDS Group">NDS</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Cisco_Systems" title="Cisco Systems">Cisco Systems</a> company. There are tight controls over use of VideoGuard decoders; they are not available as stand-alone <a href="/wiki/Digital_Video_Broadcasting" title="Digital Video Broadcasting">DVB</a> CAMs (<a href="/wiki/Conditional-access_module" title="Conditional-access module">conditional-access modules</a>). Sky has design authority over all digital satellite receivers capable of receiving their service. The receivers, though designed and built by different manufacturers, must conform to the same user interface look-and-feel as all the others. This extends to the <a href="/wiki/Personal_video_recorder" class="mw-redirect" title="Personal video recorder">Personal video recorder</a> (PVR) offering (branded <a href="/wiki/Sky%2B" title="Sky+">Sky+</a>).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Electronic_programme_guide">Electronic programme guide</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Electronic programme guide">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Technology">Technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Sky maintains an <a href="/wiki/Electronic_programme_guide" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic programme guide">electronic programme guide</a> (EPG) which provides information about upcoming programmes and a list of channels. Channels available on Sky are assigned a three digit <a href="/wiki/Logical_channel_number" class="mw-redirect" title="Logical channel number">logical channel number</a> which can be entered on a remote control to access the channel and determines in what order channels are listed.
</p><p>The EPG differs depending on the viewer's location due to limited regional availability of certain channels or conditions relating to their <a href="/wiki/Must-carry" title="Must-carry">must-carry</a> status. For example, this ensures that viewers get access to the correct BBC or ITV region or that <a href="/wiki/S4C" title="S4C">S4C</a> gets a prominent listing in Wales.
</p><p>All channels are grouped into categories depending on their content. What section of the EPG a channel gets allocated is determined by rules set up by Sky.
</p><p>Sky has no veto over the presence of channels on their EPG, with open access being an enforced part of their operating licence from <a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">Ofcom</a>. Any channel which can get carriage on a suitable beam of a satellite at 28° East is entitled to access to Sky's EPG for a fee, ranging from £15–100,000. Third-party channels which opt for encryption receive discounts ranging from reduced price to free EPG entries, free carriage on a Sky leased transponder, or actual payment for being carried. However, even in this case, Sky does not carry any control over the channel's content or carriage issues such as picture quality.
</p><p>In October 2007, Sky announced that they would not accept new applications to launch channel on their EPG, citing "very significant memory constraints" on many of its older digiboxes.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2012, Sky launched a new EPG for Sky+ HD boxes. The update boasts a new modernised look and improved functionality.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Numbering_system">Numbering system</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=29" title="Edit section: Numbering system">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>The EPG numbering is altered frequently when new channels launch or receive new numbers. A few times, the EPG has been substantially altered. For example:
</p>
<ul><li>In early 2006, most channels received new numbering. This shake-up intended to split up the original ten categories in sixteen. For example, several channels that had been listed under the 'Entertainment' category where split off into a new 'Lifestyle &amp; Culture' category while the 'News &amp; Documentaries' category was split into two. The 'Specialist' category, which had included shopping, dating, gambling, international and adult channels was split into several genres.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Following the integration of <a href="/wiki/Living_TV_Group" title="Living TV Group">Living TV Group</a> into <a href="/wiki/BSkyB" class="mw-redirect" title="BSkyB">Sky</a> in early 2011, several prominent slots were freed up as many channels were closed down. It was, reported Broadband TV News, the biggest reshuffle in EPG positions for over a decade, with MTV, Comedy Central, Universal Channel, Syfy, News Corporation's FX, and 40 HD channels moving to more prominent places.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Competition">Competition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=30" title="Edit section: Competition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 12 July 2011, former Prime Minister, <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> claimed that Sky's largest shareholder - <a href="/wiki/News_Corporation" title="News Corporation">News Corporation</a> - attempted to affect government policy with regards to the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> in pursuit of its own commercial interests.<sup id="cite_ref-Brown_accuses_News_International_of_using_known_criminals_54-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Brown_accuses_News_International_of_using_known_criminals-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> He went further, in a speech in Parliament on 13 July 2011, stating:
</p>
<blockquote><p>"Mr James Murdoch, which included his cold assertion that profit not standards was what mattered in the media, underpinned an ever more aggressive News International and Sky agenda under his and Mrs Brooks’ leadership that was brutal in its simplicity. Their aim was to cut the BBC licence fee, to force BBC online to charge for its content, for the BBC to sell off its commercial activities, to open up more national sporting events to bids from Sky and move them away from the BBC, to open up the cable and satellite infrastructure market, and to reduce the power of their regulator, Ofcom. I rejected those policies."<sup id="cite_ref-Hansard,_13_July_2011_55-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard,_13_July_2011-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup></p></blockquote>
<p>On 13 July 2011, MP <a href="/wiki/Chris_Bryant" title="Chris Bryant">Chris Bryant</a> stated to the House of Commons, in the Parliamentary Debate on the <a href="/wiki/Rupert_Murdoch" title="Rupert Murdoch">Rupert Murdoch</a> and <a href="/wiki/News_Corporation" title="News Corporation">News Corporation</a> Bid for Sky that the company was anti-competitive:
</p>
<blockquote><p>"The company has lots of technological innovation that only a robust entrepreneur could to bring to British society, but it has also often been profoundly anti-competitive. I believe that the bundling of channels so as to increase the profit and make it impossible for others to participate in the market is anti-competitive. I believe that the way in which the application programming interface—the operating system—has been used has been anti-competitive and that Sky has deliberately set about selling set-top boxes elsewhere, outside areas where they have proper rights. If one visits a flat in Spain where a British person lives, one finds that they mysteriously manage to have a Sky box there even though it is registered to a house in the United Kingdom."<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> </p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Virgin_Media_dispute">Virgin Media dispute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=31" title="Edit section: Virgin Media dispute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Virgin_Media" title="Virgin Media">Virgin Media</a> (re-branded in 2007 from <i>NTL:Telewest</i>) started to offer a <a href="/wiki/High-definition_television" title="High-definition television">high-definition television</a> (HDTV) capable set top box, although from 30 November 2006 until 30 July 2009 it only carried one linear HD channel, <a href="/wiki/BBC_HD" title="BBC HD">BBC HD</a>, after the conclusion of the <a href="/wiki/ITV_HD" title="ITV HD">ITV HD</a> trial. Virgin Media has claimed that other HD channels were "locked up" or otherwise withheld from their platform,<sup id="cite_ref-digitalspy.co.uk_57-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy.co.uk-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> although Virgin Media did in fact have an option to carry Channel 4 HD in the future.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> Nonetheless, the linear channels were not offered, Virgin Media instead concentrating on its <a href="/wiki/Video_On_Demand" class="mw-redirect" title="Video On Demand">Video On Demand</a> service<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> to carry a modest selection of HD content.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> Virgin Media has nevertheless made a number of statements over the years, suggesting that more linear HD channels are on the way.<sup id="cite_ref-digitalspy.co.uk_57-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-digitalspy.co.uk-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2007, Sky and Virgin Media became involved in a dispute over the carriage of Sky channels on cable TV. The failure to renew the existing carriage agreements negotiated with NTL and Telewest resulted in Virgin Media removing the basic channels from the network on 1 March 2007. Virgin Media claimed that Sky had substantially increased the asking price for the channels, a claim which Sky denied, on the basis that their new deal offered "substantially more value" by including HD channels and Video On Demand content which was not previously carried by cable.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In response, Sky ran a number of TV, radio and print advertisements claiming that Virgin Media 'doubted the value' of the channels concerned, at first urging Virgin Media customers to call their cable operator to show their support for Sky, and later urging Virgin Media customers to migrate to Sky to continue receiving the channels. The broadcasting regulator <a href="/wiki/Ofcom" title="Ofcom">Ofcom</a> subsequently found these commercials in breach of their code.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The availability (at an extra charge) of Sky's premium sport and movie services was not affected by the dispute, and Sky Sports 3 was offered as a replacement to Sky One on many Virgin Media packages. This impasse continued for twenty-one months, with both companies initiating High Court proceedings.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup> Amongst Virgin Media's claims to the court<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup> (denied by Sky) were that Sky had unfairly reduced the amount which it paid to VMTV for the carriage of Virgin Media's own channels on satellite.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Eventually, on 4 November 2008 it was announced that an agreement had been struck for Sky's basic channels&#160;– including <a href="/wiki/Sky_One" title="Sky One">Sky1</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Two" title="Sky Two">Sky2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Pick_(TV_channel)" title="Pick (TV channel)">Sky3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_News" title="Sky News">Sky News</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Sports_News" title="Sky Sports News">Sky Sports News</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Arts" title="Sky Arts">Sky Arts 1</a>, Sky Arts 2, <a href="/wiki/Sky_Real_Lives" title="Sky Real Lives">Sky Real Lives</a> and Sky Real Lives 2 to return to Virgin Media from 13 November 2008 until 12 June 2011. In exchange, Sky would be provided continued carriage of <a href="/wiki/Living_TV_Group" title="Living TV Group">Virgin Media Television</a>'s channels&#160;– <a href="/wiki/Sky_Living" class="mw-redirect" title="Sky Living">Living</a>, <a href="/wiki/Real_Lives_(TV_channel)" title="Real Lives (TV channel)">Livingit</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bravo_(UK_TV_channel)" title="Bravo (UK TV channel)">Bravo</a>, Bravo +1, <a href="/wiki/Trouble_(TV_channel)" title="Trouble (TV channel)">Trouble</a>, <a href="/wiki/Challenge_(TV_channel)" title="Challenge (TV channel)">Challenge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Channel_One_(UK_and_Ireland)" title="Channel One (UK and Ireland)">Virgin1</a> for the same period.<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The agreements include fixed annual carriage fees of £30m for the channels with both channel suppliers able to secure additional capped payments if their channels meet certain performance-related targets. Currently there is no indication as to whether the new deal includes the additional Video On Demand and High Definition content which had previously been offered by Sky. As part of the agreements, both Sky and Virgin Media agreed to terminate all High Court proceedings against each other relating to the carriage of their respective basic channels.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Discovery_Networks_dispute">Discovery Networks dispute</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=32" title="Edit section: Discovery Networks dispute">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 25 January 2017, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Networks_Northern_Europe" title="Discovery Networks Northern Europe">Discovery Networks</a> announced that they were in a dispute with Sky UK over the costs of payment fees to the broadcaster.
</p><p>The broadcaster threatened to blackout their channels on the Sky platform, which includes <a href="/wiki/Eurosport" title="Eurosport">Eurosport</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Channel_(UK_and_Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Discovery Channel (UK and Ireland)">Discovery Channel</a>, <a href="/wiki/TLC_(UK_and_Ireland)" title="TLC (UK and Ireland)">TLC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Animal_Planet_(UK_TV_channel)" title="Animal Planet (UK TV channel)">Animal Planet</a>, <a href="/wiki/Investigation_Discovery_Europe" class="mw-redirect" title="Investigation Discovery Europe">Investigation Discovery</a>, <a href="/wiki/DMAX_(UK_TV_channel)" title="DMAX (UK TV channel)">DMAX</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Turbo" title="Discovery Turbo">Discovery Turbo</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Shed" title="Discovery Shed">Discovery Shed</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Science_(European_TV_channel)" title="Discovery Science (European TV channel)">Discovery Science</a>, <a href="/wiki/Discovery_History" title="Discovery History">Discovery History</a> and <a href="/wiki/Discovery_Home_%26_Health" title="Discovery Home &amp; Health">Home &amp; Health</a>, unless Sky accepted their request for fair pricing. Sky indicated that they will not bow down, and the channels will likely become unavailable from 1 February 2017.
</p><p>The same day, Discovery sent out a press release on the dispute and also blocked access to its own website with the news. Discovery stated 'enough is enough' and claimed that Sky is paying less for its channels than it was ten years ago. In that time, Discovery had stated that its viewing share has grown by more than 20%. Managing Director Susanna Dinnage stated in the press release: "We believe Sky is using what we consider to be its dominant market position to further its own commercial interest over those of viewers and independent broadcasters. The vitality of independent broadcasters like Discovery and plurality in TV is under threat."
</p><p>In response, Sky stated that they had been "overpaying Discovery for years." It comes after Sky had paid £4.2 billion on Premier League rights for the following three seasons.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The channel 'blackout' would have also affect the Sky owned <a href="/wiki/Now_TV_(UK_and_Ireland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Now TV (UK and Ireland)">NOW TV</a> with the removal of Discovery Channel from both the live stream and On Demand service.
</p><p>On 31 January 2017 at around 21:00, Sky UK revealed that they would continue to broadcast the Discovery Networks Channels by releasing the following statement:
"Great news, we can confirm that Sky will continue to carry the Discovery and Eurosport channels. This means you can still watch channels including: Animal Planet, Discovery HD, Discovery History, Discovery Home &amp; Health, Discovery Science, Discovery Shed, Discovery Turbo, DMAX, Eurosport1, Eurosport2, Investigation Discovery, TLC and Quest."
At the time of the statement release, it had not been revealed how much Sky UK had paid Discovery Networks.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Litigation">Litigation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Sky_UK&amp;action=edit&amp;section=33" title="Edit section: Litigation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In July 2013, the English <a href="/wiki/High_Court_of_Justice" title="High Court of Justice">High Court of Justice</a> found that <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>’s use of the term "<a href="/wiki/SkyDrive" class="mw-redirect" title="SkyDrive">SkyDrive</a>" infringed on Sky’s right to the "Sky" trademark. On 31 July 2013, Sky and Microsoft announced their settlement, in which Microsoft will not appeal the ruling, and will rename its SkyDrive cloud storage service after an unspecified "reasonable period of time to allow for an orderly transition to a new brand," plus "financial and other terms, the details of which are confidential".<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup> On 27 January 2014, Microsoft announced "that SkyDrive will soon become OneDrive" and "SkyDrive Pro" becomes "OneDrive for Business".<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hello_Games" title="Hello Games">Hello Games</a> was in legal negotiations with Sky over the trademark on the word "Sky" used for the title of their video game <a href="/wiki/No_Man%27s_Sky" title="No Man&#39;s Sky">No Man's Sky</a> for three years. The issue was ultimately settled in June 2016, allowing Hello Games to continue to use the name.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
