<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Tony Kent Strix award</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=UKeiG&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="UKeiG (page does not exist)">UKeiG</a><sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> <b>Strix</b> award is an annual award for outstanding contributions to the field of <a href="/wiki/Information_retrieval" title="Information retrieval">information retrieval</a> and is presented in memory of Dr Tony Kent, a past Fellow of the Institute of Information Scientists (IIS), who died in 1997. Tony Kent made a major contribution to the development of information science and information services both in the UK and internationally, particularly in the field of chemistry. The name 'Strix' was chosen to reflect Tony's interest in ornithology, and as the name of the last and most successful information retrieval packages that he created.
</p><p>The Award is given in recognition of an outstanding contribution to the field of information retrieval that meets one of the following criteria:
</p>
<ul><li>a major and/or sustained contribution to the theoretical or experimental understanding of the information retrieval process;</li>
<li>development of, or significant improvement in, mechanisms, a product or service for the retrieval of information, either generally or in a specialised field;</li>
<li>development of, or significant improvement in, ease of access to an information service;</li>
<li>a sustained contribution over a period of years to the field of information retrieval; for example, by running an information service or by contributing at national or international level to organisations active in the field.</li></ul>
<p>Recipients so far have been:
Source: <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.cilip.org.uk/uk-einformation-group/awards-and-bursaries/tony-kent-strix-award/tony-kent-strix-award-winners">Cilip</a>
</p>

<p>Since 2014, the winner of the Tony Kent Strix Award in year _n_ is giving the Tony Kent Strix Annual Lecture in year _n+1_.  Annual lectures so far:
</p>
<ul><li>2015: Dr <a href="/wiki/Susan_Dumais" title="Susan Dumais">Susan Dumais</a> on "Understanding and Improving Search using Large-Scale Behavioural Data" <sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2016: Prof <a href="/wiki/Peter_Ingwersen" title="Peter Ingwersen">Peter Ingwersen</a> on "Context in Interactive IR" <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2017: Prof <a href="/w/index.php?title=Maristella_Agosti&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Maristella Agosti (page does not exist)">Maristella Agosti</a> on "Behind the Scenes of Research and Innovation" <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>2018: Prof <a href="/wiki/Maarten_de_Rijke" title="Maarten de Rijke">Maarten de Rijke</a> will give the fourth annual lecture in this series.</li></ul>
