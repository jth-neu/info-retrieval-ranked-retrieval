<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Local education authority</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Not to be confused with <a href="/wiki/Local_Education_Agency" title="Local Education Agency">Local Education Agency</a>.</div>
<p><b>Local education authorities</b> (<b>LEAs</b>) are the local councils in <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> that are responsible for education within their jurisdiction. The term is used to identify which council (district or county) is locally responsible for education in a system with several layers of local government. Local education authorities are not usually ad hoc or standalone authorities, although the former <a href="/wiki/Inner_London_Education_Authority" title="Inner London Education Authority">Inner London Education Authority</a> was one example of this. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responsible_local_authority">Responsible local authority</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Responsible local authority">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="England">England</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: England">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>England has several tiers of local government and the relevant local authority varies. Within <a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a> the 32 <a href="/wiki/London_Borough_Councils" class="mw-redirect" title="London Borough Councils">London Borough Councils</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Common_Council_of_the_City_of_London" class="mw-redirect" title="Common Council of the City of London">Common Council of the City of London</a> are the local authorities responsible for education; in the metropolitan counties it is the 36 <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan borough councils</a>; and in the non-metropolitan counties it is the 27 <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a> or, where there is no county council, the councils of the 55 <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authorities_of_England" title="Unitary authorities of England">unitary authorities</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Council_of_the_Isles_of_Scilly" title="Council of the Isles of Scilly">Council of the Isles of Scilly</a> is an education authority.<sup id="cite_ref-2004_Act_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2004_Act-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Since the <a href="/wiki/Children_Act_2004" title="Children Act 2004">Children Act 2004</a> each local education authority is also a children's services authority and responsibility for both functions is held by the director of children's services.<sup id="cite_ref-2004_Act_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2004_Act-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> There are 152 local education authorities in England.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Wales">Wales</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Wales">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In Wales the councils of the counties and county boroughs are responsible for education. Since 5 May 2010, the terms local education authority and children's services authority have been repealed and replaced by the single term 'local authority' in both primary and secondary legislation.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Functions">Functions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Functions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Local education authorities have some responsibility for all state schools in their area.
</p>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable plainlinks">This list is <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:WikiProject_Lists#Incomplete_lists" title="Wikipedia:WikiProject Lists">incomplete</a>; you can help by <a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit">expanding it</a>.</div>
<ul><li>They are responsible for distribution and monitoring of funding for the schools</li>
<li>They are responsible for co-ordination of admissions, including allocation of the number of places available at each school</li>
<li>They are the direct employers of  all staff in <a href="/wiki/Community_school_(England_and_Wales)" title="Community school (England and Wales)">community</a> and <a href="/wiki/Voluntary_controlled" class="mw-redirect" title="Voluntary controlled">VC</a> schools</li>
<li>They have a responsibility for the educational achievement of looked-after children, i.e. children in their care<sup id="cite_ref-gttl_LAC_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gttl_LAC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>They have attendance and advisory rights in relation to the employment of teachers, and in relation to the dismissal of any staff<sup id="cite_ref-gttl_HR_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gttl_HR-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>They are the owners of school land and premises in community schools.<sup id="cite_ref-gttl_land_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gttl_land-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>Until recently, local education authorities were responsible for the funding of students in <a href="/wiki/Higher_education" title="Higher education">higher education</a> (for example <a href="/wiki/Undergraduate_education" title="Undergraduate education">undergraduate courses</a> and <a href="/wiki/Professional_Graduate_Certificate_in_Education" title="Professional Graduate Certificate in Education">PGCE</a>) whose permanent address is in their area, regardless of the place of study. Based on an assessment of individual circumstances they offer grants or access to <a href="/wiki/Student_loan" title="Student loan">student loans</a> through the <a href="/wiki/Student_Loans_Company" title="Student Loans Company">Student Loans Company</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Creation">Creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The term was introduced by the <a href="/wiki/Education_Act_1902" title="Education Act 1902">Education Act 1902</a> (2 Edw.7, c. 42). The Act designated each local authority; either <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county council</a> and <a href="/wiki/County_borough" title="County borough">county borough council</a>; would set up a committee known as a <i>local education authority</i> (LEA).<sup id="cite_ref-bryne_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bryne-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The councils took over the powers and responsibilities of the <a href="/wiki/School_board_(England_%26_Wales)" class="mw-redirect" title="School board (England &amp; Wales)">school boards</a> and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Technical_instruction_committee&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Technical instruction committee (page does not exist)">technical instruction committees</a> in their area. <a href="/wiki/Municipal_borough" title="Municipal borough">Municipal boroughs</a> with a population of 10,000 and <a href="/wiki/Urban_district_(Great_Britain_and_Ireland)" title="Urban district (Great Britain and Ireland)">urban districts</a> with a population of 20,000 were to be local education authorities in their areas for elementary education only. The LEAs' role was further expanded with the introduction of school meals in 1906 and medical inspection in 1907.<sup id="cite_ref-bryne_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bryne-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1904 the <a href="/wiki/London_County_Council" title="London County Council">London County Council</a> became a local education authority, with the abolition of the <a href="/wiki/London_School_Board" title="London School Board">London School Board</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan boroughs</a> were not education authorities, although they were given the power to decide on the site for new schools in their areas, and provided the majority of members on boards of management.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Reform">Reform</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Local_education_authority&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reform">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The system continued unchanged until 1965, when the <a href="/wiki/London_County_Council" title="London County Council">London County Council</a> was replaced by the <a href="/wiki/Greater_London_Council" title="Greater London Council">Greater London Council</a>. The twenty outer <a href="/wiki/London_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="London borough">London boroughs</a> became local education authorities, while a new <a href="/wiki/Inner_London_Education_Authority" title="Inner London Education Authority">Inner London Education Authority</a>, consisting of the members of the GLC elected for the inner boroughs covering the former <a href="/wiki/County_of_London" title="County of London">County of London</a> was created.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1974 local government outside London was completely reorganised. In the new metropolitan counties of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_borough" title="Metropolitan borough">metropolitan boroughs</a> became LEAs. In the non-metropolitan counties the <a href="/wiki/County_council" title="County council">county councils</a> were the education authorities,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> as they were throughout <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>.
</p><p>In 1986, with the abolition of the Greater London Council, a directly elected <a href="/wiki/Inner_London_Education_Authority" title="Inner London Education Authority">Inner London Education Authority</a> was formed. This however only existed until 1990, when the twelve inner <a href="/wiki/London_borough" class="mw-redirect" title="London borough">London boroughs</a> assumed responsibility for education.
</p><p>In 1989, under the <a href="/wiki/Education_Reform_Act_1988" title="Education Reform Act 1988">Education Reform Act 1988</a>, the LEAs lost responsibility for higher education, with all <a href="/wiki/Polytechnic_(United_Kingdom)" title="Polytechnic (United Kingdom)">polytechnics</a> and colleges of higher education becoming independent corporations.
</p><p>A further wave of local government reorganisation during the 1990s led to the formation of <a href="/wiki/Unitary_authority" title="Unitary authority">unitary authorities</a> in parts of England and throughout <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, which became local education authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A local educational authority award is an award given <i>to</i> the local educational authority, as opposed to an award given <i>by</i> the LEA.
</p>
