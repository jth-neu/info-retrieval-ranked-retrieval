<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Home Nations</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:244px;"><a href="/wiki/File:United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg/242px-United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg.png" width="242" height="390" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg/363px-United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg/484px-United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="472" data-file-height="760" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:United_Kingdom_labelled_map7_vector.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>A map of the Home Nations of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a><br /> Yellow: <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>; Pink: <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>; Orange: <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>; and Red: <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a></div></div></div>
<p>The <b>home nations</b>, refers collectively to <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> (<a href="/wiki/Countries_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Countries of the United Kingdom">countries of the United Kingdom</a>), and in certain sports (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Rugby_football" title="Rugby football">rugby football</a>) contexts, to England, Scotland, Wales and the whole island of Ireland. The term "Home Nations" is used in this second sense partly because Northern Ireland and the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a> have a unified association structure in certain sports, such as the <a href="/wiki/Irish_Rugby_Football_Union" title="Irish Rugby Football Union">Irish Rugby Football Union</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cricket_Ireland" title="Cricket Ireland">Cricket Ireland</a>. Formerly the phrase was applied in general in this same wider sense, such as the period between 1800 and 1922, before the political partition of Ireland, when the whole island was part of the United Kingdom. The term "Home Countries" (not to be confused with the "<a href="/wiki/Home_counties" title="Home counties">home counties</a>") is also sometimes used, and is synonymous.<sup id="cite_ref-BritishCycling_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BritishCycling-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The term has yet a third meaning in the context of <a href="/wiki/British_Cycling" title="British Cycling">British Cycling</a>, where it refers collectively to the seven teams representing the four constituent countries of United Kingdom plus the three <a href="/wiki/Crown_Dependencies" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown Dependencies">Crown Dependencies</a> (the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, <a href="/wiki/Jersey" title="Jersey">Jersey</a> and <a href="/wiki/Guernsey" title="Guernsey">Guernsey</a>) that participate in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Games" title="Commonwealth Games">Commonwealth Games</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BritishCycling_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BritishCycling-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Crown_Dependencies" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown Dependencies">Crown Dependencies</a> are not part of the United Kingdom, but are politically associated with it, and together make up the <a href="/wiki/British_Islands" title="British Islands">British Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Association_football">Association football</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Nations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Association football">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In association football, the Home Nations originally referred to the then four national teams of the United Kingdom: <a href="/wiki/England_national_football_team" title="England national football team">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_football_team_(1882%E2%80%931950)" title="Ireland national football team (1882–1950)">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team" title="Scotland national football team">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_football_team" title="Wales national football team">Wales</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Today, the term refers to the teams of England, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Northern Ireland national football team">Northern Ireland</a>, Scotland and Wales – the teams that contested the <a href="/wiki/British_Home_Championship" title="British Home Championship">British Home Championship</a> until 1984<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> – although references to the Home Nations sometimes include the <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland_national_football_team" title="Republic of Ireland national football team">Republic of Ireland team</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A debate took place among the football associations that govern the sport in England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland, over whether they would allow their members to participate in the <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a> as a single <a href="/wiki/Great_Britain_Olympic_football_team" title="Great Britain Olympic football team">UK team</a>. The <a href="/wiki/The_Football_Association" title="The Football Association">English Football Association</a> (the only association in favour of participation), and the news media used the term Home Nations to describe the parties to the debate.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In the event, a team consisting only of English and Welsh players took part (with the <a href="/wiki/Football_Association_of_Wales" title="Football Association of Wales">Football Association of Wales</a> still opposed, but not placing sanctions on those Welsh players who chose to play).<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_union">Rugby union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Nations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Rugby union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1883, the first <a href="/wiki/1883_Home_Nations_Championship" title="1883 Home Nations Championship">Home Nations Championship</a> was played between <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Ireland national rugby union team">Ireland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_union_team" title="Scotland national rugby union team">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_rugby_union_team" title="Wales national rugby union team">Wales</a>. At this point in its history, the competition contained only teams from the UK. In 1910, <a href="/wiki/France_national_rugby_union_team" title="France national rugby union team">France</a> officially joined the competition and it was renamed the Five Nations Championship.  Despite the <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_Ireland" title="Partition of Ireland">partition of Ireland</a> and the secession of the Republic of Ireland from the United Kingdom, the island of Ireland still fields a single sports team and is referred to as a <i>Home Nation</i> in the context of rugby union.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> When France was expelled from the international championship in 1932, the tournament reverted to being known simply as the Home Nations tournament until the readmission of France immediately after the 1939 tournament, just before World War II caused its suspension until 1947. Since the admission of <a href="/wiki/Italy_national_rugby_union_team" title="Italy national rugby union team">Italy</a> in 2000, the tournament has been known by its current name, the <a href="/wiki/Six_Nations_Championship" title="Six Nations Championship">Six Nations Championship</a>.
</p><p>Victories by any Home Nation over all of the other three Home Nations in one Championship season is a <a href="/wiki/Triple_Crown_(rugby_union)" title="Triple Crown (rugby union)">Triple Crown</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The Home Nations also contribute players to a unified team known as the <a href="/wiki/British_and_Irish_Lions" title="British and Irish Lions">British and Irish Lions</a>. Southern Hemisphere teams who beat all four home nations in one tour are said to have a <a href="/wiki/Grand_Slam_(rugby_union)#Grand_Slam_tours" title="Grand Slam (rugby union)">Grand Slam Tour</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Rugby_league">Rugby league</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_Nations&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rugby league">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Skanska_Amateur_Four_Nations" title="Skanska Amateur Four Nations">Amateur Four Nations</a> competition in rugby league, run annually from 2002 to 2007 for the A sides of <a href="/wiki/Wales_national_rugby_league_team" title="Wales national rugby league team">Wales</a>, <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_league_team" title="England national rugby league team">England</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scotland_national_rugby_league_team" title="Scotland national rugby league team">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ireland_national_rugby_league_team" class="mw-redirect" title="Ireland national rugby league team">Ireland</a>, was also referred to as the "home nations championship".<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
