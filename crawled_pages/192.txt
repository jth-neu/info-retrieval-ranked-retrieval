<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Quaero</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">For a definition of the word "quaero", see the Wiktionary entry <a href="https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/quaero" class="extiw" title="wikt:quaero">quaero</a>.</div>
<p><b>Quaero</b> (<a href="/wiki/Latin_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Latin language">Latin</a> for <i>I seek</i>) was a <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">European</a> research and development program with the goal of developing multimedia and multilingual indexing and management tools for professional and general public applications (such as search engines).<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/European_Commission" title="European Commission">European Commission</a> approved the aid granted by <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> on 11 March 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This program was supported by the <a href="/wiki/OSEO" class="mw-redirect" title="OSEO">OSEO</a>. It was a French project with the participation of several <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">German</a> partners. The consortium was led by <a href="/wiki/Thomson_SA" class="mw-redirect" title="Thomson SA">Thomson SA</a>. Other companies involved in the consortium were <a href="/wiki/France_T%C3%A9l%C3%A9com" class="mw-redirect" title="France Télécom">France Télécom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Exalead" title="Exalead">Exalead</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bertin_Technologies&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bertin Technologies (page does not exist)">Bertin Technologies</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bertin_Technologies" class="extiw" title="fr:Bertin Technologies">fr</a>)</span>, Jouve, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Grass_Valley_GmbH&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Grass Valley GmbH (page does not exist)">Grass Valley GmbH</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grass_Valley_Germany" class="extiw" title="de:Grass Valley Germany">de</a>)</span>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vecsys&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vecsys (page does not exist)">Vecsys</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Vocapia_Research&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Vocapia Research (page does not exist)">Vocapia Research</a>, <a href="/wiki/LTU_Technologies" title="LTU Technologies">LTU Technologies</a>, and Synapse Développement. Many public research institutes were also involved, including <a href="/wiki/National_Institute_for_Research_in_Computer_Science_and_Control" class="mw-redirect" title="National Institute for Research in Computer Science and Control">National Institute for Research in Computer Science and Control</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laboratoire_d%27informatique_pour_la_m%C3%A9canique_et_les_sciences_de_l%27ing%C3%A9nieur" title="Laboratoire d&#39;informatique pour la mécanique et les sciences de l&#39;ingénieur">Laboratoire d'informatique pour la mécanique et les sciences de l'ingénieur</a>, <a href="/wiki/IRCAM" title="IRCAM">IRCAM</a>, <a href="/wiki/RWTH_Aachen_University" title="RWTH Aachen University">RWTH Aachen</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_Karlsruhe" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Karlsruhe">University of Karlsruhe</a>, Institut de recherche en informatique de Toulouse, Clips Imag, <a href="/wiki/Groupe_des_%C3%89coles_des_T%C3%A9l%C3%A9communications" class="mw-redirect" title="Groupe des Écoles des Télécommunications">Groupe des Écoles des Télécommunications</a>, <a href="/wiki/Institut_National_de_la_Recherche_Agronomique" class="mw-redirect" title="Institut National de la Recherche Agronomique">Institut National de la Recherche Agronomique</a>; as well as other public organisations such as <a href="/wiki/Institut_national_de_l%27audiovisuel" title="Institut national de l&#39;audiovisuel">Institut national de l'audiovisuel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Biblioth%C3%A8que_nationale_de_France" title="Bibliothèque nationale de France">Bibliothèque nationale de France</a>, LIPN,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (March 2012)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and <a href="/wiki/Direction_G%C3%A9n%C3%A9rale_de_l%27Armement" class="mw-redirect" title="Direction Générale de l&#39;Armement">Direction Générale de l'Armement</a>.
</p><p>According to the AII press release, the main targeted applications could be divided into three broad classes: multimedia indexing and search tools for professional and general public use, including mobile environments; professional solutions for production, post-production, management and distribution of multimedia documents; and facilitation of access to cultural heritage such as audiovisual archives and digital libraries.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Announcements_and_history">Announcements and history</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quaero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Announcements and history">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Quaero was announced by <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Chirac" title="Jacques Chirac">Jacques Chirac</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gerhard_Schr%C3%B6der" title="Gerhard Schröder">Gerhard Schröder</a> during the French-German ministerial conference in April 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> It was put forward again by Jacques Chirac in August 2005 at the inauguration of the new agency created to fund such programs, the <a href="/wiki/Agence_de_l%27innovation_industrielle" title="Agence de l&#39;innovation industrielle">Agence de l'innovation industrielle</a> (AII), and in January 2006 in his New Year addresses, which received much attention from the public. Quaero was part of the first programs selected by the AII in April 2006. In December 2006, the new German government announced that it would devote its share of the funding to a slightly modified program geared toward knowledge management and semantic web, under the name of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Theseus_Program&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Theseus Program (page does not exist)">Theseus Program</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theseus_(Forschungsprogramm)" class="extiw" title="de:Theseus (Forschungsprogramm)">de</a>)</span>. Quaero received the approval of the European Commission in March 2008.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> €99m from the French government would go towards development of the program.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_search_engine">The search engine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quaero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The search engine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Quaero was often described as a European competitor to <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> as well as to other commercial search engines such as <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a>, Microsoft's <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ask.com" title="Ask.com">Ask.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Quaero was not intended to be a text-based search engine but was mainly meant for multimedia search. The search engine would have used techniques for recognizing, transcribing, indexing, and automatic translation of audiovisual documents and would have  operated in several languages. There was also a plan to have automatic recognition and indexing of images.
</p><p>According to an article in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Quaero would have allowed users to search using a "query image", not just a group of keywords. In a process known as "image mining", software that recognizes shapes and colours would have been used to look for and retrieve still images and video clips that contained images similar to the query image. (The software was supplied by <a href="/wiki/LTU_Technologies" title="LTU Technologies">LTU Technologies</a>.)
</p><p>As France was researching image-searching, Germany was supposed to be advancing voice clip and sound media searches, with the intention of transcribing their content to text, and translating it to other languages, before they pulled out of the project. This would also allow for "query sound clips" following the paradigm of the "query image" mentioned above.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quaero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The French satirical newspaper <i><a href="/wiki/Le_Canard_encha%C3%AEn%C3%A9" title="Le Canard enchaîné">Le Canard enchaîné</a></i> pointed out that the funding behind the project was dwarfed by both Microsoft and Google. Search experts Autonomy, (<i>Financial Times</i>) called the plan "a blatant case of misguided and unnecessary nationalism". The main issues being that: by the time of Quaero's launch, the search engine market would already have been generations ahead of Quaero in media and device capabilities; some argue that Chirac was more interested in defending French pride than global advancement of the Internet.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Writing in IEEE Spectrum, <a href="/wiki/Nick_Tredennick" title="Nick Tredennick">Nick Tredennick</a> commented that "Going head-to-head with Google with a project involving well-funded, energetic entrepreneurs would be foolish.  Attempting the same with a multigovernment collaboration is beyond description." <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to the print edition of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Economist" title="The Economist">The Economist</a></i>, January 6, 2007 (pp.&#160;5), Quaero "was reportedly scrapped" since the German part "grumbled about the cost and have indicated they will produce their own, scaled-down search engine".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="German_departure">German departure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quaero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: German departure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On December 18, 2006, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Hartmut_Schauerte&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Hartmut Schauerte (page does not exist)">Hartmut Schauerte</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hartmut_Schauerte" class="extiw" title="de:Hartmut Schauerte">de</a>)</span>, a state secretary within the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Ministry_for_Economics_and_Labour_(Germany)" title="Federal Ministry for Economics and Labour (Germany)">Ministry for Economics and Labour</a>, announced during the IT-summit in Potsdam that a German consortium had put together a semantic search project called <a href="/w/index.php?title=Theseus_Program&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Theseus Program (page does not exist)">Theseus Program</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;(<a href="https://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theseus_(Forschungsprogramm)" class="extiw" title="de:Theseus (Forschungsprogramm)">de</a>)</span> that would be distinct from Quaero.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The main source of disagreement was the format of the search engine, with German engineers favoring a text-based search engine and the French engineers favoring a multimedia search engine. Many German engineers also balked at what they thought was becoming too much of an anti-Google project, rather than a project driven by its own ideals.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="End">End</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Quaero&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: End">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The project ended on December 31, 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
