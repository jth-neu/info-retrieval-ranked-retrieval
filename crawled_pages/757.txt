Welsh as a subject is taught as a first language in Welsh medium schools. In the majority of English medium schools, Welsh is taught as a second language and became compulsory for all pupils in Wales at <a href="/wiki/Key_Stage" title="Key Stage">Key Stages</a> 1, 2 and 3 (up to age 14) in 1990. In 1999, it became a compulsory subject for Key Stage 4 pupils (GCSE (ages 15 and 16)). Provision of Welsh as a subject in independent (private) schools is less widespread — only a few provide it, and it is taught as a second language. There is no private designated Welsh-medium school in Wales, although one does exist in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, known as the <a href="/wiki/London_Welsh_School" title="London Welsh School">London Welsh School</a>.
</p><p>Sixteen per cent of pupils in Wales attend Welsh-medium schools, with a further 10 per cent attending schools that are bilingual, dual-medium, or in English with significant Welsh provision.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Children wishing to join a Welsh medium school do not have to speak Welsh to attend if they are young enough to learn the language quickly. <a href="/wiki/Ysgol_Glan_Clwyd" title="Ysgol Glan Clwyd">Ysgol Glan Clwyd</a> is an example: although 70 per cent of the pupils attending this school come from homes where English is the main or only language, 95 per cent of pupils finish their education speaking Welsh as proficiently as a native speaker. <a href="/wiki/Ysgol_Glan_Clwyd" title="Ysgol Glan Clwyd">Ysgol Glan Clwyd</a> was the first Welsh-medium secondary (comprehensive) school, and opened in <a href="/wiki/Rhyl" title="Rhyl">Rhyl</a> in 1956.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Nursery_Education">Nursery Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Welsh_medium_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Nursery Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/Mudiad_Ysgolion_Meithrin" title="Mudiad Ysgolion Meithrin">Mudiad Ysgolion Meithrin</a></i> (Nursery Schools Movement) has established play groups and nurseries throughout Wales which allow children to learn Welsh through <a href="/wiki/Language_immersion" title="Language immersion">immersion</a>. It is the main Welsh medium education and care provider in Wales for the early years. There were 11,828 children in cylchoedd meithrin (Mudiad Meithrin playgroups) in 2014-2015, with 22,000 children attending Mudiad Meithrin establishments every week.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The spread of such nurseries has ensured strong demand from parents for Welsh medium primary schools. The success of <i>Mudiad Ysgolion Meithrin</i> inspired the <i><a href="/wiki/Ikastolak" class="mw-redirect" title="Ikastolak">Ikastolak</a></i> movement in the <a href="/wiki/Basque_Country_(autonomous_community)" title="Basque Country (autonomous community)">Basque Country</a> and the <i><a href="/wiki/Diwan_(school)" title="Diwan (school)">Diwan</a></i> movement in <a href="/wiki/Brittany" title="Brittany">Brittany</a>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (September 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Further_education">Further education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Welsh_medium_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Further education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During 2015/2016, 7.8 per cent of learning activities in the Further Education sector included some element of Welsh, with 0.29 per cent of activities offered through Welsh only. The subjects with the highest number of learning activities with some element of Welsh were Retail and Commercial Enterprise (18.1 per cent); Agriculture, Horticulture and Animal Care (17.7 per cent) and Business, Administration and Law (14.2 per cent).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Higher_education">Higher education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Welsh_medium_education&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Higher education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2014/15, the number of higher education students with at least some teaching through the medium of Welsh reached an all-time high with 6,355 students, or 5.1 per cent of all students at Welsh universities.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Of these 6,355 students, 53 per cent were taught entirely through the medium of Welsh and 47 per cent were taught part of their course in Welsh.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/University_of_Wales_Trinity_Saint_David" title="University of Wales Trinity Saint David">University of Wales, Trinity Saint David</a> had both the highest number of students (2,185) and the highest proportion of its students (21 per cent) receiving at least some teaching through the medium of Welsh. <a href="/wiki/Wrexham_Glynd%C5%B5r_University" title="Wrexham Glyndŵr University">Glyndŵr University</a> had both the lowest number (45) and proportion of its students (0.7 per cent) receiving at least some teaching through the medium of Welsh.
</p><p>10,030 students in Wales were fluent Welsh speakers in 2015/2016, with a further 10,330 speakers recording themselves as Welsh speakers but not fluent. Of all Universities in Wales, Cardiff University had the highest number of fluent Welsh-speaking students, amounting to 1,755 students. According to the latest data collected in 2015/2016, <a href="/wiki/Bangor_University" title="Bangor University">Bangor University</a> had the highest percentage of fluent Welsh-speaking students of all Higher Education institutions in Wales: This constituted to 41.7 per cent of all students in the University.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
