<p><b>Greater Glasgow</b> is an urban settlement in Scotland consisting of all localities which are physically attached to the city of <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, forming with it a single contiguous urban area (or conurbation). It does not relate to municipal government boundaries and its territorial extent is defined by the <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office_for_Scotland" title="General Register Office for Scotland">General Register Office for Scotland</a>, which determines settlements in Scotland for census and statistical purposes. Greater Glasgow had a population of 1,199,629 at the time of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 UK Census</a><sup id="cite_ref-GRO_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-GRO-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> making it the largest <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urban area</a> in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> and the fifth-<a href="/wiki/List_of_conurbations_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="List of conurbations in the United Kingdom">largest</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A more extensive Greater Glasgow concept covers a much larger area, and may include Ayrshire down to Ayr as well as the whole of Lanarkshire down to Lanark, Dumbartonshire, Renfrewshire and Inverclyde. At present the Glasgow City Region consists of the Glasgow City Council, North Lanarkshire, South Lanarkshire, West Dunbartonshire, East Dunbartonshire, Renfrewshire, East Renfrewshire and Inverclyde Local Authorities with a combined population of over 1.7 million. This city-region is described as a metropolitan area by its own strategic planning authority, and is similar to the Glasgow metropolitan area identified by the European Union.
</p><p>The City of Glasgow grew substantially in population during the late-nineteenth and early-twentieth centuries, and in 1912; became the eighth city in Europe to reach the one million mark after <a href="/wiki/Rome" title="Rome">Rome</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/Paris" title="Paris">Paris</a>, <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Petersburg" class="mw-redirect" title="St Petersburg">St Petersburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moscow" title="Moscow">Moscow</a>. The official population stayed over a million for fifty years.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> However, since the 1960s, successive boundary changes and large-scale relocation to suburban districts and <a href="/wiki/New_towns" class="mw-redirect" title="New towns">new towns</a> have reduced the population of the City of Glasgow council area to 593,245 at the time of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">2011 UK Census</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Transport">Transport</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Glasgow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Transport">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1973, the <i>Greater Glasgow Passenger Transport Executive</i> was created to take over control of Glasgow Corporation Transport (which included the <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Subway" title="Glasgow Subway">Glasgow Subway</a>). Following local government reorganisation in 1975, control subsequently passed to Strathclyde Regional Council. The former PTE is now the <a href="/wiki/Strathclyde_Partnership_for_Transport" title="Strathclyde Partnership for Transport">Strathclyde Partnership for Transport</a>, within <a href="/wiki/Transport_Scotland" title="Transport Scotland">Transport Scotland</a>.
</p><p>The city is served by the only <a href="/wiki/Rapid_transit" title="Rapid transit">metro</a> system in Scotland, the <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Subway" title="Glasgow Subway">Glasgow Subway</a>; and by two international airports, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_Prestwick_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Glasgow Prestwick International Airport">Glasgow Prestwick International Airport</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_International_Airport" class="mw-redirect" title="Glasgow International Airport">Glasgow International Airport</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Post_codes">Post codes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Glasgow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Post codes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Following the local government boundary changes in 1996 and the creation of <a href="/wiki/Council_areas_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Council areas of Scotland">unitary councils</a> in Scotland, replacing the former <a href="/wiki/Regions_and_districts_(Scotland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Regions and districts (Scotland)">regional and district</a> councils, the <b>Greater Glasgow Settlement Area</b> or <a href="/wiki/Urban_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Urban Area">Urban Area</a> was created for the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">2001 Census</a> from groups of neighbouring urban postcodes grouped so that each group of postcode unit contains at least a given number of addresses per unit area, and the group contains at least 500 residents.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Glasgow_City_Region">Glasgow City Region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Glasgow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Glasgow City Region">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Glasgow_City_Region" title="Glasgow City Region">Glasgow City Region</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-glasgow.gov.uk_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-glasgow.gov.uk-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> is a collection of local authorities clustered around Glasgow.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The eight constituent authorities are:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/East_Dunbartonshire" title="East Dunbartonshire">East Dunbartonshire</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/West_Dunbartonshire" title="West Dunbartonshire">West Dunbartonshire</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/North_Lanarkshire" title="North Lanarkshire">North Lanarkshire</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/South_Lanarkshire" title="South Lanarkshire">South Lanarkshire</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/East_Renfrewshire" title="East Renfrewshire">East Renfrewshire</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Renfrewshire" title="Renfrewshire">Renfrewshire</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Inverclyde" title="Inverclyde">Inverclyde</a></li></ul>
<p>The population of this area in 2011 was 1,787,515. The city region is not a <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a> as significant parts of the council areas (and the whole of Inverclyde) are separated from Greater Glasgow by open countryside.  It uses numerous other terms for itself, including <b>Metropolitan Glasgow</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-glasgow.gov.uk_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-glasgow.gov.uk-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <b>the metropolitan City-Region of Glasgow</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-gcvcore.gov.uk_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gcvcore.gov.uk-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> <b>Glasgow and the Clyde Valley</b> and <b>Clydeside</b>.
</p><p>As a collection of individual local authorities the city region has no single municipal government however, following the agreement of a City Deal with the <a href="/wiki/UK_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="UK Government">UK Government</a>, the eight constituent authorities formally established a joint Glasgow and Clyde Valley Cabinet on 20 January 2015. This cabinet consists of the leaders of all eight councils, with the leader of Glasgow City Council being Chair of the Cabinet.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Prior to 2015 the eight authorities formed only a combined strategic planning authority.<sup id="cite_ref-gcvcore.gov.uk_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gcvcore.gov.uk-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Metropolitan_Glasgow">Metropolitan Glasgow</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Greater_Glasgow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Metropolitan Glasgow">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>While the Scottish Government makes no official recognition of 'Metropolitan status' in its workings,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> the term is used by other bodies.  The European Union's statistical body Eurostat lists Glasgow as the 32nd most populous metropolitan area, or <a href="/wiki/Larger_Urban_Zones" class="mw-redirect" title="Larger Urban Zones">Larger Urban Zones</a>, in the EU. Although not defining the boundaries of this metropolitan area, Eurostat state it consists "of over 1.7 million inhabitants covering an area of 3,346 km2".,<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> which is similar to the 1.75 million population of the Glasgow City Region and may suggest a correlation between the two.  The Glasgow City Region's strategic development authority describes itself as the planning authority for the "Glasgow metropolitan area"<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and the "metropolitan city-region of Glasgow".<sup id="cite_ref-gcvcore.gov.uk_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gcvcore.gov.uk-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The former local government region of <a href="/wiki/Strathclyde" title="Strathclyde">Strathclyde</a> has also been identified as a metropolitan area surrounding the Greater Glasgow <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a>, and covers approximately 2.3 million people, 41% of Scotland's population.<sup id="cite_ref-spt_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-spt-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
