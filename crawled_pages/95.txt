<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Volunia</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><i><b>Volunia</b></i> was a <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine" title="Web search engine">web search engine</a> (or <a href="/wiki/Social_search" title="Social search">social search engine</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-corriere_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corriere-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> created by <a href="/wiki/Massimo_Marchiori" title="Massimo Marchiori">Massimo Marchiori</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lastampa_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lastampa-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It was launched in beta only for registered <i>power users</i> on February 6, 2012<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and went live on June 14, 2012. Volunia, dubbed as "the search engine of the future",<sup id="cite_ref-pgs-soft_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pgs-soft-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> was speculated to be based on <a href="/wiki/Hyper_Search" title="Hyper Search">Hyper Search</a> technology.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> On June 8, 2012 Marchiori announced with an open letter<sup id="cite_ref-letter_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-letter-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  that he had been excluded from his project:<sup id="cite_ref-corriere-june_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corriere-june-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> six days later, on June 14, 2012 the site went live, but it ceased to operate in February 2014.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volunia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Volunia_beta.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b3/Volunia_beta.png/220px-Volunia_beta.png" width="220" height="142" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b3/Volunia_beta.png/330px-Volunia_beta.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/b/b3/Volunia_beta.png 2x" data-file-width="404" data-file-height="260" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Volunia_beta.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The front page of Volunia, in March 2012.</div></div></div>
<p>The name <i>Volunia</i> stems from the words “volo” (flight) and “luna” (moon), because – as Marchiori says – he wanted to evoke the <i>quantum leap</i> his social search engine was trying to deliver.<sup id="cite_ref-corriere_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corriere-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The Volunia project has been entirely developed in Italy: the head office is located in <a href="/wiki/Padua" title="Padua">Padua</a>, the servers are located in <a href="/wiki/Sardinia" title="Sardinia">Sardinia</a> and hosted by <a href="/wiki/Tiscali" title="Tiscali">Tiscali</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-corriere_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corriere-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> and the whole team, formed by 14 people, is Italian.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template noprint Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="The_project">The project</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Volunia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: The project">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Volunia differs from normal search engines in that, while it <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">crawls</a> the web and <a href="/wiki/Web_indexing" title="Web indexing">indexes</a> websites, it builds the <a href="/wiki/Ranking" title="Ranking">ranking</a> using the comments and opinions of other users.
The Volunia service allows people to interact with each other in every page they visit, as well as with the web sites' owners. Volunia uses a system similar to <a href="/wiki/Google_Sidewiki" title="Google Sidewiki">Sidewiki</a>. Volunia also introduces for the first time a "fly-over" <a href="/wiki/Map" title="Map">map</a> visualization for every web site, where every web site is turned into a city metaphor, also representing social information.<sup id="cite_ref-searchengineland_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-searchengineland-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-searchenginewatch_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-searchenginewatch-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
According to Marchiori, Volunia is not a competitor to the existing search engines, staying on an alternative level.<sup id="cite_ref-pgs-soft_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pgs-soft-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Despite what Marchiori said, some people in the internet community consider Volunia a challenge and a potential competitor for Google.<sup id="cite_ref-corriere_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-corriere-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
