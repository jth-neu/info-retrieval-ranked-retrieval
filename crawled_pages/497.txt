<p>The <b>International Institute for Strategic Studies</b> (<b>IISS</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">British</a> research institute (or <a href="/wiki/Think_tank" title="Think tank">think tank</a>) in the area of <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">international affairs</a>. Since 1997 its headquarters have been Arundel House, in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>. The 2017 Global Go To Think Tank Index ranked IISS as the tenth-best think tank worldwide and the second best Defense and National Security think tank globally.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Overview">Overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Institute_for_Strategic_Studies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The current Director-General and Chief Executive is <a href="/wiki/John_Chipman_(chief_executive)" title="John Chipman (chief executive)">John Chipman</a>. The Chairman of the Council is Francois Heisbourg, a former Director. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Howard_(historian)" title="Michael Howard (historian)">Sir Michael Howard</a>, the British military historian, is President <a href="/wiki/Emeritus" title="Emeritus">Emeritus</a>. Sir Michael founded the institute together with the British Labour M.P. <a href="/wiki/Denis_Healey" title="Denis Healey">Denis Healey</a> (<a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Defence" title="Secretary of State for Defence">Defence Secretary 1964–1970</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer 1974–1979</a>) and journalist Alastair Buchan.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
The IISS describes itself as a: </p><style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>primary source of accurate, objective information on international strategic issues for politicians and diplomats, foreign affairs analysts, international business, economists, the military, defence commentators, journalists, academics and the informed public. The Institute owes no allegiance to any government, or to any political or other organisation.
</p></blockquote>
<p>The Institute claims 2,500 Individual Members and 450 Corporate and Institutional Members from more than 100 countries.
</p><p>Based in London, the IISS is both a <a href="/wiki/Private_company_limited_by_guarantee" title="Private company limited by guarantee">private company limited by guarantee</a>  in UK law and a <a href="/wiki/Registered_charity" class="mw-redirect" title="Registered charity">registered charity</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It has branches in <a href="/wiki/Washington,_D.C." title="Washington, D.C.">Washington, D.C.</a> (IISS-US) and in <a href="/wiki/Singapore" title="Singapore">Singapore</a> (IISS-Asia), with charitable status in each jurisdiction, and in <a href="/wiki/Manama,_Bahrain" class="mw-redirect" title="Manama, Bahrain">Manama, Bahrain</a> (IISS-Middle East).
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Institute_for_Strategic_Studies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Institute’s work is built on the activities of its 11 research programmes. Dozens of experts and consulting experts contribute to the institute’s studies. Research includes work under seven thematic programmes: Armed Conflict; Future Conflict and Cyber Security; Defence and Military Analysis; Economic and Energy Security; Geo-economics and Strategy; Non-Proliferation and Nuclear Policy; 
Security and Development. There are also four active regional security programmes: Asia-Pacific; Middle East and the Gulf; South Asia; US Foreign Policy &amp; Transatlantic Affairs.
</p><p>Notable former employees include <a href="/wiki/HR_McMaster" class="mw-redirect" title="HR McMaster">HR McMaster</a>, United States National Security Advisor, and diplomat <a href="/wiki/Rose_Gottemoeller" title="Rose Gottemoeller">Rose Gottemoeller</a>, currently Deputy Secretary General of NATO. Orwell Prize-winning academic and journalist <a href="/wiki/Anatol_Lieven" title="Anatol Lieven">Anatol Lieven</a> also worked at the Institute, as did <a href="/wiki/James_Steinberg" title="James Steinberg">James Steinberg</a>, former US Deputy Secretary of State.The institute has worked with governments, defence ministries and global organisations including <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ndc.nato.int/news/news.php?icode=544">NATO</a> and the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/events/eu-conference">European Union</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Publications">Publications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Institute_for_Strategic_Studies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Publications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The IISS publishes <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/military%20balance/issues/the-military-balance-2017-b47b">The Military Balance</a>, an annual assessment of nations' military capabilities. Since 2017 it has also published <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/military-s-balance/militarybalanceplus">Military Balance+</a>, an online database on the same subject.
</p><p>Other publications include the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://acd.iiss.org/">Armed Conflict Database</a>; <a href="/wiki/Survival_(journal)" title="Survival (journal)">Survival</a>, a journal on global politics and strategy; <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/strategicsurvey">Strategic Survey</a>, the annual review of world affairs; and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/strategic-s-comments">Strategic Comments</a>, online analysis of topical issues in international affairs. Since its inception the Institute has published the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/adelphi">Adelphi</a> series of books, covering topical strategic issues. Recent editions have covered subjects such as <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/adelphi/by%20year/2016-d199/china--39-s-cyber-power-f1db">Chinese cyber power</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/adelphi/by%20year/2017-cd3c/everyone-loses-21b7">conflict in Ukraine</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/adelphi/by%20year/2016-d199/fighting-and-negotiating-with-armed-groups-f961">negotiating with armed groups</a> and the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/publications/adelphi/by%20year/2017-cd3c/harsh-lessons-b53e">Iraq War</a>.
</p><p>In 2011 the Institute published the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.aljazeera.com/news/americas/2011/05/20115115856153893.html">FARC files</a> – documents captured from the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia that shed light on the movement’s inner workings. It regularly publishes one-off briefing papers and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/events/events/archive/2017-7df9/june-cce7/strategic-dossier-2017-4d06">dossiers</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Events">Events</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Institute_for_Strategic_Studies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Events">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Since 2002 the Institute has hosted the annual <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="https://www.iiss.org/en/events/shangri-la-dialogue">IISS Shangri-La Dialogue</a> in Singapore, a conference on Asia–Pacific security issues featuring heads of state, defense ministers and security experts from the region and around the world. In 2017 Australian Prime Minister <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/shangri-la%20voices/blogsections/2017-b8c0/sld-video-highlights-d211">Malcolm Turnbull said</a>: “The Shangri-La Dialogue has grown to become one of the world’s great strategic gatherings.” The 2017 Global Go To Think Tank Index ranked the Shangri-La Dialogue as the best Think tank conference worldwide <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The annual <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/events/manama-dialogue/archive/manama-dialogue-2015-0220/keynote-517c/al-sisi-facd">IISS Manama Dialogue</a>, held in the Kingdom of Bahrain, sees global heads of state and high-ranking ministers discuss defense and political issues related to the Middle East. In 2015 Egyptian president Abdel Fattah Al-Sisi <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/events/manama-dialogue/archive/manama-dialogue-2015-0220/keynote-517c/al-sisi-facd">described the dialogue</a> as a “major regional event focusing on regional security issues and everything that impacts upon them”.
</p><p>In recent years the Institute has hosted smaller conferences including the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/events/bahrain-bay-forum">Bahrain Bay Forum</a> and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/events/nato-s-transformation-s-seminar">NATO transformation seminar</a>, and regularly holds debates and panel <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.iiss.org/en/events/events-s-calendar">discussions</a> at its offices around the world.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Institute_for_Strategic_Studies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Founded in 1958, with its original focus nuclear deterrence and arms control, the IISS has strong establishment links, with former US and British government officials among its members. The institute claims that it "was hugely influential in setting the intellectual structures for managing the <a href="/wiki/Cold_War" title="Cold War">Cold War</a>."
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Raymond_L._Garthoff" title="Raymond L. Garthoff">Raymond L. Garthoff</a> wrote in 2004:<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<blockquote><p>In 1959 the ISS issued a pamphlet on the "military balance" between the Soviet Union and NATO. It was unfortunately replete with errors, having been put together from published sources of widely varying quality. I called this to the attention of <a href="/wiki/Alastair_Francis_Buchan" title="Alastair Francis Buchan">Alastair Buchan</a>, the director of the institute, who was quite disturbed. A new version was issued in November 1960, much more correct and accurate, though still not up to the latest intelligence. Again, I called this to Buchan's attention, and he undertook to check out with British authorities what became annual issuances.</p></blockquote>
<p>The second issue appeared under the title "The Communist Bloc and the Free World: The Military Balance 1960".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Institute_for_Strategic_Studies&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016, <a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a> reported that IISS "has been accused of jeopardising its independence after leaked documents showed it has secretly received £25m from the Bahraini royal family", noting that leaked "documents reveal that IISS and Bahrain’s rulers specifically agreed to keep the latter’s funding for the Manama Dialogues secret".<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The IISS did not dispute the authenticity of the leaked documents or deny receiving funding from Bahrain, but issued a response stating that "[a]ll IISS contractual agreements, including those with host governments, contain a clause asserting the Institute’s absolute intellectual and operational independence as an international organisation that does not participate in any manner of advocacy."<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Middle East Eye subsequently reported that IISS may have received nearly half of its total income from Bahraini sources in some years.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
