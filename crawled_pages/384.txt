<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Cuil</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><b>Cuil</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span><span title="/uː/: &#39;oo&#39; in &#39;goose&#39;">uː</span><span title="&#39;l&#39; in &#39;lie&#39;">l</span></span>/</a></span></span> <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">KOOL</span></i></a>) was a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> that organized web pages by content and displayed relatively long entries along with <a href="/wiki/Thumbnail" title="Thumbnail">thumbnail</a> pictures for many results. Cuil said it had a larger index than any other <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a>, with about 120 billion <a href="/wiki/Web_pages" class="mw-redirect" title="Web pages">web pages</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-AP1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It went live on July 28, 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-AP1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Cuil's servers were shut down on September 17, 2010, with later confirmations the service had ended.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-REF_ID_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-REF_ID-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cuil was managed and developed largely by former employees of <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Anna_Patterson" title="Anna Patterson">Anna Patterson</a> and Russell Power. The <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> and co-founder, Tom Costello, has worked for <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> and others.<sup id="cite_ref-bbcRivalGoogle_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bbcRivalGoogle-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The company had raised $33 million from <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> firms including <a href="/wiki/Greylock_Partners" title="Greylock Partners">Greylock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-crunchbase_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-crunchbase-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Cuil's privacy policy,<sup id="cite_ref-priv_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-priv-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> unlike that of other search engines,<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> said it did not store users’ search activity or <a href="/wiki/IP_addresses" class="mw-redirect" title="IP addresses">IP addresses</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-priv_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-priv-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cuil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cuil launched in July 2008 with an index of 121,617,892,992 web pages.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> About one month after launch, Cuil's Product VP and search technologist, <a href="/wiki/Louis_Monier" title="Louis Monier">Louis Monier</a>, quit the company citing disagreements with the CEO, Tom Costello.<sup id="cite_ref-RESIGN1_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RESIGN1-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> On December 19, 2008, <i><a href="/wiki/BusinessWeek" class="mw-redirect" title="BusinessWeek">BusinessWeek</a></i> listed Cuil as one of the most successful U.S. startups of 2008, based on the amount of money they raised.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> As of February, 2009, Cuil had 127 billion indexed pages. According to <a href="/wiki/Alexa_Internet" title="Alexa Internet">Alexa</a>, the site reached a peak of just over 0.2% of worldwide internet users in late July 2008 and by September 12, 2008, it had dropped to 0.02% and ranked as the 5,340th site by traffic. By October 13, 2008, it had dropped to 0.005% and ranked as the 21,960th site in traffic.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Shutdown">Shutdown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cuil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Shutdown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i><a href="/wiki/PC_Magazine" title="PC Magazine">PC Magazine</a></i> reported that on the morning of September 17, 2010 "employees were told about Cuil's demise [...] and the servers were taken offline five hours later."<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Laid-off employees were told they would not be paid. The shutdown reportedly came after an acquisition agreement fell through earlier in the week.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Their patents were sold to Google and Anna Patterson returned to Google to work for its Search Engine department.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cuil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A user could log into their <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> account via Cuil, which would then search friend updates for topics, with search links. A user could also send messages to their friends through Cuil.<sup id="cite_ref-FB_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FB-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Cuil worked on an automated encyclopedia called Cpedia, built by algorithmically summarizing and clustering ideas on the web<sup id="cite_ref-Cpedia_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Cpedia-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> to create encyclopedia-like reports. Instead of displaying search results, Cuil would show Cpedia articles matching the searched terms. This was meant to reduce duplication by combining information into one document.
</p><p>Cuil was available in 8 languages: <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a> and <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>. More were planned.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-preferences_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-preferences-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cuil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Irish ancestry of Anna Patterson's husband Tom Costello sparked the name <i>Cuil</i>, which the company states is taken from a <a href="/wiki/Fenian_Cycle" title="Fenian Cycle">series of Celtic folklore stories</a> involving a character, <a href="/wiki/Fionn_mac_Cumhaill" title="Fionn mac Cumhaill">Fionn mac Cumhaill</a>, they erroneously refer to as Finn MacCuil.<sup id="cite_ref-AP1_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The company says that <i>Cuil</i> is <a href="/wiki/Irish_language" title="Irish language">Irish</a> for <i>knowledge</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Hazel" title="Hazel">hazel</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Some linguists are unsure of this derivation and pronunciation,<sup id="cite_ref-PCW1_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PCW1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> and note that the modern Irish word for <i>hazel</i> is spelled <i><a href="/wiki/Coll_(letter)" title="Coll (letter)">coll</a></i><sup id="cite_ref-tearma_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tearma-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> (<i>coill</i> or <i>cuill</i> in genitive form, the former spelling having superseded the latter as a result of the <i><a href="/wiki/Gaeilge#An_Caighdeán_Oifigiúil" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaeilge">Caighdeán Oifigiúil</a></i> reforms of the mid-twentieth century). <a href="/wiki/Foras_na_Gaeilge" title="Foras na Gaeilge">Foras na Gaeilge</a>, the official governing body of the Irish language, did not support the assertion that 'cuil' means 'knowledge'.<sup id="cite_ref-PCW1_22-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PCW1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> "I am unaware myself of the meaning 'knowledge' being with the word 'cuil' in Irish," Stiofán Ó Deoráin, an official on Foras na Gaeilge's terminology committee, said.<sup id="cite_ref-PCW1_22-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PCW1-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Even pre-Caighdeán dictionaries such as Dineen<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> do not associate the <i>cuil</i> spelling with knowledge or hazel. Dinneen only lists two nouns and one adjective with the spelling <i>cuil</i>: "<i>f.</i>, a fly, a horse-fly...", "<i>f.</i>, a venomous aspect; great eagerness..." and "<i>gs.</i> of col, as <i>a.</i>, wicked."
</p><p>The company name had previously been spelled <i>Cuill</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Cuil&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Cuil received widely critical press coverage.<sup id="cite_ref-CNET1_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNET1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-PCMag_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PCMag-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> Concerns were expressed about the website's slow response times, irrelevant or wrong search results<sup id="cite_ref-time.com_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time.com-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost.com_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost.com-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> and in at least one case, inappropriately pornographic images displayed alongside search results.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Danny_Sullivan_(technologist)" title="Danny Sullivan (technologist)">Danny Sullivan</a> of <a href="/wiki/Search_Engine_Watch" title="Search Engine Watch">Search Engine Watch</a> questioned the validity of Cuil's claim that it had the world's largest search engine index and criticized it for focusing on size rather than <a href="/wiki/Relevance_(information_retrieval)" title="Relevance (information retrieval)">relevance</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> However, despite reported problems with search results, Net Applications reported that for the last three days of July 2008, Cuil beat Google and Yahoo in the amount of time spent on a site after referral from a search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to an interview with a Cuil representative, while other <a href="/wiki/Web_2.0" title="Web 2.0">Web 2.0</a> launches using <a href="/wiki/Massively_parallel_(computing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Massively parallel (computing)">massively parallel processing</a> might fail with a slow down or crash,<sup id="cite_ref-CNET1_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNET1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Cuil's architecture was responding with incomplete, "less-than-relevant results that then appear at the top of users' pages."<sup id="cite_ref-time.com_29-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-time.com-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-huffingtonpost.com_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-huffingtonpost.com-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> Cuil's VP of communications Vince Sollitto said the search engine was experiencing heavy first-day overloads and they were "busy putting out fires." Sollitto said Cuil "will only improve with time. It's day one. Traffic is massive. We're new. There are bugs to fix, results to improve."<sup id="cite_ref-CNET1_26-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNET1-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After the initial critical press coverage, Cuil was alleged to have caused issues for some websites, owing to how the Cuil <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">indexing robot</a> polled certain sites (including under its pre-release name, <i>Cuill</i>).<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Many website owners reported that the Twiceler crawler repeatedly hit their site with randomly generated URLs in an attempt to find pages inaccessible by links.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Others reported irrelevant images associated with their listing in Cuil's search results.<sup id="cite_ref-LOSECUIL_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LOSECUIL-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
