<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Fictional_character_biography">Fictional character biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veronica_Lodge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Fictional character biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Veronica Lodge is the only child of <a href="/wiki/Hiram_Lodge" title="Hiram Lodge">Hiram Lodge</a>, the richest man in <a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(Archie_Comics)" title="Riverdale (Archie Comics)">Riverdale</a>, and his wife <a href="/wiki/Hermione_Lodge" class="mw-redirect" title="Hermione Lodge">Hermione Lodge</a>. She is called both by her name <i>Veronica</i> and her nicknames <i>Ronnie</i> and <i>Ron.</i> <a href="/wiki/Bob_Montana" title="Bob Montana">Bob Montana</a> knew the <a href="/wiki/Lodge_family" title="Lodge family">Lodge family</a>, because he had once painted a mural for them. Montana combined that name with actress <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Lake" title="Veronica Lake">Veronica Lake</a> to create the character of Veronica Lodge. Her character was added in <a href="/wiki/Pep_Comics" title="Pep Comics">Pep Comics</a> 38, just months after <a href="/wiki/Archie_Andrews_(comics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Archie Andrews (comics)">Archie Andrews</a>, <a href="/wiki/Betty_Cooper" title="Betty Cooper">Betty Cooper</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Jughead_Jones" title="Jughead Jones">Jughead Jones</a> debuted, and just a few months before <a href="/wiki/Reggie_Mantle" title="Reggie Mantle">Reggie Mantle</a> debuted.
</p><p>Veronica is an attractive girl with black hair, favoring expensive, up-to-the-minute fashion. In some comics, Mr. Lodge claimed that he moved his family to Riverdale in order to avoid Veronica becoming spoiled, like many of the children he knew and grew up with. His plan did not succeed as well as he had hoped, as Veronica is often depicted on spending sprees, in which she maxes out her credit cards. In one story she buys out every shoe store in Riverdale to prevent any other girl from buying a pair of shoes that she herself wanted.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (November 2017)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> At time, Veronica's vain and conceited attitude infuriates her father to the point that he has to somehow "teach her a lesson" and Veronica is forced to get a job of some kind or loses access to Archie. She has also been spanked by her father several times for her behavior, sometimes in full view of everybody she knows<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (November 2017)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>, an example of the <a href="/wiki/Misogyny" title="Misogyny">misogyny</a> that pervaded comics of earlier eras.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the earliest Archie Comics, there were a few different 'Veronica origin' stories. In her debut story, in Pep #26, April 1942, Veronica was referred to as a 'sub-debutante', daughter of 'Money Bags' Lodge of Beacon Hill, who had just come to live in Riverdale.
In Pep #31, September 1942, Veronica was revealed to have lived in Boston before coming to Riverdale. Her father Burton K. Lodge was introduced as a 'big shot Boston politician'.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (November 2017)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In <i>Archie Comics</i> #1 (Winter 1942), another version of Veronica's emigration to Riverdale is depicted. In this story, Veronica was referred to as 'the elusive sub-deb' and 'that girl from New York'. Archie had never met this 'society dame', but kept daydreaming about her, and wrote letters to her he never sent. Archie wrote to her asking her to a dance in <a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(Archie_Comics)" title="Riverdale (Archie Comics)">Riverdale</a>, and the letter got sent accidentally; he really wanted to ask <a href="/wiki/Betty_Cooper" title="Betty Cooper">Betty Cooper</a>, and was only daydreaming. Even after he realized he had sent the letter, he did not think she would really come. Veronica accepted the invitation, thinking that a dance would be fun. At the time she apparently lived in New York with her mother, and she begged Mrs. Lodge to let her go to the Riverdale dance. Archie struggled trying to keep his dates with both girls, thus beginning their <a href="/wiki/Love_triangle" title="Love triangle">love triangle</a>.
</p><p>Veronica was ranked 87th in the <i><a href="/wiki/Comics_Buyer%27s_Guide" title="Comics Buyer&#39;s Guide">Comics Buyer's Guide</a>'s</i> "100 Sexiest Women in Comics" list created by Brent Frankenhoff.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lifestyle_and_relationships">Lifestyle and relationships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veronica_Lodge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Lifestyle and relationships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Veronica enjoys a very posh lifestyle, as her family are among the richest people in the world. She nevertheless chooses to hang out with her less affluent friends, including the boy she is in love with, <a href="/wiki/Archie_Andrews_(comics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Archie Andrews (comics)">Archie Andrews</a>, whom she nicknames "Archiekins". Her level of interest in him varies, however. Mr. Lodge often has nightmares that Veronica and Archie will marry. In 2009, Archie Comics published a storyline that followed this possible future, marrying Veronica to Archie in one universe and to Reggie in another.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Veronica's best friend (and sometimes arch-rival) is <a href="/wiki/Betty_Cooper" title="Betty Cooper">Betty Cooper</a>, and the two enjoy countless activities and interests. However, they are also at constant competition for Archie's affections. Veronica is often jealous of Betty, and will go to any length to steal anything that is important or a triumph for Betty, even if she (Veronica) did not want it in the first place. If Betty has a date with a new boy, Veronica will let her have Archie for a brief time, so that she can steal the date. If Betty is praised for some achievement, Veronica will do anything to ruin it for her. In one story, she gave Betty a candy bar containing nuts (which Betty is allergic to), so that Veronica could steal her modeling job. Veronica is often seen scheming to get Archie from Betty whenever she spots the two dating. Occasionally she is victorious, but usually Betty manages to successfully take revenge on her. Also, Archie occasionally has stood up to Veronica and stuck with Betty.
</p><p>Her other friends include <a href="/wiki/Big_Ethel" class="mw-redirect" title="Big Ethel">Ethel Muggs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Midge_Klump" title="Midge Klump">Midge Klump</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nancy_Woods" title="Nancy Woods">Nancy Woods</a>, and they all enjoy having slumber parties and shopping at the mall together. Aside from Archie, Veronica frequently dates <a href="/wiki/Reggie_Mantle" title="Reggie Mantle">Reggie Mantle</a>, who has the wealth and possessions her father appreciates, as well as a <a href="/wiki/Narcissism" title="Narcissism">narcissism</a> that rivals hers.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Periodically, she can be cocky and snobbish, constantly looking down on those less fortunate than herself, or sees herself as the rules of common society don't apply to her simply because she is wealthy, which causes her friends to be upset with her. During those times, she derisively refers to the public at large as "the common horde". At other times, when she can be snobbish, her father will punish her with having her credit cards confiscated.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Living in a house full of servants, Veronica often thinks that they are only there to serve her, and she doesn't see them as people. She will often yell at her maids to bring her something and "to be quick about it". At one point, her mother forced her to do all her own chores and fetch her own snacks, throughout the entire summer.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Her relationship with <a href="/wiki/Jughead_Jones" title="Jughead Jones">Jughead Jones</a> is less friendly. They constantly argue and are critical of each other's lifestyles. She cannot stand his laid-back attitude and witticisms, and he considers her an uncaring snob and enjoys arousing her temper. Jughead enjoys flouting her and supporting Betty in her rivalry with Veronica. However, Jughead and Veronica usually manage to stand being around each other for Archie's sake.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Veronica, being the only child of her wealthy parents, is outrageously spoiled and indulged by her family. Her father, Mr. Lodge, loves her dearly, and even though he frequently loses his temper with her snobbishness, frivolity, spendthrift nature, and choice of boyfriends, he can never refuse when Veronica asks him for something. Mr. Lodge does not consider most of the local boys worthy of his only daughter.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Veronica's cousin, Leroy, in Elementary School, has a mean-spirited, <a href="/wiki/Juvenile_delinquent" class="mw-redirect" title="Juvenile delinquent">juvenile delinquent</a>-like personality. When Leroy visits, he antagonizes Archie even more than Reggie does. Veronica also has a cousin named Marcy, aged 14, who enjoys science fiction and other things that Veronica finds "geeky", so Veronica considers her annoying. Most of the time, however, Marcy is helpful to Veronica, who discovers that her cousin isn't so geeky after all.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Interests_and_personality">Interests and personality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veronica_Lodge&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Interests and personality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Self-absorbed, insensitive, shallow, and easily succumbs to jealousy, the fashion-conscious Lodge stars the <i>Archie</i> spin-offs <i><a href="/wiki/Betty_and_Veronica_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Betty and Veronica Magazine">Betty and Veronica Magazine</a></i> and <i>Betty and Veronica Spectacular,</i> as well as her own series, <i>Veronica</i>. Her foibles are commonly featured in stories. When upset, she has an extremely bad temper, which it is unwise to arouse. She is an impulsive shopper who cannot go long without buying clothes.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 
</p><p>For example, she bought out every shoe store in Riverdale so she could have all the shoes which amounted to 200 pairs and so she could be totally exclusive and nobody else could have any shoes. This act of unbridled selfishness angers her father who grounds her indefinitely, orders her to return the shoes and cancels a party she wanted to throw, and she has a nightmare where she remade Riverdale in her own image, calling it Veronicaville. At first the idea of running the town her way, and basically being the queen was fun, but when Betty wore the same outfit she did, she naturally got upset and banished everyone out of town. When they leave for a new town to live in, and finally losing her father, when he leaves for greener pastures and she is left alone, she wakes up and finally wises up. She gives Betty the 200 pairs of shoes and throws the party she scheduled as a birthday party for her. (This was in a story called "The Me-ning of life").<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> 
</p><p>She is often addicted to gossip, which tends to aggravate Betty. Though commonly a <a href="/wiki/Cheerleader" class="mw-redirect" title="Cheerleader">cheerleader</a>, her athletic abilities vary from superior to nonexistent, and she lacks domestic talents and many other skills that Betty has mastered. In fact, her cooking skills are so notoriously poor that the other characters literally fear for their lives when she tries to prepare a meal. Several stories portray Veronica as an expert at <a href="/wiki/Billiards" class="mw-redirect" title="Billiards">billiards</a>, the reason being that her family owns a pool table, and she has been playing since she was a child.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>At various times, she will take a common situation (for example being named the Most Popular Student in the Riverdale High School Yearbook) and make it into a full blown out situation aimed at increasing her already inflated ego.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (November 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Her best school subject is economics, and it is implied that she will eventually take over her father's business. She also has worked as a <a href="/wiki/Fashion_model" class="mw-redirect" title="Fashion model">fashion model</a>. By the 1960s, Betty and Veronica join Archie's band, <a href="/wiki/The_Archies" title="The Archies">The Archies</a>. Betty plays the <a href="/wiki/Tambourine" title="Tambourine">tambourine</a>, Veronica plays the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_organ" class="mw-redirect" title="Electronic organ">organ</a>, and both sing accompaniment. In the 2000s, Veronica appeared a few times as Powerteen &#8212; a comic book character created by <a href="/wiki/Chuck_Clayton" title="Chuck Clayton">Chuck Clayton</a>. She also enjoys traveling to exotic places, which was the basis for <i><a href="/wiki/Alternate_Universes_in_Archie_Comics#Veronica&#39;s_Passport" class="mw-redirect" title="Alternate Universes in Archie Comics">Veronica's Passport</a></i>.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citing_sources" title="Wikipedia:Citing sources"><span title="This citation requires a reference to the specific volume in which the source appears. (November 2017)">volume&#160;&amp;&#160;issue&#160;needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Veronica_167.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/7/79/Veronica_167.png/170px-Veronica_167.png" width="170" height="266" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/7/79/Veronica_167.png 1.5x" data-file-width="252" data-file-height="394" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Veronica_167.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The cover of <i>Veronica #167</i> featuring Veronica, Archie and The Veronicas</div></div></div>
<p>Besides being in The Archies, Veronica also appeared alongside indirect namesakes <a href="/wiki/The_Veronicas" title="The Veronicas">The Veronicas</a> (Australian twins <a href="/wiki/Lisa_Origliasso" title="Lisa Origliasso">Lisa</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jessica_Origliasso" title="Jessica Origliasso">Jess Origliasso</a>) on the <i>Veronica #167</i> cover and in the feature story, <i>What's In A Name?</i>, which includes the duo performing in Riverdale and explaining to Veronica how she was an influence on their name. (The issue had been enabled after Archie Comics and <a href="/wiki/Warner_Music_Group" title="Warner Music Group">Warner Music Group</a> had settled their legal battle over the duo's name by agreeing to a cross-promotion deal).
</p>

She also appeared in the various spin-offs <i><a href="/wiki/The_Archie_Comedy_Hour" class="mw-redirect" title="The Archie Comedy Hour">The Archie Comedy Hour</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Archie%27s_Funhouse" class="mw-redirect" title="Archie&#39;s Funhouse">Archie's Funhouse</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Archie%27s_TV_Funnies" title="Archie&#39;s TV Funnies">Archie's TV Funnies</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_U.S._of_Archie" title="The U.S. of Archie">The U.S. of Archie</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Archie_and_Sabrina_Hour" title="The New Archie and Sabrina Hour">The New Archie and Sabrina Hour</a></i> produced in the same format. <li>Veronica appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Archies" title="The New Archies">The New Archies</a></i>, a 1987 re-imagining of Archie and the gang. Veronica was portrayed as a pre-teen in junior high. She was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Alyson_Court" title="Alyson Court">Alyson Court</a>.</li>
<li>Veronica appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Archie%27s_Weird_Mysteries" title="Archie&#39;s Weird Mysteries">Archie's Weird Mysteries</a></i> voiced by Camille Schmidt.</li></ul>
