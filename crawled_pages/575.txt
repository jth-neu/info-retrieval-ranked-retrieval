<p>The <b>West Midlands conurbation</b> is the large <a href="/wiki/Conurbation" title="Conurbation">conurbation</a> that includes the cities of <a href="/wiki/Birmingham" title="Birmingham">Birmingham</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wolverhampton" title="Wolverhampton">Wolverhampton</a> and the large towns of <a href="/wiki/Sutton_Coldfield" title="Sutton Coldfield">Sutton Coldfield</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dudley" title="Dudley">Dudley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich" title="West Bromwich">West Bromwich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Solihull" title="Solihull">Solihull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Stourbridge" title="Stourbridge">Stourbridge</a> and <a href="/wiki/Halesowen" title="Halesowen">Halesowen</a> in the <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">English</a> <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(region)" title="West Midlands (region)">West Midlands</a>.
</p><p>Not to be confused with the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(region)" title="West Midlands (region)">region</a> or <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">metropolitan county</a> of the same name, the conurbation does not include parts of the metropolitan county such as <a href="/wiki/Coventry" title="Coventry">Coventry</a>, but does include parts of the surrounding counties of <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire" title="Staffordshire">Staffordshire</a> (e.g. <a href="/wiki/Little_Aston" title="Little Aston">Little Aston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perton" title="Perton">Perton</a> and <a href="/wiki/Essington" title="Essington">Essington</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a> (such as <a href="/wiki/Hagley" title="Hagley">Hagley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hollywood,_Worcestershire" title="Hollywood, Worcestershire">Hollywood</a>).
</p><p>According to the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2011" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2011">2011 Census</a> the area had a population of 2,440,986,<sup id="cite_ref-BUA2011_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BUA2011-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> making it the <a href="/wiki/List_of_conurbations_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="List of conurbations in the United Kingdom">third most populated in the United Kingdom</a> behind the <a href="/wiki/Greater_London_Urban_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Greater London Urban Area">Greater London</a> and <a href="/wiki/Greater_Manchester_Urban_Area" class="mw-redirect" title="Greater Manchester Urban Area">Greater Manchester</a> Built Up Areas.  However it should be stated that the Conurbation sits within the UK's (and therefore England's) largest Metropolitan Area<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> outside London (known as the Birmingham Metropolitan Area).
</p><p>The conurbation has several other unofficial names, such as the controversial <b>Greater Birmingham</b> (though highly regarded by Businesses of the area), the <b>Birmingham conurbation</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> the <b>Birmingham-Wolverhampton conurbation</b><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>, the <b>Birmingham-Black Country conurbation</b><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and the <b>Greater Birmingham-Black Country Conurbation</b>.
</p><p>The conurbation is polycentric - Birmingham and Wolverhampton have separate <a href="/wiki/Eurostat" title="Eurostat">Eurostat</a> <a href="/wiki/Larger_Urban_Zones" class="mw-redirect" title="Larger Urban Zones">Larger Urban Zones</a>, and there are separate <a href="/wiki/Travel_to_work_area" title="Travel to work area">Travel to Work areas</a> defined for Birmingham, Wolverhampton, <a href="/wiki/Dudley" title="Dudley">Dudley</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Sandwell" title="Sandwell">Sandwell</a> and <a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Cannock" title="Cannock">Cannock</a>; whilst the extreme south-west corner of the conurbation at <a href="/wiki/Hagley" title="Hagley">Hagley</a> is within the <a href="/wiki/Kidderminster" title="Kidderminster">Kidderminster</a> Travel to Work Area, and the extreme south-east corner is within the <a href="/wiki/Warwick" title="Warwick">Warwick</a> &amp; <a href="/wiki/Stratford_upon_Avon" class="mw-redirect" title="Stratford upon Avon">Stratford upon Avon</a> TTWA.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Constituent_parts">Constituent parts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Midlands_conurbation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Constituent parts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg/220px-WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg.png" width="220" height="176" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg/330px-WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg/440px-WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1282" data-file-height="1024" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:WestMidlandsUrbanArea.svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The West Midlands Urban Area as at the 2001 Census</div></div></div>
<p>Although the exact boundaries of any conurbation are open to debate, dependent on what criteria are used to determine where an urban area ceases, the <a href="/wiki/Office_for_National_Statistics" title="Office for National Statistics">Office for National Statistics</a> defines the <b>West Midlands Built Up Area</b> as including the urban areas of Birmingham, Wolverhampton, <a href="/wiki/Solihull" title="Solihull">Solihull</a>, <a href="/wiki/West_Bromwich" title="West Bromwich">West Bromwich</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dudley" title="Dudley">Dudley</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Walsall" title="Walsall">Walsall</a> amongst others. These settlements are not coterminous with the Metropolitan Boroughs of the same name.
</p><p>The area of conurbation between Birmingham and Wolverhampton is known as the <a href="/wiki/Black_Country" title="Black Country">Black Country</a>. The Black Country has no single centre, having grown up from a number of historic market towns and industrial villages that coalesced during the 20th century. It remains essentially polycentric with many of the towns and villages remaining recognisable communities.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Coventry" title="Coventry">Coventry</a> is separated from the West Midlands conurbation by the <a href="/wiki/Meriden_Gap" title="Meriden Gap">Meriden Gap</a>, and other urban areas, such as <a href="/wiki/Cannock" title="Cannock">Cannock</a> and <a href="/wiki/Codsall" title="Codsall">Codsall</a> are also only narrowly avoided.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Administration">Administration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=West_Midlands_conurbation&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Administration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Occasionally the conurbation is seen as being coterminous with the <a href="/wiki/West_Midlands_(county)" title="West Midlands (county)">West Midlands Metropolitan county</a>; however, this includes <a href="/wiki/Coventry" title="Coventry">Coventry</a>, which is separate from the main urban area, and excludes the parts of the surrounding counties of <a href="/wiki/Staffordshire" title="Staffordshire">Staffordshire</a>, <a href="/wiki/Warwickshire" title="Warwickshire">Warwickshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Worcestershire" title="Worcestershire">Worcestershire</a> that fall within the conurbation.
</p><p>For administrative purposes, the vast majority of the conurbation falls within the six <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough" class="mw-redirect" title="Metropolitan Borough">Metropolitan Boroughs</a> of Birmingham, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Dudley" title="Metropolitan Borough of Dudley">Dudley</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sandwell" title="Sandwell">Sandwell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Solihull" title="Metropolitan Borough of Solihull">Solihull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Metropolitan_Borough_of_Walsall" title="Metropolitan Borough of Walsall">Walsall</a> and Wolverhampton.
</p><p>Two <a href="/wiki/Local_enterprise_partnership" title="Local enterprise partnership">Local enterprise partnerships (LEPs)</a> cover the majority of the conurbation area:  Black Country LEP comprises the local authorities of Dudley, Sandwell, Walsall and Wolverhampton while the <a href="/wiki/Greater_Birmingham_%26_Solihull_Local_Enterprise_Partnership" class="mw-redirect" title="Greater Birmingham &amp; Solihull Local Enterprise Partnership">Greater Birmingham &amp; Solihull LEP</a> includes those two authorities and a number of satellite boroughs, many remote from the conurbation and not traditionally associated with it (Bromsgrove, Cannock Chase, East Staffordshire, Lichfield, Redditch, Tamworth and Wyre Forest).
</p>

<p><b>Notes</b>: <small>
</small></p><small></small><ul><small><li>Knowle and Bentley Heath are considered as one settlement in 2001, but are considered separately in 1991 and 1981.  Bentley Heath was not considered to be a settlement within the West Midlands Urban Area in 1981.</li>
<li>Coleshill and Water Orton were not considered to be part of the West Midlands Urban Area in 1981, but a separate Coleshill/Water Orton Urban Area with a total population of 9,554; made up of Coleshill (6,113) and Water Orton (3,441).</li>
<li>Yew Tree is only considered part of the West Midlands Urban Area in the 2001 census.</li>
<li>Cheswick Green was not considered to be a settlement within the West Midlands Urban Area in 1981.</li>
</small><li><small>Shelly Green was not considered to be a settlement within the West Midlands Urban Area in 1981 or 1991.</small></li></ul>
