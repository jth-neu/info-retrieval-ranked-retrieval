<p>The <b>Autumn Budget of the British Government</b> is an annual <a href="/wiki/Budget" title="Budget">budget</a> set by <a href="/wiki/HM_Treasury" title="HM Treasury">HM Treasury</a> for the following financial year, with the <a href="/wiki/Revenue" title="Revenue">revenues</a> to be gathered by <a href="/wiki/HM_Revenue_and_Customs" title="HM Revenue and Customs">HM Revenue and Customs</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Cost" title="Cost">expenditures</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Public_sector" title="Public sector">public sector</a>, in compliance with <a href="/wiki/Government_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Government of the United Kingdom">government</a> policy.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The budget is one of two statements given by the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a>, with the <b><a href="/wiki/Spring_Statement" title="Spring Statement">Spring Statement</a></b> being given the following year.
</p><p>Budgets are usually set once every year and are announced in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a> by the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a>. The <a href="/wiki/November_2017_United_Kingdom_budget" title="November 2017 United Kingdom budget">latest budget</a> took place on Wednesday 22 November 2017. From 2017 the United Kingdom budget will take place in the <a href="/wiki/Autumn" title="Autumn">Autumn</a> in order to allow major tax changes to occur annually, well before the start of the fiscal year.<sup id="cite_ref-aut2016_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aut2016-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Budget_process">Budget process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Budget_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Budget process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The UK <a href="/wiki/Fiscal_year" title="Fiscal year">fiscal year</a> ends on 5 April each year. The financial year ends on 31 March of each year. Thus, the UK budget for financial year 2011 would cover the period from 1 April 2011 to 31 March 2012 and is often referred to as 2011–12.
</p><p>Historically, the budget was usually released in March, less than one month before the beginning of the new fiscal year. Parliament was not expected to take action on a budget for the fiscal year until the summer, several months <i>after</i> the start of the fiscal year. For that reason, Parliament typically passed a "Vote on Account" in early spring that provided continuity of funding into the new fiscal year, up until the point that the new budget was enacted. The spending authorized in the Vote on Account was normally 45% of the amounts already authorized in the current fiscal year, taking into account the Main Estimates and any revised or Supplementary Estimates already approved by Parliament.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Legislative action on the proposed budget generally aligned with the executive's original budget request; failure to carry the budget would regarded as tantamount to a vote of No Confidence in the Government.
</p><p>Since November 2017 the budget has been moved in the Autumn, with a view to passing the Finance Act before the commencment of the Financial Year. Votes on Account should no longer be necessary.<sup id="cite_ref-aut2016_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-aut2016-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Governmental departments submit their funding requests&#160;— called "Main Supply Estimates" – to <a href="/wiki/HM_Treasury" title="HM Treasury">HM Treasury</a>. The government then releases this data in a large consolidated document titled "Central Government Supply Estimates (Budget Year-Following Year): Main Supply Estimates" <sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The government reserves the right to submit "Supplementary Estimates" in the spring and winter of a given fiscal year to update its agencies' spending totals for the current financial year and report any governmental re-organizations. When an agency submits a Supplementary Estimate, it is customary to also submit an "Estimate Memorandum" to the agency's relevant oversight committee in Parliament describing and justifying the changes. This condenses two functions – reporting supplemental spending requests and agency re-organizations.
</p>
