<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Muxlim</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p><b>Muxlim</b> was primarily known as a <a href="/wiki/Social_networking" class="mw-redirect" title="Social networking">social networking</a> website for <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslims</a> that was focused on lifestyle aspects of the community. Founded in 2006 by Mohamed El-Fatatry and Pietari Päivänen of <a href="/wiki/Finland" title="Finland">Finland</a>, the company was created as a Muslim lifestyle social media network with primary focus on Muslims in Muslim-minority countries.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When the social network did not develop as planned, the company shifted its focus to provide marketing gateway services to the global Muslim consumer market, utilizing Muxlim.com as a launch pad for marketing messages by global companies.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2010, the company received the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tasavallan_Presidentin_kansainv%C3%A4listymispalkinto&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tasavallan Presidentin kansainvälistymispalkinto (page does not exist)">Tasavallan Presidentin kansainvälistymispalkinto</a> (in English: The Internationalization Award of the President of Finland).<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2012, Muxlim effectively shut down its social network services, as well as its Muxlim advisory services. This came after years of monetary losses. It is reported from the company's most recent financial reports that Muxlim's income was approximately 46,000 Euros while employer costs alone amounted to 300,000 Euros, with a total net loss for the year amounting to 865,000 Euros. In 2009 there was a recorded loss of 700,000 Euros.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> This came after an influx of investments jump-starting the company, in 2007 receiving USD $2 million from <a href="/w/index.php?title=Rite_Internet_Ventures&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Rite Internet Ventures (page does not exist)">Rite Internet Ventures</a>, and was soon looking for more investments<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Muxlim domain, as well as other services provided by the company such as IslamicTorrents.net, redirect to a promotional landing page for the founder's authorised biography. There has yet to be an official announcement regarding the fate of Muxlim.
</p>
