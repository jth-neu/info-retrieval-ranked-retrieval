<p><b>Helen Nissenbaum</b> is <a href="/wiki/Professor" title="Professor">professor</a> of <a href="/wiki/Information_science" title="Information science">information science</a> at <a href="/wiki/Cornell_Tech" title="Cornell Tech">Cornell Tech</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> She is best known for the concept of "<a href="/wiki/Contextual_Integrity" title="Contextual Integrity">contextual integrity</a>" and her work on <a href="/wiki/Privacy" title="Privacy">privacy</a>, <a href="/wiki/Privacy_law" title="Privacy law">privacy law</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trust_(emotion)" title="Trust (emotion)">trust</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Security" title="Security">security</a> in the online world. Specifically, contextual integrity has influenced the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> government's thinking about <a href="/wiki/Internet_privacy" title="Internet privacy">privacy issues</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-alexis_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-alexis-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Helen_Nissenbaum&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Nissenbaum has received grants from the <a href="/wiki/National_Science_Foundation" title="National Science Foundation">National Science Foundation</a>, Air Force Office of Scientific Research, <a href="/wiki/Ford_Foundation" title="Ford Foundation">Ford Foundation</a>, the <a href="/wiki/U.S._Department_of_Health_and_Human_Services" class="mw-redirect" title="U.S. Department of Health and Human Services">U.S. Department of Health and Human Services</a> Office of the National Coordinator, and the <a href="/wiki/Defense_Advanced_Research_Projects_Agency" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency">Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-bio_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-bio-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She is a contributor to the TrackMeNot privacy-through-obfuscation <a href="/wiki/Browser_extension" title="Browser extension">extension</a> for <a href="/wiki/Firefox" title="Firefox">Firefox</a> and <a href="/wiki/Google_Chrome" title="Google Chrome">Chrome</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> as well as the <a href="/w/index.php?title=AdNauseam&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="AdNauseam (page does not exist)">AdNauseam</a> extension for <a href="/wiki/Firefox" title="Firefox">Firefox</a>, <a href="/wiki/Google_Chrome" title="Google Chrome">Chrome</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Opera_(web_browser)" title="Opera (web browser)">Opera</a> that employs similar privacy-through-obfuscation tactics against ad network data collection.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
