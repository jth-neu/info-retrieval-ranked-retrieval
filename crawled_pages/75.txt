The holding company of the group was incorporated in the <a href="/wiki/Cayman_Islands" title="Cayman Islands">Cayman Islands</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-ir.baidu.c_Baidu_offers_various_services,_including_a_&#91;&#91;Chinese_language&#124;Chinese&#93;&#93;_search_engine,_as_well_as_a_mapping_service_called_&#91;&#91;Baidu_Maps&#93;&#93;._Baidu_offers_about_57_search_and_community_services,_for_example,_&#91;&#91;Baidu_Baike&#93;&#93;_(an_online_&#91;&#91;encyclopedia&#93;&#93;)_and_a_keyword-based_discussion_forum.&lt;ref_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ir.baidu.c_Baidu_offers_various_services,_including_a_[[Chinese_language|Chinese]]_search_engine,_as_well_as_a_mapping_service_called_[[Baidu_Maps]]._Baidu_offers_about_57_search_and_community_services,_for_example,_[[Baidu_Baike]]_(an_online_[[encyclopedia]])_and_a_keyword-based_discussion_forum.&lt;ref-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Baidu was established in 2000 by <a href="/wiki/Robin_Li" title="Robin Li">Robin Li</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eric_Xu" title="Eric Xu">Eric Xu</a>. Baidu is currently ranked 4th overall in the <a href="/wiki/Alexa_Internet" title="Alexa Internet">Alexa Internet</a> rankings.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>Baidu's Global Business Unit, formed under the name of <b>DU Group</b> or <b>DU Apps Studio</b>, is an app developer with various apps and services. It has over 2 billion active users worldwide.  Baidu also provides an official international and Chinese version of its popular online digital distribution services <b>Baidu App Store</b> and <b>Shouji Baidu</b> respectively, both hosting downloadable content and applications. Baidu's advertisement platform is <a href="/wiki/DU_Ad_Platform" title="DU Ad Platform">DU Ad Platform</a>.
</p><p>Baidu's <b>Apollo Project</b> is one of the world's leading <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous car">autonomous driving</a> and AI programs, with one of the largest partner ecosystems and over 100 global partners as of 2018, including <a href="/wiki/BYD_Auto" title="BYD Auto">BYD</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dongfeng_Motor_Corporation" title="Dongfeng Motor Corporation">Dongfeng</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>, <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Daimler_AG" title="Daimler AG">Daimler AG</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>, <a href="/wiki/ZTE" title="ZTE">ZTE</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grab_(company)" title="Grab (company)">Grab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ford_Motor_Company" title="Ford Motor Company">Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hyundai" title="Hyundai">Hyundai</a> and <a href="/wiki/Honda" title="Honda">Honda</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baidu has the 2nd largest search engine in the world, and held a 76.05% market share in China's search engine market. In December 2007, Baidu became the first Chinese company to be included in the <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ-100" title="NASDAQ-100">NASDAQ-100</a> index.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  As of May 2018, Baidu's <a href="/wiki/Market_capitalization" title="Market capitalization">market cap</a> rose to US$99 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_development">Early development</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Early development">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 1994, <a href="/wiki/Robin_Li" title="Robin Li">Robin Li</a> (Li Yanhong, 李彦宏) joined IDD Information Services, a <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a> division of <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_and_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="Dow Jones and Company">Dow Jones and Company</a>, where he helped develop software for the online edition of the <i><a href="/wiki/Wall_Street_Journal" class="mw-redirect" title="Wall Street Journal">Wall Street Journal</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He also worked on developing better algorithms for search engines and remained at IDD Information Services from May 1994 to June 1997.
</p><p>In 1996, while at IDD, Li developed the RankDex site-scoring algorithm for search engines results page ranking<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and received a US patent for the technology.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> He later used this technology for the Baidu search engine.
</p><p>In 2000, the company Baidu launched in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, China. The first office was located in a hotel room, which was near <a href="/wiki/Peking_University" title="Peking University">Peking University</a> from where Robin graduated.
</p><p>In 2003, Baidu launched a news search engine and picture search engine, adopting a special identification technology capable of identifying and grouping the articles.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_name_redirection_attack">Domain name redirection attack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Domain name redirection attack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On January 12, 2010, Baidu.com's DNS records in the United States were altered such that browsers to baidu.com were redirected to a website purporting to be the <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Cyber_Army" title="Iranian Cyber Army">Iranian Cyber Army</a>, thought to be behind the attack on <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">Twitter</a> during the <a href="/wiki/2009_Iranian_election_protests" class="mw-redirect" title="2009 Iranian election protests">2009 Iranian election protests</a>, making the proper site unusable for four hours.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Internet users were met with a page saying "This site has been attacked by <a href="/wiki/Iranian_Cyber_Army" title="Iranian Cyber Army">Iranian Cyber Army</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Chinese hackers later responded by attacking Iranian websites and leaving messages.<sup id="cite_ref-guard_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guard-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
Baidu later launched legal action against <a href="/wiki/Register.com" title="Register.com">Register.com</a> for gross negligence after it was revealed that Register.com's technical support staff changed the email address for Baidu.com on the request of an unnamed individual, despite failing security verification procedures. Once the address had been changed, the individual was able to use the forgotten password feature to have Baidu's domain passwords sent directly to them, allowing them to accomplish the <a href="/wiki/Domain_hijacking" title="Domain hijacking">domain hijacking</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baidu_workers_arrested">Baidu workers arrested</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Baidu workers arrested">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On August 6, 2012, the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a> reported that three employees of Baidu were arrested on suspicion that they accepted bribes. The bribes were allegedly paid for deleting posts from the forum service. Four people were fired in connection with these arrests.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="91_Wireless_acquisition">91 Wireless acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: 91 Wireless acquisition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 16, 2013, Baidu announced its intention to purchase <a href="/wiki/91_Wireless" title="91 Wireless">91 Wireless</a> from <a href="/wiki/NetDragon" class="mw-redirect" title="NetDragon">NetDragon</a>. 91 Wireless is best known for its app store, but it has been reported that the app store faces piracy and other legal issues.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> On August 14, 2013, Baidu announced that its wholly owned subsidiary Baidu (Hong Kong) Limited has signed a definitive merger agreement to acquire 91 Wireless Web-soft Limited from <a href="/wiki/NetDragon" class="mw-redirect" title="NetDragon">NetDragon</a> Web-soft Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> for 1.85 billion dollars in what was reported to be the biggest deal ever in China's IT sector.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="2010s_announcements">2010s announcements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2010s announcements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On July 31, 2012, Baidu announced they would team up with <a href="/wiki/Sina_Corp" title="Sina Corp">Sina</a> to provide mobile search results.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On November 18, 2012, Baidu announced that they would be partnering with <a href="/wiki/Qualcomm" title="Qualcomm">Qualcomm</a> to offer free cloud storage to <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a> users with <a href="/wiki/Snapdragon_(system_on_chip)" class="mw-redirect" title="Snapdragon (system on chip)">Snapdragon</a> processors.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On August 2, 2013, Baidu launched its Personal Assistant app, designed to help CEOs, managers and the white-collar workers manage their business relationships.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 16, 2014, Baidu appointed <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Ng" title="Andrew Ng">Dr. Andrew Ng</a> as chief scientist. Dr. Ng will lead Baidu Research in Silicon Valley and Beijing.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On July 18, 2014, the company launched a Brazilian version of the search engine, <i>Baidu Busca</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 9, 2014, Baidu announced acquisition of Brazilian local e-commerce site Peixe Urbano.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2017, Baidu announced to launch Apollo project, its self-driving vehicle platform in a bid to help drive the development of <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous car">autonomous cars</a> including vehicle platform, hardware platform, software platform and cloud data services.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> Baidu plans to launch  this project in July this year, before gradually introducing fully autonomous driving capabilities on highways and open city roads by 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2017, Baidu partnered with <a href="/wiki/Continental_AG" title="Continental AG">Continental</a> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Bosch_GmbH" title="Robert Bosch GmbH">Bosch</a>, auto industry suppliers, on automated driving and connected cars.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2017, Baidu has rolled out a new portable talking translator that can listen and speak in several different languages. Smaller than a typical smartphone, the 140-gram translation device can also be used as a portable Wi-Fi router and is able to operate on networks in 80 countries, it is still under development currently. Baidu will also be inserting artificial intelligence (AI) technology into smartphones, through its deep learning platform.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> At the same period, it has also led a joint investment of US$12 billion with <a href="/wiki/Alibaba_Group" title="Alibaba Group">Alibaba Group</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tencent" title="Tencent">Tencent</a>, <a href="/wiki/JD.com" title="JD.com">JD.com</a> and <a href="/wiki/Didi_Chuxing" class="mw-redirect" title="Didi Chuxing">Didi Chuxing</a>, acquiring 35% of <a href="/wiki/China_Unicom" title="China Unicom">China Unicom</a>'s stakes.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2017, According to <a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a>, Baidu would be launching self-driving bus in China the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In the same month, Baidu also announced its first annual <b>Baidu World</b> technology conference (<i>Bring AI to Life</i>) would be held and live-streamed on 16 November 2017 at China World Summit Wing and Kerry Hotel, bringing together Baidu executives, employees, partners, developers, and media to discuss the company’s mission and strategy, technology breakthroughs, new product developments, as well as its open artificial intelligence (AI) ecosystem.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Name">Name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The name <i>Baidu</i> (百度) literally means "a hundred times", or alternatively, "countless times". It is a quote from the last line of <a href="/wiki/Xin_Qiji" title="Xin Qiji">Xin Qiji</a> (辛弃疾)'s classical poem "Green Jade Table in The Lantern Festival" (青玉案·元夕) saying: "Having searched <b>hundreds of times</b> in the crowd, suddenly turning back, he is there in the dimmest candlelight." (众里寻他千<b>百度</b>，蓦然回首，那人却在灯火阑珊处。)<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baidu offers several services<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> to locate information, products and services using Chinese-language search terms, such as, search by <a href="/wiki/Modern_Standard_Chinese_phonology" class="mw-redirect" title="Modern Standard Chinese phonology">Chinese phonetics</a>, advanced search, snapshots, spell checker, stock quotes, news, knows, postbar, images, video and space information, and weather, train and flight schedules and other local information. The <a href="/wiki/User-agent_string" class="mw-redirect" title="User-agent string">user-agent string</a> of Baidu search engine is <i>Baiduspider</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li><i><a href="/wiki/Baidu_Maps" title="Baidu Maps">Baidu Maps</a></i> (<span lang="zh" title="Chinese language text">百度地图</span>) is a desktop and mobile mapping solution similar to Google Maps, but covering only the Greater China region</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertisements">Advertisements</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Advertisements">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baidu's primary advertising product is called Baidu Tuiguang and is similar to <a href="/wiki/Google_Adwords" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Adwords">Google Adwords</a> and <a href="/wiki/Adsense" class="mw-redirect" title="Adsense">Adsense</a>. It is a <a href="/wiki/Pay_per_click" class="mw-redirect" title="Pay per click">pay per click</a> advertising platform that allows advertisers to have their ads shown in Baidu search results pages and on other websites that are part of Baidu Union. However, Baidu's search results are also based on payments by advertisers. This has prompted criticism and skepticism among Chinese users, with <a href="/wiki/People%27s_Daily" title="People&#39;s Daily">People's daily</a> commenting in 2018 on issues regarding reliability of Baidu results. Often as many as the first two pages of search results tend to be paid advertisers.<sup id="cite_ref-79" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-79">&#91;79&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baidu sells its advertising products via a network of resellers.<sup id="cite_ref-80" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-80">&#91;80&#93;</a></sup> Baidu's web administrative tools are all in Chinese, which makes it tough for non-Chinese speakers to use. Recently, a third-party company began to develop a tool with an English-language interface for Baidu advertising programs.<sup id="cite_ref-81" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-81">&#91;81&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-82" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-82">&#91;82&#93;</a></sup> Paid advertising can only be used by advertisers with a registered business address either in China or in a list of other East Asian countries.<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Pay_for_placement_.28P4P.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Pay_for_placement_(P4P)">Pay for placement (P4P)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Pay for placement (P4P)">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baidu focuses on generating revenues primarily from <a href="/wiki/Online_marketing" class="mw-redirect" title="Online marketing">online marketing</a> services. Baidu's <a href="/wiki/Pay_for_placement" title="Pay for placement">pay for placement</a> (P4P) <a href="/wiki/Computing_platform" title="Computing platform">platform</a> enables its customers to reach users who search for information related to their products or services. Customers use automated online tools to create text-based descriptions of their web pages and bid on <a href="/wiki/Index_term" title="Index term">keywords</a> that trigger the display of their webpage information and link. Baidu's P4P platform features an automated online sign-up process that customers use to activate their accounts at any time. The P4P platform is an online marketplace that introduces Internet search users to customers who bid for priority <a href="/wiki/Favored_placement" title="Favored placement">placement</a> in the search results. Baidu also uses third-party distributors to sell some of its online marketing services to end customers and offers discounts to these distributors in consideration of their services.
</p><p>Baidu offers certain consultative services, such as keyword suggestions, account management and performance reporting. Baidu suggests synonyms and associated phrases to use as keywords or text in search listings. These suggestions can improve <a href="/wiki/Clickthrough_rate" class="mw-redirect" title="Clickthrough rate">clickthrough rates</a> of the customer's listing and increase the likelihood that a user will enter into a transaction with the customer. Baidu also provides online daily reports of the number of clickthroughs, clicked keywords and the total costs incurred, as well as statistical reports organized by geographic region.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> However, this too has invited criticism amongst Chinese internet users.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="ProTheme">ProTheme</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: ProTheme">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baidu offers ProTheme services to some of its Baidu Union members, which enable these members to display on their properties its customers' <a href="/wiki/Advertising_campaign" title="Advertising campaign">promotional</a> links that are relevant to the subject and content of such members' properties. Baidu generates revenues from ProTheme services based on the number of clicks on its customers' links and share the revenues with its Baidu Union members in accordance with pre-agreed terms. Baidu's fixed-ranking services allow customers to display query-sensitive text links at a designated location on its search results pages. Its Targetizement services enable customers to reach their targeted Internet users by displaying their advertisements only when their targeted Internet users browse Baidu's certain Web pages.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Baidu_TV">Baidu TV</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Baidu TV">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baidu operates its advertising service, Baidu TV, in partnership with Ads it! Media Corporation, an online advertising agency and technology company. Baidu TV provides advertisers access to the websites of its Baidu Union members, allowing advertisers to choose Websites on which they post their video advertisements with the aid of its advertisement targeting and matching system. It also offers a brand advertising service, Brand-Link. In June 2008, Baidu launched My Marketing Center, a customized platform integrating industry information, market trends and business, and industry news and reports to assist existing customers in their sales and marketing efforts. Other forms of its online advertising services allow customers to display <a href="/wiki/Web_search_query" title="Web search query">query</a> sensitive and non-query sensitive advertisements on its websites, including graphical advertisements.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Baidu_Union">Baidu Union</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Baidu Union">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baidu Union consists of several third-party websites and <a href="/wiki/Software_application" class="mw-redirect" title="Software application">software applications</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup> Union members incorporate a Baidu search box or <a href="/wiki/Toolbar" title="Toolbar">toolbar</a> and match its <a href="/wiki/Sponsored_link" class="mw-redirect" title="Sponsored link">sponsored links</a> with the content on their properties. Their users can conduct search via the Baidu search box or toolbar and can click the sponsored links located on their properties. Baidu has also launched programs through which it displays the <a href="/wiki/Online_advertising" title="Online advertising">online advertising</a> of its customers on Baidu Union websites, and share the fees generated by these advertisements with the owners of these Baidu Union websites. As of May 2011, there were 230,000 partner websites that displayed Baidu Union ads on their websites.<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

China">Yahoo! China</a>, <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> and <a href="/wiki/MSN_Messenger" class="mw-redirect" title="MSN Messenger">MSN Messenger</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sina.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Sina.com">Sina</a>, <a href="/wiki/NetEase" title="NetEase">NetEase</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Youdao" title="Youdao">Youdao</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tencent" title="Tencent">Tencent</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Soso.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Soso.com">Soso.com</a> and PaiPai, <a href="/wiki/Alibaba_Group" title="Alibaba Group">Alibaba</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Taobao" title="Taobao">Taobao</a>, <a href="/wiki/TOM_Online" title="TOM Online">TOM Online</a>, and <a href="/wiki/EachNet" class="mw-redirect" title="EachNet">EachNet</a>.
</p><p>Baidu is the No. 1 search engine in China, controlling 76.05 percent of China's market share. The <a href="/wiki/List_of_countries_by_number_of_Internet_users" title="List of countries by number of Internet users">number of Internet users</a> in China had reached 705 million by the end of   2015, according to a report by the internetlivestats.com.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In an August 2010 <i>Wall Street Journal</i> article,<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup>  Baidu played down its benefit from <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>'s having moved its China search service to <a href="/wiki/Hong_Kong" title="Hong Kong">Hong Kong</a>, but Baidu's share of revenue in China's search-advertising market grew six percentage points in the second quarter to 70%, according to Beijing-based research firm Analysys International.
</p><p>It is also evident that Baidu is attempting to enter the Internet social network market. As of  2011<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>, it is discussing the possibility of working with Facebook, which would lead to a Chinese version of the international social network, managed by Baidu.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup> This plan, if executed, would face off Baidu with competition from the three popular Chinese social networks <a href="/wiki/Qzone" title="Qzone">Qzone</a>, <a href="/wiki/Renren" title="Renren">Renren</a><sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Kaixin001" title="Kaixin001">Kaixin001</a><sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup> as well as induce rivalry with instant-messaging giant, <a href="/wiki/Tencent_QQ" title="Tencent QQ">Tencent QQ</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-92" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-92">&#91;92&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 22, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Hudong" class="mw-redirect" title="Hudong">Hudong</a> submitted a complaint to the <a href="/wiki/State_Administration_for_Industry_and_Commerce" title="State Administration for Industry and Commerce">State Administration for Industry and Commerce</a> asking for a review of the behavior of Baidu, accusing it of being monopolistic.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 9, 2013, Baidu was still number one in the market, with 64.5% of the users, the closest competitor, Qihoo 360, who launched its own search engine in August, has already taken hold of 10.2% users. Following are Google and Sogou.<sup id="cite_ref-94" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-94">&#91;94&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By August 2014, Baidu's search market share in China has dropped to 56.3%, where Qihoo 360, its closest competitor who has rebranded its search engine as so.com, has increased its market share to 29.0%, according to report from CNZZ.com.<sup id="cite_ref-95" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-95">&#91;95&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2015, Baidu was alleged to use anticompetitive tactics in Brazil against the Brazilian online security firm PSafe and <a href="/wiki/Qihoo_360" title="Qihoo 360">Qihoo 360</a> (the largest investor of PSafe).<sup id="cite_ref-96" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-96">&#91;96&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-97" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-97">&#91;97&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Research_and_patents">Research and patents</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Research and patents">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Baidu has started to invest in <a href="/wiki/Deep_learning" title="Deep learning">deep learning</a> research and is integrating new deep learning technology into some of its apps and products, including Phoenix Nest. Phoenix Nest is Baidu's ad-bidding platform.<sup id="cite_ref-98" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-98">&#91;98&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2012 Baidu applied for a patent for its "DNA copyright recognition" technology. This technology automatically scans files that are uploaded by Internet users, and recognizes and filters out content that may violate copyright law. This allows Baidu to offer an infringement-free platform.<sup id="cite_ref-99" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-99">&#91;99&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-100" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-100">&#91;100&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Baidu has applied for a utility patent in the UK, for its proprietary site-wise search technology which is currently available in China.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Censorship">Censorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Censorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the <i><a href="/wiki/China_Digital_Times" title="China Digital Times">China Digital Times</a></i>, Baidu has a long history of being the most active and restrictive online censor in the search arena. Documents leaked in April 2009 from an employee in Baidu's internal monitoring and censorship department show a long list of blocked websites and censored topics on Baidu search.<sup id="cite_ref-101" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-101">&#91;101&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2011, activists sued Baidu in the United States for violating the U.S. Constitution by the censorship it conducts in accord with the demand of the Chinese government.<sup id="cite_ref-102" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-102">&#91;102&#93;</a></sup> A U.S. judge has ruled<sup id="cite_ref-103" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-103">&#91;103&#93;</a></sup> that the Chinese search engine Baidu has the right to block pro-democracy works from its query results, dismissing a lawsuit that sought to punish the company for Internet censorship.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Death_of_Wei_Zexi">Death of Wei Zexi</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Death of Wei Zexi">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Death_of_Wei_Zexi" title="Death of Wei Zexi">Death of Wei Zexi</a></div>
<p>In May 2016, Baidu's P4P search results reportedly caused the death of a student who tried an experimental cancer therapy he found online. The 21-year-old college student was named Wèi Zéxī  (魏则西), who studied in <a href="/wiki/Xidian_University" title="Xidian University">Xidian University</a>. Wei was diagnosed with <a href="/wiki/Synovial_sarcoma" title="Synovial sarcoma">synovial sarcoma</a>, a rare form of cancer. He found the Second Hospital of the Beijing Armed Police Corps (武警北京市总队第二医院) through the search engine Baidu, on which the hospital had been promoting itself. The treatment proved to be unsuccessful.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>After Wei's family spent around 200,000 yuan (around 31,150 USD) for treatment in the hospital, Wei Zexi died on April 12, 2016. The incident triggered massive online discussions after Wei's death. The fact that Baidu valued money way more than life kindled nationwide rage.<sup id="cite_ref-104" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-104">&#91;104&#93;</a></sup> On May 2, 2016, <a href="/wiki/Cyberspace_Administration_of_China" title="Cyberspace Administration of China">Cyberspace Administration of China</a> (CAC), the top watchdog for China's Internet space, dispatched a team of investigators to Baidu.<sup id="cite_ref-105" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-105">&#91;105&#93;</a></sup> The case is still ongoing. One report claimed medical advertising makes up for 30% of Baidu's ad revenue, much of which comes from for-profit hospitals that belong to the "Putian Network", a collection of hospitals across the country founded by medical entrepreneurs associated with the <a href="/wiki/Putian" title="Putian">Putian</a> region of Fujian province.<sup id="cite_ref-106" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-106">&#91;106&#93;</a></sup> The investigation led Chinese regulators to impose several restrictions on Baidu, including adding disclaimers to promotional content and establishing channels for complaints about Baidu services.<sup id="cite_ref-107" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-107">&#91;107&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Commercialization_of_Tieba">Commercialization of Tieba</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Baidu&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Commercialization of Tieba">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Baidu sold the hemophilia online community, one of the communities of Tieba, to unqualified hospitals. In January 2016, Baidu announced that it will stop selling all of its illness-related Tieba.<sup id="cite_ref-108" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-108">&#91;108&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
