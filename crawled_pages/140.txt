<p>A <b>home page</b> or a <b>start page</b> is the initial or main <a href="/wiki/Web_page" title="Web page">web page</a> of a <a href="/wiki/Website" title="Website">website</a> or a <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">browser</a>. The initial page of a website is sometimes called <b>main page</b> as well.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Website_home_page">Website home page</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_page&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Website home page">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A home page is generally the main page a visitor navigating to a website from a <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine" title="Web search engine">web search engine</a> will see, and it may also serve as a <a href="/wiki/Landing_page" title="Landing page">landing page</a> to attract visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-wdi76_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wdi76-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The home page is used to facilitate navigation to other pages on the site by providing links to prioritized and recent articles and pages, and possibly a search box.<sup id="cite_ref-nn_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nn-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> For example, a news website may present headlines and first paragraphs of top stories, with links to full articles, in a <a href="/wiki/Dynamic_web_page" title="Dynamic web page">dynamic web page</a> that reflects the popularity and recentness of stories.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Meanwhile, other websites use the homepage to attract users to create an account. Once they are logged in, the homepage may be redirected to their profile page. This may in turn be referred to as the "personal home page".<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A website may have multiple home pages, although most have one.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>, for example, has a home page at wikipedia.org, as well as language-specific home pages, such as en.wikipedia.org and de.wikipedia.org.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Website_structure">Website structure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_page&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Website structure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The majority of websites have a home page with underlying content pages, although some websites contain only a single page.<sup id="cite_ref-wdi94_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wdi94-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Uniform_resource_locator" class="mw-redirect" title="Uniform resource locator">uniform resource locator</a> (URL) of a home page is most often the base-level <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain name</a>, such as <a class="external free" href="https://wikipedia.org">https://wikipedia.org</a>. Historically it may also be found at http://domain.tld/index.html or http://domain.tld/default.html, where "tld" refers to the <a href="/wiki/Top-level_domain" title="Top-level domain">top-level domain</a> used by the website.<sup id="cite_ref-url_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-url-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>If a home page has not been created for a web site, many <a href="/wiki/Web_server" title="Web server">web servers</a> will default to display a list of files located in the site's directory, if the security settings of the directory permit.<sup id="cite_ref-dir_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dir-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> This list will include <a href="/wiki/Hyperlinks" class="mw-redirect" title="Hyperlinks">hyperlinks</a> to the files, allowing for simple <a href="/wiki/File_sharing" title="File sharing">file sharing</a> without maintaining a separate HTML file.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Browser_home_page">Browser home page</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_page&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Browser home page">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A home page also refers to the first page that appears upon opening a <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browser</a>, sometimes called the <b>start page</b>, although the home page of a website can be used as a start page. This start page can be a website, or it can be a page with various browser functions such as the display of thumbnails of frequently visited websites. Multiple websites can be set as a start page, to open in different <a href="/wiki/Tab_(GUI)" title="Tab (GUI)">tabs</a>. Some websites are intended to be used as start pages, such as <a href="/wiki/IGoogle" title="IGoogle">iGoogle</a> (now defunct), <a href="/wiki/My_Yahoo!" title="My Yahoo!">My Yahoo!</a>, and <a href="/wiki/MSN#MSN.com" title="MSN">MSN.com</a>, and provide links to commonly used services such as <a href="/wiki/Webmail" title="Webmail">webmail</a> and online weather forecasts.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_of_home_pages">History of home pages</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_page&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: History of home pages">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the early days of the <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Web" title="World Wide Web">World Wide Web</a> in the first half of the 1990s, an important part of web pages belonged to students or teachers with a <a href="/wiki/UNIX" class="mw-redirect" title="UNIX">UNIX</a> account in their university. <a href="/wiki/System_administrators" class="mw-redirect" title="System administrators">System administrators</a> of such systems installed an <a href="/wiki/HTTP_server" class="mw-redirect" title="HTTP server">HTTP server</a> pointing its <a href="/wiki/Root_directory" title="Root directory">root directory</a> to the directory containing the users accounts. On UNIX, the base directory of an account is called "<a href="/wiki/Home_directory" title="Home directory">home</a>", and the <code>HOME</code> environment variable contains its <a href="/wiki/Path_(computing)" title="Path (computing)">path</a> (for example <code>/home/my_username</code>). The URL of the home page usually has the format <code>https://example.edu/~my_username/</code>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Thus the term home page appeared and then spread to its current usage.
</p><p>A personal home page historically has served as a means of self-portrayal, job-related presentation, and pure enjoyment, giving way to professional advancement and social interaction.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Owing to the rise of <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> sites, personal home pages are no longer as common as during the mid-late 1990s and early-2000s.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_uses">Other uses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Home_page&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Other uses">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A <a href="/wiki/Personal_web_page" title="Personal web page">personal web page</a> is also commonly called a home page, although such websites can contain many pages.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> In Germany the term "homepage" is often used as a synonym for the term "website".
</p><p>A home page can also be used outside the context of web browsers, such as to refer to the principal screen of a <a href="/wiki/User_interface" title="User interface">user interface</a>, frequently referred to as a <a href="/wiki/Home_screen" title="Home screen">home screen</a> on mobile devices such as <a href="/wiki/Mobile_phone" title="Mobile phone">mobile phones</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
