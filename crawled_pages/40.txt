<p><b>Qwant</b> is a <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">French</a> company that was founded by security specialist <a href="/w/index.php?title=%C3%89ric_Leandri&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Éric Leandri (page does not exist)">Éric Leandri</a>, investor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Manuel_Rozan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jean Manuel Rozan (page does not exist)">Jean Manuel Rozan</a> and search-engine expert <a href="/w/index.php?title=Patrick_Constant&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Patrick Constant (page does not exist)">Patrick Constant</a> in 2011. It launched its eponymous <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine" title="Web search engine">web search engine</a> in July 2013. It claims not to employ <a href="/wiki/Website_visitor_tracking" title="Website visitor tracking">user tracking</a>, and it doesn't personalize search results in order to avoid trapping users in a <a href="/wiki/Filter_bubble" title="Filter bubble">filter bubble</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The website processes well over 10 million search requests per day and over 50 million individual users a month worldwide<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>, spread over its three main entry points: the normal homepage, a 'lite' version and a 'Qwant Junior' portal for children that filters results.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company claims it makes money through commissions it receives when users visit websites like eBay and Tripadvisor from its search results.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2017, press articles suggest that Qwant search results are mainly based on Bing search results, except in France and Germany.<sup id="cite_ref-auto_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Qwant also confirmed the use of <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> advertising network.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_engine">Search engine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Qwant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Search engine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The search engine entered public beta in 16 February 2013, after two years of research and development.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The service initially launched on 16 February 2013, as a <a href="/wiki/Software_release_life_cycle#Beta" title="Software release life cycle">beta</a> version, with a stable version released on 4 July 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> A new version was made available in April 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Qwant offers several ways to filter results to areas of interest:<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>Web</li>
<li>News</li>
<li>Social</li>
<li>Images</li>
<li>Videos</li>
<li>Music (Beta)</li></ul>
<p>In October 2015, Qwant released Qwant Lite, a lighter and faster version of Qwant.com, aimed at being <a href="/wiki/User-friendly" class="mw-redirect" title="User-friendly">user-friendly</a> for those with older browsers and others that do not have powerful or resource-rich computers. Integrated features like video playback and JavaScript were removed and the on-site content was streamlined.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The design of this version mimics that of Google's minimalist interface.
</p><p>The child-friendly version was developed in cooperation with the French <a href="/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Education_(France)" title="Ministry of National Education (France)">ministry of Education</a>.
</p><p>According to its founder, Qwant does not want to compete with <a href="/wiki/Google_Search" title="Google Search">Google</a> but prefers "to show something different". Users can create a free account, which allows posting on the 'boards', a feature with functions similar to those of a <a href="/wiki/Social_bookmarking" title="Social bookmarking">social bookmarking</a> platform.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Earlier stand-out features, such as a knowledge graph (called the Qnowledge Graph) based on Wikipedia, seem to have been discontinued.
</p><p>In July 2016, Mozilla signed a deal with Qwant to allow them to distribute an officially sanctioned version of the <a href="/wiki/Firefox" title="Firefox">Firefox</a> web browser with Qwant as default search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Currently, Qwant has a web browser based on Firefox on the <a href="/wiki/App_Store_(iOS)" title="App Store (iOS)">Apple App Store</a> available for <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a>.
</p><p>With the first release on 21 July 2017, <a href="/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)" title="Brave (web browser)">Brave</a> web browser features Qwant as one of its default search engines.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Company">Company</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Qwant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Company">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was cofounded in 2011 by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Jean_Manuel_Rozan&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Jean Manuel Rozan (page does not exist)">Jean-Manuel Rozan</a>, a financier, and Éric Leandri, a specialist in <a href="/wiki/Computer_security" title="Computer security">computer security</a>. It employs between 25 and 50 people, spread over three French cities, including Paris and <a href="/wiki/Ajaccio" title="Ajaccio">Ajaccio</a>, Corsica.
</p><p>From 2011 to 2014 Qwant acquired a total funding of <a href="/wiki/%E2%82%AC" class="mw-redirect" title="€">€</a>3.5 million, part of it a loan. It was aided in its operation by special tax breaks from the French Government. In 2014 it received additional funding, including a €6 million investment from <a href="/wiki/Axel_Springer_SE" title="Axel Springer SE">Axel Springer</a> Digital Ventures in return for a 20% stake in the company.<sup id="cite_ref-Scott_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Scott-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 2016 the <a href="/wiki/European_Investment_Bank" title="European Investment Bank">European Investment Bank</a> invested €25 million.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Privacy">Privacy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Qwant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Privacy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By virtue of being based in Europe, users gain some protection due to stringent European privacy laws. Qwant offers protections based on the new <a href="/wiki/General_Data_Protection_Regulation" title="General Data Protection Regulation">General Data Protection Regulation</a>, which took effect in May 2018.
</p><p>Qwant seems<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Words_to_watch#Unsupported_attributions" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Words to watch"><span title="The material near this tag possibly uses too-vague attribution or weasel words. (February 2018)">who?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> to take numerous technical precautions to limit third parties from gaining insight into users search queries. For example, image search results are made by routing images through Qwant servers, so that the websites serving the images are not informed of the user's identity.
</p><p>
The website also states, in its privacy statement, that it resists French government surveillance:<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></p><blockquote><p>"Regarding the French law on intelligence passed on 24 June 2015, we found that the recommendations of the CNIL had gained very little currency: as a search engine, we emphatically do not endorse the measures adopted, especially as they are particularly intrusive from the point of view of individual privacy. Accordingly, we will make every effort to ensure the protection of our users' personal data".</p></blockquote>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Criticism">Criticism</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Qwant&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Criticism">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Shortly after the release, some observers expressed doubts about the nature of Qwant. According to them, Qwant may not really be a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> but simply a website aggregating results of other search engines like <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a>, and that the "Qnowledge Graph" is based on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The company has rejected the reports and asserts that they do have their own <a href="/wiki/Web_crawler" title="Web crawler">Web crawler</a> and used other search engines in their primary developmental phase only for semantic indexing related purposes.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>
While respecting privacy, the terms of service state that these may be changed at any time:<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> </p><blockquote><p>"Qwant reserves the right to unilaterally modify these terms of service to adapt to the future developments of the site and/or its operation".</p></blockquote><p>The early versions of Qwant copied many design cues from Google.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2017, news articles reported Qwant displays mainly search results from <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a>, except in France and Germany, despite several commitments to be exclusively "made in France".<sup id="cite_ref-auto_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-auto-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
