<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">123people</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p>Initially, <b>123people</b> was developed as a real time people search service, which gathered information from the <a href="/wiki/Deep_Web_(search_indexing)" class="mw-redirect" title="Deep Web (search indexing)">deep web</a> and over 200 external datasources, to present it in a structured search result dashboard view. 123people only gathers publicly available information from an extensive list of international as well as regional sources.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Later additional services were Webcleaner which was designed to delete web content (such as pictures, comments, or articles) from the Internet,and also the Social Network Monitor which checked the user's Facebook profile towards privacy and reputation threats. 123people stopped trading in 2014 and is defunct.<sup id="cite_ref-down_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-down-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=123people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>123people was a product of the 123people Internetservices GmbH. 123people Internetservices GmbH was founded in 2007 by European IT incubator i5invest with initial funding from Austrian venture capital company Gamma Capital Partners. The company was based in <a href="/wiki/Vienna" title="Vienna">Vienna</a>, Austria. Since March 2010, 123people was part of the French <a href="/wiki/Pages_Jaunes" class="mw-redirect" title="Pages Jaunes">Solocal Group</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> At the beginning of 2012 the 123people Internetservices GmbH renamed itself into Yelster Digital GMBH.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This was also the starting point for the development of new activities by yelster digital: In January 2012 the company had announced the launch of 123pages in France, an online search service for businesses and services; in April 2012 123pages was launched in Austria.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In January 2013 the company also gave a face lift and a product extension to the people search engine, 123people. Webcleaner and Social Network Monitor were the two new paid products that aimed to help web-users to monitor and protect their online reputation and personal safety.
</p><p>The service was taken down permanently March 31, 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-down_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-down-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_model">Business model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=123people&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Business model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>123people had a multi-tier business model offering standard and customized banner campaigns in all of its 12 top-level domains, applying traditional <a href="/wiki/Pay_per_click" class="mw-redirect" title="Pay per click">CPC</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cost_per_mille" title="Cost per mille">CPM</a> pricing models. Affiliate partnerships were also a main focus to 123people. 123people integrated over 200 local and international partners providing content such as directory services, premium public records and social networks, as well as products such as wish lists and books. Partners were integrated via teasers or text links within each of its sections. 123people's pricing models for partners included <a href="/wiki/Pay_per_click" class="mw-redirect" title="Pay per click">CPC</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cost_per_mille" title="Cost per mille">CPM</a> and <a href="/wiki/Cost_per_action" title="Cost per action">CPA</a>.
</p>
