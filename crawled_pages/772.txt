<p>The <b>Council for the Curriculum, Examinations &amp; Assessment</b> (<b>CCEA</b>) is a <a href="/wiki/Non-departmental_public_body" title="Non-departmental public body">Non-departmental public body</a> (NDPB) of the <a href="/wiki/Department_of_Education_(Northern_Ireland)" title="Department of Education (Northern Ireland)">Department of Education (Northern Ireland)</a>.
</p><p>CCEA’s principal products and services are described in the Education (NI) Order 1998. CCEA’s duties and functions are to:
</p>
<ul><li>keep under review all aspects of the <a href="/wiki/Curriculum" title="Curriculum">curriculum</a>, examinations and assessment for grant aided schools and <a href="/wiki/Colleges_of_Further_Education" class="mw-redirect" title="Colleges of Further Education">colleges of further education</a> and to undertake statutory consultation on proposals relating to legislation;</li>
<li>advise the DE on matters concerned with the curriculum, assessment, examinations and external qualifications and accredit and approve qualifications;</li>
<li>conduct and moderate examinations and assessments, ensuring that standards are recognised as equivalent to standards of examinations and assessments conducted by other bodies or authorities exercising similar functions in the United Kingdom;</li>
<li>publish and disseminate information relating to the curriculum, assessment and examinations;</li>
<li>develop and produce teaching support materials for use in schools; and</li>
<li>carry out research and development.</li></ul>
<p>CCEA also has a remit for the development of <a href="/wiki/Educational_technology" title="Educational technology">educational technology</a> and the production of multimedia resources and is considered a leader in this field.
</p><p>CCEA was established on 1 April 1994<sup id="cite_ref-ccea.org.uk_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ccea.org.uk-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> as a NDPB and is based in <a href="/wiki/Belfast" title="Belfast">Belfast</a>. It replaced the Northern Ireland Schools Examination and Assessment Council and the Northern Ireland Schools Examination Council.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Awarding_Qualifications">Awarding Qualifications</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Council_for_the_Curriculum,_Examinations_%26_Assessment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Awarding Qualifications">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CCEA offers a wide range of qualifications, such as GCSEs, including the new GCSE Double Award specifications in vocational subjects, GCE A and AS levels, Entry Level Qualifications, Keyskills, Essential Skills, and Graded Objectives in Modern Languages. Due to educational reforms of the <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative Party</a> under <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister" class="mw-redirect" title="Prime Minister">Prime Minister</a> <a href="/wiki/David_Cameron" title="David Cameron">David Cameron</a> CCEA (among other UK examination boards i.e. <a href="/wiki/Edexcel" title="Edexcel">Edexcel</a>, <a href="/wiki/Assessment_and_Qualifications_Alliance" class="mw-redirect" title="Assessment and Qualifications Alliance">AQA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxford,_Cambridge_and_RSA_Examinations" title="Oxford, Cambridge and RSA Examinations">OCR</a> and <a href="/wiki/WJEC_(exam_board)" title="WJEC (exam board)">WJEC</a>) continuously redevelops <a href="/wiki/Syllabi" class="mw-redirect" title="Syllabi">syllabi</a> for GCSEs and GCE A Levels.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Principal_Products_and_Services">Principal Products and Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Council_for_the_Curriculum,_Examinations_%26_Assessment&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Principal Products and Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>CCEA’s principal products and services are to meet the requirements outlined in the Education (NI) Order.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> CCEA’s duties and functions are therefore to:
</p>
<ul><li>Keep under constant review all aspects of the curriculum, examinations and assessment for grant aided schools and colleges of further education and to undertake statutory consultation on proposals relating to legislation;</li>
<li>Advise the Department of Education (NI) or The Minister for Education<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  on matters concerning curriculum, assessments, examinations and external qualifications and to accredit and approve qualifications;</li>
<li>Conduct and moderate examinations and assessments, keeping standards in line with the rest of the UK;</li>
<li>Publish and disseminate information relating to the curriculum, assessment and examinations;</li>
<li>Develop and produce teaching support materials for use in schools and other educational Centres;</li>
<li>Carry out research and development into curriculums, examinations, assessments, new media and software/hardware environments.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
