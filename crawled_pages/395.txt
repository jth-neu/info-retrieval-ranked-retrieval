<p><b>Motorsport</b> or <b>motor sport</b> is a global term used to encompass the group of competitive <a href="/wiki/Sports" class="mw-redirect" title="Sports">sporting</a> events which primarily involve the use of <a href="/wiki/Motor_vehicles" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor vehicles">motorised vehicles</a>, whether for racing or non-racing competition.  The <a href="/wiki/Terminology" title="Terminology">terminology</a> can also be used to describe forms of competition of two-wheeled motorised vehicles under the banner of <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle_racing" title="Motorcycle racing">motorcycle racing</a>, and includes off-road racing such as <a href="/wiki/Motocross" title="Motocross">motocross</a>.
</p><p>Four- (or more) wheeled motorsport competition is globally governed by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA); and the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_Motocyclisme" title="Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme">Fédération Internationale de Motocyclisme</a> (FIM) governs two-wheeled competition.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1894, a French newspaper organised a race from <a href="/wiki/Paris%E2%80%93Rouen_(motor_race)" title="Paris–Rouen (motor race)">Paris to Rouen</a> and back, starting city to city racing.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGifford7_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGifford7-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  In 1900, the <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Bennett_Cup_(auto_racing)" title="Gordon Bennett Cup (auto racing)">Gordon Bennett Cup</a> was established.  Closed <a href="/wiki/Circuit_racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Circuit racing">circuit racing</a> arose as open road racing, on public roads, was banned.  <a href="/wiki/Brooklands" title="Brooklands">Brooklands</a> was the first dedicated motor racing track in the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGifford9_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGifford9-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Following <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">World War I</a>, European countries organised <a href="/wiki/Grand_Prix_motor_racing" title="Grand Prix motor racing">Grand Prix races</a> over closed courses.  In the United States, dirt track racing became popular.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGifford10_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGifford10-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>After World War II, the Grand Prix circuit became more formally organised.  In the United States, <a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">stock car racing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Drag_racing" title="Drag racing">drag racing</a> became firmly established.<sup id="cite_ref-FOOTNOTEGifford11_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FOOTNOTEGifford11-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Motorsports ultimately became divided by types of <a href="/wiki/Motor_vehicles" class="mw-redirect" title="Motor vehicles">motor vehicles</a> into racing events, and their appropriate organisations.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Racing"><span id="Racing"></span><span id="Motor_racing"></span> Racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">"Motor racing" redirects here. For other uses, see <a href="/wiki/Motor_racing_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Motor racing (disambiguation)">Motor racing (disambiguation)</a>.</div>
<p><b>Motor racing</b><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> is the subset of motorsport activities which involve competitors <a href="/wiki/Racing" title="Racing">racing</a> against each other.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_wheel_racing">Open wheel racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Open wheel racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_racing">Formula racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Formula racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/220px-Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" width="220" height="147" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/330px-Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/19/Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg/440px-Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2560" data-file-height="1707" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Nico_Rosberg_2015_Malaysia_FP1.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The <a href="/wiki/Mercedes_F1_W06_Hybrid" title="Mercedes F1 W06 Hybrid">Mercedes F1 W06 Hybrid</a>, the 2015 Formula One World Championship winning car</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Formula_racing" title="Formula racing">Formula racing</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Formula_racing" title="Formula racing">Formula racing</a> is a set of classes of motor vehicles, with their wheels outside, and not contained by, any bodywork of their vehicle.  These have been globally classified as specific 'Formula' series - the most common being <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a>, and many others include the likes of <a href="/wiki/Formula_3" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula 3">Formula 3</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formula_Ford" title="Formula Ford">Formula Ford</a>, <a href="/wiki/Formula_Renault" title="Formula Renault">Formula Renault</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_Palmer_Audi" title="Formula Palmer Audi">Formula Palmer Audi</a>.  However, in North America, the <a href="/wiki/IndyCar" title="IndyCar">IndyCar</a> series is their pinnacle open-wheeled racing series.  More recently, new open-wheeled series have been created, originating in Europe, which omit the 'Formula' moniker, such as <a href="/wiki/GP2_Series" title="GP2 Series">GP2</a> and <a href="/wiki/GP3_Series" title="GP3 Series">GP3</a>.  Former 'Formula' series include <a href="/wiki/Formula_5000" title="Formula 5000">Formula 5000</a> and <a href="/wiki/Formula_Two" title="Formula Two">Formula Two</a>.
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_One">Formula One</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Formula One">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> is a class of single-seat and <a href="/wiki/Open-wheel_car" title="Open-wheel car">open-wheel</a> grand prix closed course racing, governed by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile</a> (FIA), and currently organized by the privately owned company <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_Group" title="Formula One Group">Formula One Group</a>. The formula regulations contain a very strict set of rules which govern vehicle power, weight and size.<sup id="cite_ref-f1fd_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-f1fd-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="Formula_E">Formula E</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Formula E">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Formula_E" title="Formula E">Formula E</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Formula_E" title="Formula E">Formula E</a> is a class of open-wheel auto racing that uses only electric-powered cars. The series was conceived in 2012, and the inaugural championship started in <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a> on 13 September 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The series is also sanctioned by the <a href="/wiki/F%C3%A9d%C3%A9ration_Internationale_de_l%27Automobile" title="Fédération Internationale de l&#39;Automobile">FIA</a> and races a spec chassis/battery combination with manufacturers allowed to develop their own electric power-trains. The series has gained significant traction in recent years.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h5><span class="mw-headline" id="IndyCar_Series">IndyCar Series</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: IndyCar Series">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h5>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/IndyCar_Series" title="IndyCar Series">IndyCar Series</a></div>
<p>A series originated on June 12th, 1909 in <a href="/wiki/Portland,_Oregon" title="Portland, Oregon">Portland, Oregon</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> at its first race. Shortly after, <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_Motor_Speedway" title="Indianapolis Motor Speedway">Indianapolis Motor Speedway </a> opened in 1909 and held races that ranged from 50-200 miles.  Its premier race is the <a href="/wiki/Indianapolis_500" title="Indianapolis 500">Indianapolis 500</a> which began on May 11th, 1911 and a tradition was born. Today, <a href="/wiki/Indycar" class="mw-redirect" title="Indycar">Indycar</a> operates a full schedule with over 20 teams and 40 different drivers. The current schedule includes 14 tracks over the course of 17 races per season <sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>. <a href="/wiki/Josef_Newgarden" title="Josef Newgarden">Josef Newgarden</a> was crowned current champion of the <a href="/wiki/Indycar" class="mw-redirect" title="Indycar">Indycar</a> Series at <a href="/wiki/Sonoma_Raceway" title="Sonoma Raceway">Sonoma Raceway</a> on September 17th, 2017 in Sonoma, California.
</p><p><br />
</p>

<p>Enclosed wheel racing is a set of classes of vehicles, where the wheels are primarily enclosed inside the bodywork of the vehicle, similar to a North American 'stock car'.
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports_car_racing">Sports car racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Sports car racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Sports_car_racing" title="Sports car racing">Sports car racing</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Sports_car_racing" title="Sports car racing">Sports car racing</a> is a set of classes of vehicles, over a closed course track, including sports cars, and specialised racing types.  The premiere race is the <a href="/wiki/24_Hours_of_Le_Mans" title="24 Hours of Le Mans">24 Hours of Le Mans</a> which takes place annually in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> during the month of June.  Sports car racing rules and specifications differentiate in North America from established international sanctioning bodies.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Stock_car_racing">Stock car racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Stock car racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">Stock car racing</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">Stock car racing</a> is a set of vehicles, that race over a speedway track, organized by <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a>. While once stock cars, the vehicles are now purpose built, but resemble the body design and shape of production cars. Bootleggers throughout the Carolinas are often credited for the origins of <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> due to the resistance during the prohibition.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Many of the vehicles were modified to increase top speed and handling, to provide the bootleggers with an advantage toward the vehicles local law enforcement would use in the area.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> An important part to the modifications of stock cars, was to increase the performance of the vehicle while also maintaining the same exterior look giving it the name <a href="/wiki/Stock_car_racing" title="Stock car racing">Stock car racing</a>. Many legends in <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> originated as bootleggers in the <a href="/wiki/Great_Smoky_Mountains" title="Great Smoky Mountains">Great Smoky Mountains</a> of <a href="/wiki/North_Carolina" title="North Carolina">North Carolina</a> like <a href="/wiki/Junior_Johnson" title="Junior Johnson">Junior Johnson</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>  Organized oval racing began on Daytona Beach in <a href="/wiki/Florida" title="Florida">Florida</a> as a hobby but quickly gained interest from all over the country. As oval racing became larger and larger, a group gathered in hopes to form a sanctioning body for the sport.<sup id="cite_ref-history.com_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history.com-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> was organized in 1947, to combine flat track oval racing of production cars.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Daytona_Beach_and_Road_Course" title="Daytona Beach and Road Course">Daytona Beach and Road Course</a> was founded where land speed records were set on the beach, and including part of <a href="/wiki/A1A" class="mw-redirect" title="A1A">A1A</a>. The highlight of the stock car calendar is the season-opening <a href="/wiki/Daytona_500" title="Daytona 500">Daytona 500</a>, also nicknamed 'The Great American Race' which is held at <a href="/wiki/Daytona_International_Speedway" title="Daytona International Speedway">Daytona International Speedway</a> in Daytona Beach, Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/NASCAR" title="NASCAR">NASCAR</a> has now held over 2,500 sanctioned events over the course of 70 seasons<sup id="cite_ref-history.com_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history.com-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>. <a href="/wiki/Richard_Petty" title="Richard Petty">Richard Petty</a> is known as the king of NASCAR with over 200 recorded wins in the series and has competed in 1,184 races in his career.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Touring_car_racing">Touring car racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Touring car racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">Touring car racing</a></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/Touring_car_racing" title="Touring car racing">Touring car racing</a> is a set of vehicles,<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> modified street cars, that race over closed purpose built race tracks and street courses.
</p><p><br />
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Off-Road_Racing">Off-Road Racing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Motorsport&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Off-Road Racing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Off-Road_Racing" class="mw-redirect" title="Off-Road Racing">Off-Road Racing</a></div>
<p>Off-Road Racing is a group of vehicles that specialize in off-road racing and are modified street cars that can race on close purpose built off-road tracks and courses.  Off-Road racing is popular in areas like <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arizona" title="Arizona">Arizona</a>.  Premier off-road events in the United States include the <a href="/wiki/Baja_1000" title="Baja 1000">Baja 1000</a> which takes place in the <a href="/wiki/Baja_California_Peninsula" title="Baja California Peninsula">Baja California Peninsula</a><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and endures nearly 1000 miles of racing on a closed and pre-designed Baja Truck course. Series like the <a href="/wiki/National_Off-Road_Racing_Association" title="National Off-Road Racing Association">National Off-Road Racing Association</a> (NORRA) utilize off-road vehicles which was founded in 1967 to sanction events through the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Baja_Desert&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Baja Desert (page does not exist)">Baja Desert</a><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>. The first event sanctioned by the organization was the 1967 Mexican 1000 rally that began in <a href="/wiki/Tijuana" title="Tijuana">Tijuana</a> and ended in <a href="/wiki/La_Paz" title="La Paz">La Paz</a>.
</p>
