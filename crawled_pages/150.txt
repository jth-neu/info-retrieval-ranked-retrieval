<p><b>Powerset</b> was an American company based in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco,_California" class="mw-redirect" title="San Francisco, California">San Francisco, California</a>, that, in 2006, was developing a <a href="/wiki/Natural_language_search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Natural language search engine">natural language search engine</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> On July 1, 2008, Powerset was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> for an estimated $100 million.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Powerset was working on building a  <a href="/wiki/Natural_language" title="Natural language">natural language</a> search engine that could find targeted answers to user questions (as opposed to keyword based search). For example, when confronted with a question like "Which <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">U.S.</a> state has the highest <a href="/wiki/Income_tax" title="Income tax">income tax</a>?", conventional search engines ignore the question phrasing and instead do a search on the <a href="/wiki/Keyword_(Internet_search)" class="mw-redirect" title="Keyword (Internet search)">keywords</a> "state", "highest", "income", and "tax". Powerset on the other hand, attempts to use natural language processing to understand the nature of the question and return pages containing the answer.
</p><p>The company was in the process of "building a natural language search engine that reads and understands every sentence on the Web".<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The company has licensed natural language technology from <a href="/wiki/PARC_(company)" title="PARC (company)">PARC</a>, the former <a href="/wiki/Xerox" title="Xerox">Xerox</a> <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto_Research_Center" class="mw-redirect" title="Palo Alto Research Center">Palo Alto Research Center</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 11, 2008, the company unveiled a tool for searching a fixed subset of <a href="/wiki/English_Wikipedia" title="English Wikipedia">English Wikipedia</a> using conversational phrases rather than keywords.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Powerlabs">Powerlabs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Powerset_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Powerlabs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In a form of <a href="/wiki/Beta_test" class="mw-redirect" title="Beta test">beta testing</a>, Powerset opened an online community called <a href="/wiki/Powerlabs" class="mw-redirect" title="Powerlabs">Powerlabs</a> on September 17, 2007. <i><a href="/wiki/Business_Week" class="mw-redirect" title="Business Week">Business Week</a></i> said: "The company hopes the site will marshal thousands of people to help build and improve its search engine before it goes public next year."<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Said <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i>: "[Powerset Labs] goes far beyond the 'alpha' or 'beta' testing involved in most software projects, when users put a new product through rigorous testing to find its flaws. Powerset doesn’t have a product yet, but rather a collection of promising natural language technologies, which are the fruit of years of research at Xerox PARC."<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Powerlabs' initial search results are taken from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="People">People</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Powerset_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: People">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><b>Barney Pell</b> (born March 18, 1968, in <a href="/wiki/Hollywood" title="Hollywood">Hollywood</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-nasa_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nasa-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> is co-founder and CTO of Powerset. Pell received his <a href="/wiki/Bachelor_of_science" class="mw-redirect" title="Bachelor of science">bachelor of science</a> degree in symbolic systems from <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> in 1989, where he graduated <a href="/wiki/Phi_Beta_Kappa" title="Phi Beta Kappa">Phi Beta Kappa</a> and was a <a href="/wiki/National_Merit_Scholar" class="mw-redirect" title="National Merit Scholar">National Merit Scholar</a>. Pell received a <a href="/wiki/PhD" class="mw-redirect" title="PhD">PhD</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> from <a href="/wiki/Cambridge_University" class="mw-redirect" title="Cambridge University">Cambridge University</a> in 1993, where he was a <a href="/wiki/Marshall_Scholar" class="mw-redirect" title="Marshall Scholar">Marshall Scholar</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-pellblog_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pellblog-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> He has worked at <a href="/wiki/NASA" title="NASA">NASA</a>, as chief strategist and vice president of business development at StockMaster.com (acquired by <a href="/wiki/Red_Herring_(magazine)" title="Red Herring (magazine)">Red Herring</a> in March, 2000) and at Whizbang! Labs. Prior to joining Powerset, Pell was an Entrepreneur-in-Residence at <a href="/wiki/Mayfield_Fund" title="Mayfield Fund">Mayfield Fund</a>, a venture capital firm in Silicon Valley.<sup id="cite_ref-team_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-team-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
Pell is also a founder of <a href="/wiki/Moon_Express" title="Moon Express">Moon Express</a>, Inc., a U.S. company awarded a $10M commercial lunar contract by NASA and a competitor in the <a href="/wiki/Google_Lunar_X_PRIZE" class="mw-redirect" title="Google Lunar X PRIZE">Google Lunar X PRIZE</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lat20110408_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lat20110408-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Steve Newcomb</b> was the COO and co-founder of Powerset.  Prior to joining Powerset, he was a co-founder of Loudfire, General Manager at Promptu, and was on the board of directors at <a href="/wiki/Jaxtr" title="Jaxtr">Jaxtr</a>.  He left Powerset in October 2007 to form Virgance, a social startup incubator.
</p><p><b>Lorenzo Thione</b> (born in <a href="/wiki/Como,_Italy" class="mw-redirect" title="Como, Italy">Como, Italy</a>) was the product architect and co-founder of Powerset. Prior to joining Powerset, he worked at <a href="/wiki/FXPAL" class="mw-redirect" title="FXPAL">FXPAL</a><sup id="cite_ref-team_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-team-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> in natural language processing and related research fields. Thione earned his <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's degree</a> in software engineering from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Texas_at_Austin" title="University of Texas at Austin">University of Texas at Austin</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-team_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-team-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ronald_Kaplan" title="Ronald Kaplan">Ronald Kaplan</a>, former manager of research in Natural Language Theory and Technology at PARC, served as the company's CTO and CSO.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><b>Ryan Ferrier</b> is a member of the founding team of Powerset. He managed personnel and internal operations. After 2008 he went on to co-found Serious Business, which made Facebook applications and was later bought by <a href="/wiki/Zynga" title="Zynga">Zynga</a>.
</p><p>Another Powerset alumnus, <b>Alex Le</b>, became CTO of Serious Business and went on to become an executive producer at Zynga when it bought the company. <b>Siqi Chen</b> founded a stealth startup in mobile computing after leaving Powerset.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Investors">Investors</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Powerset_(company)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Investors">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Powerset attracted a wide range of investors, many of whom had considerable experience in the <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> field.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> The company received $12.5 million in Series A funding during November 2007, co-led by the venture capital firms <a href="/wiki/Foundation_Capital" title="Foundation Capital">Foundation Capital</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Founders_Fund" class="mw-redirect" title="The Founders Fund">The Founders Fund</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Among the better-known investors:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Esther_Dyson" title="Esther Dyson">Esther Dyson</a>, founding chairman of <a href="/wiki/ICANN" title="ICANN">ICANN</a>, founder of the <a href="/wiki/Newsletter" title="Newsletter">newsletter</a> <i>Release 1.0</i> and editor at <a href="/wiki/Cnet" class="mw-redirect" title="Cnet">Cnet</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Peter_Thiel" title="Peter Thiel">Peter Thiel</a>, founder and former <a href="/wiki/CEO" class="mw-redirect" title="CEO">CEO</a> of <a href="/wiki/PayPal" title="PayPal">PayPal</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Luke_Nosek" title="Luke Nosek">Luke Nosek</a>, founder of PayPal</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Reid_Hoffman" title="Reid Hoffman">Reid Hoffman</a>, executive vice president of PayPal and founder of <a href="/wiki/LinkedIn" title="LinkedIn">LinkedIn</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/First_Round_Capital" title="First Round Capital">First Round Capital</a>, seed-stage venture firm</li></ul>
