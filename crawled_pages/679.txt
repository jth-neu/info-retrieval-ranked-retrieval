<p>A <b>provost</b> (introduced into <a href="/wiki/Scots_language" title="Scots language">Scots</a> from <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>) is the ceremonial head of many <a href="/wiki/Scottish_local_authorities" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish local authorities">Scottish local authorities</a>, and under the name <i><b><a href="/wiki/Pr%C3%A9v%C3%B4t" title="Prévôt">prévôt</a></b></i> (<small>French pronunciation:&#160;&#8203;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/French" title="Help:IPA/French">[pʁeˈvoː]</a></span>) was a governmental position of varying importance in <a href="/wiki/Ancien_R%C3%A9gime" title="Ancien Régime">Ancien Régime</a> <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Provost_(civil)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a <a href="/wiki/Secular" class="mw-redirect" title="Secular">secular</a> title, <i>praepositus</i> is also very old, dating to the <i><a href="/wiki/Praepositus_sacri_cubiculi" title="Praepositus sacri cubiculi">praepositus sacri cubiculi</a></i> of the late <a href="/wiki/Roman_Empire" title="Roman Empire">Roman Empire</a>, and the <i>praepositus palatii</i> of the <a href="/wiki/Carolingian" class="mw-redirect" title="Carolingian">Carolingian</a> court. The title developed in <a href="/wiki/France" title="France">France</a> from where it found its way into <a href="/wiki/Scots_language" title="Scots language">Scots</a>, where in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> it became the style (as <i>provost</i>) of the principal magistrates of the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Burgh" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Burgh">Royal Burghs</a> (roughly speaking, the equivalent of "<a href="/wiki/Mayors_in_the_United_Kingdom" class="mw-redirect" title="Mayors in the United Kingdom">mayor</a>" in the rest of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">UK</a>) ("<a href="/wiki/Lord_Provost" title="Lord Provost">Lord Provost</a>" in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Aberdeen, Scotland">Aberdeen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Dundee,_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Dundee, Scotland">Dundee</a>), and into <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, where it is applied to certain officers charged with the maintenance of military discipline. A <a href="/wiki/Provost_Marshal" class="mw-redirect" title="Provost Marshal">Provost Marshal</a> is an officer of the army originally appointed when troops are on service abroad (and now in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> as well) for the prompt repression of all offenses. He may at any time arrest and detain for trial persons subject to military law committing offences, and may also carry into execution any punishments to be inflicted in pursuance of a court martial (Army Act 1881, § 74). A <a href="/wiki/Provost_sergeant" title="Provost sergeant">provost sergeant</a> is in charge of the <a href="/wiki/Garrison" title="Garrison">garrison police</a> or <a href="/wiki/Regimental_police" class="mw-redirect" title="Regimental police">regimental police</a>. The 'Provost' also refers to the <a href="/wiki/Military_police" title="Military police">military police</a> in general. The British Army pronunciation is 'Prov-oh', though the U.S. Army pronunciation is 'Pro-vost' as written.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Scotland">Scotland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Provost_(civil)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Scotland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Historically the provost was the chief magistrate or convener of a <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scottish</a> <a href="/wiki/Burgh" title="Burgh">burgh</a> council, the equivalent of a <a href="/wiki/Mayor" title="Mayor">mayor</a> in other parts of the English-speaking world. Previous to the enactment of the <a href="/wiki/Police_burgh" title="Police burgh">Town Councils (Scotland) Act 1900</a> various titles were used in different burghs, but the legislation standardised the name of the governing body as “the provost, magistrates, and councillors” of the burgh. After the re-organisation of <a href="/wiki/Local_government_in_Scotland" title="Local government in Scotland">local government in Scotland</a> in 1975, the title of <a href="/wiki/Lord_Provost" title="Lord Provost">Lord Provost</a> was reserved to <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dundee" title="Dundee">Dundee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, while other district councils could choose the title to be used by the convener; in 1994 twenty-two councils had provosts.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Similar provisions were included in the <a href="/wiki/Local_Government_etc._(Scotland)_Act_1994" title="Local Government etc. (Scotland) Act 1994">Local Government etc. (Scotland) Act 1994</a> which established unitary <a href="/wiki/Council_areas" class="mw-redirect" title="Council areas">council areas</a> in 1996. The area councils are allowed to adopt the title of provost (or any other) for the convener of the council, as are the <a href="/wiki/Area_committee" title="Area committee">area committees</a> of the council. Some <a href="/wiki/Community_council" title="Community council">community councils</a> which include former burghs also use the style for their chairmen.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_fiction">In fiction</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Provost_(civil)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: In fiction">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Cadfael" title="Cadfael">Cadfael</a> series of historical detective novels by <a href="/wiki/Ellis_Peters" class="mw-redirect" title="Ellis Peters">Ellis Peters</a>, taking place at 12th Century <a href="/wiki/Shrewsbury" title="Shrewsbury">Shrewsbury</a>, <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>, an important role is played by the town's Provost - who in addition to this position is a prominent <a href="/wiki/Shoemaker" class="mw-redirect" title="Shoemaker">shoemaker</a>.
</p><p>The Provost plays an important role in <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Measure_for_Measure" title="Measure for Measure">Measure for Measure</a></i>. He is promoted at the end of the play.
</p>
