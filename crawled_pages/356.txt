<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">AlltheWeb</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><b>AlltheWeb</b> was an Internet <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_(computing)" title="Search engine (computing)">search engine</a> that made its debut in mid-1999 and was closed in 2011.  It grew out of <i>FTP Search</i>, Tor Egge's doctorate thesis at the <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_University_of_Science_and_Technology" title="Norwegian University of Science and Technology">Norwegian University of Science and Technology</a>, which he started in 1994, which in turn resulted in the formation of <a href="/wiki/Fast_Search_%26_Transfer" class="mw-redirect" title="Fast Search &amp; Transfer">Fast Search &amp; Transfer</a> (FAST), established on July 16, 1997.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>  It was used primarily as a showpiece site for FAST's enterprise search engine.  According to FAST, AllTheWeb once rivaled <a href="/wiki/Google_(search_engine)" class="mw-redirect" title="Google (search engine)">Google</a> in size and technology.<sup id="cite_ref-PR2002-06-17_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR2002-06-17-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  AlltheWeb never became as popular as Google, though, and its userbase declined after it was bought out by Overture in 2003.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Traits">Traits</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AlltheWeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Traits">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>When AlltheWeb started in 1999, FAST aimed to provide their database to other search engines, copying the successful case of <a href="/wiki/Inktomi_(company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Inktomi (company)">Inktomi</a>.  In January 2000, <a href="/wiki/Lycos" title="Lycos">Lycos</a> used their results in the Lycos PRO search. By that time, the AlltheWeb database had grown from 80 million <a href="/wiki/Uniform_Resource_Identifier" title="Uniform Resource Identifier">URIs</a> to 200 million. Their aim was to index all the publicly accessible web. Their crawler indexed over 2 billion pages by June 2002<sup id="cite_ref-PR2002-06-17_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR2002-06-17-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and started a fresh round of the search engine size war. Before their purchase by Yahoo!, the database contained about 3.3 billion URIs.
</p><p>AlltheWeb claimed a few advantages over Google, such as a fresher database, more advanced search features, search clustering and a completely customizable look.<sup id="cite_ref-PR2002-06-17_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PR2002-06-17-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Its image search would also take the viewer directly to the image rather than to the page where it was displayed.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Closure">Closure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=AlltheWeb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Closure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In February 2003 FAST's web search division was bought by <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Search_Marketing" title="Yahoo! Search Marketing">Overture</a>.  In March 2004, Overture was taken over by <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a>. Shortly after Yahoo!'s acquisition, the AlltheWeb site started using Yahoo!'s database and some of the advanced functions were removed, such as <a href="/wiki/File_Transfer_Protocol" title="File Transfer Protocol">FTP</a> search and direct image search. In March 2011, Yahoo! stated on the AlltheWeb website that they intend to close the engine. Starting on April 4, 2011, the site redirected to <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Search" title="Yahoo! Search">Yahoo! Search</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
