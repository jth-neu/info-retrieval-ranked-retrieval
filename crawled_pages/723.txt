<p><b>Sir Ben Kingsley</b> (born <b>Krishna Pandit Bhanji</b>; 31 December 1943)<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Moreton_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moreton-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> is an English actor with a career spanning over 50 years. He has won an <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Academy Award">Oscar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy</a>, <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA Award">BAFTA</a>, two <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award" title="Golden Globe Award">Golden Globes</a>, and a <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award" title="Screen Actors Guild Award">Screen Actors Guild Award</a>. He is known for his starring role as <a href="/wiki/Mohandas_Karamchand_Gandhi" class="mw-redirect" title="Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi">Mohandas Gandhi</a> in the 1982 film <i><a href="/wiki/Gandhi_(film)" title="Gandhi (film)">Gandhi</a></i>, for which he won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Academy Award for Best Actor</a>. He has appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Schindler%27s_List" title="Schindler&#39;s List">Schindler's List</a></i> (1993), <i><a href="/wiki/Twelfth_Night_(1996_film)" title="Twelfth Night (1996 film)">Twelfth Night</a></i> (1996), <i><a href="/wiki/Sexy_Beast" title="Sexy Beast">Sexy Beast</a></i> (2000), <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_Sand_and_Fog_(film)" title="House of Sand and Fog (film)">House of Sand and Fog</a></i> (2003), <i><a href="/wiki/Lucky_Number_Slevin" title="Lucky Number Slevin">Lucky Number Slevin</a></i> (2006), <i><a href="/wiki/Shutter_Island_(film)" title="Shutter Island (film)">Shutter Island</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Prince_of_Persia:_The_Sands_of_Time_(film)" title="Prince of Persia: The Sands of Time (film)">Prince of Persia: The Sands of Time</a></i> (2010), <i><a href="/wiki/Hugo_(film)" title="Hugo (film)">Hugo</a></i> (2011), <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_3" title="Iron Man 3">Iron Man 3</a></i> (2013), <i><a href="/wiki/The_Boxtrolls" title="The Boxtrolls">The Boxtrolls</a></i> (2014), and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jungle_Book_(2016_film)" title="The Jungle Book (2016 film)">The Jungle Book</a></i> (2016).
</p><p>Kingsley was appointed <a href="/wiki/Knight_Bachelor" title="Knight Bachelor">Knight Bachelor</a> in 2002 for services to the British film industry.<sup id="cite_ref-Knighthood_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Knighthood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, he was awarded a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WalkofFame_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WalkofFame-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, he received the <a href="/wiki/British_Academy_of_Film_and_Television_Arts" title="British Academy of Film and Television Arts">BAFTA</a> Los Angeles Britannia Award for Worldwide Contribution to Filmed Entertainment.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_life">Early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Kingsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kingsley was born Krishna Pandit Bhanji in <a href="/wiki/Snainton" title="Snainton">Snainton</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Scarborough,_North_Yorkshire" title="Scarborough, North Yorkshire">Scarborough</a> in the <a href="/wiki/North_Riding_of_Yorkshire" title="North Riding of Yorkshire">North Riding of Yorkshire</a>. He is the son of Anna Lyna Mary (born Goodman; 1914–2010), an actress and model who appeared in films in the 1920s and 1930s, and Rahimtulla Harji Bhanji (1914–1968), a doctor.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kingsley's mother was British; she was born out of wedlock, and "was loath to speak of her background".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Kingsley's father, born in <a href="/wiki/Kenya" title="Kenya">Kenya</a>, was of <a href="/wiki/Gujarati_people" title="Gujarati people">Gujarati</a> descent.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Kingsley's paternal grandfather was an extremely successful <a href="/wiki/Spice_trade" title="Spice trade">spice trader</a> who had moved from India to <a href="/wiki/Zanzibar" title="Zanzibar">Zanzibar</a>, where Kingsley's father lived until moving to Britain at the age of 14.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Kingsley's maternal grandfather was believed by the family to have been of <a href="/wiki/Russian_Jew" class="mw-redirect" title="Russian Jew">Russian</a>- or <a href="/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_Germany" title="History of the Jews in Germany">German-Jewish</a> descent, while Kingsley's maternal grandmother was of English background, and worked in the garment district of <a href="/wiki/East_End_of_London" title="East End of London">East London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-highbeamref1_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-highbeamref1-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Kingsley stated in 1994: "I'm not Jewish... and though there might be some Russian-Jewish heritage way back on my mother's side, the thread is so fine there's no real evidence."<sup id="cite_ref-Moreton_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moreton-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kingsley grew up in <a href="/wiki/Pendlebury" title="Pendlebury">Pendlebury</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Manchester" title="Manchester">Manchester</a>. He was educated at <a href="/wiki/Manchester_Grammar_School" title="Manchester Grammar School">Manchester Grammar School</a>, where one of his classmates was the actor <a href="/wiki/Robert_Powell" title="Robert Powell">Robert Powell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Kingsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Early_career">Early career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Kingsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Early career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kingsley studied at <a href="/wiki/Pendleton_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Pendleton College">De La Salle College</a> in <a href="/wiki/City_of_Salford" title="City of Salford">Salford</a>, which later became home to the Ben Kingsley Theatre. While at college he became involved in amateur dramatics in Manchester, making his professional stage debut on graduation, aged 23. In 1967, he made his <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> <a href="/wiki/West_End_theatre" title="West End theatre">West End theatre</a> debut at the <a href="/wiki/Aldwych_Theatre" title="Aldwych Theatre">Aldwych Theatre</a>. Later, he was spotted by music producer and manager <a href="/wiki/Dick_James" title="Dick James">Dick James</a>, who offered to mould Kingsley into a pop star, but Kingsley chose to join the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a> after an audition before <a href="/wiki/Trevor_Nunn" title="Trevor Nunn">Trevor Nunn</a>.
</p><p>Devoting himself almost exclusively to stage work for the next 15 years, he made his <a href="/wiki/Broadway_theatre" title="Broadway theatre">Broadway</a> debut in 1971 with the RSC. Kingsley played Mosca in <a href="/wiki/Peter_Hall_(theatre_director)" class="mw-redirect" title="Peter Hall (theatre director)">Peter Hall</a>'s 1977 production of Ben Jonson's <i><a href="/wiki/Volpone" title="Volpone">Volpone</a></i> for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_National_Theatre" title="Royal National Theatre">Royal National Theatre</a>, and in <a href="/wiki/Peter_Brook" title="Peter Brook">Peter Brook</a>'s acclaimed production of <i><a href="/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream" title="A Midsummer Night&#39;s Dream">A Midsummer Night's Dream</a></i>. At about this time, he changed his name to Ben Kingsley, fearing that a foreign name would hamper his career.<sup id="cite_ref-actors_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-actors-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> He took his stage name from his father's nickname (at <a href="/wiki/Dulwich_College" title="Dulwich College">Dulwich College</a>) of "Benji" and his paternal grandfather's nickname of "King Cloves".<sup id="cite_ref-Moreton_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Moreton-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> He also starred in the role of <a href="/wiki/Willy_Loman" title="Willy Loman">Willy Loman</a> in a 1982 <a href="/wiki/Sydney" title="Sydney">Sydney</a> production of <i><a href="/wiki/Death_of_a_Salesman" title="Death of a Salesman">Death of a Salesman</a></i> opposite <a href="/wiki/Mel_Gibson" title="Mel Gibson">Mel Gibson</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_and_television">Film and television</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Kingsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Film and television">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kingsley made the transition to film roles early on, with his first role coming in <i><a href="/wiki/Fear_Is_the_Key_(film)" title="Fear Is the Key (film)">Fear Is the Key</a></i>, released in 1972. Kingsley continued starring in bit roles in both film and television, including a role as Ron Jenkins on the soap opera <i><a href="/wiki/Coronation_Street" title="Coronation Street">Coronation Street</a></i> from 1966 to 1967 and regular appearances as a defence counsel in the long-running British legal programme <i><a href="/wiki/Crown_Court_(TV_series)" title="Crown Court (TV series)">Crown Court</a></i>. In 1975, he starred as <a href="/wiki/Dante_Gabriel_Rossetti" title="Dante Gabriel Rossetti">Dante Gabriel Rossetti</a> in the BBCs historical drama <i><a href="/wiki/The_Love_School" title="The Love School">The Love School</a></i> and appeared in the TV miniseries <i><a href="/wiki/Dickens_of_London" title="Dickens of London">Dickens of London</a></i> the following year. He found fame as <a href="/wiki/Mahatma_Gandhi" title="Mahatma Gandhi">Mohandas Gandhi</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Awards" title="Academy Awards">Academy Award</a>-winning film <i><a href="/wiki/Gandhi_(film)" title="Gandhi (film)">Gandhi</a></i> in 1982. The film was a critical and financial success, and Kingsley won the <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Actor">Academy Award for Best Actor</a> for his performance.<sup id="cite_ref-actors_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-actors-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kingsley has since appeared in a variety of roles. His credits included the films <i><a href="/wiki/Turtle_Diary" title="Turtle Diary">Turtle Diary</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Maurice_(film)" title="Maurice (film)">Maurice</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Pascali%27s_Island_(film)" title="Pascali&#39;s Island (film)">Pascali's Island</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Without_a_Clue" title="Without a Clue">Without a Clue</a></i> (as <a href="/wiki/Dr._Watson" title="Dr. Watson">Dr. Watson</a> alongside <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Sherlock_Holmes" title="Sherlock Holmes">Sherlock Holmes</a>), <i><a href="/wiki/Suspect_Zero" title="Suspect Zero">Suspect Zero</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Bugsy" title="Bugsy">Bugsy</a></i> (nominated for <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Supporting_Actor" title="Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor">Best Supporting Actor</a>), <i><a href="/wiki/Sneakers_(1992_film)" title="Sneakers (1992 film)">Sneakers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dave_(film)" title="Dave (film)">Dave</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Searching_for_Bobby_Fischer" title="Searching for Bobby Fischer">Searching for Bobby Fischer</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Schindler%27s_List" title="Schindler&#39;s List">Schindler's List</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Silas_Marner" title="Silas Marner">Silas Marner</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Death_and_the_Maiden_(1994_film)" class="mw-redirect" title="Death and the Maiden (1994 film)">Death and the Maiden</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Sexy_Beast" title="Sexy Beast">Sexy Beast</a></i>, for which he received another Academy Award nomination for Best Supporting Actor, and <i><a href="/wiki/House_of_Sand_and_Fog_(film)" title="House of Sand and Fog (film)">House of Sand and Fog</a></i>, which led to an Oscar nomination for Best Actor. He won a <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Globe" title="Crystal Globe">Crystal Globe</a> award for outstanding artistic contribution to <a href="/wiki/World_cinema" title="World cinema">world cinema</a> at the <a href="/wiki/Karlovy_Vary_International_Film_Festival" title="Karlovy Vary International Film Festival">Karlovy Vary International Film Festival</a> in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1997, he provided a voice in the video game <i><a href="/wiki/Ceremony_of_Innocence" title="Ceremony of Innocence">Ceremony of Innocence</a></i>. In 1998, he was the head of the jury at the <a href="/wiki/48th_Berlin_International_Film_Festival" title="48th Berlin International Film Festival">48th Berlin International Film Festival</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Berlinale_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Berlinale-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2006, he received an <a href="/wiki/Emmy" class="mw-redirect" title="Emmy">Emmy</a> nomination for his performance in the made-for-TV film <i><a href="/wiki/Mrs._Harris" title="Mrs. Harris">Mrs. Harris</a></i>, in which he played famed <a href="/wiki/Cardiologist" class="mw-redirect" title="Cardiologist">cardiologist</a> <a href="/wiki/Herman_Tarnower" title="Herman Tarnower">Herman Tarnower</a>, who was murdered by his jilted lover, <a href="/wiki/Jean_Harris" title="Jean Harris">Jean Harris</a>. Later that year, Kingsley appeared in an episode of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Sopranos" title="The Sopranos">The Sopranos</a></i> entitled "<a href="/wiki/Luxury_Lounge" title="Luxury Lounge">Luxury Lounge</a>", playing himself. In 2007, Kingsley appeared as a <a href="/wiki/Polish_American" class="mw-redirect" title="Polish American">Polish American</a> mobster in the <a href="/wiki/Mafia_comedy" title="Mafia comedy">Mafia comedy</a> <i><a href="/wiki/You_Kill_Me" title="You Kill Me">You Kill Me</a></i>, and a hitman in <i><a href="/wiki/War,_Inc." title="War, Inc.">War, Inc.</a></i>
</p><p>In 2010, Kingsley worked voicing a character named Sabine in <a href="/wiki/Lionhead_Studios" title="Lionhead Studios">Lionhead Studios</a> game <i><a href="/wiki/Fable_III" title="Fable III">Fable III</a></i> and starred alongside <a href="/wiki/Leonardo_DiCaprio" title="Leonardo DiCaprio">Leonardo DiCaprio</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Shutter_Island_(film)" title="Shutter Island (film)">Shutter Island</a></i>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Martin_Scorsese" title="Martin Scorsese">Martin Scorsese</a>. He also appeared in Scorsese's next film, <i><a href="/wiki/Hugo_(film)" title="Hugo (film)">Hugo</a></i>, and signed up to appear in a new feature by <a href="/wiki/Neil_Jordan" title="Neil Jordan">Neil Jordan</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_Boorman" title="John Boorman">John Boorman</a> entitled <i>Broken Dream</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Broken_Dream_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Broken_Dream-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, he appeared as <a href="/wiki/Mandarin_(comics)" title="Mandarin (comics)">Trevor Slattery</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Iron_Man_3" title="Iron Man 3">Iron Man 3</a></i>, and as the hero <a href="/wiki/Mazer_Rackham" class="mw-redirect" title="Mazer Rackham">Mazer Rackham</a> in <i><a href="/wiki/Ender%27s_Game_(film)" title="Ender&#39;s Game (film)">Ender's Game</a></i>.
</p><p>Kingsley's 2014 film roles included <i><a href="/wiki/Exodus:_Gods_and_Kings" title="Exodus: Gods and Kings">Exodus: Gods and Kings</a></i>, as <a href="/wiki/Nun_(biblical_figure)" title="Nun (biblical figure)">Nun</a>, a Hebrew slave, and <i><a href="/wiki/Night_at_the_Museum:_Secret_of_the_Tomb" title="Night at the Museum: Secret of the Tomb">Night at the Museum: Secret of the Tomb</a></i>, as Merenkahre, a <a href="/wiki/Simulacrum" title="Simulacrum">simulacrum</a> of an Egyptian pharaoh (in one scene, the character discusses his Hebrew slaves).<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, Kingsley played a driving instructor in the film <i><a href="/wiki/Learning_to_Drive_(film)" title="Learning to Drive (film)">Learning to Drive</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> He voiced <a href="/wiki/Bagheera" title="Bagheera">Bagheera</a> in Disney's reboot of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Jungle_Book_(2016_film)" title="The Jungle Book (2016 film)">The Jungle Book</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> and recorded <a href="/wiki/Yogananda" class="mw-redirect" title="Yogananda">Yogananda</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Autobiography_of_a_Yogi" title="Autobiography of a Yogi">Autobiography of a Yogi</a></i> in book-on-tape format.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Kingsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Kingsley has been married four times and has four children: Thomas Bhanji and artist Jasmin Bhanji, with actress <a href="/wiki/Angela_Morant" title="Angela Morant">Angela Morant</a>; and Edmund Kingsley and <a href="/wiki/Ferdinand_Kingsley" title="Ferdinand Kingsley">Ferdinand Kingsley</a>, both of whom became actors, with theatrical director <a href="/wiki/Alison_Sutcliffe" title="Alison Sutcliffe">Alison Sutcliffe</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> He divorced Alexandra Christmann, originally from Germany, in 2005, having been "deeply, deeply shocked" after pictures of her kissing another man surfaced on the internet.<sup id="cite_ref-contactmusic.com_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-contactmusic.com-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> On 3 September 2007, Kingsley married <a href="/wiki/Daniela_Lavender" title="Daniela Lavender">Daniela Lavender</a>, a Brazilian actress, at Eynsham Hall, in <a href="/wiki/North_Leigh" title="North Leigh">North Leigh</a>, <a href="/wiki/Oxfordshire" title="Oxfordshire">Oxfordshire</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Honours">Honours</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Kingsley&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Honours">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:172px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg/170px-Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg" width="170" height="262" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg/255px-Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg/340px-Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1272" data-file-height="1962" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Sir_Ben_Kingsley_by_David_Shankbone.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Kingsley at the 2008 <a href="/wiki/Tribeca_Film_Festival" title="Tribeca Film Festival">Tribeca Film Festival</a></div></div></div>
<p>Kingsley was created a <a href="/wiki/Knight_Bachelor" title="Knight Bachelor">Knight Bachelor</a> in the <a href="/wiki/2002_New_Year_Honours" title="2002 New Year Honours">2002 New Year Honours</a> for services to the British film industry.<sup id="cite_ref-Knighthood_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Knighthood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The award was announced on 31 December 2001, which happened to be Kingsley's 58th birthday.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> After receiving his award from <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a> at <a href="/wiki/Buckingham_Palace" title="Buckingham Palace">Buckingham Palace</a>, Kingsley stated; "I told the Queen that winning an Oscar pales into insignificance – this is insurmountable. I'm fascinated by the ancient, by mythology, by these islands and their tradition of story telling. I feel that I am a story teller and to receive a knighthood is really recognition of that."<sup id="cite_ref-Knighthood_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Knighthood-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> His demand to be called 'Sir' was documented by the BBC, to some criticism.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Since then, Kingsley appears to have altered his stance; credits for his latest films refer to him as Ben Kingsley. Co-star <a href="/wiki/Pen%C3%A9lope_Cruz" title="Penélope Cruz">Penélope Cruz</a> was reportedly unsure what to call him during the filming of <i><a href="/wiki/Elegy_(film)" title="Elegy (film)">Elegy</a></i> as someone had told her she needed to refer to him as "Sir Ben". One day it slipped out as such, and she called him that for the remainder of the shoot.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Kingsley has denied accusations that he prefers to be referred to by his title, saying, "If I've ever insisted on being called 'Sir' by colleagues on a film set then I am profoundly sorry. I don't remember ever doing that and I tend not to forget."<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1984, he won a Grammy Award for Best Spoken Word or Nonmusical Recording for <i>The Words of Gandhi</i>. He was awarded the Indian civilian honour <a href="/wiki/Padma_Shri" title="Padma Shri">Padma Shri</a> in 1984.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
In May 2010, Kingsley was awarded a star on the <a href="/wiki/Hollywood_Walk_of_Fame" title="Hollywood Walk of Fame">Hollywood Walk of Fame</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WalkofFame_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WalkofFame-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
In April 2013, Kingsley was honoured with the Fellowship Award at <a href="/wiki/The_Asian_Awards" title="The Asian Awards">The Asian Awards</a> in London.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
