<p><b>Go.com</b> (also known as <b>The Go Network</b>) is a landing page for Disney content, created as a joint venture between <a href="/wiki/Infoseek" title="Infoseek">Infoseek</a> and <a href="/wiki/Disney_Interactive_Media_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Disney Interactive Media Group">Disney Interactive</a>. It is currently operated by <a href="/wiki/Walt_Disney_Parks,_Experiences_and_Consumer_Products" title="Walt Disney Parks, Experiences and Consumer Products">Walt Disney Parks, Experiences and Consumer Products</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Division_(business)" title="Division (business)">Division</a> of <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>. It began as a <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">web portal</a> launched by Jeff Gold.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Go.com includes content from <a href="/wiki/ABC_News" title="ABC News">ABC News</a> which is associated with Disney and is hosted under a .go.com name. Along with <a href="/wiki/Time_Warner" class="mw-redirect" title="Time Warner">Time Warner</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Pathfinder.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Pathfinder.com">Pathfinder.com</a>, Go.com proved to be an expensive failure for its parent company, as web users preferred to use <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engines</a> to access content directly, rather than start at a top-level corporate portal. In 2013, the site was transitioned from a portal to a simple landing page.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Go.com_1998_logo.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/0a/Go.com_1998_logo.png/220px-Go.com_1998_logo.png" width="220" height="220" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/0/0a/Go.com_1998_logo.png 1.5x" data-file-width="316" data-file-height="316" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Go.com_1998_logo.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Go.com's original logo used from 1998 to 1999.</div></div></div>
<p>In January 1998, Disney registered Go.com.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Infoseek and Disney Interactive Media Group joint ventured in developing the Go Network, an internet portal.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Go.com was launched in January 1999 as a portal with content from ABCNews.com, ESPN.com and Disney.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>With Disney's purchase of the remainder of Infoseek in July 1999, the Go Network, Infoseek, the Disney Catalog, Disney Online (Disney.com and DisneyStore.com), ABC News Internet Ventures, ESPN Internet Ventures, and Buena Vista Internet Group were merged into the Go.com company; the company was 72% Disney-owned, with the other outstanding ownership in a <a href="/wiki/Tracking_stock" title="Tracking stock">tracking stock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 1999, the Go Guides program, a user-edited directory like <a href="/wiki/DMOZ" title="DMOZ">DMOZ</a>, was launched.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sew_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sew-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2000, Go.com was forced to abandon its original <a href="/wiki/Stoplight" class="mw-redirect" title="Stoplight">stoplight</a> <a href="/wiki/Logo" title="Logo">logo</a> because of a complaint of similar-looking logos filed by <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Search_Marketing" title="Yahoo! Search Marketing">GoTo.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Concurrently, Go.com company officials announced it was shifting from a general appeal portal to featuring entertainment content.<sup id="cite_ref-sew_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sew-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> A court later ruled that Go.com had to pay <a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">$</a>21.5 million in damages to GoTo.com.<sup id="cite_ref-sew0_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sew0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>However, in January 2001, Disney announced that it would be closing Go.com and its search engine, <a href="/wiki/Lay_off" class="mw-redirect" title="Lay off">laying off</a> approximately 400 employees and retiring the go.com <a href="/wiki/Tracking_stock" title="Tracking stock">tracking stock</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>   Also announced was the closing of Go.com's volunteer-edited directory. With the closing, some volunteers created, or migrated to, offshoot directories including JoeAnt, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Goguides.org&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Goguides.org (page does not exist)">Goguides.org</a>, and <a href="/w/index.php?title=Skaffe.com&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Skaffe.com (page does not exist)">Skaffe.com</a>.
</p><p>In March 2001, Go.com dropped its internal search engine in favor of <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Search_Marketing" title="Yahoo! Search Marketing">GoTo.com</a> search results.<sup id="cite_ref-sew0_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sew0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Current_status">Current status</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Go.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Current status">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Despite its announcement, go.com never ceased operations. Instead, in March 2001, the site ended its search engine and switched to a search engine provided by goto.com, whose parent, <a href="/wiki/Inktomi_(company)" class="mw-redirect" title="Inktomi (company)">Inktomi</a>, was eventually purchased by <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-sew0_10-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sew0-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of January 21, 2016, some of Disney's sites, including ABC's network and news divisions and Disney's cable networks continue to be hosted on the go.com domain, although a base address is used for branding and simplicity purposes; for example, the website for <a href="/wiki/Freeform_(TV_channel)" title="Freeform (TV channel)">Freeform</a> redirects to <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://freeform.go.com/">freeform.go.com</a>. Go.com terminated its email service on August 31, 2010.
</p><p>In 2013, Disney retired the Go.com logo and branding, turning the domain into a Disney-branded landing page without a search engine. The properties of Disney Interactive then began the process of moving the connection of user accounts off from depending on the Go.com domain to a different type of system not dependent solely on one domain. Throughout 2014 and 2015, the <a href="/wiki/ABC_Owned_Television_Stations" title="ABC Owned Television Stations">ABC Owned Television Stations</a> began to move to only using their on-air domains, mainly for <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> considerations.
</p><p>In August 2016, <a href="/wiki/ESPN.com" title="ESPN.com">ESPN.com</a> switched to solely using that domain rather than <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://espn.go.com/">espn.go.com</a>, tying into the improved Disney Enterprise Technology user account/registration process rather than a rumored issue involving the resolution of "<a href="/wiki/HTTP_301" title="HTTP 301">301</a>" error notices from the former espn.com redirect causing issues with lower search result rankings.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
