<p><b>Malawi</b> was a predecessor to the modern-day <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Malawi" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic of Malawi">Republic of Malawi</a>. It existed between 1964 and 1966.<sup id="cite_ref-Hansard19640511_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard19640511-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Hansard19660614_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard19660614-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> When British rule ended in 1964, by the Malawi Independence Act 1964,<sup id="cite_ref-Hansard19640511_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Hansard19640511-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> the <a href="/wiki/Nyasaland_Protectorate" class="mw-redirect" title="Nyasaland Protectorate">Nyasaland Protectorate</a>, formerly a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/Federation_of_Rhodesia_and_Nyasaland" title="Federation of Rhodesia and Nyasaland">Federation of Rhodesia and Nyasaland</a>, became an independent <a href="/wiki/Sovereign_state" title="Sovereign state">sovereign state</a>. The <a href="/wiki/British_monarch" class="mw-redirect" title="British monarch">British monarch</a> was <a href="/wiki/Head_of_state" title="Head of state">head of state</a> and Malawi shared the sovereign, Queen <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">Elizabeth II</a>, with the other <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_realm" title="Commonwealth realm">Commonwealth realms</a>. The monarch's constitutional roles were mostly delegated to the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Governors-General_of_Malawi" class="mw-redirect" title="List of Governors-General of Malawi">Governor-General of Malawi</a>, Sir <a href="/wiki/Glyn_Smallwood_Jones" title="Glyn Smallwood Jones">Glyn Smallwood Jones</a> (6 July 1964 – 6 July 1966).<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Elizabeth II did not reside in or visit Malawi in the 1960s but she did visit in 1979 (22–25 July).
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Hastings_Banda" title="Hastings Banda">Hastings Banda</a> held office as <a href="/wiki/Prime_minister" title="Prime minister">prime minister</a> (and <a href="/wiki/Head_of_government" title="Head of government">head of government</a>). Following the abolition of the monarchy, the Republic of Malawi came into existence on 6 July 1966 and Banda became the first <a href="/wiki/President_of_Malawi" title="President of Malawi">President of Malawi</a>.
</p>
