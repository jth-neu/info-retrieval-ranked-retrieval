<p><b>Eli Pariser</b> (born December 17, 1980) is the chief executive of <a href="/wiki/Upworthy" title="Upworthy">Upworthy</a>, a website for "meaningful" viral content.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> He is a <a href="/wiki/Left-wing" class="mw-redirect" title="Left-wing">left-wing</a> <a href="/wiki/Political_activist" class="mw-redirect" title="Political activist">political</a> and <a href="/wiki/Internet_activist" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet activist">internet activist</a>, the board president of <a href="/wiki/MoveOn.org" class="mw-redirect" title="MoveOn.org">MoveOn.org</a> and a co-founder of <a href="/wiki/Avaaz.org" class="mw-redirect" title="Avaaz.org">Avaaz.org</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eli_Pariser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pariser's rise to prominence as a political activist began when he and college student David H. Pickering launched an online petition calling for a nonmilitary response to the attacks of September 11. At the time, he was working as a program assistant for the national nonprofit More Than Money. In less than a month, half a million people had signed the petition.
</p><p>Pariser joined <a href="/wiki/Moveon.org" class="mw-redirect" title="Moveon.org">Moveon.org</a> in November 2001, when founders <a href="/wiki/Wes_Boyd" title="Wes Boyd">Wes Boyd</a> and <a href="/wiki/Joan_Blades" title="Joan Blades">Joan Blades</a> invited him to merge his efforts with theirs.<sup id="cite_ref-smart_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smart-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> During the <a href="/wiki/United_States_presidential_election,_2004" title="United States presidential election, 2004">2004 U.S. presidential campaign</a>, Pariser co-created the <i><a href="/wiki/Bush_in_30_Seconds" title="Bush in 30 Seconds">Bush in 30 Seconds</a></i> ad contest and raised over $30 million from small donors to run ads and back Democratic and progressive candidates. Writing for <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times_Magazine" title="The New York Times Magazine">The New York Times Magazine</a></i> in 2003, journalist <a href="/wiki/George_Packer" title="George Packer">George Packer</a> referred to MoveOn as the "mainstream" element of what "may be the fastest-growing protest movement in American history."<sup id="cite_ref-smart_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smart-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Pariser was the Executive Director of <a href="/wiki/MoveOn.org" class="mw-redirect" title="MoveOn.org">MoveOn.org</a> from 2004 to 2008 and since 2008 has been Board President.
</p><p>Pariser later became concerned about the development of <a href="/wiki/Web_personalization" class="mw-redirect" title="Web personalization">web personalization</a>. He noticed a pattern of differing responses to <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> queries based on a user's past Internet search history, such that a person with a liberal orientation might get an entirely different set of responses than a conservative if he or she used <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, or <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a> to search for a phrase or term on the Internet.<sup id="cite_ref-twsT42_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twsT42-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> For example, a liberal typing "BP" might get information about the oil spill in the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico" title="Gulf of Mexico">Gulf of Mexico</a>, while a conservative typing "BP" might get investment information about the oil company. This led to his development of the concept of a <a href="/wiki/Filter_bubble" title="Filter bubble">filter bubble</a>, a danger that people do not get exposed to viewpoints different from their own.<sup id="cite_ref-twsT42_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twsT42-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-twsT41_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-twsT41-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
In 2013, Pariser joined the board of advisors for tech startup <a href="/wiki/State_(website)" title="State (website)">State.com</a>, creating a network of structured opinions.<sup id="cite_ref-state_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-state-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Works">Works</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eli_Pariser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Works">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Eli Pariser, <i>The Filter Bubble: What the Internet Is Hiding from You</i>, <a href="/wiki/Penguin_Press" class="mw-redirect" title="Penguin Press">Penguin Press</a> (New York, May 2011) <a href="/wiki/International_Standard_Book_Number" title="International Standard Book Number">ISBN</a>&#160;<a href="/wiki/Special:BookSources/978-1-59420-300-8" title="Special:BookSources/978-1-59420-300-8">978-1-59420-300-8</a></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Personal_life">Personal life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Eli_Pariser&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Personal life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Pariser was born on December 17, 1980, the son of Dora Lievow of <a href="/wiki/Camden,_Maine" title="Camden, Maine">Camden, Maine</a> and Emanuel Pariser of <a href="/wiki/Waterville,_Maine" title="Waterville, Maine">Waterville, Maine</a>. He grew up in <a href="/wiki/Lincolnville,_Maine" title="Lincolnville, Maine">Lincolnville, Maine</a>, and in 2000 graduated <a href="/wiki/Summa_cum_laude" class="mw-redirect" title="Summa cum laude">summa cum laude</a> from <a href="/wiki/Bard_College_at_Simon%27s_Rock" title="Bard College at Simon&#39;s Rock">Bard College at Simon's Rock</a> with a B.A. in law and political science.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2005, he returned to Simon's Rock to give the commencement speech.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Pariser is married to Gena Konstantinakos.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
