<p>The <b>national flag of the United Kingdom</b> is the <b>Union Jack</b>, also known as the <b>Union Flag</b>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The current design of the Union Jack dates from the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Union_1800" class="mw-redirect" title="Act of Union 1800">union of Ireland and Great Britain</a> in 1801. It consists of the <a href="/wiki/St_George%27s_Cross" class="mw-redirect" title="St George&#39;s Cross">red cross</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saint_George" title="Saint George">Saint George</a> (<a href="/wiki/Patron_saint" title="Patron saint">patron saint</a> of <a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a>), edged in white, superimposed on the <a href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick%27s_Flag" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Patrick&#39;s Flag">Cross of</a> <a href="/wiki/St_Patrick" class="mw-redirect" title="St Patrick">St Patrick</a> (patron saint of <a href="/wiki/Ireland" title="Ireland">Ireland</a>), which are superimposed on the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Scotland" title="Flag of Scotland">Saltire</a> of <a href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew" class="mw-redirect" title="Saint Andrew">Saint Andrew</a> (patron saint of <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a>). <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> is not represented in the Union Flag by Wales's patron saint, <a href="/wiki/Saint_David" title="Saint David">Saint David</a>, as at the time the flag was designed Wales was part of the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_England" title="Kingdom of England">Kingdom of England</a>.
</p><p>The flag's standard height-to-length proportions are 1:2.<sup id="cite_ref-Flag_propartions_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Flag_propartions-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/War_flag" title="War flag">war flag</a> variant used by the <a href="/wiki/British_Army" title="British Army">British Army</a> modifies the proportions to 3:5 and crops two of the red diagonals.
</p><p>The earlier <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Great_Britain" title="Flag of Great Britain">flag of Great Britain</a> was established in 1606 by a <a href="/wiki/Proclamation" title="Proclamation">proclamation</a> of <a href="/wiki/James_I_of_England" class="mw-redirect" title="James I of England">King James VI and I</a> of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Scottish_monarchs" title="List of Scottish monarchs">Scotland</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_English_monarchs" title="List of English monarchs">England</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The new flag of the United Kingdom was officially created by an <a href="/wiki/Order_in_Council" title="Order in Council">Order in Council</a> of 1801, reading as follows:
</p>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r856303468">.mw-parser-output .templatequote{overflow:hidden;margin:1em 0;padding:0 40px}.mw-parser-output .templatequote .templatequotecite{line-height:1.5em;text-align:left;padding-left:1.6em;margin-top:0}</style><blockquote class="templatequote"><p>The Union Flag shall be <a href="/wiki/Azure_(heraldry)" title="Azure (heraldry)">azure</a>, the Crosses <a href="/wiki/Saltire" title="Saltire">saltire</a> of Saint Andrew and Saint Patrick quarterly per saltire, counter-changed, <a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">argent</a> and <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">gules</a>, the latter <a href="/wiki/Fimbriation" title="Fimbriation">fimbriated</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Argent" title="Argent">second</a>, surmounted by the Cross of Saint George of the <a href="/wiki/Gules" title="Gules">third</a> fimbriated as the saltire.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p></blockquote>

<p>The Union Flag can be flown by any individual or organisation in Great Britain on any day of their choice. Legal regulations restrict the use of the <a href="/wiki/Union_Jack" title="Union Jack">Union Flag</a> on Government buildings in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>. Long-standing restrictions on Government use of the flag elsewhere were abolished in July 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-dcms_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dcms-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-gob_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gob-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Upside-down">Upside-down</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Upside-down">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>While the flag appears symmetric, the white lines above and below the diagonal red are different widths. On the side closer to the flagpole (or on the left when depicted on paper), the white lines above the diagonals are wider; on the side farther from the flagpole (or on the right when depicted on paper), the converse is true. Thus, no change will be apparent when rotating the flag 180 degrees, but if mirrored the flag will be upside-down.
</p><p>Placing the flag upside down is considered <a href="/wiki/L%C3%A8se_majest%C3%A9" class="mw-redirect" title="Lèse majesté">lèse majesté</a> and is offensive to some.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> However, it can be flown upside down as a distress signal. While this is rare, it was used by groups under siege during the <a href="/wiki/Boer_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Boer War">Boer War</a> and during campaigns in India in the late 18th century.
</p>
<h3><span id="St_Patrick.27s_saltire"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="St_Patrick's_saltire">St Patrick's saltire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: St Patrick&#039;s saltire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The reason that the UK flag is not symmetrical is because of the relative positions of the saltires of St Patrick and St Andrew. The red saltire of St Patrick is offset such that it does not relegate the white saltire of St Andrew to a mere border. St Andrew's saltire has the higher position at the hoist side with St Patrick's saltire in the higher position on the opposite side.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Half-mast">Half-mast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Half-mast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Union Flag is flown from Government buildings at <a href="/wiki/Half-mast" title="Half-mast">half-mast</a> in the following situations:<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>from the announcement of the death of the <a href="/wiki/British_monarch" class="mw-redirect" title="British monarch">Sovereign</a>  (an exception is made for <a href="/wiki/Proclamation_Day" title="Proclamation Day">Proclamation Day</a> – the day the new Sovereign is proclaimed, when the Flag is flown at full mast from 11&#160;am to sunset)</li>
<li>the day of the funeral of a member of the <a href="/wiki/British_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="British Royal Family">British Royal Family</a></li>
<li>the funeral of a foreign Head of State</li>
<li>the funeral of a former <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">British Prime Minister</a></li></ul>
<p>The Sovereign sometimes declares other days when the Union Flag is to fly at <a href="/wiki/Half-mast" title="Half-mast">half-mast</a>. Half-mast means the flag is flown two-thirds of the way up the flagpole with at least the height of the flag between the top of the flag and the top of the flagpole.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Flying_from_public_buildings">Flying from public buildings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Flying from public buildings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Until July 2007, the Union Flag was only flown on Government buildings on a limited number of special days each year. The choice of days was managed by the <a href="/wiki/Department_for_Culture,_Media_and_Sport" class="mw-redirect" title="Department for Culture, Media and Sport">Department for Culture, Media and Sport</a> (DCMS).<sup id="cite_ref-dcms_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dcms-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Government buildings are those used by civil servants, <a href="/wiki/The_Crown" title="The Crown">the Crown</a>, or the armed forces. They were not applicable to private citizens, corporations, or local authorities.<sup id="cite_ref-dcms_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dcms-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 3 July 2007, the <a href="/wiki/Secretary_of_State_for_Justice" title="Secretary of State for Justice">Justice Secretary</a> <a href="/wiki/Jack_Straw" title="Jack Straw">Jack Straw</a> laid a <a href="/wiki/Green_paper" title="Green paper">green paper</a> before Parliament entitled <i>The Governance of Britain</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-gob_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gob-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Alongside a range of proposed changes to the constitutional arrangements of the UK was a specific announcement that there would be consultation on whether the rules on flag-flying on Government buildings should be relaxed.
</p><p>Two days later, Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Gordon_Brown" title="Gordon Brown">Gordon Brown</a> announced that with immediate effect the Union Flag would fly from the flag pole above the front entrance of <a href="/wiki/10_Downing_Street" title="10 Downing Street">10 Downing Street</a> on every day of the year. The intention was to increase feelings of <a href="/wiki/British_national_identity" class="mw-redirect" title="British national identity">British national identity</a>. Other Government departments were asked to follow this lead, and all Government buildings in Whitehall did so.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/James_Purnell" title="James Purnell">James Purnell</a>, <a href="/wiki/Culture_Secretary" class="mw-redirect" title="Culture Secretary">Culture Secretary</a> from June 2007 to January 2008 in Brown's administration, subsequently concurred with the abolition of the restrictions – pending consultation on longer term arrangements.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Flag_days">Flag days</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Flag days">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The flag days directed by the DCMS include birthdays of members of the <a href="/wiki/British_Royal_Family" class="mw-redirect" title="British Royal Family">Royal Family</a>, the wedding anniversary of the Monarch, <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Day" title="Commonwealth Day">Commonwealth Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Accession_Day" class="mw-redirect" title="Accession Day">Accession Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coronation_Day" title="Coronation Day">Coronation Day</a>, <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Birthday" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen&#39;s Birthday">The Queen's official birthday</a>, <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Sunday" title="Remembrance Sunday">Remembrance Sunday</a> and on the days of the <a href="/wiki/State_Opening_of_Parliament" title="State Opening of Parliament">State Opening</a> and <a href="/wiki/Legislative_session#Procedure_in_Commonwealth_realms" title="Legislative session">prorogation</a> of <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament</a>.
</p><p>Since the beginning of 2013, the relevant days have been:
</p>
<ul><li>9 January: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Catherine,_Duchess_of_Cambridge" title="Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge">The Duchess of Cambridge</a></li>
<li>20 January: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Sophie,_Countess_of_Wessex" title="Sophie, Countess of Wessex">The Countess of Wessex</a></li>
<li>6 February: anniversary of <a href="/wiki/Elizabeth_II" title="Elizabeth II">The Queen</a>'s accession in 1952</li>
<li>19 February: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Andrew,_Duke_of_York" title="Prince Andrew, Duke of York">The Duke of York</a></li>
<li>Second Sunday in March: <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_Day" title="Commonwealth Day">Commonwealth Day</a></li>
<li>10 March: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Edward,_Earl_of_Wessex" title="Prince Edward, Earl of Wessex">The Earl of Wessex</a></li>
<li>21 April: The Queen's birthday</li>
<li>9 May: <a href="/wiki/Europe_Day" title="Europe Day">Europe Day</a></li>
<li>2 June: anniversary of The Queen's 1953 <a href="/wiki/Coronation_Day" title="Coronation Day">coronation</a></li>
<li>10 June: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh</a></li>
<li>A Saturday in June: <a href="/wiki/Queen%27s_Birthday" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen&#39;s Birthday">The Queen's Official Birthday</a></li>
<li>21 June: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Prince_William,_Duke_of_Cambridge" title="Prince William, Duke of Cambridge">The Duke of Cambridge</a></li>
<li>17 July: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Camilla,_Duchess_of_Cornwall" title="Camilla, Duchess of Cornwall">The Duchess of Cornwall</a></li>
<li>15 August: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Anne,_Princess_Royal" title="Anne, Princess Royal">The Princess Royal</a></li>
<li>Second Sunday in November: <a href="/wiki/Remembrance_Sunday" title="Remembrance Sunday">Remembrance Sunday</a></li>
<li>14 November: birthday of <a href="/wiki/Charles,_Prince_of_Wales" title="Charles, Prince of Wales">The Prince of Wales</a></li>
<li>20 November: anniversary of the wedding of The Queen and The Duke of Edinburgh</li></ul>
<p>In addition, the flag should be flown in the following areas on the specified days:
</p>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> – 1 March: <a href="/wiki/Saint_David%27s_Day" title="Saint David&#39;s Day">Saint David's Day</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> – 17 March: <a href="/wiki/Saint_Patrick%27s_Day" title="Saint Patrick&#39;s Day">Saint Patrick's Day</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/England" title="England">England</a> – 23 April: <a href="/wiki/Saint_George%27s_Day" title="Saint George&#39;s Day">Saint George's Day</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> – 30 November: <a href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew%27s_Day" title="Saint Andrew&#39;s Day">Saint Andrew's Day</a></li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Greater_London" title="Greater London">Greater London</a>: The day of the opening or proroguing of Parliament</li></ul>
<p>Some non central government bodies still continue to follow the flag days.
</p><p>In Scotland, the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Government" title="Scottish Government">Scottish Government</a> has decreed that the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Scotland" title="Flag of Scotland">Flag of Scotland</a> ("the Saltire") will fly on all its buildings every day from 8&#160;am until sunset, but there is no specific policy on flying the Union Flag and as such it is sometimes flown alongside the Saltire and sometimes omitted. An exception is made for "national days". On these days, the Saltire shall be lowered and replaced with the Union Flag. These are the same as the flag days noted above with the exception of:
</p>
<ul><li>3 September: <a href="/wiki/Merchant_Navy" class="mw-redirect" title="Merchant Navy">Merchant Navy</a> Day</li></ul>
<p>Another difference is that on <a href="/wiki/Saint_Andrew%27s_Day" title="Saint Andrew&#39;s Day">Saint Andrew's Day</a>, the Union Flag can only be flown if the building has more than one flagpole – the Saltire will not be lowered to make way for the Union Flag if there is only one flagpole.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Northern_Ireland">Northern Ireland</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Northern Ireland">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a>, the Union Flag is flown from buildings of the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Office" title="Northern Ireland Office">Northern Ireland Office</a> as decreed by Regulations published in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The Regulations were amended in 2002 to remove the requirement to fly the flag on the birthdays of <a href="/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth,_the_Queen_Mother" class="mw-redirect" title="Queen Elizabeth, the Queen Mother">Queen Elizabeth, the Queen Mother</a> and <a href="/wiki/Princess_Margaret,_Countess_of_Snowdon" title="Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon">Princess Margaret, Countess of Snowdon</a> who both died that year.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The current flag days are now the same as the United Kingdom government days noted above with the exception of the Duchess of Cornwall's birthday, which was only added to the UK flag days after her wedding to the Prince of Wales in 2005, and has not yet been extended to Northern Ireland.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Police_Service_of_Northern_Ireland" title="Police Service of Northern Ireland">Police Service of Northern Ireland</a> is the only body in the United Kingdom that is not permitted to fly the Union Flag, and is only permitted to fly its service flag or the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Standard_(United_Kingdom)" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Standard (United Kingdom)">Royal Standard</a> in the event of a visit by the Sovereign.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Proposed_redesigns">Proposed redesigns</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Proposed redesigns">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Suggested_incorporation_of_the_Welsh_dragon">Suggested incorporation of the Welsh dragon</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Suggested incorporation of the Welsh dragon">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Union_Flag_(including_Wales).svg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Union_Flag_%28including_Wales%29.svg/220px-Union_Flag_%28including_Wales%29.svg.png" width="220" height="110" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Union_Flag_%28including_Wales%29.svg/330px-Union_Flag_%28including_Wales%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6a/Union_Flag_%28including_Wales%29.svg/440px-Union_Flag_%28including_Wales%29.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1200" data-file-height="600" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Union_Flag_(including_Wales).svg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>One suggested redesign of the Union Jack with the red dragon from the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Wales" title="Flag of Wales">flag of Wales</a> added in the centre</div></div></div>
<p>In November 2007 the then culture minister <a href="/wiki/Margaret_Hodge" title="Margaret Hodge">Margaret Hodge</a> said she would consider a redesign of the Union Flag to incorporate the <a href="/wiki/Welsh_dragon" class="mw-redirect" title="Welsh dragon">Welsh dragon</a>, during a debate in the House of Commons on the frequency with which the flag flies above public buildings. The issue was initially raised by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Lucas" title="Ian Lucas">Ian Lucas</a>, another <a href="/wiki/Labour_Party_(UK)" title="Labour Party (UK)">Labour</a> MP, who complained that the flag introduced in 1606 following the accession of <a href="/wiki/James_VI_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="James VI of Scotland">James VI of Scotland</a> to the English throne as James I combined the cross of St George and the saltire of St Andrew. This principle continued in 1801 when the St Patrick cross was incorporated following the <a href="/wiki/Union_with_Ireland_Act_1800" class="mw-redirect" title="Union with Ireland Act 1800">Union with Ireland Act 1800</a>. Lucas claimed the identity of <a href="/wiki/Wales" title="Wales">Wales</a> had been suppressed ever since the <a href="/wiki/Laws_in_Wales_Acts_1535%E2%80%931542" class="mw-redirect" title="Laws in Wales Acts 1535–1542">Laws in Wales Acts 1535–1542</a>. In the debate, <a href="/wiki/Albert_Owen" title="Albert Owen">Albert Owen</a> MP said that "we in Wales do not feel part of the union flag because the dragon or the <a href="/wiki/Flag_of_Saint_David" title="Flag of Saint David">cross of St David</a> is not on it."<sup id="cite_ref-guardian_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-guardian-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>  <a href="/wiki/Conservative_Party_(UK)" title="Conservative Party (UK)">Conservative</a> MP <a href="/wiki/Stewart_Jackson" title="Stewart Jackson">Stewart Jackson</a> described the comments as "eccentric".<sup id="cite_ref-telegraph_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-telegraph-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Scottish_independence">Scottish independence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Flag_of_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Scottish independence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>As of 2013, numerous proposals were made about how the Union Flag might be altered to create a flag for the union of England, Wales and Northern Ireland after possible <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence" title="Scottish independence">Scottish independence</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/College_of_Arms" title="College of Arms">College of Arms</a> stated that there would be no need to change the flag in those circumstances, and the existing flag could continue to be used if desired.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> Regarding the removal of Scottish heraldic features from the Union Flag, the <a href="/wiki/Court_of_the_Lord_Lyon" title="Court of the Lord Lyon">Court of the Lord Lyon</a> stated in 2012 that "[that] would be speculation at this stage, and we could only cross that bridge if we came to it."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
