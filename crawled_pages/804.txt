<p><b>National and University Library in Zagreb</b> (NUL) (<a href="/wiki/Croatian_language" title="Croatian language">Croatian</a>: <i lang="hr">Nacionalna i sveučilišna knjižnica u Zagrebu</i>, NSK; formerly <i lang="hr" title="Croatian language text">Nacionalna i sveučilišna biblioteka u Zagrebu</i>, NSB) is the <a href="/wiki/National_library" title="National library">national library</a> of <a href="/wiki/Croatia" title="Croatia">Croatia</a> and central library of the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Zagreb" title="University of Zagreb">University of Zagreb</a>.
</p><p>The Library was established in 1607.<sup id="cite_ref-Stipčević2007_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stipčević2007-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Its primary mission is the development and preservation  of Croatian national written heritage. It holds around 3 million items.
</p><p>Since 1995 the NUL has been located in a purpose-built cubical building in central Zagreb.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_and_University_Library_in_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Services provided include lending and reference services (bibliographic-reference and catalogue information, subject search, science citation index search); interlibrary loan; national bibliographic database; IT services (reprographic services, microfilming, digitization, use of computer equipment); and learning programmes for users. Exhibitions are mounted, and parts of the Library’s premises may be leased.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Statistics">Statistics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_and_University_Library_in_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Statistics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Library houses approximately 3 million volumes, on 12,900m of shelving in open-access reading rooms and an additional 110,000m of mobile shelving in closed stacks. The net floor area is 36,478m<sup>2</sup>, and the gross floor area 44,432m<sup>2</sup>.
</p><p>Acquisitions under legal deposit total 18,194 monographic publications and 3,625 serial publications. There are 1,030 foreign serial titles and 4,865 foreign books. The holdings in the special collections number 11,430 items. There are 7,281 items of non-book materials, and 986 items ofelectronic materials.
</p><p>In 2011 there were 19,360 registered users and 357,291 visitors to the Library, of whom 22,445 used late hours study services. In the same year there were 718,850 online visitors. For users there are 1,100 seats, with an additional 64 seats in the Reading Rooms and 150 seats in the evening hours study room. The Special Collections are provided with 8 audio booths, 7 individual and 2 group work study rooms, and there are 10 reading-and-study compartments. There is a 100-seat conference room.
</p>
<h2><span id="The_Library.E2.80.99s_tasks"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="The_Library’s_tasks">The Library’s tasks</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_and_University_Library_in_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: The Library’s tasks">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some of the principal tasks of the Library are:
</p><p>1. the assembling and organizing of the Croatian national collection of library materials and the coordination of the acquisition of international scientific works at both the national and the University level,
</p><p>2. the preservation and restoration of library materials in the context of the international Preservation and Conservation programme (PAC),
</p><p>3. the promotion of Croatian printed and electronic publications,
</p><p>4. the integration of the Library’s bibliographic activities  and information services into international programmes,
</p><p>5. the organization of the Library as the centre of the library system of the Republic of Croatia and the University of Zagreb,
</p><p>6. scientific research in the field of library and information sciences,
</p><p>7. publishing and various promotional activities and the organization of exhibitions.
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_collections">Special collections</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_and_University_Library_in_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Special collections">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>The <b>Manuscripts and Old Books Collection</b></li></ul>
<p>The Collection assembles, preserves, processes and makes available the items from the richest Croatian collection of national manuscripts and old books, as well as the manuscripts and numerous rare and old books belonging to other cultures.
</p><p>The Manuscripts and Old Books Collection contains a vast legacy of manuscripts – correspondence including nearly 100,000 letters and 3,670 call numbers for individual manuscripts (1,720 in Croatian, 920 in Latin, 454 in German, 230 in Italian, etc.). The Collection also includes the photographic collection containing 865 items. In total the Collection contains 9,236 items (10,295 volumes).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The <b>Print Collection</b></li></ul>
<p>Valuable drawings and prints have constituted a significant part of the holdings of the National and University Library in Zagreb since the foundation of the Library four hundred years ago, while the Print Collection, as a separate organizational unit of the Library, was established in 1919. Apart from being the oldest Croatian collection of this type, the Print Collection of the National and University Library in Zagreb is also the largest print collection in Croatia. In addition to the works by many great names of the Croatian visual arts, the holdings of the Collection include works by numerous leading world artists.
</p><p>The collection includes works by the 16th-century artist <a href="/wiki/Andrija_Meduli%C4%87" class="mw-redirect" title="Andrija Medulić">Andrija Medulić</a>, architectural drawings by <a href="/wiki/Johann_Bernhard_Fischer_von_Erlach" title="Johann Bernhard Fischer von Erlach">Johann Bernhard Fischer von Erlach</a> from the 18th century, and many more modern Croatian artists.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The <b>Map Collection</b></li></ul>
<p>The Collection assembles, preserves, processes and makes available all types of maps and atlases. Special attention is given to older and more valuable cartographic items, national cartographic materials and the control of legal deposit procedures. The members of the Collection supply users with information in the field of cartography and provide professional assistance for researchers and students in the preparation and writing of their papers, articles or theses.
</p><p>The Collection comprises nearly 42,000 maps, almost 1,500 atlases, and approximately 600 books in the accompanying reference library.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<ul><li>The <b>Music Collection</b></li></ul>
<p>The Collection assembles, processes, archives and makes available sheet music, the rich legacy of Croatian composers as well as a large stock of sound recordings. All materials in the collection are available to the users of the National and University Library in Zagreb and they include nearly 17,000 printed music scores, 3,000 manuscript scores, 23,600 gramophone records, 5,700 cassettes, and 7,447 CDs.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_overview">Historical overview</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=National_and_University_Library_in_Zagreb&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Historical overview">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 1607 the <a href="/wiki/Jesuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Jesuit">Jesuit</a> order established itself in Zagreb. In addition to founding a grammar school, the Jesuits founded a Jesuit College with an accompanying library. By 1645 the library was housed in a special hall, it had a librarian and rules were established regarding the preservation and lending of books. In 1669 the Jesuit College acquired the status of an  Academy by the Charter of King Leopold I and became <i>Academia Zagrabiensis</i>. The library and its holdings were moved into an old building of the abandoned Dominican monastery in today’s 5 St. Catherine’s Square in the Old Town of Zagreb.
</p><p>The Jesuit order was disbanded in 1773. The College continued with its provisional activities until 1776, when the library became part of the Royal Zagreb Academy of Sciences (<i>Regia Academia Zagrabiensis</i>) as an academy of law, philosophy and theology. The following year the library received a bequest containing historical material from the clergyman Adam Baltazar Krčelić, with a stipulation that this material be made available to the wider public.
</p><p>As a result of <a href="/wiki/Legal_deposit" title="Legal deposit">legal deposit</a> regulations introduced in 1816 bearing on the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Pest" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Pest">University of Pest</a>, the library's national importance began to increase. Such legal deposit regulations were extended to the whole territory of Croatia and Slavonia in 1837. Antun Kukuljević, politician and the founder of the Croatian educational system, changed the name of the library to the Latin name, <i>Nationalis Academica Bibliotheca</i>, highlighting the dual nature of the library’s educational and national functions, which it has jointly developed since then and symbolically preserved in its name to this day.
</p><p>The Franz Josef I Royal University in Zagreb was founded in 1874, and the Academy library (<i>Bibliotheca Regiae Academiae Zagrabiensis</i>) became part of the University and changed its name to University Library, increasing its significance and role in higher education.
</p><p>In 1913 the library with its approximately 110,000 volumes moved from the building of the present-day Rector’s Office into an impressive Art Nouveau edifice located in 21 Marko Marulić Square, the first building built specifically for the purposes of the library. As it was designed to house 500,000 volumes, its premises soon become inadequate for the library’s 2,500,000 volumes – books, journals, newspapers and the particularly valuable items of the library’s special collections.<sup id="cite_ref-Stam2016_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stam2016-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>  A new library building, the Pantheon of the Croatian Book, was commissioned: its foundation stone was laid in 1988 and the building opened on 28 May 1995 as part of the celebration of the fifth anniversary of Croatia’s independence. This modern edifice was designed by Croatian architects <a href="/wiki/Velimir_Neidhardt" title="Velimir Neidhardt">Velimir Neidhardt</a>, Marijan Hržić, Zvonimir Krznarić and Davor Mance.<sup id="cite_ref-Stam2016_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Stam2016-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
