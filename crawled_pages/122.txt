<p><b>Yandex N.V.</b> (<span class="nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/j/: &#39;y&#39; in &#39;yes&#39;">j</span><span title="/ʌ/: &#39;u&#39; in &#39;cut&#39;">ʌ</span><span title="&#39;n&#39; in &#39;nigh&#39;">n</span><span title="&#39;d&#39; in &#39;dye&#39;">d</span><span title="/ɛ/: &#39;e&#39; in &#39;dress&#39;">ɛ</span><span title="&#39;k&#39; in &#39;kind&#39;">k</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Russian_language" title="Russian language">Russian</a>&#58; <span lang="ru" title="Russian language text">Яндекс</span>, <small>IPA:&#160;</small><span title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)" class="IPA"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Russian" title="Help:IPA/Russian">[ˈjandəks]</a></span>) is a Russian <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational corporation</a> specializing in <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">Internet</a>-related products and services, including search and information services, eCommerce, transportation, navigation, mobile applications, and online advertising. Yandex provides over 70 services in total.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
Incorporated in the Netherlands, Yandex primarily serves audiences in <a href="/wiki/Russia" title="Russia">Russia</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a>. The company founders and most of the team members are located in Russia. The company has 18 commercial offices worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It is the largest <a href="/wiki/Technology_company" title="Technology company">technology company</a> in Russia<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and the largest <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> on the <a href="/wiki/Internet_in_Russian" title="Internet in Russian">internet in Russian</a>, with a market share of over 52%.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> The Yandex.ru home page is the 4th most popular website in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> It also has the largest market share of any search engine in the <a href="/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States" title="Commonwealth of Independent States">Commonwealth of Independent States</a> and is the 5th largest search engine worldwide after <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Baidu" title="Baidu">Baidu</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a>.
</p><p>Yandex's main competitors on the Russian market are <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mail.ru" class="mw-redirect" title="Mail.ru">Mail.ru</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Rambler_(portal)" title="Rambler (portal)">Rambler</a>.
</p><p>According to the company, one of its biggest advantages for Russian-language users is the ability to recognize Russian <a href="/wiki/Inflection" title="Inflection">inflection</a> in search queries.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yandex has invested in companies including Vizi Labs, <a href="/wiki/Face.com" title="Face.com">Face.com</a>, <a href="/wiki/Blekko" title="Blekko">Blekko</a>, Seismotech, Multiship, SalesPredict, and <a href="/wiki/Doc%2B" title="Doc+">Doc+</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Offices">Offices</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yandex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Offices">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yandex has offices in 17 countries.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yandex launched Yandex Labs in <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a> in 2008,<sup id="cite_ref-labs_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-labs-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> and also in <a href="/wiki/Turkey" title="Turkey">Turkey</a> in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-turkey_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-turkey-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yandex opened its first sales office in <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a> in <a href="/wiki/Lucerne" title="Lucerne">Lucerne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a> in 2012 to serve its European advertising clients.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, they also opened a <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> office in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yandex opened its first office in <a href="/wiki/Shanghai" title="Shanghai">Shanghai</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> during 2015 to work with Chinese companies that work on the Russian language market.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yandex&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yandex traces its roots to 1990, when <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Volozh" title="Arkady Volozh">Arkady Volozh</a> and Arkady Borkovsky founded Arkadia, a company that developed <a href="/wiki/MS-DOS" title="MS-DOS">MS-DOS</a> software for use in patents and goods classification. Their software featured a full-text search with Russian <a href="/wiki/Morphology_(linguistics)" title="Morphology (linguistics)">morphology</a> support. In 1993, Arkadia became a subdivision of Comptek International, another company founded by Volozh in 1989.
</p><p>In 1993, <a href="/wiki/Arkady_Volozh" title="Arkady Volozh">Arkady Volozh</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ilya_Segalovich" title="Ilya Segalovich">Ilya Segalovich</a>, friends since their school days and by then working together to develop search software,<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> invented the word "Yandex" to describe their search technologies. The name initially stood for "Yet Another iNDEXer".<sup id="cite_ref-history_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> The Russian word "Я" ("Ya") corresponds to the English <a href="/wiki/Personal_pronoun" title="Personal pronoun">personal pronoun</a> "I", making "Яndex" a <a href="/wiki/Bilingual_pun" title="Bilingual pun">bilingual pun</a> on "index". Another pun is based on the <i><a href="/wiki/Yin_and_yang" title="Yin and yang">yin and yang</a></i> contrast (Russian: инь – индекс, ян – яндекс).
</p><p>Between 1993 and 1996, the company continued developing its search technologies and released software for searching through the Bible.<sup id="cite_ref-history_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Yandex.ru search engine was launched on September 23, 1997, and was publicly presented at the Softool exhibition in Moscow.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Initially, the search engine was developed by Comptek. In 2000, Yandex was incorporated as a standalone company by Arkady Volozh.<sup id="cite_ref-history_19-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 1998, Yandex launched <a href="/wiki/Contextual_advertisement" class="mw-redirect" title="Contextual advertisement">contextual advertisement</a> on its search engine. In 2001, it launched the <a href="/wiki/Yandex.Direct" title="Yandex.Direct">Yandex.Direct</a> <a href="/wiki/Online_advertising" title="Online advertising">online advertising</a> network.<sup id="cite_ref-history_19-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-history-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company became profitable in November 2002. In 2004, Yandex sales increased to $17 million, up 1000% in 2 years. The net income of the company in 2004 was $7 million. In June 2006, the weekly revenue of <a href="/wiki/Yandex.Direct" title="Yandex.Direct">Yandex.Direct</a> context ads system exceeded $1 million. The company's accounting has been audited by <a href="/wiki/Deloitte" title="Deloitte">Deloitte</a> since 1999.
</p><p>In September 2005, Yandex opened an office in Ukraine<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and launched www.yandex.ua.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, Yandex introduced a customized search engine for Ukrainian users;<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Yandex also opened its development center in Kiev in May 2007. In 2008, Yandex extended its presence in Ukraine by increasing bandwidth between Moscow data centers and UA-IX in Ukraine fivefold.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, all services of www.yandex.ua were localized for the Ukrainian market.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> In 2010, Yandex launched its "Poltava" search engine algorithm for Ukrainian users, based on its <a href="/wiki/MatrixNet" title="MatrixNet">MatrixNet</a> technology.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2007, Yandex acquired moikrug.ru, a Russian <a href="/wiki/Social_networking_service" title="Social networking service">social networking service</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 16, 2008, Yandex acquired SMILink, a Russian road traffic monitoring agency, to merge it with Yandex. Maps services.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 20, 2008, Yandex announced the formation of Yandex Labs in <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a>. The main objective of the division is to foster "innovation in search and advertising technology".<sup id="cite_ref-labs_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-labs-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2008, Yandex acquired the rights to the Punto Switcher software program, an automatic Russian to English keyboard layout switcher.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In January 2009, <a href="/wiki/Mozilla_Firefox_3.5" class="mw-redirect" title="Mozilla Firefox 3.5">Mozilla Firefox 3.5</a>, replaced Google with Yandex as the default search provider for Russian-language builds.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2009, Yandex introduced a player of free legal music in its search results. In September 2010, Yandex launched the Yandex. Music service and significantly extended its music catalogue to 800,000 tracks from 58,000 performers.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 19, 2010, Yandex launched an English-only web search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2010, Yandex invested in a $4.3 million financing round by <a href="/wiki/Face.com" title="Face.com">Face.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The company was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a> in 2012.
</p><p>In December 2010, Yandex launched Yandex.Start to find startups and to work with them systematically. As a result of the program, Yandex purchased WebVisor's behavior analysis technology in December 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 26, 2011, Yandex introduced premium placement opportunity in its Business directory; advertisers' local small businesses are highlighted on a map for relevant queries.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On January 27, 2011, the company acquired single sign-in service Loginza.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 3, 2011, as part of a disclosure on the "legal and political risks" of investing in Russia, before the listing on NASDAQ, Yandex revealed that it had passed private customer information to the <a href="/wiki/Federal_Security_Service" title="Federal Security Service">Federal Security Service</a>, including that of users who had donated money via Yandex to an anti-corruption website set-up by <a href="/wiki/Alexei_Navalny" title="Alexei Navalny">Alexei Navalny</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On May 24, 2011, Yandex raised $1.3 billion in an <a href="/wiki/Initial_public_offering" title="Initial public offering">initial public offering</a> on <a href="/wiki/NASDAQ" title="NASDAQ">NASDAQ</a>. It was the biggest initial public offering for a <a href="/wiki/Dot-com_company" title="Dot-com company">dot-com company</a> since <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>'s offering in 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> Among the largest investors in Yandex were <a href="/wiki/Baring_Vostok_Capital_Partners" title="Baring Vostok Capital Partners">Baring Vostok Capital Partners</a>, which owned a 30% stake, and <a href="/wiki/Tiger_Management" title="Tiger Management">Tiger Management</a>, which owned a 15% stake.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2011, Yandex acquired The Tweeted Times, a news delivery startup.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2011, Yandex launched a search engine and a range of other services in Turkey. The company also opened an office in Istanbul.<sup id="cite_ref-turkey_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-turkey-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2011, the company invested in <a href="/wiki/Blekko" title="Blekko">Blekko</a> as part of a $30 million financing round.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2011, Yandex acquired <a href="/wiki/SPB_Software" title="SPB Software">SPB Software</a>, a software developer, for $38 million.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2012, Yandex acquired a 25% stake in Seismotech, a company that provides services in the area of interpretative processing of seismic data and software development, for $1 million.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2013, the company added an English <a href="/wiki/User_interface" title="User interface">user interface</a> to its translation <a href="/wiki/Mobile_app" title="Mobile app">mobile app</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In July 2013, <a href="/wiki/Mail.Ru" title="Mail.Ru">Mail.Ru</a> started placing Yandex.Direct ads on its search result pages.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In October 2013, Yandex acquired <a href="/wiki/KinoPoisk" title="KinoPoisk">KinoPoisk</a>, the biggest Russian movie search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2013, Yandex became the largest media property in Russia by revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2014, Yandex invested several million dollars in MultiShip.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2014, Yandex acquired Israeli <a href="/wiki/Geolocation" title="Geolocation">geolocation</a> startup <a href="/wiki/KitLocate" title="KitLocate">KitLocate</a> and opened a <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> office in <a href="/wiki/Israel" title="Israel">Israel</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In April 2014, a <a href="/wiki/Movie" class="mw-redirect" title="Movie">movie</a> called <i>Startup</i>, about the history of Yandex was released.<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In June 2014, Yandex acquired Auto.ru, an <a href="/wiki/Online_marketplace" title="Online marketplace">online marketplace</a> and <a href="/wiki/Classified_advertising" title="Classified advertising">classified advertising</a> website for automobiles, for $175 million.<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2015, Yandex acquired <a href="/wiki/Agnitum" title="Agnitum">Agnitum</a>, an <a href="/wiki/Internet_security" title="Internet security">internet security</a> company.<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 1, 2017, Yandex closed its offices in <a href="/wiki/Kiev" title="Kiev">Kiev</a> and <a href="/wiki/Odessa" title="Odessa">Odessa</a>, Ukraine after the <a href="/wiki/Security_Service_of_Ukraine" title="Security Service of Ukraine">Security Service of Ukraine</a> raided the offices and accused the company of illegally collecting Ukrainian users’ data and sending it to Russian security agencies.<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On June 6, 2017, the company invested in a $5 million financing round by <a href="/wiki/Doc%2B" title="Doc+">Doc+</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-66" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-66">&#91;66&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On October 10, 2017, Yandex introduced its <a href="/wiki/Intelligent_personal_assistant" class="mw-redirect" title="Intelligent personal assistant">intelligent personal assistant</a>, <a href="/wiki/Alisa_(virtual_assistant)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alisa (virtual assistant)">Alisa</a> (Alice) for <a href="/wiki/Android_(operating_system)" title="Android (operating system)">Android</a>, <a href="/wiki/IOS" title="IOS">iOS</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Microsoft_Windows" title="Microsoft Windows">Microsoft Windows</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-67" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-67">&#91;67&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">&#91;68&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">&#91;69&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In December 2017, the company acquired Foodfox, a <a href="/wiki/Food_delivery" title="Food delivery">food delivery</a> service.<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">&#91;70&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, within the framework of a joint anti-phishing project of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Bank_of_Russia" title="Central Bank of Russia">Bank of Russia</a> and search engine Yandex, a special <a href="/wiki/Check_mark" title="Check mark">check mark</a> (a green circle with a tick and <span lang="ru" title="Russian language text">'Реестр ЦБ РФ'</span> (Bank of Russia Register) text box) appeared in the search results, informing the consumer that the website is really owned by a legally registered company licensed by the Bank of Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">&#91;71&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">&#91;72&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 7, 2018, <a href="/wiki/Uber" title="Uber">Uber</a> and Yandex NV merged their businesses in Russia, <a href="/wiki/Kazakhstan" title="Kazakhstan">Kazakhstan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Armenia" title="Armenia">Armenia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Belarus" title="Belarus">Belarus</a> and <a href="/wiki/Georgia_(country)" title="Georgia (country)">Georgia</a>. Uber invested $225 million and owns 36.6% stake in the venture while Yandex invested $100 million and owns a 59.3% stake.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">&#91;73&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 16, 2018, the company showed off the first tests of its <a href="/wiki/Autonomous_car" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomous car">autonomous cars</a> in Moscow.<sup id="cite_ref-74" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-74">&#91;74&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-75" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-75">&#91;75&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2018, Sberbank and Yandex completed joint venture deal  to develop a B2C eCommerce ecosystem.<sup id="cite_ref-76" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-76">&#91;76&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
