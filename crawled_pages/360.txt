<p><b>Blekko</b>, trademarked as <b>blekko</b> (lowercase),<sup id="cite_ref-about_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> was a company that provided a <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine" title="Web search engine">web search engine</a> with the stated goal of providing better search results than those offered by <a href="/wiki/Google_Search" title="Google Search">Google Search</a>, with results gathered from a set of 3 billion trusted webpages and excluding such sites as <a href="/wiki/Content_farm" title="Content farm">content farms</a>. The company's site, launched to the public on November 1, 2010, used <a href="/wiki/Slashtag" title="Slashtag">slashtags</a> to provide results for common searches. Blekko also offered a downloadable search bar. It was acquired by <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> in March 2015, and the service was discontinued.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blekko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The company was co-founded in 2007 by <a href="/wiki/Rich_Skrenta" title="Rich Skrenta">Rich Skrenta</a>, who had created Newhoo, which was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Netscape" title="Netscape">Netscape</a> and renamed as the <a href="/wiki/DMOZ" title="DMOZ">Open Directory Project</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Skrenta "is still remembered most for unleashing the <a href="/wiki/Elk_Cloner" title="Elk Cloner">Elk Cloner</a> virus on the world".<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> 
Blekko raised $24 million in <a href="/wiki/Venture_capital" title="Venture capital">venture capital</a> from such individuals as Netscape founder <a href="/wiki/Marc_Andreessen" title="Marc Andreessen">Marc Andreessen</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ron_Conway" title="Ron Conway">Ron Conway</a>, as well as from <a href="/wiki/U.S._Venture_Partners" title="U.S. Venture Partners">U.S. Venture Partners</a> and CMEA Capital.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The company's goal was to be able to provide useful search results without the extraneous links often provided by Google. Individuals who enter searches for such frequently searched categories as cars, finance, health and hotels received results prescreened by Blekko editors who used what the <i><a href="/wiki/New_York_Times" class="mw-redirect" title="New York Times">New York Times</a></i> described as "<a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>-style policing" to weed out pages created by content farms and focus on results from professionals.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Use of slashtags restricted the set of search results to those matching the specified characteristic and a slashtag was to be automatically added for search categories with prescreened results.<sup id="cite_ref-CNN_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Queries related to personal health were limited to a prescreened list of sites that Blekko editors had determined to be trustworthy, excluding many sites that rank highly in Google searches.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> As of Blekko's launch date, its 8,000 beta editors had developed 3,000 slashtags corresponding to the site's most frequent searches.<sup id="cite_ref-CNN_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CNN-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The company hoped to use editors to develop prepared lists of the 50 sites that best match its 100,000 most frequent search targets.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Additional tools allowed users to see the IP address that a website is running on and let registered users label a site as spam.<sup id="cite_ref-Dvorak_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dvorak-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>At the time, Blekko announced plans to earn revenue by selling ads based on slashtags and search results. The company also planned to provide data on its algorithm for ranking search results, including details for inbound links to specific sites.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As part of a permanent post in Blekko's help section was the following "Web search bill of rights":<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em;">
<ol><li>Search shall be open</li>
<li>Search results shall involve people</li>
<li>Ranking data shall not be kept secret</li>
<li>Web data shall be readily available</li>
<li>There is no one-size-fits-all for search</li>
<li>Advanced search shall be accessible</li>
<li>Search engine tools shall be open to all</li>
<li>Search and community go hand-in-hand</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Spamming" title="Spamming">Spam</a> does not belong in search results</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Privacy" title="Privacy">Privacy</a> of searchers shall not be violated</li></ol>
</div>
<p>One writer referred to the bill of rights as "what we assume is a poke at Google".<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Observer11_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Observer11-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2011 Blekko announced blocking "content farmy sites", to reduce spam, in line with its bill of rights.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2012 <a href="/wiki/Mozilla" title="Mozilla">Mozilla</a> announced an "instant search" browser <a href="/wiki/Plug-in_(computing)" title="Plug-in (computing)">plugin</a> for Firefox designed to cache repetitive search requests, in partnership with Blekko.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In August 2012 Blekko put all its SEO statistics behind a <a href="/wiki/Paywall" title="Paywall">paywall</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-SEOpay_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEOpay-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> despite previously declaring that "ranking data shall not be kept secret"<sup id="cite_ref-Observer11_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Observer11-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> in its bill of rights.<sup id="cite_ref-SEOpay_14-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEOpay-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_and_closure">Acquisition and closure</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blekko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Acquisition and closure">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>IBM bought Blekko and closed the search service on 27 March 2015, redirecting searches to a page announcing "The blekko technology and team have joined IBM Watson!" and linking to a blog post announcing that the blekko service was closed, with blekko's web-crawling abilities to be integrated into <a href="/wiki/Watson_(computer)" title="Watson (computer)">IBM Watson</a>, adding advanced Web-crawling, categorization and intelligent filtering technology.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Slashtags">Slashtags</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blekko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Slashtags">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blekko used an initiative called slashtags,<sup id="cite_ref-about_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-about-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> consisting of a text tag preceded by a "/" slash character, to allow ease of searching and categorise searches. System and pre-defined slashtags allowed users to start searching right away. Users could create slashtags after signup, to perform custom-sorted searches and to reduce spam.<sup id="cite_ref-NYT_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-NYT-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blekko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The following features were available to all users:
</p>
<div class="div-col columns column-width" style="-moz-column-width: 30em; -webkit-column-width: 30em; column-width: 30em;">
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Search_engine_optimization" title="Search engine optimization">Search engine optimization</a> statistics. From 2012 subject to payment.<sup id="cite_ref-SEOpay_14-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SEOpay-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Linking pages (in and out statistics)</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/IP_address" title="IP address">IP address</a> lookup</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Web_cache" title="Web cache">Cached</a> pages</li>
<li>Tagging of pages</li>
<li>Finding <a href="/wiki/Duplicate_content" title="Duplicate content">duplicate content</a></li>
<li>Comparing sites</li>

<li>Page count</li>
<li>Location of <a href="/wiki/Robots_exclusion_standard" title="Robots exclusion standard">robots.txt</a></li>
<li>Cohosted sites</li>
<li>Page latency</li>

</div>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Toolbar">Toolbar</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blekko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Toolbar">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Blekko offered a downloadable <a href="/wiki/Browser_toolbar" title="Browser toolbar">browser toolbar</a> or search bar which changes default search and <a href="/wiki/Home_page" title="Home page">home page</a> URLs of the user's <a href="/wiki/Web_browser" title="Web browser">web browsers</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blekkobar_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blekkobar-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blekko&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2010, <a href="/wiki/John_C._Dvorak" title="John C. Dvorak">John Dvorak</a> described the site as adding "so much weird dimensionality" to search, and recommended it as "the best out-of-the-chute new engine I've seen in the last 10 years".<sup id="cite_ref-Dvorak_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Dvorak-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>  In Matthew Rogers' review of the site, he found it "slow and cumbersome", and stated that he did not understand the necessity or utility for slashtags.<sup id="cite_ref-Rogers_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Rogers-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>  In his PCMag.com review, Jeffrey L. Wilson expressed approval of some search results, but criticized the site's social features which "bog down the search experience."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
