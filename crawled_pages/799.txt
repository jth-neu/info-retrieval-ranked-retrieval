<p>Public <b>water supply and sanitation in the United Kingdom</b> is characterised by universal access and generally good <a href="/wiki/Service_quality" title="Service quality">service quality</a>. A salient feature of the sector in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> compared to other developed countries is the diversity of institutional arrangements between the constituting parts of the UK (<a href="/wiki/Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_England_and_Wales" title="Water supply and sanitation in England and Wales">England and Wales</a>; <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_Scotland" title="Water supply and sanitation in Scotland">Scotland</a>; and <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Water" title="Northern Ireland Water">Northern Ireland</a>), which are each described in separate articles, while this article is devoted to some common issues across the United Kingdom.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Access">Access</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Access">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Access to improved <a href="/wiki/Water_supply" title="Water supply">water supply</a> and sanitation in the UK is universal. In 2015, 100% of the population had access to improved water supply and 99% of the population had access to "<a href="/wiki/Improved_sanitation" title="Improved sanitation">improved" sanitation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Water_sources">Water sources</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Water sources">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>According to the Environment Agency, total water abstraction for public water supply in the UK was 16,406 <a href="/wiki/Megalitre" class="mw-redirect" title="Megalitre">megalitres</a> per day in 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Groundwater contributes 30 per cent of public supply water in England. In Wales and Scotland groundwater provides about five per cent of public supply. <sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>.  The majority of the UK's abstraction of surface water is from reservoirs, where rainwater is transported via rivers and streams and contained in an artificial or natural lake until it's required.  <sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Responsibility_for_water_supply_and_sanitation">Responsibility for water supply and sanitation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Responsibility for water supply and sanitation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Policy_and_regulation">Policy and regulation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Policy and regulation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In England and Wales the economic regulator of water companies is the <a href="/wiki/Water_Services_Regulation_Authority" class="mw-redirect" title="Water Services Regulation Authority">Water Services Regulation Authority</a> (Ofwat). The <a href="/wiki/Environment_Agency" title="Environment Agency">Environment Agency</a> is responsible for environmental regulation, and the <a href="/wiki/Drinking_Water_Inspectorate" title="Drinking Water Inspectorate">Drinking Water Inspectorate</a> for regulating drinking water quality. The economic water industry regulator in <a href="/wiki/Scotland" title="Scotland">Scotland</a> is the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.watercommission.co.uk/">Water Industry Commission for Scotland</a> and the environmental regulator is the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Environment_Protection_Agency" title="Scottish Environment Protection Agency">Scottish Environment Protection Agency</a>.  Drinking water standards and wastewater discharge standards in the UK, as in other countries of the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, are determined by the EU (see <a href="/wiki/Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_European_Union" title="Water supply and sanitation in the European Union">Water supply and sanitation in the European Union</a>).
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Service_provision">Service provision</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Service provision">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Further information: <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_water_companies" title="United Kingdom water companies">United Kingdom water companies</a></div>
<p>In <b>England and Wales</b> water and sewerage services are provided by 10 private regional water and sewerage companies and 13 mostly smaller private "water only" companies. In <b>Scotland</b> water and sewerage services are provided by a single public company, <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Water" title="Scottish Water">Scottish Water</a>. In <b>Northern Ireland</b> water and sewerage services are also provided by a single public entity, <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland_Water" title="Northern Ireland Water">Northern Ireland Water</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Employment">Employment</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Employment">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Total employment by UK water companies amounted to 41,000 <a href="/wiki/Full-time_equivalent" title="Full-time equivalent">full-time equivalent</a> jobs in 2012/13, according to an analysis by the consulting firm <a href="/wiki/Deloitte" title="Deloitte">Deloitte</a>. In addition, 86,000 jobs were supported indirectly.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Financial_aspects">Financial aspects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Financial aspects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Tariffs">Tariffs</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Tariffs">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>According to a 2006 survey by NUS consulting the average water tariff (price) without sewerage in the U.K. for large consumers was the equivalent of US$1.90 per cubic metre. This was the third-highest tariff among the 14 mostly OECD countries covered by the report.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Metering">Metering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Water_supply_and_sanitation_in_the_United_Kingdom&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Metering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A particularity of water tariffs in the U.K. is the low share of metering. Most users are not billed on a volumetric basis and have no financial incentive for water conservation. Since the 1990s efforts have been made to increase the share of household metering, which reached 33% in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Water_UK_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Water_UK-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Environment Agency would like to see 75% of households metered by 2025. The Fairness on Tap coalition (including <a href="/wiki/National_Trust" class="mw-redirect" title="National Trust">National Trust</a>, <a href="/wiki/The_Waterwise_Project" title="The Waterwise Project">Waterwise</a>, <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">WWF</a> and <a href="/wiki/RSPB" class="mw-redirect" title="RSPB">RSPB</a>) is calling for the government to set out a strategy to install water meters in at least 80% of England where there is the greatest pressure on the freshwater environment and people's pockets, by 2020. Studies show that water meters lead to a 5-15% reduction in household water use.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
