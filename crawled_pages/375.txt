<p><b>Blackle</b> is a website powered by <a href="/wiki/Google_Custom_Search" title="Google Custom Search">Google Custom Search</a> and created by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Heap_Media&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Heap Media (page does not exist)">Heap Media</a>, which aims to save energy by displaying a black background and using grayish-white font color for search results.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Blackle claims having saved nearly 6 <a href="/wiki/Kilowatt_hours#Watt_hour_multiples_and_billing_units" class="mw-redirect" title="Kilowatt hours">MWh</a> of electrical energy up to December 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> a claim currently under dispute.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> For comparison, the average American household consumes 11 MWh of electrical energy per year.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept">Concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Blackle&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The concept behind Blackle is that <a href="/wiki/Computer_monitors" class="mw-redirect" title="Computer monitors">computer monitors</a> can be made to use less energy by displaying much darker colors. Blackle is based on a study which tested a variety of <a href="/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube" title="Cathode ray tube">CRT</a> and <a href="/wiki/LCD_monitor" class="mw-redirect" title="LCD monitor">LCD monitors</a>. There is dispute over whether there really are any energy saving effects, especially for users of LCD screens, where there is a constant <a href="/wiki/Backlight" title="Backlight">backlight</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Roberson_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Roberson-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>This concept was first brought to the attention of Heap Media by a <a href="/wiki/Blog_post" class="mw-redirect" title="Blog post">blog post</a>, which estimated that Google could save 750 <a href="/wiki/Kilowatt_hours#Watt_hour_multiples_and_billing_units" class="mw-redirect" title="Kilowatt hours">megawatt hours</a> a year by utilizing it for <a href="/wiki/Cathode_ray_tube" title="Cathode ray tube">CRT</a> screens.<sup id="cite_ref-smh_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-smh-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Homepage" class="mw-redirect" title="Homepage">homepage</a> of Blackle provides a count of the number of watt hours claimed to have been saved by enabling this concept.
</p>
