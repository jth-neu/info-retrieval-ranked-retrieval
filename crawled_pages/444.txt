<p><b>Saskia Sassen</b> (born January 5, 1947) is a <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Dutch</a>-American <a href="/wiki/Sociology" title="Sociology">sociologist</a> noted for her analyses of <a href="/wiki/Globalization" title="Globalization">globalization</a> and international <a href="/wiki/Human_migration" title="Human migration">human migration</a>. She is <a href="/wiki/Robert_S._Lynd" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert S. Lynd">Robert S. Lynd</a> Professor of Sociology at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> and Centennial visiting Professor at the <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">London School of Economics</a>. Sassen coined the term <a href="/wiki/Global_city" title="Global city">global city</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_early_life">Family and early life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saskia_Sassen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Family and early life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Sassen was born in <a href="/wiki/The_Hague,_Netherlands" class="mw-redirect" title="The Hague, Netherlands">The Hague, Netherlands</a> in 1947. In 1948 Sassen's parents, <a href="/wiki/Willem_Sassen" title="Willem Sassen">Willem Sassen</a> and Miep van der Voort, moved to <a href="/wiki/Argentina" title="Argentina">Argentina</a> and the family lived in <a href="/wiki/Buenos_Aires" title="Buenos Aires">Buenos Aires</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Her father was a Dutch collaborator with the <a href="/wiki/National_Socialism" class="mw-redirect" title="National Socialism">Nazis</a>, a Nazi journalist and a member of the <a href="/wiki/Waffen-SS" title="Waffen-SS">Waffen-SS</a>. In the 1950s Willem Sassen was close to <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Eichmann" title="Adolf Eichmann">Adolf Eichmann</a> when both were living in Argentina and she recalls him visiting her childhood home.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Saskia Sassen also spent part of her youth in Italy and says she was "brought up in five languages."<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>She is married to sociologist <a href="/wiki/Richard_Sennett" title="Richard Sennett">Richard Sennett</a>. Artist Hilary Koob-Sassen is her son from her first marriage.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Education">Education</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saskia_Sassen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Education">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>From 1966, Sassen spent a year each at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Poitiers" title="University of Poitiers">Université de Poitiers</a>, France, the <a href="/wiki/Sapienza_University_of_Rome" title="Sapienza University of Rome">Università degli Studi di Roma</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Buenos_Aires" title="University of Buenos Aires">University of Buenos Aires</a>, for studies in <a href="/wiki/Philosophy" title="Philosophy">philosophy</a> and <a href="/wiki/Political_science" title="Political science">political science</a>. From 1969, Sassen studied sociology and economics at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Notre_Dame" title="University of Notre Dame">University of Notre Dame</a>, <a href="/wiki/Indiana" title="Indiana">Indiana</a>, where she obtained a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">M.A.</a> in 1971 and a <a href="/wiki/Ph.D." class="mw-redirect" title="Ph.D.">Ph.D.</a> degree in 1974. She also received a master's degree in philosophy from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Poitiers" title="University of Poitiers">University of Poitiers</a> in 1974.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_posts">Academic posts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Saskia_Sassen&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Academic posts">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>After being a post-doctoral fellow at the Center for International Affairs at <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a>, Sassen held various academic positions in and outside the USA, such as the Ralph Lewis Professor of Sociology at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Chicago" title="University of Chicago">University of Chicago</a>. She is currently Robert S. Lynd Professor of Sociology at <a href="/wiki/Columbia_University" title="Columbia University">Columbia University</a> and Centennial Visiting Professor of Political Economy in the <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.lse.ac.uk/collections/sociology/">Department of Sociology</a> at the <a href="/wiki/London_School_of_Economics" title="London School of Economics">London School of Economics</a>.
</p><p>During the 1980s and 1990s, Sassen emerged as a prolific author in <a href="/wiki/Urban_sociology" title="Urban sociology">urban sociology</a>. She studied the impacts of <a href="/wiki/Globalisation" class="mw-redirect" title="Globalisation">globalisation</a> such as <a href="/wiki/Economic_restructuring" title="Economic restructuring">economic restructuring</a>, and how the movements of labour and capital influence urban life. She also studied the influence of communication technology on governance. Sassen observed how <a href="/wiki/Nation_state" title="Nation state">nation states</a> begin to lose power to control these developments, and she studied increasing general <a href="/wiki/Transnationalism" title="Transnationalism">transnationalism</a>, including <a href="/wiki/International" title="International">transnational</a> <a href="/wiki/Human_migration" title="Human migration">human migration</a>. She identified and described the phenomenon of the <i>global city</i>. Her 1991 book bearing this title made her a widely quoted author on globalisation. An updated edition of her book was published in 2001. In the early 2000s, Sassen focused on immigration and globalization, with her "denationalization" and "transnationalism" projects (see Bibliography and External Links, below). Her books have been translated into 21 languages.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
