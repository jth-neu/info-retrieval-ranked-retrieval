<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Wikiseek</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><table class="infobox vcard" style="width:22em"><caption class="fn org">Wikiseek</caption><tbody><tr><th scope="row"><div style="padding:0.1em 0;line-height:1.2em;">Type of site</div></th><td>

<p><b>Wikiseek</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> that indexed <a href="/wiki/English_Wikipedia" title="English Wikipedia">English Wikipedia</a> pages and pages that were linked to from Wikipedia articles.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The search engine was funded by a <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto" class="mw-redirect" title="Palo Alto">Palo Alto</a> based <a href="/wiki/Internet_startup" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet startup">Internet startup</a> <a href="/wiki/SearchMe" title="SearchMe">SearchMe</a> and was officially launched on January 17, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-sel_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sel-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Most of the funding came from <a href="/wiki/Sequoia_Capital" title="Sequoia Capital">Sequoia Capital</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-iw_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iw-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> It used <a href="/wiki/Google_ads" class="mw-redirect" title="Google ads">Google ads</a> on its search returns to generate profit. As of 2008 it is no longer active.
</p><p>Wikiseek was granted permission by the <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_Foundation" title="Wikimedia Foundation">Wikimedia Foundation</a> to index the Wikipedia website.<sup id="cite_ref-sel_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sel-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Wikiseek has made financial contributions to the Wikimedia Foundation,<sup id="cite_ref-sel_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sel-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and the group-edited blog, <a href="/wiki/TechCrunch" title="TechCrunch">TechCrunch</a> reported that it was donating the "majority" of advertising revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Wikipedia pages were re-indexed whenever Wikipedia had a database clean-up; external links were re-indexed weekly.<sup id="cite_ref-sel_2-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sel-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Search results included <a href="/wiki/Tag_cloud" title="Tag cloud">tag clouds</a> of Wikipedia categories that contained the search term.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The first three results of any search would always be Wikipedia articles, and the remainder were a mix of Wikipedia content and websites linked to from Wikipedia.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_1-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The service used user feedback to reduce the likelihood of spam.<sup id="cite_ref-iw_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-iw-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>TechCrunch commented that the search engine may cause confusion with the <a href="/wiki/Wikia" title="Wikia">Wikia</a> <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> that had been announced the month previous to Wikiseek's launch.<sup id="cite_ref-techcrunch_1-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-techcrunch-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
