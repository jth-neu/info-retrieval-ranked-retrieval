<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Teoma</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>

<p><b>Teoma</b>, pronounced <i>tay o mah</i><sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2014)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> (from <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Gaelic" title="Scottish Gaelic">Scottish Gaelic</a> <i>teòma</i> "expert"), was an Internet <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> founded in 2000 by Professor <a href="/wiki/Apostolos_Gerasoulis" title="Apostolos Gerasoulis">Apostolos Gerasoulis</a> and his colleagues at <a href="/wiki/Rutgers_University" title="Rutgers University">Rutgers University</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Jersey" title="New Jersey">New Jersey</a>. Professor <a href="/wiki/Tao_Yang_(Teoma)" title="Tao Yang (Teoma)">Tao Yang</a> from the <a href="/wiki/University_of_California,_Santa_Barbara" title="University of California, Santa Barbara">University of California, Santa Barbara</a> co-led technology R&amp;D. Their research grew out of the 1998 DiscoWeb project. The original research was published in the paper, "DiscoWeb: Applying Link Analysis to Web Search".<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Teoma used a unique link-popularity algorithm. Unlike Google's <a href="/wiki/PageRank" title="PageRank">PageRank</a>, Teoma's technology (Subject-Specific Popularity) analyzed links in context to rank a web page's importance within its specific subject. For instance, a web page about "baseball" would rank higher if other web pages about "baseball" linked to it.
</p><p>Many parts of Teoma's relevancy algorithm stemmed from the methodology which <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> developed for its <a href="/wiki/CLEVER_project" title="CLEVER project">CLEVER project</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Ask.com" title="Ask.com">Ask Jeeves</a> acquired Teoma on September 11, 2001; it has powered <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ask.com">ask.com</a> and other international Ask Jeeves sites (such as <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ask.co.uk">ask.co.uk</a>, <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://www.ask.jp">ask.jp</a>) and <a rel="nofollow" class="external text" href="http://es.ask.com">Ask Jeeves Spain</a> since then. On 26 February 2006 Teoma was rebranded and redirected to <a href="/wiki/Ask.com" title="Ask.com">Ask.com</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of  2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Teoma&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> Ask.com referred to the Teoma algorithm as the <b>ExpertRank</b> algorithm.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The official word on Teoma from Ask.com as of  May&#160; 2010<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Teoma&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup>: "We re-launched Teoma.com in mid-April 2010 to provide a simplified interface for everyday keyword web search. Teoma.com's organic results will be similar to, but not exactly the same as, those on Ask.com, primarily for testing purposes, but also to provide a more simplified search <a href="/wiki/Experience_(disambiguation)" class="mw-disambig" title="Experience (disambiguation)">experience</a>."<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2016)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Currently, the Web search is powered by <a href="/wiki/Google_Search" title="Google Search">Google</a>, Images search is powered by <a href="/wiki/Picsearch" title="Picsearch">Picsearch</a>, News search is powered by <a href="/wiki/IAC_Applications" class="mw-redirect" title="IAC Applications">IAC Applications</a>, Videos search is powered by <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, Shopping search is powered by <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a>, Maps search is powered by <a href="/wiki/Bing_Maps" title="Bing Maps">Bing</a> and Recipes search is powered by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Total_Recipe_Search&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Total Recipe Search (page does not exist)">Total Recipe Search</a>.
</p>
