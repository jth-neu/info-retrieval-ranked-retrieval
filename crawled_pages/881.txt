<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Craig Armstrong (composer)</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p><b>Craig Armstrong</b>, <a href="/wiki/Order_of_the_British_Empire" title="Order of the British Empire">OBE</a> (born 29 April 1959)<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> is a <a href="/wiki/Scottish_people" title="Scottish people">Scottish</a> composer of modern orchestral music, <a href="/wiki/Electronica" title="Electronica">electronica</a> and <a href="/wiki/Film_score" title="Film score">film scores</a>. He graduated from the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Music" title="Royal Academy of Music">Royal Academy of Music</a> in 1981, and has since written music for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Scottish_National_Orchestra" title="Royal Scottish National Orchestra">Royal Scottish National Orchestra</a> and the <a href="/wiki/London_Sinfonietta" title="London Sinfonietta">London Sinfonietta</a>.
</p><p>Armstrong's score for <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_%2B_Juliet" title="Romeo + Juliet">Romeo + Juliet</a></i> earned him a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> for Achievement in Film Music and an <a href="/wiki/Ivor_Novello" title="Ivor Novello">Ivor Novello</a>. His composition for <a href="/wiki/Baz_Luhrmann" title="Baz Luhrmann">Baz Luhrmann</a>'s musical <i><a href="/wiki/Moulin_Rouge!" title="Moulin Rouge!">Moulin Rouge!</a></i> earned him the 2001 <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Institute" title="American Film Institute">American Film Institute</a>'s composer of the Year award, a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score">Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score</a> and a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a>. Armstrong was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Original Score in 2004 for the biopic <i><a href="/wiki/Ray_(film)" title="Ray (film)">Ray</a></i>. His other feature film scoring credits include <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Actually" title="Love Actually">Love Actually</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Stone" title="Oliver Stone">Oliver Stone</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Center_(film)" title="World Trade Center (film)">World Trade Center</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth:_The_Golden_Age" title="Elizabeth: The Golden Age">Elizabeth: The Golden Age</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(film)" title="The Incredible Hulk (film)">The Incredible Hulk</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Career">Career</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craig_Armstrong_(composer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Career">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Armstrong studied <a href="/wiki/Musical_composition" title="Musical composition">musical composition</a>, violin and piano at the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Academy_of_Music" title="Royal Academy of Music">Royal Academy of Music</a> from 1977 to 1981, where he was awarded the Charles Lucas prize and the Harvey Lohr scholarship for composition. He was also awarded the FTCL Fellowship in composition, and won the GLAA Young Jazz Musician of the Year in 1982. Upon completing his studies, Armstrong served as music and dance specialist at the <a href="/wiki/Strathclyde_Regional_Council" class="mw-redirect" title="Strathclyde Regional Council">Strathclyde Regional Council</a> in 1984. 1985 Armstrong toured with <a href="/wiki/Midge_Ure" title="Midge Ure">Midge Ure</a> on his Gift World Tour. Between 1994–2002 he was commissioned by the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Shakespeare_Company" title="Royal Shakespeare Company">Royal Shakespeare Company</a> to write music for <i><a href="/wiki/The_Broken_Heart" title="The Broken Heart">The Broken Heart</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Tempest" title="The Tempest">The Tempest</a></i>, both directed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Boyd_(director)" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael Boyd (director)">Michael Boyd</a>.
</p><p>He sees no difference in credibility between popular and classical forms<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and this respect for the pop genre was cemented in 1994 with a one-off collaboration with the Bristol band <a href="/wiki/Massive_Attack" title="Massive Attack">Massive Attack</a> on their album <a href="/wiki/Protection_(Massive_Attack_album)" title="Protection (Massive Attack album)"><i>Protection</i></a> after which the band formed their <i>Melankolic</i> <a href="/wiki/Vanity_label" title="Vanity label">vanity record label</a> releasing his first two solo albums.
</p><p>In 2001 Armstrong received <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a>, <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Globe">Golden Globe</a>, and <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Institute" title="American Film Institute">American Film Institute</a> Award, as well as World Soundtrack Award and a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Satellite_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Golden Satellite Award">Golden Satellite Award</a> for <i><a href="/wiki/Moulin_Rouge!" title="Moulin Rouge!">Moulin Rouge!</a></i>. In 2002 he wrote the meditative piece <i>Visconti</i>, commissioned by <a href="/wiki/Barbican_Centre" title="Barbican Centre">Barbican Centre</a> Elektronika festival for the <a href="/wiki/London_Sinfonietta" title="London Sinfonietta">London Sinfonietta</a>. In 2004 Armstrong collaborated with visual artists <a href="/wiki/Dalziel_%2B_Scullion" title="Dalziel + Scullion">Dalziel + Scullion</a> on <i>One Minute</i> to celebrate the opening of Perth's Horsecross Hall. In 2005 he received a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Score Soundtrack album for the film <a href="/wiki/Ray_(film)" title="Ray (film)">Ray</a>, directed by Taylor Hackford. In 2008 his first classical release, Memory Takes My Hand, featuring a violin concerto for <a href="/wiki/Clio_Gould" title="Clio Gould">Clio Gould</a>, was released on <a href="/wiki/EMI_Classics" title="EMI Classics">EMI Classics</a>. Armstrong has had many collaborations including recording and performing the album Dolls with the Berlin laptop artist AGF and Vladislav Delay.  He has worked with a wide variety of more well-known musical artists, including <a href="/wiki/U2" title="U2">U2</a>, <a href="/wiki/Madonna_(entertainer)" title="Madonna (entertainer)">Madonna</a>, <a href="/wiki/Texas_(band)" title="Texas (band)">Texas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Luciano_Pavarotti" title="Luciano Pavarotti">Luciano Pavarotti</a>.
</p><p>Armstrong has written several classical commissions for the <a href="/wiki/Royal_Scottish_National_Orchestra" title="Royal Scottish National Orchestra">Royal Scottish National Orchestra</a>, the <a href="/wiki/London_Sinfonietta" title="London Sinfonietta">London Sinfonietta</a>, the Hebrides Ensemble and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Ensemble" title="Scottish Ensemble">Scottish Ensemble</a>. In 2006 Armstrong collaborated with the visual artists <a href="/wiki/Dalziel_%2B_Scullion" title="Dalziel + Scullion">Dalziel + Scullion</a> for the reopening of the <a href="/wiki/Kelvingrove_Art_Gallery" class="mw-redirect" title="Kelvingrove Art Gallery">Kelvingrove Art Gallery</a> in Glasgow with a joint exhibition called Once. In 2007 Armstrong's first opera was premiered as part of the Scottish Opera, titled '5:15  – Opera's made in Scotland', a 15min opera with a libretto by <a href="/wiki/Ian_Rankin" title="Ian Rankin">Ian Rankin</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Film_scores">Film scores</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craig_Armstrong_(composer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Film scores">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>His score to <a href="/wiki/William_Shakespeare" title="William Shakespeare">William Shakespeare</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Romeo_%2B_Juliet" title="Romeo + Juliet">Romeo + Juliet</a></i> (with Baz Luhrmann) earned him a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> for Achievement in Film Music and an <a href="/wiki/Ivor_Novello" title="Ivor Novello">Ivor Novello</a>.  Armstrong's score for <a href="/wiki/Baz_Luhrmann" title="Baz Luhrmann">Baz Luhrmann</a>'s groundbreaking musical <i><a href="/wiki/Moulin_Rouge!" title="Moulin Rouge!">Moulin Rouge!</a></i> earned him AFI's Composer of the Year award, a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score">Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score</a> of the Year and a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA" class="mw-redirect" title="BAFTA">BAFTA</a> for Achievement in Film Music.  His score for Phillip Noyce's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Quiet_American" title="The Quiet American">The Quiet American</a></i> garnered him the <a href="/wiki/Ivor_Novello_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivor Novello Award">Ivor Novello Award</a> for Best Original Film Score.  His other feature film scoring credits include the <a href="/wiki/Oliver_Stone" title="Oliver Stone">Oliver Stone</a> drama <i><a href="/wiki/World_Trade_Center_(film)" title="World Trade Center (film)">World Trade Center</a></i>, the Oscar-winning bio-pic <i><a href="/wiki/Ray_(film)" title="Ray (film)">Ray</a></i> for which Armstrong was awarded a <a href="/wiki/Grammy_Award" title="Grammy Award">Grammy Award</a> for Best Original Score and the worldwide ensemble comedy smash <i><a href="/wiki/Love_Actually" title="Love Actually">Love Actually</a></i>. His scores can also be heard in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Magdalene_Sisters" title="The Magdalene Sisters">The Magdalene Sisters</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Kiss_of_the_Dragon" title="Kiss of the Dragon">Kiss of the Dragon</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Bone_Collector" title="The Bone Collector">The Bone Collector</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Clearing_(film)" title="The Clearing (film)">The Clearing</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Best_Laid_Plans_(1999_film)" title="Best Laid Plans (1999 film)">Best Laid Plans</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Orphans_(1998_film)" title="Orphans (1998 film)">Orphans</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Elizabeth:_The_Golden_Age" title="Elizabeth: The Golden Age">Elizabeth: The Golden Age</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Incredible_Hulk_(film)" title="The Incredible Hulk (film)">The Incredible Hulk</a></i>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Escape">Escape</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Craig_Armstrong_(composer)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Escape">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>One of the most popular tracks composed by Armstrong is "Escape" from <i><a href="/wiki/Plunkett_%26_Macleane" title="Plunkett &amp; Macleane">Plunkett &amp; Macleane</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-APR_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-APR-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The composition was used in the pilot of the 2000 science fiction series <a href="/wiki/Dark_Angel_(2000_TV_series)" title="Dark Angel (2000 TV series)">Dark Angel</a>, as well as having been used in various <a href="/wiki/Film_trailers" class="mw-redirect" title="Film trailers">film trailers</a> and sports broadcasts due to the emotional buildup caused by its rising <a href="/wiki/Crescendo" class="mw-redirect" title="Crescendo">crescendo</a> and choir. 
</p><p>It was used in the trailer for the <a href="/wiki/BBC" title="BBC">BBC</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Euro_2012" class="mw-redirect" title="Euro 2012">Euro 2012</a> coverage, and has often been used in the BBC's <i><a href="/wiki/Top_Gear_(current_format)" class="mw-redirect" title="Top Gear (current format)">Top Gear</a></i> series at the climax of the season finale "epic challenges".  Armstrong lists the songs which <i>Top Gear</i> has used on his web site.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> "Escape" has been used in a number of action film trailers, including <i><a href="/wiki/Spider-Man_2" title="Spider-Man 2">Spider-Man 2</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Daredevil_(film)" title="Daredevil (film)">Daredevil</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>"Escape" has also been used as entrance music by various sports teams, including the <a href="/wiki/Professional_wrestling" title="Professional wrestling">professional wrestling</a> <a href="/wiki/List_of_professional_wrestling_terms#S" class="mw-redirect" title="List of professional wrestling terms">stable</a> Team Vision, <a href="/wiki/Premier_League" title="Premier League">Premier League</a> football team <a href="/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C." title="Aston Villa F.C.">Aston Villa</a> when playing at their home ground <a href="/wiki/Villa_Park" title="Villa Park">Villa Park</a>, as well as fellow Premier League team <a href="/wiki/Wigan_Athletic" class="mw-redirect" title="Wigan Athletic">Wigan Athletic</a> when at the <a href="/wiki/DW_Stadium" title="DW Stadium">DW Stadium</a>. It has also been used as entrance music for the <a href="/wiki/England_national_rugby_union_team" title="England national rugby union team">England rugby union team</a> at <a href="/wiki/Twickenham_stadium" class="mw-redirect" title="Twickenham stadium">Twickenham stadium</a>, Premiership rugby union team Leeds Carnegie before home games, and Kent County Cricket Club in <a href="/wiki/Twenty20" title="Twenty20">Twenty20</a> matches.
</p>
