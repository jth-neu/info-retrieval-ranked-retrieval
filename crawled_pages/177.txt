<p><b>Oscar Marius Nierstrasz</b>, born  <span style="display:none">(<span class="bday">1957-10-15</span>)</span>October 15, 1957, is a Professor at the Computer Science Institute (IAM) at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Berne" class="mw-redirect" title="University of Berne">University of Berne</a>, and a specialist in <a href="/wiki/Software_engineering" title="Software engineering">software engineering</a> and <a href="/wiki/Programming_languages" class="mw-redirect" title="Programming languages">programming languages</a>.  He is active in the field of
</p>
<ul><li>programming languages and mechanisms to support the flexible composition of high-level, component-based abstractions, and</li>
<li>tools and environments to support the understanding, analysis and transformation of software systems to more flexible, component-based designs.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p>He has led the Software Composition Group at the University of Bern since 1994 to date (December 2011).<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/w/index.php?title=CyberChair&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="CyberChair (page does not exist)">CyberChair</a>, an Online Submission and Reviewing System, is based on Oscar Nierstrasz's publication called <i>Identify the champion</i>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> where he described the peer review process for contributions to scientific conferences using an organizational <a href="/wiki/Pattern_language" title="Pattern language">pattern language</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nierstrasz co-authored several books such as <i>Object-Oriented Reengineering Patterns</i><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and <i>Pharo by Example</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> He was editor of the <i><a href="/wiki/Journal_of_Object_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Journal of Object Technology">Journal of Object Technology</a></i> from 2010 to 2013, succeeding the founding editor, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Richard_Wiener&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Richard Wiener (page does not exist)">Richard Wiener</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nierstrasz won the Senior <a href="/wiki/Dahl%E2%80%93Nygaard_Prize" title="Dahl–Nygaard Prize">Dahl–Nygaard_Prize</a> in 2013.
</p>
