<p>The <b>First Lord of the Treasury</b> is the head of the <a href="/wiki/Government_agency" title="Government agency">commission</a> exercising the ancient office of <a href="/wiki/Lord_High_Treasurer" title="Lord High Treasurer">Lord High Treasurer</a> in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, and is now always also the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister</a>. This office is not equivalent to the usual position of the "<a href="/wiki/Finance_minister" title="Finance minister">Treasurer</a>" in other governments; the closer equivalent of a Treasurer in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a>, who is the Second Lord of the Treasury.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Lords_of_the_Treasury">Lords of the Treasury</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Lord_of_the_Treasury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Lords of the Treasury">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of the beginning of the 17th century, the running of <a href="/wiki/HM_Treasury" title="HM Treasury">the Treasury</a> was frequently entrusted to a commission, rather than to a single individual. Since 1714, it has permanently been in commission. The commissioners have always since that date been referred to as <a href="/wiki/Lords_Commissioners_of_the_Treasury" title="Lords Commissioners of the Treasury">Lords Commissioners of the Treasury</a>, and adopted <a href="/wiki/Ordinal_number_(linguistics)" title="Ordinal number (linguistics)">ordinal numbers</a> to describe their seniority. Eventually in the middle of the same century, the First Lord of the Treasury came to be seen as the natural head of the overall ministry running the country, and, as of the time of <a href="/wiki/Robert_Walpole" title="Robert Walpole">Robert Walpole</a> (<a href="/wiki/Whigs_(British_political_party)" title="Whigs (British political party)">Whig</a>), began to be known, unofficially, as the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Prime Minister of the United Kingdom">Prime Minister</a>.  The term Prime Minister was initially, but decreasingly, used as a term of derogation: it was first used officially in a royal warrant only in 1905. <a href="/wiki/William_Pitt_the_Younger" title="William Pitt the Younger">William Pitt the Younger</a> once opined that the Prime Minister "ought to be the person at the head of the finances."<sup id="cite_ref-historyandpolicy_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-historyandpolicy-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to 1841 the First Lord of the Treasury also held the office of <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_the_Exchequer" title="Chancellor of the Exchequer">Chancellor of the Exchequer</a> unless he was a peer and thus barred from that office; in this case, the <a href="/wiki/Second_Lord_of_the_Treasury" class="mw-redirect" title="Second Lord of the Treasury">Second Lord of the Treasury</a> usually served as Chancellor. As of 1841, the Chancellor of the Exchequer has always been Second Lord of the Treasury when he was not also Prime Minister. By convention, the other Lords Commissioners of the Treasury are also Government <a href="/wiki/Whip_(politics)" title="Whip (politics)">Whips</a> in the <a href="/wiki/House_of_Commons_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="House of Commons of the United Kingdom">House of Commons</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Official_residence">Official residence</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=First_Lord_of_the_Treasury&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Official residence">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tleft"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:152px;"><a href="/wiki/File:10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg/150px-10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg" width="150" height="87" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg/225px-10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg/300px-10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg 2x" data-file-width="3000" data-file-height="1738" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:10_Downing_Street._MOD_45155532.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/10_Downing_Street" title="10 Downing Street">10 Downing Street</a> in <a href="/wiki/Whitehall" title="Whitehall">Whitehall</a> is the official residence of the First Lord of the Treasury and headquarters of <a href="/wiki/Her_Majesty%27s_Government" class="mw-redirect" title="Her Majesty&#39;s Government">Her Majesty's Government</a>.</div></div></div>
<p><a href="/wiki/10_Downing_Street" title="10 Downing Street">10 Downing Street</a> is the official residence of the First Lord of the Treasury, and not of the Prime Minister.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Chequers" title="Chequers">Chequers</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Country_house" class="mw-redirect" title="Country house">country house</a> in <a href="/wiki/Buckinghamshire" title="Buckinghamshire">Buckinghamshire</a>, is the official country residence of the Prime Minister, used as a weekend and holiday home, although the residence is also used by other senior members of government.
</p>
