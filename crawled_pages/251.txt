<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Veronica (search engine)</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Veronica</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_(computing)" title="Search engine (computing)">search engine</a> system for the <a href="/wiki/Gopher_(protocol)" title="Gopher (protocol)">Gopher protocol</a>, released in November 1992<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> by Steven Foster and Fred Barrie at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Nevada,_Reno" title="University of Nevada, Reno">University of Nevada, Reno</a>.
</p><p>During its existence, Veronica was a constantly updated database of the names of almost every menu item on thousands of Gopher servers. The Veronica database could be searched from most major Gopher menus. Although the original Veronica database is no longer accessible,<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (May 2018)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> various local Veronica installations and at least one complete rewrite ("Veronica-2") still exist.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag needs further explanation. (January 2017)">further explanation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup><sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Please_clarify" title="Wikipedia:Please clarify"><span title="The text near this tag may need clarification or removal of jargon. (January 2017)">clarification needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Naming">Naming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Veronica_(search_engine)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Naming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The search engine was named after the character <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Lodge" title="Veronica Lodge">Veronica Lodge</a> from <a href="/wiki/Archie_Comics" title="Archie Comics">Archie Comics</a>, an intentional analogy with the naming of the <a href="/wiki/Archie_search_engine" title="Archie search engine">Archie search engine</a>, a search engine for <a href="/wiki/File_Transfer_Protocol" title="File Transfer Protocol">FTP servers</a>.  A <a href="/wiki/Backronym" title="Backronym">backronym</a> for Veronica is "Very Easy Rodent-Oriented Net-wide Index to Computer Archives".<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
