<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Barry Schwartz (technologist)</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the technologist. For other people with this name, see <a href="/wiki/Barry_Schwartz_(disambiguation)" class="mw-redirect mw-disambig" title="Barry Schwartz (disambiguation)">Barry Schwartz</a>.</div>
<p class="mw-empty-elt">
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:282px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Rand-fishkin.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Rand-fishkin.jpg/280px-Rand-fishkin.jpg" width="280" height="210" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Rand-fishkin.jpg/420px-Rand-fishkin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Rand-fishkin.jpg/560px-Rand-fishkin.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Rand-fishkin.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Barry Schwartz (middle) and Chris Boggs (left) interview Rand Fishkin (right) on Webmaster Radio</div></div></div> <p><b>Barry Schwartz</b> (born 1980) is a blogger who writes about <a href="/wiki/Search_engines" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engines">search engines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_marketing" title="Search engine marketing">search engine marketing</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>  Schwartz is the founder<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and currently the editor of <a href="/wiki/Search_Engine_Roundtable" title="Search Engine Roundtable">Search Engine Roundtable</a>, an online news site covering the <a href="/wiki/Search_engines" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engines">search engines</a> and <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_marketing" title="Search engine marketing">search engine marketing</a>.  He also is the CEO &amp; President of RustyBrick, Inc., a New York-based web development company, and a news editor at <a href="/wiki/Search_Engine_Land" class="mw-redirect" title="Search Engine Land">Search Engine Land</a>, a search engine news site founded by <a href="/wiki/Danny_Sullivan_(technologist)" title="Danny Sullivan (technologist)">Danny Sullivan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Previously, Schwartz was a writer for Search Engine Watch.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>  He also <a href="/wiki/Forum_moderator" class="mw-redirect" title="Forum moderator">moderates</a> online and offline panels at <a href="/wiki/Search_Engine_Watch" title="Search Engine Watch">Search Engine Watch</a>, Cre8asite Forums and WebmasterWorld's PubCon.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>He has been interviewed by NBC Nightly News and USA Today about Search Marketing,<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and has been quoted by news organizations reporting on the Internet Marketing space.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  He is also the organizer of the annual Search Marketing Expo (SMX) in Israel,<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and is a member of the Internet Marketers – New York.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> He also currently hosts the Search Pulse <a href="/wiki/Podcast" title="Podcast">podcast</a> on WebmasterRadio.FM.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Biography">Biography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Barry_Schwartz_(technologist)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Biography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Schwartz is a graduate of <a href="/wiki/Baruch_College" title="Baruch College">Baruch College</a>, <a href="/wiki/City_University_of_New_York" title="City University of New York">City University of New York</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and lives in Rockland County, New York. He is married and has three daughters and two sons.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
