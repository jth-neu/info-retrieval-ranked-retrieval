<p><b>wikiHow</b> is an online <a href="/wiki/Wiki" title="Wiki">wiki</a>-style community consisting of an extensive database of <a href="/wiki/How-to" title="How-to">how-to</a> guides. Founded in 2005 by Internet entrepreneur <a href="/wiki/Jack_Herrick" title="Jack Herrick">Jack Herrick</a>, the website aims to create the world’s most helpful how-to instructions to enable everyone in the world to learn how to do anything.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>wikiHow is a <a href="/wiki/Hybrid_organization" title="Hybrid organization">hybrid organization</a>, a for-profit company run for a social mission.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WikiHow&#39;s_Recipe_for_Success_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WikiHow&#39;s_Recipe_for_Success-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> wikiHow is an <a href="/wiki/Open_source" class="mw-redirect" title="Open source">open source</a> and <a href="/wiki/Open_content" title="Open content">open content</a> project.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> The modified <a href="/wiki/MediaWiki" title="MediaWiki">MediaWiki</a> software is freely released<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> and the content is released under a <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">Creative Commons</a> (BY-NC-SA) license.<sup id="cite_ref-wikiHow_Reaches_25,000_Articles_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikiHow_Reaches_25,000_Articles-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2005, wikiHow had over 35.5 million unique visitors.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In August 2017, wikiHow contains more than 190,000 free how-to articles and over 1.6 million registered users. On April 11, 2010, a wikiHow article titled "How to Lose Weight Fast" reached 5 million page views, a first for the site. "How to Take a Screenshot in Microsoft Windows" is the site's most popular article.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> According to wikiHow, four babies have been born in emergency situations referencing instruction from wikiHow articles.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>wikiHow was founded by <a href="/wiki/Jack_Herrick" title="Jack Herrick">Jack Herrick</a> on January 15, 2005, with the goal of creating an extensive how-to manual with accurate, up-to-date instructions in multiple languages.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The day of January 15 was intentionally selected as the launch date in order to honor <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>, which was launched four years earlier on January 15, 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> Herrick drew inspiration to start wikiHow because he and <a href="/wiki/Josh_Hannah" title="Josh Hannah">Josh Hannah</a> had previously purchased another how-to website, <a href="/wiki/EHow" title="EHow">eHow</a> in 2004. After running eHow, Herrick concluded eHow’s business model prevented it from creating the extensive, high quality how-to manual he desired to create.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Herrick and Hannah sold eHow in 2006, allowing Herrick to focus on wikiHow full-time.<sup id="cite_ref-xconomy.com_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xconomy.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2006, the non-profit foundation <a href="/wiki/One_Laptop_per_Child" title="One Laptop per Child">One Laptop per Child</a> selected wikiHow as one of the content sources for the educational laptops it distributed around the world.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> In 2007, its 25,000th article was published on September 21, 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-wikiHow_Reaches_25,000_Articles_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-wikiHow_Reaches_25,000_Articles-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, the website surpassed 20 million monthly visitors and completed a redesign.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> In 2014, <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a> selected wikiHow as one of the launch partners for <a href="/wiki/Google_Contributor" title="Google Contributor">Google Contributor</a>, an ad-free internet product.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Guidecentral_acquisition">Guidecentral acquisition</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Guidecentral acquisition">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It was announced on March 24, 2016 that wikiHow acquired Guidecentral,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> a website focused on providing information about "hands on" projects.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> The terms of the acquisition have not been released, although Guidecentral raised over $1 million from investors including NXTP Labs, Enterprise Ireland and South Ventures prior to the acquisition.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Operations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>wikiHow provides how-to content on the internet by allowing anyone to edit pages.<sup id="cite_ref-South_China_Morning_Post_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-South_China_Morning_Post-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> As of February 2015, wikiHow contains over 180,000 how-to articles. Most how-to articles follow a similar format with steps, tips, warnings, a listing of things you'll need, and are complemented with images to help a reader learn how to complete a task.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>wikiHow uses the wiki method of continuous improvement, allowing editors to add, delete, or otherwise modify content. Once an article is created, community members <a href="/wiki/Collaboration" title="Collaboration">collaborate</a> on it to improve its quality. Each edit is scrutinized during a process called <a href="/wiki/Recent_Changes" class="mw-redirect" title="Recent Changes">Recent Changes</a> Patrol where volunteers review the content according to wikiHow’s standards, discarding bad edits, like vandalism and test edits, and keeping improvements.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The central hub for using contribution tools is the Community Dashboard,<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> which displays several dynamic applets that link users to different editing tools, such as the Spell Checker, in which articles are proofread for spelling errors; the Categorizer, in which articles are assigned categories according to their topic; and the Cleanup Greenhouse, in which low-quality articles are copyedited and rewritten for style and format.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Quality_review_process">Quality review process</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Quality review process">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>By default, newly created articles are <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_indexing" title="Search engine indexing">de-indexed from search engines</a>; the article text is blurred and a notice indicating that the page is invisible to readers is shown, but logged in users can dismiss the warning in order to view the contents of the article. A system called New Article Boost allows certain users with the "New Article Booster" right to review these articles, and bring them up to standards if possible.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Articles that meet these standards are "promoted", which removes the blurring effect as well as the notice, and makes the article publicly visible and searchable. If a New Article Booster finds a newly-written high-quality article, they can mark it as a Rising Star.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> By default, RS-marked articles get promoted and placed on a main page for an unspecified period of time.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Oppositely, articles below standards and that will take too much work to "get ready for readers" are "demoted", which removes them from the New Article Boost list and retains the blurring and notice, and moves the page to the "Articles in Quality Review" category.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Articles with content that goes against the site's deletion policy are also demoted (such as articles centered around joke, sexually explicit, or hate-based topics, as well as severely inaccurate or incomplete instructions).<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Titles that are identified as duplicates per wikiHow's Merge Policy are merged.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG/200px-WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG" width="200" height="145" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG/300px-WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG/400px-WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG 2x" data-file-width="2490" data-file-height="1800" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:WomenonWikiHow2-WM2012.JPG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Workshop on women on wikiHow at <a href="/wiki/Wikimania_2012" class="mw-redirect" title="Wikimania 2012">Wikimania 2012</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Like many other wikis, registration is optional and privileges such as creating and editing articles are still available to those without an account. wikiHow complies with the <a href="/wiki/Children%27s_Online_Privacy_Protection_Act" title="Children&#39;s Online Privacy Protection Act">Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA)</a> and users are required to be at least 13 years old to register an account. COPPA is regularly enforced and site administrators are expected to block anyone confirmed to be underage, though editing privileges may be restored if the company receives a consent form signed by the user's parents or legal guardian.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> Over 1 million people have created accounts and over 13,000 people edit wikiHow a month.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> This community of volunteer contributors call themselves "wikiHowians". The most active, experienced, and trusted wikiHowians may gain additional editing privileges which help them administer and protect the website. New Article Boost rights are granted to users who have done at least 300 edits and pass a test (containing three different parts) demonstrating they understand wikiHow policies.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> People with these rights gain access to a special tool that allows them to edit recently written articles and ensure that their quality meets wikiHow’s standards before the articles are publicly visible to all readers. The most experienced users with levelheadedness and good judgment can be considered for adminship, which grants them additional powers to protect and improve wikiHow through various maintenance tools.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_engagement">Community engagement</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Community engagement">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>At least once a year, wikiHow organizes conferences where a small group of active community members are invited to gather offline for an extended weekend to get to know one another, in different cities each time. These conferences are known as "meetups".<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>wikiHow’s headquarters are located in a house in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto</a>, <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>, dubbed the "wikiHaus". The staff team consists of a small group of employees ranging in specialties from engineering to marketing to community management.<sup id="cite_ref-WikiHow&#39;s_Recipe_for_Success_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WikiHow&#39;s_Recipe_for_Success-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-xconomy.com_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-xconomy.com-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Business_model">Business model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Business model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The site's initial start-up costs were, to some extent, financed from Herrick's sale of eHow. It is now funded from advertising on its pages.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> It does not seek contributions, and it is run as a "hybrid organization" – a "for-profit company focused on creating a global public good in accordance with our mission".<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Licensing">Licensing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Licensing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>wikiHow's content is published under the <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">Creative Commons</a> Attribution-Noncommercial-Share Alike 3.0 (by-nc-sa 3.0) license, which means that the content can be modified and reused for non-commercial purposes as long as the original authors are attributed and the license is not substantially changed. The authors retain full copyright to their content and may publish it elsewhere under different licenses. They grant wikiHow an irrevocable license to use the material for any purpose.<sup id="cite_ref-terms_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-terms-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Opt-out_ads">Opt-out ads</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Opt-out ads">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>wikiHow allows readers to control whether <a href="/wiki/Internet_advertising" class="mw-redirect" title="Internet advertising">advertising</a> appears alongside content. Those who are registered and logged in do not see ads<sup id="cite_ref-hide_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hide-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> unless it is an external video provider.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2014, wikiHow was announced to be a participant in <a href="/wiki/Google_Contributor" title="Google Contributor">Google Contributor</a>, a service that allows website users to make a monthly donation in support of their favorite websites in order to not be shown advertising.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=WikiHow&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>wikiHow has won multiple awards, including a <a href="/wiki/Webby_Award" title="Webby Award">Webby Award</a> for Community in 2009<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> and the Co-Creation award in the Open Innovation competition, organized by <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Nesta_(charity)" title="Nesta (charity)">Nesta</a>, in 2010.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> In addition, <a href="/wiki/Mashable" title="Mashable">Mashable</a> selected wikiHow as the runner-up for best wiki in the Open Web Awards in 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A <a href="/wiki/PBS" title="PBS">PBS</a> journalist reported that the "wikiHow <a href="/wiki/Mobile_app" title="Mobile app">app</a> has an excellent set of articles to help you in just about any situation, from helping someone who is <a href="/wiki/Choking" title="Choking">choking</a> to handling vehicle emergencies, to <a href="/wiki/Natural_disaster" title="Natural disaster">natural disasters</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_York_Times" title="The New York Times">The New York Times</a></i> reported: "Type in a few key words about the problem into the app’s Search page and the guide will return some advice. Its information pages are clear and well laid out. They begin with an introductory description, then offer a list of steps to follow. The app displays the necessary tools and items, and includes tips and warnings."<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Lifehacker" title="Lifehacker">Lifehacker</a> has described wikiHow as the "ever handy guide site."<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> wikiHow has been positively described in many other media sources, including ones as diverse as <i><a href="/wiki/Inc._(magazine)" title="Inc. (magazine)">Inc. Magazine</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-WikiHow&#39;s_Recipe_for_Success_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WikiHow&#39;s_Recipe_for_Success-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Cosmopolitan_(magazine)" title="Cosmopolitan (magazine)">Cosmopolitan</a></i>,<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/TechRepublic" title="TechRepublic">TechRepublic</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Cond%C3%A9_Nast_Traveler" title="Condé Nast Traveler">Condé Nast Traveler</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup> and <i><a href="/wiki/PC_Magazine" title="PC Magazine">PC Magazine</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>wikiHow has also been the target of satire and criticism due to its notable abundance of arguably eccentric articles. For example, <a href="/wiki/American_Public_Radio" class="mw-redirect" title="American Public Radio">American Public Radio</a> show Wits has a segment called "wikiHow theater" where actors read obvious or ludicrous wikiHow topics, such as "How to Make People Respect Your Pet", for comic effect.<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Two accomplished poets published a book called, "How To Feel Confident with Your Special Talents", in which each poem title is taken directly from a wikiHow article.<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup> Vice.com parodied wikiHow's article "How to Break Up with Your Boyfriend".<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Huffington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="The Huffington Post">The Huffington Post</a></i> created a list of bizarre life skills, such as "How to React to an Ugly Baby", that "you could only learn from wikiHow".<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup> Other publishers have criticized wikiHow for hosting instructions on controversial topics of questionable social value such "how to get a thigh gap",<sup id="cite_ref-61" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> or "how to stop a wedding".<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup> Other websites have created "worst of wikiHow" lists to highlight topics that are "deranged",<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup> "brilliantly bizarre",<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup> or otherwise problematic.
</p>
