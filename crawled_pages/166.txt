<p><b>Ecosia</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Web_search_engine" title="Web search engine">web search engine</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Berlin" title="Berlin">Berlin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>, which donates 80% of its surplus income to <a href="/wiki/Nonprofit_organization" title="Nonprofit organization">non-profit</a> <a href="/wiki/Conservation_movement" title="Conservation movement">conservationist</a> organizations, with a focus on tree planting. Ecosia considers itself a "social business", is CO<sub>2</sub>-neutral<sup id="cite_ref-TC_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and claims to support full <a href="/wiki/Transparency_(social)" class="mw-redirect" title="Transparency (social)">financial transparency</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-G2_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and is certified by <a href="/wiki/B_Lab" title="B Lab">B-Lab</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Benefit_corporation" title="Benefit corporation">benefit corporation</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-BLabs_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLabs-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> According to Ecosia, over 37 million trees have been planted with its support as of 12 September 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_engine">Search engine</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecosia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Search engine">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ecosia can be searched using a web browser or a mobile app on Android and iOS devices;<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> results are powered by Microsoft <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> and Ecosia's own search algorithms.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ecosia shows <a href="/wiki/Advertising" title="Advertising">advertisements</a> next to its search results, and are paid by their partners at Bing for every click on a sponsored link for having directed users to their advertisers. Considering that not every Ecosia user clicks on an ad every time they search, Ecosia states that on average a search raises around half a Euro cent (0.005 EUR).
</p><p>Ecosia also raises funds through its second product EcoLinks, a Browser Extension that allows users to donate for free to Ecosia through their online purchases. Operators of online shops pay a commission to Ecosia (usually a rate between 2 to 10%) if a user lands on their shop through clicking on an EcoLink and purchases an item or any service.
</p><p>Ecosia supports country-based country filtering but does not support the displaying of worldwide information as of July 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecosia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In September 2009, Ecosia received $21,500 in seed capital,<sup id="cite_ref-TC_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and the search engine launched on 7 December 2009, to coincide with UN climate talks in Copenhagen.<sup id="cite_ref-TWE_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TWE-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Over time, Ecosia has donated to different tree-planting programs. Until December 2010 Ecosia’s donations went to a program by <a href="/wiki/World_Wide_Fund_for_Nature" title="World Wide Fund for Nature">WWF</a> Germany that protected the <a href="/wiki/Juruena_National_Park" title="Juruena National Park">Juruena National Park</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Amazon_basin" title="Amazon basin">Amazon basin</a>. In order to make sure the protection was kept up, the program also drew up and financed plans with timber companies and the local communities.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>Between December 2009 and August 2013, Ecosia donated to the Plant a Billion Trees program run by the Nature Conservancy, which the program aimed to restore the Brazilian Atlantic Forest by planting one billion native trees by 2015. By 2011, the search engine had raised over €250,000.<sup id="cite_ref-G2_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-G2-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 2013, about 200,000 people were using Ecosia, and 116,000 seedlings had been funded by Ecosia's donations.<sup id="cite_ref-SA_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SA-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-What_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-What-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In spring 2013 Ecosia announced an improvement of its donation model as well as the quality of the search results. However, due to technical difficulties, the company temporarily had to use a feed subject to charges. At the same time, an Ecolink partner had suddenly terminated their partnership. As a result Ecosia made an agreement with WWF whereby donations from November 2012 until February 2013 were restricted to 20,000 Euros per month. Once the financial bottleneck had been overcome, the normal donation model was immediately restored.
</p><p>With a relaunch of the search engine on December 14, 2013 came the switch to a new reforestation program by <a href="/wiki/The_Nature_Conservancy" title="The Nature Conservancy">The Nature Conservancy</a> (TNC). The switch signified a slight change of paradigm since the company was no longer protecting forested areas but rather financing a reforestation program. TNC's program "Plant a Billion Trees" aimed at reforesting the Mata Atlântica, the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (July 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2015 Ecosia began funding <a href="/wiki/Reforestation" title="Reforestation">reforestation</a> in <a href="/wiki/Burkina_Faso" title="Burkina Faso">Burkina Faso</a> as part of the <a href="/wiki/Great_Green_Wall" title="Great Green Wall">Great Green Wall</a> project, backed by the <a href="/wiki/African_Union" title="African Union">African Union</a> and the <a href="/wiki/World_Bank" title="World Bank">World Bank</a>, that aims to prevent desertification.<sup id="cite_ref-What_13-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-What-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to B-labs, as of January 2015, "In donating 80 percent of its ad revenue, the search engine has raised over $1.5 million for rainforest protection since its founding in December 2009."<sup id="cite_ref-BLabs_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BLabs-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> According to Ecosia, by 2015, the search engine had almost 2.5 million active users, and searches through it had resulted in more than 2 million trees being planted.<sup id="cite_ref-What_13-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-What-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2015, Ecosia was shortlisted for The Europas, the European Tech Startups Awards, under the category Best European Startup Aimed At Improving Society.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of 27 April 2016, Ecosia ranks in the top 2 start-up for Germany in the StartupRanking.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
Ecosia reached the milestone of 5 million trees planted on 7 September 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> 10 million on 29 July 2017 and over 5.5 million active users,<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> and 15 million on October 16 2017 and 17.5 million on 4 December 2017 and over 7 million active users on 10 December 2017. 21 million trees planted as of 24 February 2018. 30 million on 12 June 2018. 38 million trees planted on 22nd September 2018. <sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Impact">Impact</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecosia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Impact">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ecosia actively plants trees in 10 countries throughout <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Latin_America" title="Latin America">Central &amp; South America</a>, <a href="/wiki/Africa" title="Africa">Africa</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>.
</p><p>As of August 2018, Ecosia had planted over 35 million trees throughout the world.<sup id="cite_ref-TC_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TC-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> About two-thirds of these trees were planted in the previous year.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Browser_integration">Browser integration</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ecosia&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Browser integration">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>With the first release on July 21, 2017, <a href="/wiki/Brave_(web_browser)" title="Brave (web browser)">Brave</a> web browser features Ecosia as one of its default search engines.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> With the release of version 26 (on January 26, 2016), the <a href="/wiki/Pale_Moon_(web_browser)" title="Pale Moon (web browser)">Pale Moon</a> web browser adds Ecosia as one of the default search engines. As of version 8 (on February 15, 2016), Polarity web browser switched to Ecosia as its default search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Since version 44.0.2, Ecosia is the default search engine of the <a href="/wiki/Waterfox" title="Waterfox">Waterfox</a> web browser.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Since version 1.9, <a href="/wiki/Vivaldi_(web_browser)" title="Vivaldi (web browser)">Vivaldi browser</a> includes Ecosia as a default search engine option.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In March 2018, <a href="/wiki/Firefox" title="Firefox">Firefox</a> 59.0 added Ecosia as a search engine option for the German version.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
