<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">World Prison Brief</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"><span class="mw-redirectedfrom">  (Redirected from <a href="/w/index.php?title=International_Centre_for_Prison_Studies&amp;redirect=no" class="mw-redirect" title="International Centre for Prison Studies">International Centre for Prison Studies</a>)</span></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>The World Prison Brief at PrisonStudies.org is an online database providing free access to information on prison systems around the world. It is now hosted by the <b>Institute For Criminal Policy Research</b> (<b>ICPR</b>), <a href="/wiki/Birkbeck_College" class="mw-redirect" title="Birkbeck College">Birkbeck College</a>, <a href="/wiki/University_of_London" title="University of London">University of London</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-WPB-home_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WPB-home-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-WPB-about_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WPB-about-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It was previously hosted by the <b>International Centre for Prison Studies</b> (<b>ICPS</b>). It was a research centre at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Essex" title="University of Essex">University of Essex</a>. It was launched at the House of Lords on 4 April 2011. Between 1997 and 2010 ICPS was based in <a href="/wiki/King%27s_College_London" title="King&#39;s College London">King's College London</a> and was launched formally by <a href="/wiki/Home_Secretary" title="Home Secretary">Home Secretary</a> <a href="/wiki/Jack_Straw" title="Jack Straw">Jack Straw</a> in October 1997. In July 2010 the International Centre for Prison Studies incorporated and registered as a charity with the Charities Commission of England and Wales.  From the outset the Centre was independent of governmental and intergovernmental agencies, although it would work closely with them. 
</p><p>The Centre is self-funded and a number of charitable trusts gave generous grants which allowed the Centre to start work. The centre seeks to assist governments and other relevant agencies to develop appropriate policies on prisons and the use of imprisonment. It carries out its work on a project or consultancy basis for international agencies, governmental and non-governmental organisations.
</p><p>It aims to make the results of its academic research and projects widely available to groups and individuals, both nationally and internationally, who might not normally use such work. These include policy makers, practitioners and administrators, the media and the general public.
</p>
