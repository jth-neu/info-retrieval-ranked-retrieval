<p><b>Pakistan</b><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> (<a href="/wiki/Bengali_language" title="Bengali language">Bengali</a>: <span lang="bn">পাকিস্তান অধিরাজ্য</span> <i><i lang="bn-Latn" title="Bengali-language transliteration">pakistan ôdhirajyô</i></i>; <a href="/wiki/Urdu_language" class="mw-redirect" title="Urdu language">Urdu</a>: <span lang="ur" dir="rtl"><span class="Nastaliq" dir="rtl" title="Nastaliq" style="font-family: &#39;Jameel Noori Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Urdu Typesetting&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu&#39;, &#39;Noto Nastaliq Urdu Draft&#39;, &#39;Hussaini Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;AlQalam Taj Nastaleeq&#39;, IranNastaliq, &#39;Awami Nastaliq&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta3&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta2&#39;, &#39;Awami Nastaliq Beta1&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;Nafees Nastaleeq v1.01&#39;, &#39;Pak Nastaleeq&#39;, &#39;PDMS_Jauhar&#39;, &#39;Alvi Lahori Nastaleeq&#39;; font-size: 125%; font-style: normal;">مملکتِ پاکستان</span>&#x202c;</span>&#8206; <i><i lang="sa-Latn" title="International Alphabet of Sanskrit transliteration">mumlikāt-ē pākistān</i></i>), also called the <b>Dominion of Pakistan</b>, was an independent federal <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">dominion</a> in South Asia that was established in 1947 as a result of the <a href="/wiki/Pakistan_movement" class="mw-redirect" title="Pakistan movement">Pakistan movement</a>, followed by the simultaneous <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Partition of British India">partition of British India</a> to create a new country called Pakistan. The dominion, which included much of modern-day <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>, was conceived under the <a href="/wiki/Two-nation_theory" title="Two-nation theory">two-nation theory</a> as an independent country composed of the Muslim-majority areas of the former <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a>.
</p><p>To begin with, it did not include the <a href="/wiki/Princely_states_of_Pakistan" title="Princely states of Pakistan">princely states of Pakistan</a>, which acceded slowly between 1947 and 1948. Dominion status ended in 1956 with the creation of the Islamic Republic of Pakistan, which was administratively split into <a href="/wiki/West_Pakistan" title="West Pakistan">West Pakistan</a> and <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a>. In 1971 <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War" title="Bangladesh Liberation War">East Pakistan seceded from the union</a> to become Bangladesh.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Partition_of_British_India">Partition of British India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_of_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Partition of British India">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Section 1 of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Independence_Act_1947" title="Indian Independence Act 1947">Indian Independence Act 1947</a> provided that from "the fifteenth day of August, nineteen hundred and forty-seven, two independent dominions shall be set up in India, to be known respectively as <i>India</i> and <i>Pakistan</i>." The <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_India" title="Dominion of India">Dominion of India</a> held seventy-five percent of the territory and eighty percent of the population of British India and was treated by the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations" title="United Nations">United Nations</a> as the successor state to the former <a href="/wiki/British_India" class="mw-redirect" title="British India">British India</a>. As it was already a member of the United Nations, India continued to hold its seat there and did not apply for a new membership. However, Pakistan needed to apply to join. It was admitted as a UN member on 30 September 1947, a few weeks after its independence.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The British monarch became head of state of the new dominion, with Pakistan sharing a king with the United Kingdom and the other <a href="/wiki/Dominion" title="Dominion">Dominions</a> of the British Commonwealth, but the monarch's constitutional roles were delegated to the <a href="/wiki/Governor-General_of_Pakistan" title="Governor-General of Pakistan">Governor-General of Pakistan</a>, and most real powers resided with the new government headed by <a href="/wiki/Jinnah" class="mw-redirect" title="Jinnah">Jinnah</a>.
</p><p>Before August 1947, about half of the area of present-day Pakistan was part of the <a href="/wiki/Presidencies_and_provinces_of_British_India" title="Presidencies and provinces of British India">Presidencies and provinces of British India</a>, in which the agents of the <a href="/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Monarchy of the United Kingdom">sovereign</a> as <a href="/wiki/Emperor_of_India" title="Emperor of India">Emperor of India</a> had full authority, while the remainder was a series of <a href="/wiki/Princely_states_of_Pakistan" title="Princely states of Pakistan">princely states</a> in <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary_alliance" title="Subsidiary alliance">subsidiary alliances</a> with the British, enjoying internal self-government. The British abandoned these alliances in August 1947, leaving the states entirely independent, and between 1947 and 1948 the states all acceded to Pakistan, while retaining internal self-government for several years.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Territory">Territory</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_of_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Territory">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Partition_of_India" title="Partition of India">Partition of India</a></div>
<p>The dominion began as a federation of five provinces: <a href="/wiki/East_Bengal" title="East Bengal">East Bengal</a> (later to become <a href="/wiki/Bangladesh" title="Bangladesh">Bangladesh</a>), <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_Pakistan" title="Punjab, Pakistan">West Punjab</a>, <a href="/wiki/Balochistan_(Pakistan)" class="mw-redirect" title="Balochistan (Pakistan)">Balochistan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Sindh" title="Sindh">Sindh</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Khyber_Pakhtunkhwa" title="Khyber Pakhtunkhwa">North-West Frontier Province</a> (NWFP).  Each province had its own governor, who was appointed by the Governor-General of Pakistan. In addition, over the following year the princely states of Pakistan, which covered a significant area of West Pakistan, acceded to Pakistan. They included <a href="/wiki/Bahawalpur_(princely_state)" title="Bahawalpur (princely state)">Bahawalpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Khairpur_(princely_state)" title="Khairpur (princely state)">Khairpur</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swat_(princely_state)" title="Swat (princely state)">Swat</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dir_(princely_state)" title="Dir (princely state)">Dir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Hunza_(princely_state)" title="Hunza (princely state)">Hunza</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chitral_(princely_state)" title="Chitral (princely state)">Chitral</a>, <a href="/wiki/Makran_(princely_state)" title="Makran (princely state)">Makran</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Kalat_(princely_state)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kalat (princely state)">Khanate of Kalat</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Radcliffe_Line">Radcliffe Line</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_of_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Radcliffe Line">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Radcliffe Award">Radcliffe Award</a></div>
<p>The controversial <a href="/wiki/Radcliffe_Award" class="mw-redirect" title="Radcliffe Award">Radcliffe Award</a>, not published until 17 August 1947 specified the Radcliffe Line which demarcated the border between the parts of British India allocated to India and Pakistan. The Radcliffe Boundary Commission sought to separate the Muslim-majority regions in the east and northwest from the areas with a Hindu majority. This entailed the partition of two British provinces which did not have a uniform majority&#160;— <a href="/wiki/Bengal" title="Bengal">Bengal</a> and <a href="/wiki/Punjab_region" class="mw-redirect" title="Punjab region">Punjab</a>. The western part of Punjab became the Pakistani province of <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_Pakistan" title="Punjab, Pakistan">Punjab</a> and the eastern part became the Indian state of <a href="/wiki/Punjab,_India" title="Punjab, India">Punjab</a>. Bengal was similarly divided into East Bengal (in Pakistan) and <a href="/wiki/West_Bengal" title="West Bengal">West Bengal</a> (in India).
</p><p>The Radcliffe commission had no power to divide the territory of the <a href="/wiki/Princely_states_of_India" class="mw-redirect" title="Princely states of India">princely states of India</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reign_of_Elizabeth_II">Reign of Elizabeth II</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dominion_of_Pakistan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Reign of Elizabeth II">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/Coronation_of_Queen_Elizabeth_II" class="mw-redirect" title="Coronation of Queen Elizabeth II">coronation of Queen Elizabeth II</a> in 1953, she was crowned as Queen of seven independent Commonwealth countries, including Pakistan,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> which was still a dominion at the time, whereas India was not, as the <a href="/wiki/Dominion_of_India" title="Dominion of India">dominion of India</a> had become a republic under the new Indian constitution of 1950.
</p><p>Pakistan ceased being a dominion on <a href="/wiki/Republic_Day_(Pakistan)" class="mw-redirect" title="Republic Day (Pakistan)">23 March 1956</a> on the adoption of a <a href="/wiki/Constitution_of_Pakistan" title="Constitution of Pakistan">republican constitution</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Bowman2000_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bowman2000-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> However, Pakistan became a <a href="/wiki/Republics_in_the_Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Republics in the Commonwealth of Nations">republic within the Commonwealth of Nations</a>.
</p><p>The Queen visited Pakistan as <a href="/wiki/Head_of_the_Commonwealth" title="Head of the Commonwealth">Head of the Commonwealth</a> in 1961 and 1997, accompanied by <a href="/wiki/Prince_Philip,_Duke_of_Edinburgh" title="Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh">Prince Philip, Duke of Edinburgh</a>.
</p><p>Pakistan left the Commonwealth in 1972 over the issue of the former <a href="/wiki/East_Pakistan" title="East Pakistan">East Pakistan</a> province becoming independent as Bangladesh. It rejoined in 1989, then was <a href="/wiki/Suspension_from_the_Commonwealth_of_Nations" title="Suspension from the Commonwealth of Nations">suspended from the Commonwealth</a> twice: firstly from 18 October 1999 to 22 May 2004 and secondly from 22 November 2007 to 22 May 2008.
</p>
