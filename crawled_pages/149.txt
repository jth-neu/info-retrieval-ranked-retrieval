<p><b>Kakao</b> (<a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>: <span lang="ko">카카오</span>) is a South Korean internet company that was established in 2014. It formed as a result of a merger between <a href="/wiki/Daum_(web_portal)" title="Daum (web portal)">Daum Communications</a> and Kakao. In 2014, the company was renamed to <b>Daum Kakao.</b><sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The company rebranded once more, reverting to just Kakao from Daum Kakao in 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May 2015, the company acquired <a href="/wiki/Path_(social_network)" title="Path (social network)">Path</a>, an American <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> company that had become successful in Asia.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In January, the following year, Kakao acquired a 76.4% stake in <a href="/wiki/Kakao_M" title="Kakao M">LOEN Entertainment</a>, a large South Korean entertainment company for $1.5 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> It was later rebranded as <b>Kakao M</b>. In May 2017, the company gained further prominence from <a href="/wiki/KakaoTalk" title="KakaoTalk">KakaoTalk</a>, a free mobile <a href="/wiki/Instant_messaging" title="Instant messaging">instant messaging</a> <a href="/wiki/Application_software" title="Application software">application</a> for <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphones</a> with free text and free call features, with 220 million registered users and 47 million active monthly users.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2010.E2.80.932014:_Founding_of_Kakao_Corp."></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2010–2014:_Founding_of_Kakao_Corp.">2010–2014: Founding of Kakao Corp.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2010–2014: Founding of Kakao Corp.">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kakao Corp is the company behind KakaoTalk, which serves as its main platform and flagship application. It was founded in 2010 by <a href="/wiki/Kim_Bum-soo_(businessman)" class="mw-redirect" title="Kim Bum-soo (businessman)">Kim Bum-soo</a>, the former CEO of NHN Corporation (the organization that emerged from the Hangame and <a href="/wiki/Naver" title="Naver">Naver.com</a> merger).<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Kakao Corp. is based in <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>, South Korea. Manson Yeo and Sean Joh serve as the current Co-CEOs.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Within the first three years after their entrance into the mobile market, Kakao Corp. introduced ten related services. The first of these releases took place in 2010 with a "gifting" feature that enabled users to buy gifts and coupons virtually through KakaoTalk and send it to their friends via the application. 
</p><p>In 2011, Plus Friend was introduced in which users receive messages, announcements, coupons from celebrities and brand name stores. This is a feature which acts as a source of advertisement and marketing for companies and celebrities since KakaoTalk does not have banner ads. KakaoTalk further features emoticons designed by web cartoonists which can be purchased in the "Item Store". 
</p><p>In 2012, Kakao Corp. introduced their gaming system "Playing Game" in which gaming items can be purchased. Kakao also expanded their social networking system by introducing Kakao Story, a mobile photo sharing application. Kakao Style, a fashion trend and retail application, was introduced along with Choco, Kakao's cyber money. In the same year, KakaoTalk's $42 million revenue is broken down to 67.5 percent ($31.1M) gaming, 26.2 percent ($12.1M)  advertising, and 6.3 percent ($2.8M) emoticon sales. In a struggle to beat its deficit from 2011, the free mobile messenger application launched gaming, retail, advertising services without banner ads. It is expected that the largest portion of revenue can be generated through games due to KakaoTalk's massive social network presence in the host country. KakaoTalk's method of brand advertisement is demonstrated through advertising channels called Plus Friends, in which users can add the brand as their friend on the mobile messenger. Users will receive content from brands or celebrities as a personal message instead of banner ads. Companies pay KakaoTalk to create an account (starting prices $20K).<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>  Third main source of revenue comes from emoticons that can be purchased within KakaoTalk for $2–3.
</p><p>In August 2013, three of App Annie’s Global Top 10 Android games are tied into the KakaoTalk platform - <a href="/w/index.php?title=Everybody%E2%80%99s_Marble&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Everybody’s Marble (page does not exist)">Everybody’s Marble</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Cookie_Run_(video_game)" title="Cookie Run (video game)">Cookie Run</a></i>, and <a href="/wiki/Anipang" title="Anipang">Anipang</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> With 93 percent of South Korea's users on KakaoTalk, the free downloads of the games Ani Pang and Dragon Flight, which can only be played with a Kakao Talk account, were deemed "national" games. To maintain simplicity of all provided services, Kakao applications can be purchased and logged in with links to KakaoTalk.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Kakao Corp. generated revenue of approximately $200M (USD) through gaming, digital content, <a href="/wiki/Mobile_commerce" title="Mobile commerce">mobile commerce</a>, and its marketing channels for brands and celebrities.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Kakao Corp. was named a "Top Developer" on Google's <a href="/wiki/Android_Market" class="mw-redirect" title="Android Market">Android Market</a>. and KakaoTalk was chosen as the No.1 Free SMS App by <a href="/wiki/Cnet" class="mw-redirect" title="Cnet">Cnet</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to a December 2013 <a href="/wiki/App_Annie" title="App Annie">App Annie</a> report, Kakao is the world’s third top publisher by monthly revenue at Google Play. Kakao Corp. is the #1 publisher for iOS and Google Play in South Korea, and KakaoTalk is the #1 app for iOS and Google Play revenue in South Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> KakaoTalk was nominated for the Most Innovative Mobile App Award at the Global Mobile Awards 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Kakao Corp. agreed to buy Daum Communications Corp, to cut costs and save time to jump-start growth and gain a listing in Seoul, South Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Kakao Corp’s full suite of apps includes: KakaoTalk, KakaoStory, KakaoTaxi, KakaoAccount, KakaoMap, KakaoDriver, KakaoBus, KakaoMusic, KakaoMusic, KakaoGroup, KakaoHome, KakaoPlace, KakaoAlbum, KakaoPage, KakaoStyle, and KakaoAgit.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2014.E2.80.932015:_Merger_of_Daum_and_Kakao"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2014–2015:_Merger_of_Daum_and_Kakao">2014–2015: Merger of Daum and Kakao</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2014–2015: Merger of Daum and Kakao">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Daum_(web_portal)" title="Daum (web portal)">Daum (web portal)</a></div>
<p>In May 26, 2014, Kakao Corp. announced that it has decided to merge with Daum Communications - one of Korea's top Internet portal - through a stock swap. Once the two firms were combined, a new tech giant is expected to debut. The emergent company would have 3 trillion won ($2.9 billion) market capitalization, enabling it to lodge a credible threat to Naver, which is South Korea's biggest web portal.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2014, Kakao Corp and Daum was merged through an equity swap. Thus, creating a company that achieved the initially projected market capitalization. The new entity Daum Kakao was valued close to 10 trillion won (US $9.45 billion).<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In 2015, the company then changed its name to 'Kakao'. Due to gambling and censorship issues within the Kakao ecosystem, the organization's board of directors ejected <a href="/wiki/Kim_Beom-soo_(businessman)" title="Kim Beom-soo (businessman)">Kim-beom-soo</a> as CEO and decided to replace him with <a href="/wiki/Rim_Ji-hoon" title="Rim Ji-hoon">Rim Ji-hoon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Kim_Beom-soo_(businessman)" title="Kim Beom-soo (businessman)">Kim Beom-soo</a> become the largest shareholder in the new pro forma company with a 22.2 percent stake. <sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="2015.E2.80.93present:_New_business_model"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015–present:_New_business_model">2015–present: New business model</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2015–present: New business model">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On March 10, 2015, Daum Kakao launched their <a href="/wiki/KakaoTaxi" class="mw-redirect" title="KakaoTaxi">KakaoTaxi</a> service that allows users to call a taxi around the user using the KakaoTaxi application.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Thanks to the many taxi companies cooperating with the KakaoTaxi service,<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> about 600,000 taxi-consumers used the ride-hailing platform every day within eight months of its launch.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Internet_bank">Internet bank</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Internet bank">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>Kakao was approved by South Korean regulators to become the nation's first Internet-only bank in 2017. The internet bank engage in the same business as commercial banks, including processing deposits, loans and wiring money. Consumers will no longer need to visit bank to open new bank accounts or get loans. Their business plan was considered innovative, and the company's business model was expected to secure a sizable customer sign-ups relatively easily, based on the users of KakaoTalk, which is the country's most popular messaging application.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Although K-Bank eventually became South Korea's internet-only bank having been launched several months prior, Kakao Bank immediately attracted more 820,000 customers four days after its launch on July 27, 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The dedicated Kakao Bank app itself has also been downloaded 1.5 million times within the same period.<sup id="cite_ref-:3_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> The customers ballooned to 3.5 million after a month.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> These figures trounced the 400,000 users that K-Bank amassed within 100 days of its existence.<sup id="cite_ref-:4_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:4-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>By September 26, 2017, Kakao Bank lent ₩1.4 trillion ($1.2 billion), constituting 40 percent of the total loans in all of South Korea for that particular month.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The bank's unprecedented expansion is seen as an exception to the closure of banks, particularly foreign-owned institutions. The fledgling performance for these banks is being blamed on the high cost of maintaining brick-and-mortar operations and the popularity of internet finance among Koreans consumers.<sup id="cite_ref-:5_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>
<h4><span class="mw-headline" id="Transportation_services">Transportation services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Transportation services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h4>
<p>In November 2015, Kakao launched the taxi business model <a href="/wiki/Kakao_T" title="Kakao T">Kakao T</a>. The service includes the premium extension 'Kakao Taxi Black,' which allows users to book rides in Seoul via the messenger app that are exclusively carried out in luxury imported cars such as Mercedes Benz, Lexus, and BMW. Fares for the premium service start at ₩8,000. Kakao announced plans to expand the activity field to other Korean cities within the following year.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="KakaoTalk_censorship">KakaoTalk censorship</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: KakaoTalk censorship">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/KakaoTalk" title="KakaoTalk">KakaoTalk</a></div>
<p>When the announcement by the Korean government that it would tighten its real-time monitoring to prevent people from spreading false information, for which the company cooperated fully by providing reams of conversation data. <a href="/wiki/KakaoTalk" title="KakaoTalk">KakaoTalk</a> users expressed their displeasure to the censorship by saying that they would migrate to other messenger services. Because of this, as many as 1.5 million users are reported to have recently signed up for the hitherto obscure mobile messenger service <a href="/wiki/Telegram_(software)" class="mw-redirect" title="Telegram (software)">Telegram</a>, which is known for its security.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Daum Kakao has explained the censorship, saying, "It is unthinkable not to follow the rules in a constitutional country." "The move (by Daum Kakao) reflects users' repulsion and fear against cyber censorship," said Yoo Ki-hong, a spokesman of the main opposition New Politics Alliance for Democracy. "The government should understand the sense of resistance of the people on the issue, rather than justifying its execution of warrants."
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Kakao_Corp_chairman_accused_of_gambling">Kakao Corp chairman accused of gambling</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Kakao&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Kakao Corp chairman accused of gambling">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Kakao board chairman, Kim Beom-soo, has been accused of gambling in Las Vegas in the early years of Kakao Corp from 2007-2010. Overseas gambling is illegal under the Korean law. Korean prosecutors have reportedly obtained information from the U.S. Department of Justice and the Department of Treasury that Kakao mobile messenger founder Kim had spent 20 hours and 51 minutes at the Bellagio Hotel in Las Vegas in 2007, when he served as NHN global CEO. He betted an average of $2,440 per session and lost $16,993 within the night, local Hankook Ilbo newspaper reported.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The company’s decision to comply with the prosecution’s warrants requesting monitoring of chatting records in criminal cases, a reversal from its earlier stance, with rumors that he succumbed to the investigative powers.<sup id="cite_ref-:2_38-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:2-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
