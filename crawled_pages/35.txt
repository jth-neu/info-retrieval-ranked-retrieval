<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Ixquick</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>

<p><b>Ixquick</b> (styled "<b>ixquick</b>") was a <a href="/wiki/Metasearch_engine" title="Metasearch engine">metasearch engine</a> based in <a href="/wiki/Zeist" title="Zeist">Zeist</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> which highlights privacy as its distinguishing feature.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2015_07_20_ixquick_eu_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr_2015_07_20_ixquick_eu-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-startpage_2016_03_notice_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startpage_2016_03_notice-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pr_2015_03_30_ixquick_eu_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr_2015_03_30_ixquick_eu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pr_2008_07_14_ixquick_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr_2008_07_14_ixquick-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1st_euro_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_europrise_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1st_euro_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_europrise-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1st_eu_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_privacy_org_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1st_eu_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_privacy_org-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-expressvpn_review2016&#95;_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-expressvpn_review2016_-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ixquick now redirects users to <a href="/wiki/Startpage.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Startpage.com">startpage.com</a>.
</p><p>Founded by David Bodnick in 1998, Ixquick is owned by Surfboard Holding BV of the Netherlands, which acquired the internet company in 2000.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> Ixquick and its sibling project Startpage.com reached their latest record (28-day average) daily direct queries of 5.7 million on 2 February 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company also provides the stand-alone proxy service, Ixquick Proxy, which has also been incorporated into both its Ixquick and Startpage search engines, allowing users the option to open all search results via proxy. The company  developed a privacy protecting <a href="/wiki/Email" title="Email">email</a> service, called <i>StartMail</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> This service was released to the public in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ixquick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ixquick was re-launched on 23 March 2005, with  new features including a redesigned metasearch algorithm, an international phone, and lowest-price directory.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 27 June 2006, following <a href="/wiki/Criticism_of_Google#Google_Shopping_rankings" title="Criticism of Google">criticism of Google Shopping</a>, Ixquick.com began to delete private details of its users.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> IP addresses and other personal information were deleted within 48 hours of a search.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Ixquick has stated that it does not share its users' personal information with other search engines or with the provider of its sponsored results.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Ixquick was awarded the first European Privacy Seal (EuroPriSe) for its privacy practices on 14 July 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-1st_euro_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_europrise_8-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1st_euro_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_europrise-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-1st_eu_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_privacy_org_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1st_eu_privacy_seal_2008_07_14_privacy_org-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-pr_2008_07_14_ixquick_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr_2008_07_14_ixquick-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> This European Union-sponsored initiative guarantees compliance with EU laws and regulations on data security and privacy, through a series of design and technical audits.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> It was re-certified together with Startpage in 2009, 2011, 2013<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> and 2015.<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2015_07_20_ixquick_eu_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr_2015_07_20_ixquick_eu-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 28 January 2009, Ixquick completely ended the recording of user IP addresses.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 29 March 2016, Ixquick.com was  officially "merged" with the same company's Startpage search engine<sup id="cite_ref-startpage_2016_03_notice_5-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startpage_2016_03_notice-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> (a search engine with the privacy features of Ixquick, but using only <a href="/wiki/Google_search" class="mw-redirect" title="Google search">Google search</a> results<sup id="cite_ref-pr_2015_03_30_ixquick_eu_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-pr_2015_03_30_ixquick_eu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>). Users entering ixquick.com are now automatically re-routed to startpage.com. However, at the end of Ixquick's merger notice, the company reports: "We think you're going to love StartPage, but if you prefer original Ixquick search results, don't worry. They are still available at ixquick.eu." Ixquick had long declared on its sites, that it operated in compliance with European Union privacy standards, and it retains its original European search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-expressvpn_review2016&#95;_10-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-expressvpn_review2016_-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> As of 21 April 2018, ixquick.eu also redirects to startpage.com.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Search_mechanism_and_interface">Search mechanism and interface</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ixquick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Search mechanism and interface">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Ixquick returns the top ten results from multiple search engines. It uses a "Star System" to rank its results by awarding one star for every result that has been returned from a search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> Thereby, the top search results are the ones that have been returned from the most search engines. Ixquick can search in 17 languages: <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Simplified and Traditional Chinese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Danish_language" title="Danish language">Danish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dutch_language" title="Dutch language">Dutch</a>, <a href="/wiki/English_language" title="English language">English</a>, <a href="/wiki/Finnish_language" title="Finnish language">Finnish</a>, <a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>, <a href="/wiki/German_language" title="German language">German</a>, <a href="/wiki/Italian_language" title="Italian language">Italian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Korean_language" title="Korean language">Korean</a>, <a href="/wiki/Norwegian_language" title="Norwegian language">Norwegian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Polish_language" title="Polish language">Polish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Portuguese_language" title="Portuguese language">Portuguese</a>, <a href="/wiki/Spanish_language" title="Spanish language">Spanish</a>, <a href="/wiki/Swedish_language" title="Swedish language">Swedish</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Turkish_language" title="Turkish language">Turkish</a>. Each language version includes local search engines.
</p><p>Ixquick uses only one cookie, called "preferences", which remembers a user's search preferences for future searches and is deleted after a user does not visit Ixquick for 90 days.<sup id="cite_ref-ixquick_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ixquick-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> It is however possible to save preferences in a bookmarked URL, thus avoiding cookies completely.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Preferences are not saved automatically. Only a visitor can decide to save his or her preferences.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Startpage.com">Startpage.com</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ixquick&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Startpage.com">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>On 7 July 2009 Ixquick launched Startpage.com to offer its service at a <a href="/wiki/Uniform_resource_locator" class="mw-redirect" title="Uniform resource locator">URL</a> that is both easier to remember and spell, and in contrast to ixquick.eu, fetches results from the Google search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> This is done without saving the users' IP addresses or giving any personal user information to Google's servers.
</p><p>On 29 March 2016, Ixquick.com was  officially "merged" with the same company's Startpage search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-startpage_2016_03_notice_5-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-startpage_2016_03_notice-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Prior to the release of <a href="/wiki/Tor_(anonymity_network)#Tor_Browser" title="Tor (anonymity network)">Tor Browser</a> version 4.5, Startpage.com was its default search engine.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
