<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Infoseek</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

<p><b>Infoseek</b> was a popular internet <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> founded in 1994 by <a href="/wiki/Steve_Kirsch" title="Steve Kirsch">Steve Kirsch</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Infoseek was originally operated by the <b>Infoseek Corporation</b>, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Sunnyvale,_California" title="Sunnyvale, California">Sunnyvale, California</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Infoseek was bought by <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> in 1999,<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> and the technology was merged with that of the Disney-acquired <a href="/wiki/Starwave" title="Starwave">Starwave</a> to form the <a href="/wiki/Go.com" title="Go.com">Go.com</a> network.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Infoseek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>By September 1997, Infoseek had 7.3 million visitors per month.<sup id="cite_ref-source_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-source-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> It bought out the <a href="/wiki/WebChat_Broadcasting_System" title="WebChat Broadcasting System">WebChat Broadcasting System</a> in April 1998.
</p><p>In 1998, Disney purchased a 43% stake of Infoseek, and incorporated the site into its various media businesses. Around the same time, Disney acquired the <a href="/wiki/Starwave" title="Starwave">Starwave</a> Corporation, which included ESPN.com and ABCNews.com.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In 1999, Disney acquired the remaining Infoseek stock it didn't own. Disney bundled its Starwave properties and Infoseek and formed the GO.com portal.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Infoseek was among the first search engines to sell advertising on a <a href="/wiki/Cost_per_impression" title="Cost per impression">CPM</a>, Cost Per Thousand Impressions, basis.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> In 1997, the first Cost Per Click programs, as well as the precursor to pop-ups called daughter windows, was sold by east coast sales executive Robert Formentin to <a href="/wiki/Grey_Global_Group" title="Grey Global Group">Grey Advertising</a> for a <a href="/wiki/Procter_%26_Gamble" title="Procter &amp; Gamble">Procter &amp; Gamble</a> <a href="/wiki/Pampers" title="Pampers">Pampers</a> campaign.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 1998, Infoseek was the first internet company to develop and launch <a href="/wiki/Behavioral_targeting" class="mw-redirect" title="Behavioral targeting">behavioral targeting</a> via its UltraMatch targeting algorithms.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2018)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In 1999, Infoseek engineer <a href="/wiki/Li_Yanhong" class="mw-redirect" title="Li Yanhong">Li Yanhong</a> moved to <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/China" title="China">China</a> and co-founded the search engine <a href="/wiki/Baidu" title="Baidu">Baidu</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In February 2001, Disney decided to cancel the service and lay off all staff.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Also in 2001, Bernt Wahl, Andy Bensky and 15 software engineers, all Infoseek employees, led a management buyout attempt from Disney but were ultimately rebuffed.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Infoseek's Ultraseek Server software technology, an enterprise search engine product, was sold in 2000 to <a href="/wiki/Inktomi" title="Inktomi">Inktomi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_6-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Under Inktomi, Ultraseek Server was renamed "Inktomi Enterprise Search". In December 2002 (prior to the <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> acquisition of Inktomi), the Ultraseek product suite was sold to a competitor <a href="/w/index.php?title=Verity_Inc&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Verity Inc (page does not exist)">Verity Inc</a>, who re-established the Ultraseek brand name and continued development of the product.
</p><p>In December 2005, Verity was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Autonomy_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomy Corporation">Autonomy PLC</a>. Under Autonomy, Ultraseek ceased to be a stand-alone product and became a modular component under the IDOL platform. It continued to be developed and marketed as Autonomy's entry-level keyword-based site search offering until after <a href="/wiki/Autonomy_Corporation" class="mw-redirect" title="Autonomy Corporation">Autonomy</a> was acquired by <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a> (HP) in October 2011.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_name">Domain name</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Infoseek&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Domain name">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The "infoseek.com" <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain name</a> redirects to the "go.com" website. The brand name Infoseek is only used in <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japan</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
