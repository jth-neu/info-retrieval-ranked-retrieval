<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">Alan Emtage</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">

				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p><b>Alan Emtage</b> (born November 27, 1964) conceived and implemented the first version of <a href="/wiki/Archie_search_engine" title="Archie search engine">Archie</a>, a pre-Web internet search engine for locating material in public <a href="/wiki/File_Transfer_Protocol" title="File Transfer Protocol">FTP</a> archives.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Life">Life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alan_Emtage&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Alan Emtage was born in <a href="/wiki/Barbados" title="Barbados">Barbados</a>, the son of Sir Stephen and Lady Emtage. He attended <a href="/wiki/High_school" class="mw-redirect" title="High school">high school</a> at <a href="/wiki/Harrison_College_(Barbados)" title="Harrison College (Barbados)">Harrison College</a> from 1975 to 1983 (and in 1981 became the owner of a <a href="/wiki/Sinclair_ZX81" class="mw-redirect" title="Sinclair ZX81">Sinclair ZX81</a> with 1K of memory), where he graduated at the top of his class, winning the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Barbados_Scholarship&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Barbados Scholarship (page does not exist)">Barbados Scholarship</a>.
</p><p>In 1983 Emtage entered <a href="/wiki/McGill_University" title="McGill University">McGill University</a> in <a href="/wiki/Montreal" title="Montreal">Montreal, Quebec</a>, Canada, studying for an honors <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">Bachelor's degree</a> in <a href="/wiki/Computer_science" title="Computer science">computer science</a> which was followed by a <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">Master's degree</a> in 1987 from which he graduated in 1991. Emtage was part of the team that brought the first Internet link to eastern <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a> (and only the second link in the country) in 1986. In 1989 while a student and working as a <a href="/wiki/Systems_administrator" class="mw-redirect" title="Systems administrator">systems administrator</a> for the School of Computer Science, Emtage conceived and implemented the original version of the <a href="/wiki/Archie_search_engine" title="Archie search engine">Archie search engine</a>, the world's first Internet <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a>.
</p><p>In 1992, Emtage along with Peter Deutsch, also a McGill graduate, formed <a href="/w/index.php?title=Bunyip_Information_Systems&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Bunyip Information Systems (page does not exist)">Bunyip Information Systems</a> in Montreal—the world's first company expressly founded for and dedicated to providing Internet information services with a licensed commercial version of the Archie search engine.
</p><p>Emtage was a founding member of the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Society" title="Internet Society">Internet Society</a> and went on to create and chair several working groups at the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Engineering_Task_Force" title="Internet Engineering Task Force">Internet Engineering Task Force</a> (IETF), the standard-setting body for the Internet. Working with other pioneers such as <a href="/wiki/Tim_Berners-Lee" title="Tim Berners-Lee">Tim Berners-Lee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Marc_Andreessen" title="Marc Andreessen">Marc Andreessen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Mark_McCahill" class="mw-redirect" title="Mark McCahill">Mark McCahill</a> (creator of <a href="/wiki/Gopher_(protocol)" title="Gopher (protocol)">Gopher</a>) and <a href="/wiki/Jon_Postel" title="Jon Postel">Jon Postel</a>, Emtage co-chaired the <a href="/wiki/Uniform_Resource_Identifier" title="Uniform Resource Identifier">Uniform Resource Identifier</a> working group which created the standard for <a href="/wiki/Uniform_Resource_Locator" class="mw-redirect" title="Uniform Resource Locator">Uniform Resource Locators</a> (URLs).
</p><p>On September 18, 2017 Emtage was inducted as an Innovator by the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Society" title="Internet Society">Internet Society</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Internet_Hall_of_Fame" title="Internet Hall of Fame">Internet Hall of Fame</a> in a ceremony in <a href="/wiki/Los_Angeles" title="Los Angeles">Los Angeles</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Emtage has spoken and lectured on Internet Information Systems and is <a href="/wiki/Chief_technical_officer" class="mw-redirect" title="Chief technical officer">chief technical officer</a> at <a href="/wiki/Mediapolis_(company)" title="Mediapolis (company)">Mediapolis</a>, a web engineering company in <a href="/wiki/New_York_City" title="New York City">New York City</a>.
</p>
