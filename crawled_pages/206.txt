<p><b>A9.com</b> is a <a href="/wiki/Subsidiary" title="Subsidiary">subsidiary</a> of <a href="/wiki/Amazon_(company)" title="Amazon (company)">Amazon</a> that develops <a href="/wiki/Search_engine_technology" title="Search engine technology">search engine</a> and <a href="/wiki/Search_advertising" title="Search advertising">search advertising</a> technology. A9 is based in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>, with teams in <a href="/wiki/Bangalore" title="Bangalore">Bangalore</a>, <a href="/wiki/Beijing" title="Beijing">Beijing</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dublin" title="Dublin">Dublin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ia%C8%99i" title="Iași">Iași</a>, <a href="/wiki/Munich" title="Munich">Munich</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>. A9 has development efforts in areas of product search, cloud search, visual search, augmented reality, advertising technology and community question answering.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>A9 was created by Amazon.com in 2003 as an independent company aimed at producing technology for search and advertising. They moved into the building previously used by the <a href="/wiki/DEC_Systems_Research_Center" title="DEC Systems Research Center">DEC Systems Research Center</a> in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>. 
One purpose of A9.com was to leverage <a href="/wiki/Algorithm" title="Algorithm">algorithms</a>, and the name was chosen as a <a href="/wiki/Numeronym" title="Numeronym">numeronym</a> to represent that word (i.e. 'A' + 9 other letters).
The office was in <a href="/wiki/Silicon_Valley" title="Silicon Valley">Silicon Valley</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-udi04_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-udi04-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
Under the direction of its first president, <a href="/wiki/Udi_Manber" title="Udi Manber">Udi Manber</a>, A9 focused on several areas, including the A9.com destination website,
product search, and a search advertising platform.  Some early A9 services such as "search inside the book" continued, while others have been discontinued.  
The A9 search engine powers product search for Amazon.com and several other eCommerce retailers.
</p><p>In February 2006, Manber was replaced by <a href="/wiki/David_L._Tennenhouse" title="David L. Tennenhouse">David L. Tennenhouse</a> as president.<sup id="cite_ref-post_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-post-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> 
In September 2006, William Stasior, one of A9's founders and a former <a href="/wiki/AltaVista" title="AltaVista">AltaVista</a> and Amazon.com executive, was named president.
In June 2009, A9 acquired SnapTell, which developed smartphone-based visual search applications.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Stasior left in October 2012 to join <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc.</a> and work on <a href="/wiki/Siri" title="Siri">Siri</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
Brian Pinkerton, who had developed <a href="/wiki/WebCrawler" title="WebCrawler">WebCrawler</a> in the 1990s,  became general manager of A9 in 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Past_projects">Past projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Past projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="A9.com_search_portal">A9.com search portal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: A9.com search portal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A9.com originally operated a <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">search portal</a>, along with an A9 <a href="/wiki/Toolbar" title="Toolbar">toolbar</a>, which was first demonstrated on April 14, 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>  The service featured some innovative design elements, allowing users to choose from over 400 sources to search and view results in separate columns grouped by source.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
In addition to web search results, users could get product search results from Amazon, encyclopedia results from <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia" title="Wikipedia">Wikipedia</a>, movie information from <a href="/wiki/IMDb" title="IMDb">IMDb</a>, and others, starting in September 2004.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
Web search results were provided by different search engines over time.  The search portal functionality was discontinued in 2008.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Open_Search">Open Search</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Open Search">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A9 developed a protocol called <a href="/wiki/OpenSearch" title="OpenSearch">OpenSearch</a> that enabled the "plug-in" search source functionality from the A9.com portal.  The original specification, OpenSearch 1.0, was released in March 2005.  A9 made the protocol freely available through a <a href="/wiki/Creative_Commons" title="Creative Commons">Creative Commons</a> license. OpenSearch is now<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="Wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="The time period mentioned near this tag is ambiguous. (November 2016)">when?</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> maintained by the OpenSearch.org community.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="BlockView">BlockView</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: BlockView">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A9 developed the first service that allowed users of maps and "<a href="/wiki/Yellow_pages" title="Yellow pages">yellow pages</a>" (a <a href="/wiki/Telephone_directory" title="Telephone directory">telephone directory</a>) to view buildings at street level.  Called BlockView, the service was announced in January 2005.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>  Within a year, the service included images for 24 US cities.  Both BlockView and maps (as well as support for the A9 tool bar) were discontinued in September 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Clickriver">Clickriver</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Clickriver">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2006, A9 announced Clickriver, an advertising program that let third parties place advertisements on Amazon.com pages.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
Clickriver focused primarily on products and services that complemented Amazon's own offerings.  For example, if a customer viewed a television on Amazon, a Clickriver ad might offer installation services in his or her local area.  Clickriver was discontinued in 2010, replaced by other Amazon advertising programs.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Community_question_answering">Community question answering</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Community question answering">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A9 operated <a href="/wiki/Askville" title="Askville">Askville</a>, a community-based <a href="/wiki/Question-and-answer_site" class="mw-redirect" title="Question-and-answer site">question-and-answer site</a> first tested in December 2006. Service began in November 2007.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>  Compared with other question-and-answer sites, Askville focused more on social interaction, allowing users to follow other users as well as track specific topics.
On October 25, 2013, Askville was shut down.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Cloud_search">Cloud search</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Cloud search">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>CloudSearch is a managed <a href="/wiki/Amazon_Web_Services" title="Amazon Web Services">Amazon Web Services</a> service that makes client data searchable.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>  For example, a business that wanted to allow customers to search their catalog could upload the catalog entries to CloudSearch and then route search requests from their application or web site to the service.  CloudSearch instances could be brought online as data or traffic volume demanded.  Sites using CloudSearch included <a href="/wiki/SmugMug" title="SmugMug">SmugMug</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Sage_Bionetworks" title="Sage Bionetworks">Sage Bionetworks</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2014, Amazon moved away from using A9 for CloudSearch to technology based on <a href="/wiki/Apache_Solr" title="Apache Solr">Apache Solr</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Recent_projects">Recent projects</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Recent projects">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Product_search">Product search</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Product search">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A9 continued to develop and operate the service that provides search on all nine Amazon.com sites, as well as other retail sites owned by Amazon such as <a href="/wiki/Endless.com" title="Endless.com">Endless.com</a>, Javari.de, and <a href="/wiki/Audible.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Audible.com">Audible.com</a>.  In addition, A9 search is also used by other retailers, such as <a href="/wiki/Marks_%26_Spencer" title="Marks &amp; Spencer">Marks &amp; Spencer</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> that license the Amazon retail platform for their online presence.
</p><p>A9 provided the "search inside the book" capability for Amazon.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Announced in 2003, this service allows customers to search through the full text of hundreds of thousands of books.  This feature is available both for searching individual books and for the entire book catalog. <sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Visual_search">Visual search</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Visual search">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The visual search group at A9 created  mobile applications that recognize objects and allow users to find these products on Amazon.com.  Their first augmented reality app, Flow, provided both object and barcode recognition. It was released in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>A9 visual search technology powers features to let customers search for certain products (such as shoes) by shape, and find visually similar items on Amazon.com, <a href="/wiki/Zappos.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Zappos.com">Zappos.com</a>, Endless.com, and other retail sites.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. <h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising_technology">Advertising technology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A9.com&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Advertising technology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A9's advertising technology group began delivering sponsored link advertisements on the Amazon.com retail Web site in 2003 and continued to manage this program for Amazon properties.
