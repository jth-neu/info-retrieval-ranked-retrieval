<p>The <b>University of Geneva</b> (<a href="/wiki/French_language" title="French language">French</a>: <i>Université de Genève</i>) is a public research university located in <a href="/wiki/Geneva" title="Geneva">Geneva</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. 
</p><p>It was founded in 1559 by <a href="/wiki/John_Calvin" title="John Calvin">John Calvin</a> as a <a href="/wiki/Theology" title="Theology">theological</a> <a href="/wiki/Seminary" title="Seminary">seminary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Law_school" title="Law school">law school</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Geneva_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Geneva-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It remained focused on theology until the 17th century, when it became a center for <a href="/wiki/The_Enlightenment" class="mw-redirect" title="The Enlightenment">Enlightenment</a> scholarship. In 1873, it dropped its religious affiliations and became officially secular.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Today, the university is the third largest university in Switzerland by number of students.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, the University of Geneva celebrated the 450th anniversary of its founding.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Almost 40% of the students come from foreign countries.
</p><p>The university holds and actively pursues teaching, <a href="/wiki/Research_university" title="Research university">research</a>, and community service as its primary objectives. In 2016, it was ranked 53rd worldwide by the <a href="/wiki/Shanghai_ranking" class="mw-redirect" title="Shanghai ranking">Shanghai Academic Ranking of World Universities</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> 89th by the <a href="/wiki/QS_World_University_Rankings" title="QS World University Rankings">QS World University Rankings</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> and 131st in <a href="/wiki/Times_Higher_Education_World_University_Rankings" title="Times Higher Education World University Rankings">the TIMES Higher Education World University Ranking</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>UNIGE is a member of the <a href="/wiki/League_of_European_Research_Universities" title="League of European Research Universities">League of European Research Universities</a> (including academic institutions such as Amsterdam, Cambridge, Heidelberg, Helsinki and Milan) the <a href="/wiki/Coimbra_Group" title="Coimbra Group">Coimbra Group</a> and the <a href="/wiki/European_University_Association" title="European University Association">European University Association</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Location">Location</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Location">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The University of Geneva is located in several districts in the eastern part of the city and in the nearby city of <a href="/wiki/Carouge" title="Carouge">Carouge</a> (on the left bank of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_Geneva" title="Lake Geneva">Lake Léman</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Rhone" class="mw-redirect" title="Rhone">Rhône</a>), and the different buildings are sometimes very distant from each other (the Battelle buildings are for instance more than three kilometers away from the Bastions). The oldest building (1559) is the <a href="/wiki/Coll%C3%A8ge_Calvin" title="Collège Calvin">Collège Calvin</a>, and is not anymore a university building. Lectures are given in six different main locations, <i>Les Bastions</i>, <i>Uni Dufour</i>, <i>Sciences</i> <i>I</i>, <i>II</i> and <i>III</i>, <i>Uni Mail</i> and <i>Uni Pignon</i>, <i>Centre Médical Universitaire</i> (CMU), and <i>Battelle</i>;<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> as well as in other less important locations (for instance part of the Mathematics Section is located at the second and (partly) third and sixth storeys rented by the university in an office building in Carouge).<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uni_Bastions"><i>Uni Bastions</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Uni Bastions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Built between 1868 and 1871, <i>Uni Bastions</i> is the symbol of Geneva's academic life. It is located in the middle of a park and is host to the faculty of Protestant Theology and to the Faculty of Arts.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uni_Dufour"><i>Uni Dufour</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Uni Dufour">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Its architecture was inspired by Le Corbusier. It hosts the <i>Rectorat</i> and the administration of the University.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Uni_Mail"><i>Uni Mail</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Uni Mail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>It is Switzerland's biggest building dedicated to social sciences. It currently hosts the Faculty of Law, of Economics and Management,<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> of Psychology and Education and the <a href="/wiki/Faculty_of_Translation_and_Interpreting_of_the_University_of_Geneva" title="Faculty of Translation and Interpreting of the University of Geneva">Faculty of Translation and Interpreting</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> 
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Finances">Finances</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Finances">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries_and_press">Libraries and press</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Libraries and press">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Libraries">Libraries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Libraries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>UNIGE's library facilities are spread across four sites.
</p><p><i>Uni Arve</i> is host to seven libraries: the <i>Bibliothèque Ernst &amp; Lucie Schmidheiny, the Bibliothèque d'Anthropologie</i>, the <i>Bibliothèque du Centre universitaire d'informatique</i>, the <i>Bibliothèque Georges de Rham (Mathematics)</i>, the <i>Bibliothèque de l'Institut des Sciences de l'environnement (ISE)</i>, <i>Bibliothèque de l'Observatoire (Astronomy)</i> and the <i>Bibliothèque des Sciences de la Terre et de l'environnement</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Uni Bastions</i> hosts the language libraries, as well as the university's libraries focused on history and musicology.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Uni CMU</i> is home to an extensive collection of medical issues. Besides, it is also hosts the <i>Centre de documentation en santé</i> (CDS) and the <i>Bibliothèque de l’Institut de la médecine et de la santé</i> et de l’<i>Institut d’éthique biomédicale</i> (IHMS - IEB).<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Uni Mail</i>'s collection is focused on the following themes: Economics and social sciences, Law, Psychology and Learning Sciences, Translation and Interpreting, European studies, French as a foreign language and Musicology. Besides, it also hosts UNIGE's multimedia library.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Press">Press</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Press">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The journal de l'UNIGE</i> is released biweekly. Its purpose is to ease communication inside the university, to inform the students about the research being carried at UNIGE, to convey new opinions and to inform students and teachers of upcoming university events via <i>l'Agenda</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i>Campus</i> is released monthly with the objective to ease communication between the scientific community and the citizens and to be a "bridge between science and city".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Academics">Academics</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Academics">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Admission_and_fees">Admission and fees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Admission and fees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To be enrolled in a bachelor programme, one must hold a Swiss <a href="/wiki/Maturity_diploma" class="mw-redirect" title="Maturity diploma">maturity diploma</a> or a secondary diploma considered by the University of Geneva to be equivalent.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> If the degree was not pursued in French, applicants must pass an eliminatory French language test at the beginning of September, which consists of an oral and a written comprehension test and of a piece of argumentative writing.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> Tuition fees are of CHF 500 per semester.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Academic_year">Academic year</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: Academic year">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>UNIGE's academic year runs from mid-September to mid-June. It is divided in two semesters, each one being concluded by an examination session, held respectively at the beginning of January and at the beginning of June. An examination session is held at the end of August and beginning of September as a retake for students who failed their January or June examinations.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the three days before the start of the new academic year, the <i>Journées d'accueil</i> (Welcome Days) are organized by the University to introduce the new students to the city and the facilities, tips are also given on how to succeed at university. A second chapter including city tours, outdoor concerts and animations is also organized by the student association UniAccueil (AUA) to celebrate the new academic year.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Teaching_and_degrees">Teaching and degrees</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Teaching and degrees">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Before 2005, the University applied various very different models, depending on Faculties, and sometimes even on Departments (or "Sections"). Some Faculties applied the <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree#France" title="Academic degree">French education model</a> of granting <a href="/wiki/Academic_degree" title="Academic degree">academic degrees</a>, with some minor differences: <i>demi-licence</i> (two years), <i>trois-quarts de licence</i> (three years), <i><a href="/wiki/Licentiate_(degree)" title="Licentiate (degree)">licence</a></i> (four years), <a href="/wiki/Master_of_Advanced_Studies" title="Master of Advanced Studies"><i>diplôme d'études approfondies</i> and <i>diplôme d'études superieures spécialisées</i></a> (DEA/DESS) (1–2 years), and <a href="/wiki/Doctorate" title="Doctorate">doctorate</a> (3–5 years).
</p><p>The University now follows the requirements of the <a href="/wiki/Bologna_process" class="mw-redirect" title="Bologna process">Bologna process</a>: <a href="/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree" title="Bachelor&#39;s degree">bachelor's</a> (three years), <a href="/wiki/Master%27s_degree" title="Master&#39;s degree">master's</a> (1–2 years), in some departments or sections Master of Advanced Studies (1–2 years), doctorate (3–5 years).<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>UNIGE offers more than 240 types of diplomas: about 30 bachelor's degrees,<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> 70 masters and 78 doctorates. It also provides more than 200 programmes of <a href="/wiki/Continuing_education" title="Continuing education">continuing education</a> in various sectors.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="International_partnerships">International partnerships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: International partnerships">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Research">Research</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Research">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The key sectors of research at the University of Geneva are sciences (molecular biology, bio-informatics, etc.), elementary physics, astrophysics, economics, social sociences, psychology, chemistry, biochemistry and biophysics.
</p><p>UNIGE is home to six national research centers: in genetics (Frontiers in Genetics), in material sciences (MaNEP), in study of emotions (Affective Sciences), in chemical biology (with EPFL), in study of mental illness (Synaptic, with EPFL and Unil), in study of life path (with Unil). UNIGE also carries research in international studies since the creation in 2013 of the Global Studies Institute, in finance with the Geneva Finance Research Institute,<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> and in environmental studies, with the creation in 2009 of the <i>Institut des sciences de l'environnement</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Famous discoveries have been made by researcher working at UNIGE including the discoveries of extrasolar planets by <a href="/wiki/Michel_Mayor" title="Michel Mayor">Michel Mayor</a>, and of quantum teleportation by Nicolas Gisin.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_body">Student body</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=24" title="Edit section: Student body">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In 2016, 16,530 students were studying at UNIGE, of whom 61% were female. 37% of the students were non-Swiss, originating from 151 countries. 
4,449 teachers and collaborators, of whom 49% are female, are working for UNIGE.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Student_life">Student life</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=25" title="Edit section: Student life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sports">Sports</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=26" title="Edit section: Sports">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The <i>Bureau des sports</i> organizes all the sports related activity at UNIGE. Free sports lessons are given everyday and it suffices to show one's student card to access. Other lessons organization with the university's partners demand a small fee.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> UNIGE is home to the Geneva university championships in basketball, indoor football, rowing, badminton, outdoor football.<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> The university also sends teams to the Swiss university championship in badminton, indoor football, skiing, basketball, fencing, football, golf, ice-hockey, table tennis and volleyball.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> UNIGE also provides special schedules for students wishing to pursue their high level sporting career and to study at the same time.<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Associations">Associations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=27" title="Edit section: Associations">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Alumni UNIGE</i> is the alumni association of the University of Geneva, it offers a network of several thousand people to its members, as well as other advantages, such as discount prizes, special events, access to the official networking platform.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> <i>Atout-lettres</i> is the alumni association of the literature students of the University, founded in 1997. Its purpose is to prepare the professional insertion of the literature students, to establish links between literature student and the working world and to promote the formation given by the  <i>Faculté de Lettres</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Alumni">Alumni</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=University_of_Geneva&amp;action=edit&amp;section=28" title="Edit section: Alumni">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:229px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg/227px-Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg" width="227" height="269" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg/340px-Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/26/Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg/453px-Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg 2x" data-file-width="497" data-file-height="590" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Kofi_Annan_crop.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">Kofi Annan</a>, UNIGE alumnus, Nobel Peace Prize in 2001</div></div></div>
<p>Over the course of its history, a sizeable number of UNIGE alumni have become notable in their fields, both academic, and in the wider world. Affiliates of the University of Geneva have won 10 Nobel prizes. Graduate alumni (<a href="/wiki/Martin_Hairer" title="Martin Hairer">Martin Hairer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Vaughan_Jones" title="Vaughan Jones">Vaughan Jones</a>) have won 2 Fields Medals.
</p><p>The University has hosted several Nobel laureates as students, researchers and/or professors: <a href="/wiki/Norman_Angell" title="Norman Angell">Norman Angell</a> (1872–1967), Nobel Peace Prize 1933; <a href="/wiki/Karl_Gunnar_Myrdal" class="mw-redirect" title="Karl Gunnar Myrdal">Karl Gunnar Myrdal</a> (1898–1987) Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences 1974; <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Bovet" title="Daniel Bovet">Daniel Bovet</a> (1907–1992), Nobel Prize in Medicine 1957; <a href="/wiki/Niels_Kaj_Jerne" title="Niels Kaj Jerne">Niels Kaj Jerne</a> (1911–1994), Nobel Prize in Medicine 1984; <a href="/wiki/Maurice_Allais" title="Maurice Allais">Maurice Allais</a> (1911–2010), Nobel Prize in Economic Sciences 1988; <a href="/wiki/Edmond_H._Fischer" title="Edmond H. Fischer">Edmond H. Fischer</a> (1920–), Nobel Prize in Medicine 1992; <a href="/wiki/Martin_Rodbell" title="Martin Rodbell">Martin Rodbell</a> (1925–1998), Nobel Prize in Medicine 1994; <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jay_Heeger" class="mw-redirect" title="Alan Jay Heeger">Alan Jay Heeger</a> (1936- ), Nobel Prize in Chemistry 2000; <a href="/wiki/Werner_Arber" title="Werner Arber">Werner Arber</a> (1929– ), Nobel Prize in Medicine 1978; <a href="/wiki/Kofi_Annan" title="Kofi Annan">Kofi Annan</a> (1938– ), Nobel Peace Prize 2001.
</p><p>It has also hosted or graduated three Fields medal laureates: <a href="/wiki/Vaughan_Jones" title="Vaughan Jones">Vaughan Jones</a> (1952– ), <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> laureate in 1990, <a href="/wiki/Stanislav_Smirnov" title="Stanislav Smirnov">Stanislav Smirnov</a> (1970– ), <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> laureate in 2010 and <a href="/wiki/Martin_Hairer" title="Martin Hairer">Martin Hairer</a> (1975- ), <a href="/wiki/Fields_Medal" title="Fields Medal">Fields Medal</a> laureate in 2014.
</p>
