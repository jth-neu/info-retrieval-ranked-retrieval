<p>The <b>London Symphony Chorus</b> (abbreviated to LSC) is a large <a href="/wiki/Symphonic" class="mw-redirect" title="Symphonic">symphonic</a> concert <a href="/wiki/Choir" title="Choir">choir</a> based in London, UK, consisting of over 150 amateur singers, and is one of the major symphony choruses of the United Kingdom.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>   It was formed in 1966 as the <i>LSO Chorus</i> to complement the work of the <a href="/wiki/London_Symphony_Orchestra" title="London Symphony Orchestra">London Symphony Orchestra</a> (LSO).  The LSC is today an independent self-run organisation governed by a council of nine elected representatives.  It continues to maintain a close association with the LSO but also takes part in projects with other orchestras and organisations both in the UK and abroad.<sup id="cite_ref-lsc-about_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lsc-about-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The LSC performs mainly with the LSO at the <a href="/wiki/Barbican_Centre" title="Barbican Centre">Barbican Centre</a> in London as well as appearing at other concert venues around the UK and Europe and regularly at the <a href="/wiki/Avery_Fisher_Hall" class="mw-redirect" title="Avery Fisher Hall">Avery Fisher Hall</a>, New York.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Repertoire">Repertoire</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Symphony_Chorus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Repertoire">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The Chorus's core repertoire consists of the major nineteenth and twentieth century orchestral choral works.<sup id="cite_ref-lsc-repertoire_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lsc-repertoire-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The Chorus has performed and recorded works such as Elgar's <i><a href="/wiki/The_Dream_of_Gerontius" title="The Dream of Gerontius">The Dream of Gerontius</a></i>, Mahler's <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._2_(Mahler)" title="Symphony No. 2 (Mahler)">Second</a>, <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._3_(Mahler)" title="Symphony No. 3 (Mahler)">Third</a> and <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._8_(Mahler)" title="Symphony No. 8 (Mahler)">Eighth</a> Symphonies, Ravel's <i><a href="/wiki/Daphnis_et_Chlo%C3%A9" title="Daphnis et Chloé">Daphnis et Chloé</a></i>, Dvořák's <a href="/wiki/Stabat_Mater_(Dvo%C5%99%C3%A1k)" title="Stabat Mater (Dvořák)">Stabat Mater</a>, Janáček's <i><a href="/wiki/Glagolitic_Mass" title="Glagolitic Mass">Glagolitic Mass</a></i>, Britten's <i><a href="/wiki/War_Requiem" title="War Requiem">War Requiem</a></i>, Beethoven's <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._9_(Beethoven)" title="Symphony No. 9 (Beethoven)">Ninth Symphony</a> and <i><a href="/wiki/Missa_Solemnis_(Beethoven)" class="mw-redirect" title="Missa Solemnis (Beethoven)">Missa Solemnis</a></i>, Berlioz's <i><a href="/wiki/La_damnation_de_Faust" title="La damnation de Faust">La damnation de Faust</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Rom%C3%A9o_et_Juliette_(Berlioz)" title="Roméo et Juliette (Berlioz)">Roméo et Juliette</a></i>, Schoenberg's <i><a href="/wiki/Gurre-Lieder" title="Gurre-Lieder">Gurre-Lieder</a></i>, Brahms's <i><a href="/wiki/A_German_Requiem_(Brahms)" title="A German Requiem (Brahms)">Ein deutsches Requiem</a></i>, Rossini's <a href="/wiki/Stabat_Mater_(Rossini)" title="Stabat Mater (Rossini)">Stabat Mater</a>, Stravinsky's <i><a href="/wiki/Oedipus_rex_(opera)" title="Oedipus rex (opera)">Oedipus Rex</a></i> &amp; <i><a href="/wiki/Symphony_of_Psalms" title="Symphony of Psalms">Symphony of Psalms</a></i>, Tippett's <i><a href="/wiki/A_Child_of_Our_Time" title="A Child of Our Time">A Child of Our Time</a></i> and Verdi's <a href="/wiki/Requiem_(Verdi)" title="Requiem (Verdi)">Requiem</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-lsc.org.uk_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lsc.org.uk-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Chorus has also taken part in concert performances and commercial recordings of operas including Beethoven's <i><a href="/wiki/Fidelio" title="Fidelio">Fidelio</a></i>, Berlioz's <i><a href="/wiki/Les_Troyens" title="Les Troyens">Les Troyens</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Benvenuto_Cellini" title="Benvenuto Cellini">Benvenuto Cellini</a></i>, Bernstein's <i><a href="/wiki/Candide_(operetta)" title="Candide (operetta)">Candide</a></i>, Britten's <i><a href="/wiki/Peter_Grimes" title="Peter Grimes">Peter Grimes</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Billy_Budd" title="Billy Budd">Billy Budd</a></i>, Verdi's <i><a href="/wiki/Rigoletto_(opera)" class="mw-redirect" title="Rigoletto (opera)">Rigoletto</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Falstaff_(opera)" title="Falstaff (opera)">Falstaff</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Otello" title="Otello">Otello</a></i>, Wagner's <i><a href="/wiki/G%C3%B6tterd%C3%A4mmerung" title="Götterdämmerung">Götterdämmerung</a></i> and Richard Strauss's <i><a href="/wiki/Elektra_(opera)" title="Elektra (opera)">Elektra</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-lsc.org.uk_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lsc.org.uk-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Notable_recordings">Notable recordings</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=London_Symphony_Chorus&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Notable recordings">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The London Symphony Chorus's discography consists of over 140 recordings, and many of these recordings feature collaborations with the London Symphony Orchestra. Since 2000 the LSC has taken part in productions for the orchestra's new CD label, LSO Live which launched in 2000 and specialises in recordings of live performances in front of audiences. Among works recorded by the choir are <a href="/wiki/Johannes_Brahms" title="Johannes Brahms">Brahms</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/A_German_Requiem_(Brahms)" title="A German Requiem (Brahms)">German Requiem</a></i> (LSO, <a href="/wiki/Andr%C3%A9_Previn" title="André Previn">André Previn</a> 2000); <a href="/wiki/Gustav_Mahler" title="Gustav Mahler">Mahler</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Symphony_No._8_(Mahler)" title="Symphony No. 8 (Mahler)">Symphony No. 8</a> (CBSO, <a href="/wiki/Simon_Rattle" title="Simon Rattle">Simon Rattle</a> 2005); <a href="/wiki/Requiem_(Mozart)" title="Requiem (Mozart)">Mozart's Requiem</a> (LSO, Sir Colin Davis 2008); <a href="/wiki/War_Requiem" title="War Requiem">Britten: War Requiem</a> (LSO, Giandrea Noseda 2012); <a href="/wiki/Berlioz" class="mw-redirect" title="Berlioz">Berlioz</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Requiem_(Berlioz)" title="Requiem (Berlioz)">Requiem</a> (LSO, <a href="/wiki/Colin_Davis" title="Colin Davis">Colin Davis</a>, 2013); and <a href="/wiki/Weber" title="Weber">Weber</a>'s <i><a href="/wiki/Der_Freisch%C3%BCtz" title="Der Freischütz">Der Freischütz</a></i> (LSO, Sir Colin Davis, 2013).<sup id="cite_ref-lsc-discography_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lsc-discography-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Notable solo artists who have featured on LSC releases include <a href="/wiki/Ian_Bostridge" title="Ian Bostridge">Ian Bostridge</a>, <a href="/w/index.php?title=Simon_Keenleyside&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Simon Keenleyside (page does not exist)">Simon Keenleyside</a>,<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Felicity_Palmer" title="Felicity Palmer">Felicity Palmer</a> and <a href="/wiki/Anne_Sofie_von_Otter" title="Anne Sofie von Otter">Anne Sofie von Otter</a>.
