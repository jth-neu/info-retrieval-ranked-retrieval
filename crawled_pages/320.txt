<p><b>Yahoo! Japan Corporation</b><span style="font-weight: normal">&#32;(<span lang="ja">ヤフー株式会社</span><span style="display:none">,</span>&#32;<i>Yafū Kabushiki-gaisha</i>)</span> is a <a href="/wiki/Japan" title="Japan">Japanese</a> <a href="/wiki/Internet" title="Internet">internet</a> company originally formed as a <a href="/wiki/Joint_venture" title="Joint venture">joint venture</a> between the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">American</a> internet company <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a> (now <a href="/wiki/Altaba" title="Altaba">Altaba</a>) and the Japanese company <a href="/wiki/SoftBank" class="mw-redirect" title="SoftBank">SoftBank</a>. It is headquartered at <a href="/w/index.php?title=Kioi_Tower&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Kioi Tower (page does not exist)">Kioi Tower</a> in the <a href="/w/index.php?title=Tokyo_Garden_Terrace_Kioicho&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Tokyo Garden Terrace Kioicho (page does not exist)">Tokyo Garden Terrace Kioicho</a> complex in Kioicho, <a href="/wiki/Chiyoda,_Tokyo" title="Chiyoda, Tokyo">Chiyoda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Tokyo" title="Tokyo">Tokyo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Yahoo! Japan's web portal is the most visited website in Japan, and its internet services are mostly dominant in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo!_Japan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yahoo! and SoftBank formed Yahoo! Japan in January 1996 to set up the first <a href="/wiki/Web_portal" title="Web portal">web portal</a> in Japan. Yahoo! Japan went live on April 1, 1996.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Yahoo! Japan was listed on <a href="/wiki/JASDAQ_Securities_Exchange" title="JASDAQ Securities Exchange">JASDAQ</a> in November 1997. In January 2000, it became the first stock in Japanese history to trade for more than ¥100 million per share. The company was listed on the <a href="/wiki/Tokyo_Stock_Exchange" title="Tokyo Stock Exchange">Tokyo Stock Exchange</a> in October 2003 and became part of the <a href="/wiki/Nikkei_225" title="Nikkei 225">Nikkei 225</a> <a href="/wiki/Stock_market_index" title="Stock market index">stock market index</a> in 2005.
</p><p>Yahoo! Japan acquired the <a href="/wiki/Naming_rights" title="Naming rights">naming rights</a> for the <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_Dome" class="mw-redirect" title="Fukuoka Dome">Fukuoka Dome</a> in 2005, renaming the dome as the "Fukuoka Yahoo! Japan Dome". The "Yahoo Dome" is the home field for the <a href="/wiki/Fukuoka_SoftBank_Hawks" title="Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks">Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks</a>, a professional <a href="/wiki/Baseball" title="Baseball">baseball</a> team majority owned by SoftBank.
</p><p>Since 2010, Yahoo! Japan's search engine has been based on Google's search technology. In exchange, Google receives user activity data from Yahoo! Japan's various products.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, <a href="/wiki/Verizon_Communications" title="Verizon Communications">Verizon Communications</a> purchased the core internet business of America-based <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a>, and merged them into <a href="/wiki/Oath,_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="Oath, Inc.">Oath, Inc.</a> Yahoo! Japan was not affected. It continued as a joint venture between Softbank and what remained of Yahoo! Inc., renamed <a href="/wiki/Altaba" title="Altaba">Altaba</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Yahoo! had been declining in popularity and economically since the late 2000s, but the situation has been quite the opposite for Yahoo! Japan, which continues to dominate Japan's internet industry.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>  Following the sale, Yahoo! Japan will continue to use the name "Yahoo!" <a href="/wiki/License" title="License">under license</a> from Verizon Communications.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Industry_body_affiliation">Industry body affiliation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo!_Japan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Industry body affiliation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yahoo! Japan was a founding member of <a href="/wiki/Rakuten" title="Rakuten">Rakuten</a> CEO <a href="/wiki/Hiroshi_Mikitani" title="Hiroshi Mikitani">Hiroshi Mikitani</a>'s Japan e-business association in February 2010, but after Rakuten withdrew from the <a href="/wiki/Japan_Business_Federation" title="Japan Business Federation">Japan Business Federation (Keidanren)</a> in June 2011 and made moves to become the Japan Association of New Economy as a rival to Keidanren, Yahoo! Japan withdrew from the e-business association in March 2012. It joined Keidanren in July 2012.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Design">Design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo!_Japan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Yahoo! Japan continues to use the classic website layout and <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!#Logos_and_themes" title="Yahoo!">logo</a> that was used by Yahoo! internationally before modern redesigns in 2009 and 2013, respectively. However, it is translated into <a href="/wiki/Japanese_language" title="Japanese language">Japanese</a>.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Services">Services</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo!_Japan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Services">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="Yahoo.21_Japan_Mail"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Yahoo!_Japan_Mail">Yahoo! Japan Mail</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Yahoo!_Japan&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Yahoo! Japan Mail">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><b>Yahoo! Japan Mail</b> maintains the classic look of <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Mail" title="Yahoo! Mail">Yahoo! Mail</a>, but remains a separate service operated in Japan. Another notable change is the 10 GB storage limit, in contrast to Yahoo! Mail's 1 TB of storage and its former unlimited-storage offering.
</p>
