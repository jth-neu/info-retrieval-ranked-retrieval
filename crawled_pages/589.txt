<p>The <b>Ruskin School of Art</b>, known as the Ruskin, is an <a href="/wiki/Art_school" title="Art school">art school</a> at the <a href="/wiki/University_of_Oxford" title="University of Oxford">University of Oxford</a>, England.<sup id="cite_ref-hibbert_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-hibbert-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> It is part of Oxford's <a href="/wiki/Humanities_Division,_University_of_Oxford" class="mw-redirect" title="Humanities Division, University of Oxford">Humanities Division</a>. 
</p><p>Operating across two sites, the School provides undergraduate and postgraduate qualifications in the production and study of visual art and the subject is taught as a living element of contemporary culture with a broad range of historical and theoretical references.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> The Head of School is Dr Anthony Gardner. 
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Ruskin">Ruskin</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Ruskin_School_of_Drawing_and_Fine_Art&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Ruskin">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The School can trace its origins to 1871 when <a href="/wiki/John_Ruskin" title="John Ruskin">John Ruskin</a> founded a drawing school in the University Galleries (subsequently the <a href="/wiki/Ashmolean_Museum_of_Art_and_Archaeology" class="mw-redirect" title="Ashmolean Museum of Art and Archaeology">Ashmolean Museum of Art and Archaeology</a>) to encourage artisanship and technical skills. The Ruskin remained at the Ashmolean until 1975 when it moved to 74 <a href="/wiki/High_Street,_Oxford" title="High Street, Oxford">High Street</a>. It also occupies a further building at 128 Bullingdon Road opened in 2015.
</p><p>The School was originally called the Ruskin School of Drawing. Fine Art was added as a discrete focus in 1945. In 2014 the school was renamed the Ruskin School of Art.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Slade_School_of_Fine_Art" title="Slade School of Fine Art">Slade School of Fine Art</a> relocated to the Ruskin for the duration of the <a href="/wiki/Second_World_War" class="mw-redirect" title="Second World War">Second World War</a>.
</p>
