<p><b>I'mHalal.com</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Search_engine" class="mw-redirect" title="Search engine">search engine</a> built on top of social-cultural Islamic<sup id="cite_ref-HP_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HP-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> values. The search engines algorithm differed from regular search engines because the relevancy of the results was based on the culture, mindset and lifestyle of Muslims. The engine did not index content such as <a href="/wiki/Pornography" title="Pornography">pornography</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nudity" title="Nudity">nudity</a>, or anything else that might be "<a href="/wiki/Haraam" class="mw-redirect" title="Haraam">haram</a>".<sup id="cite_ref-IT_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IT-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-tjp_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tjp-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The founders of I'mHalal.com, launched the search engine to attract more Middle Easterners to the World Wide Web by providing all households a relevant and safe search portal to gather information and explore the web. The company was expecting a large percentage of their userbase from the <a href="/wiki/Middle_East" title="Middle East">Middle East</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-tds_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-tds-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The website received mostly positive comments from Muslims,<sup id="cite_ref-IO_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-IO-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The search engine was going to opensource a part of its source by publishing an API<sup id="cite_ref-nu_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-nu-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> It was subsequently closed in 2011 due to financial difficulties.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In late 2011, I'mHalal.com closed its search service due to lack of funds.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
