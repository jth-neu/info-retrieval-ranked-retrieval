<p><b>Forsythe Pendleton</b> "<b>Jughead</b>" <b>Jones III</b> is one of the fictional characters created by <a href="/wiki/Bob_Montana" title="Bob Montana">Bob Montana</a> and <a href="/wiki/John_L._Goldwater" title="John L. Goldwater">John L. Goldwater</a> in <a href="/wiki/Archie_Comics" title="Archie Comics">Archie Comics</a> who first appeared in the first Archie story, from <i><a href="/wiki/Pep_Comics" title="Pep Comics">Pep Comics</a></i> #22 (December 1941). He is the son of John Jones II; in one of the early Archie newspaper comic strips, he himself is identified as John Jugworth Jones III (and in one strip, likely due to <a href="/wiki/Continuity_(fiction)" title="Continuity (fiction)">continuity</a> error, as Forsythe Van Jones). He has a white <a href="/wiki/Old_English_Sheepdog" title="Old English Sheepdog">sheepdog</a> named Hot Dog and a younger sister, <a href="/wiki/Jellybean_Jones" class="mw-redirect" title="Jellybean Jones">Forsythia "Jellybean" Jones</a>. 
</p><p>Jughead (sometimes shortened to Jug or Juggie) is the best friend of <a href="/wiki/Archie_Andrews" title="Archie Andrews">Archie Andrews</a>. Jughead is a smart, sharp-tongued, laid-back, easy going and eccentric high school student. He's obsessed with eating food, and in some storylines is <a href="/wiki/Asexuality" title="Asexuality">asexual</a>. Most see him as being lazy. He can be identified by his long nose, half-closed eyes, "S" sweatshirt, and crown-like button <a href="/wiki/Beanie_(seamed_cap)" title="Beanie (seamed cap)">beanie</a> hat, called a <a href="/wiki/Whoopee_cap" title="Whoopee cap">whoopee cap</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-blogspot1_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blogspot1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Jughead is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Cole_Sprouse" title="Cole Sprouse">Cole Sprouse</a> in the live action series <i><a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(2017_TV_series)" title="Riverdale (2017 TV series)">Riverdale</a></i>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Concept_and_creation">Concept and creation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Concept and creation">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Bob_Montana" title="Bob Montana">Bob Montana</a> stated that Jughead was a character he imagined, unlike other characters in the series who were based on people he knew.<sup id="cite_ref-Erickson92_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Erickson92-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Montana's widow, Peg Bertholet, stated that high school friend of Montana's named Skinny Linehan supposedly had some of Jughead's peculiar traits.<sup id="cite_ref-Erickson92_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Erickson92-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Bertholet has stated that the "S" insignia refers to a location called Skunk Hill in <a href="/wiki/Haverhill,_Massachusetts" title="Haverhill, Massachusetts">Haverhill, Massachusetts</a> which Montana turned into Squirrel Hill.<sup id="cite_ref-ew-fa_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ew-fa-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> The "S" alludes to a combination of the location and Montana's elementary school athletic team near Haverhill called the Tigers.<sup id="cite_ref-ew-fa_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ew-fa-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Bertholet has stated that "S" stands for "'Squirrel Hill Independent Tigers,' and you couldn't abbreviate it any other way."<sup id="cite_ref-ew-fa_3-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ew-fa-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History_and_character">History and character</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History and character">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jughead generally has a characteristic wry and <a href="/wiki/Sarcasm" title="Sarcasm">sarcastic</a> sense of humor. He is considered a bit of an oddity, but prefers his <a href="/wiki/Nonconformism" class="mw-redirect" title="Nonconformism">nonconformism</a> as opposed to going along with others' styles. His many quirks make him the butt of teasing and abuse from Reggie, Veronica, and even other classmates and teachers. Many episodes involve Reggie and Jughead trying to outdo one another with pranks and bets, and Jughead almost always comes out the victor. He is revealed to be extremely clever and creative when necessary and he often takes advantage of Reggie's and his other tormentors' weaknesses (and has fun all the while).
</p><p>In the earlier comics, a running gag involved various characters trying to discover Jughead's real name, while Jughead thwarted their efforts. In one story, <a href="/wiki/Archie_Andrews_(comics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Archie Andrews (comics)">Archie Andrews</a> and <a href="/wiki/Reggie_Mantle" title="Reggie Mantle">Reggie Mantle</a> go to the school office, where a woman tells them that Jughead's real first name is Steve. After Archie and Reggie leave the office, the audience learns that the woman is actually Jughead's aunt who has just lied as a favor to Jughead to help keep his real first name (Forsythe) hidden. In another story (issue #1 of "The Jughead Jones Comics Digest," June 1977) we find out he is named after his ancestor who is an American hero. For a brief time, Jughead started to use his given name in honor of his ancestor. After learning that this ancestor was married nine times, Jughead reverted to his nickname. In the Little Archie series, his real name is revealed by <a href="/wiki/Miss_Grundy" title="Miss Grundy">Miss Grundy</a> at the start of class.
He also believes his name attracts girls as in a strip, his name was mentioned and it made the girls crazed for him.
</p><p>Another mystery that follows Jughead's character is the meaning of the "S" on his sweatshirt.  This remains a mystery to this day,  although many stories have hinted at a meaning. In Jughead #30 (1992), when his psychiatrist, Sara, asked him "why an 'S'?" he replied "I dunno!  My third cousin was called skinny..." The triangular banner on the cover of issue 140 of <i>Archie &amp; Friends</i> shows that the "S" stands for <i>Silby</i>, as in Silby High School, which he attended for a few months as a freshman. At one time after his sweater was filled with holes due to what he thought was a moth when it was actually a chemical accident caused in lab class, he reveals that he simply likes the letter S and finds it "compatible," because the letter can stand for "soup, sandwich, steak and all kinds of goodies!" after his friends ask him. Betty rolls her eyes at the explanation and adds, "S stands for "sorry I asked"!" Another theory is that he is a non-conformist so when everyone wears "R" which stands for Riverdale he just goes with the next letter "S".
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Food_obsession">Food obsession</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Food obsession">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jughead is known for his love of food, especially <a href="/wiki/Hamburger" title="Hamburger">hamburgers</a>, and his ability to consume absurdly large quantities in a single sitting without getting sick or gaining weight, although he often sports a pot-belly immediately after a particularly large meal.  Jughead is a preferred customer of most of <a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(Archie_Comics)" title="Riverdale (Archie Comics)">Riverdale's</a> food establishments, especially <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#Pop_Tate" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Pop Tate</a>'s Chock'lit Shoppe, except when he is behind on paying his usually lengthy tab.  In one story, he was given a "Restaurant Club Card" and ate out at high-class restaurants until he found out how much interest the card charged, and Pop Tate loaned him the money on the condition that he eat exclusively at the Chok'lit Shoppe.  His ability to eat so much food without gaining weight is attributed to a very rare and implausibly high <a href="/wiki/Metabolism" title="Metabolism">metabolism</a>.  He once claimed that he weighed 300 pounds after a meal, although he is usually portrayed as slim and healthy.
</p><p>Jughead's special abilities concerning food extend to being able to identify food in a sealed can by smell, and being able to detect the slightest flaws in food preparation by taste.  As such, he is a respected food critic, as well as a gourmet chef himself. One time, when he sought out <a href="/wiki/Miss_Grundy" title="Miss Grundy">Miss Grundy</a>'s help with his creative writing, she suggested that he write about a subject he knew, leading to the "Forsythe P. Jones Cookbook".
</p><p>Jughead often participates in eating contests, usually winning outright, or else easily coming from behind after comic distractions, and often with room to spare while the competitor retires unwell.  In a citywide eating contest, he ate a colossal burger made up of sixteen different burgers. Jughead also set two world records for eating pizza; one for speed-eating a pizza, and another for eating the most pizzas in one sitting: twelve.  Jughead's appetite is so large that even if he eats shortly before such contests, it does not affect his performance.  For example, a rival high school once sent a girl, Jane Dough, to take Jughead to various restaurants and feed him as much as possible. Amazingly, Jughead still managed to win the contest.  He explained to Jane that top athletes need to train constantly in order to stay in shape, and she actually helped him "train" for the contest.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>One running gag depicts Jughead visiting restaurants that promise "all you can eat" special deals, with the devastated restaurateur closing down as Jughead leaves.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Despite his appetite, Jughead is not known to be stingy with food, or to let others go hungry.  When a store selling <a href="/wiki/Penny_candy" class="mw-redirect" title="Penny candy">penny candy</a> opened in Riverdale, he spent his whole allowance on a huge bag of candy, only to meet a penniless young boy outside the shop. Jughead wrestled with his desire, but he chose to give the candy to the boy.  In a similar story, Jughead met a homeless person who liked the same kind of pizza as he did, and decided to give him the pizza he had just purchased. In another story, after winning three pizzas a week for six months for being a pizzeria's one-millionth customer, he donated them all to a soup kitchen where he volunteered.
</p><p>Because of his well-known food obsession, Jughead was shown eating a hamburger on the facades of the short-lived <a href="/wiki/Archie%27s_Family_Restaurants" title="Archie&#39;s Family Restaurants">Archie's Family Restaurants</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-How_the_Industry_Works_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-How_the_Industry_Works-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Jughead.27s_hat"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Jughead's_hat">Jughead's hat</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Jughead&#039;s hat">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jughead is almost always seen wearing his trademark beanie with both a round and square pin. This type of crown-shaped cap, called a <a href="/wiki/Whoopee_cap" title="Whoopee cap">whoopee cap</a>, was popular among boys in the 1930s and 1940s. It was made from a man's felt <a href="/wiki/Fedora" title="Fedora">fedora</a> hat with the brim trimmed in a zig-zag and turned up.<sup id="cite_ref-blogspot1_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-blogspot1-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Bottle caps could be 'pinned' in place using the cap's removable cork lining. In the 1920s and 1930s, college freshmen were sometimes required to wear them for initiation purposes, and such caps were often worn by mechanics.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Similar caps have appeared on other comic book/strip, cartoon, and children’s book characters such as <a href="/wiki/List_of_Little_Lulu_characters#Main_characters" title="List of Little Lulu characters">Eddie Stimson</a> in Little Lulu, <a href="/wiki/Little_Audrey#Comic_books" title="Little Audrey">Melvin Wisenheimer</a> in Little Audrey, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Berenstain_Bears_characters" title="List of Berenstain Bears characters">Skuzz</a> in The Berenstain Bears, and <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedia_Brown#Recurring_characters" title="Encyclopedia Brown">Bugs Meany</a> in Encyclopedia Brown as well as on <a href="/wiki/Goober_Pyle" title="Goober Pyle">Goober Pyle</a> on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Andy_Griffith_Show" title="The Andy Griffith Show">The Andy Griffith Show</a></i> and <a href="/wiki/Jeff_Goldblum" title="Jeff Goldblum">Jeff Goldblum</a>'s character "Freak #1" in <i><a href="/wiki/Death_Wish_(1974_film)" title="Death Wish (1974 film)">Death Wish</a></i>. Jughead's hat was recolored in black in the <a href="/wiki/Filmation" title="Filmation">Filmation</a> cartoons and pink in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Archies" title="The New Archies">The New Archies</a></i>. Over the course of the character's publication, Jughead's hat has evolved from a modified fedora to its more recognisable "crown" appearance.
</p><p>Though some view the hat as strange and immature, Jughead considers it a good luck charm and when it is taken from him, misfortune comes his way. While on the school baseball team, Jughead was pulled aside and reprimanded by school principal Mr. Weatherbee for wearing the hat instead of a proper baseball uniform cap. Jughead complied, but then started performing so badly that Weatherbee was forced to relent.
</p><p>In the "Time Police" comic, there is a double of his beanie given to him by an unknown benefactor (later revealed to be himself) that allows him to travel in time.
</p><p>Some stories showed him to wear a unique pin on his hat which attracts many girls to him, so he hid it to avoid the crowd of girls rushing him.
</p><p>The hat also seems to define part of Jughead's personality. One story has the gang try to convince him to try a different hat, but it's revealed that whatever hat he tried on changed his personality to suit the hat. When they had him try on a detective hat, he zeroed in on clues that would have gotten Archie in trouble with Betty and Veronica, and took a hair from Reggie's sweater that implied that he sneaked a date with Midge behind Moose's back (the look on both of their faces imply he was on to something). Jughead also stated that he felt naked without a hat. Finally, Veronica gave him a special hat to try on. When he did, everyone said it suited him (it was an ugly thing but looked comical. She said she was saving it for a Mad Hatter party). Jughead tried wearing it for a day at school, but scared several students, teachers, and even Miss Beazly the cafeteria lady) they begged him to go back to his trademark hat. When he asked what they'd do if he did, they offered him a platter of hamburgers and he happily agreed.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Family_and_friends">Family and friends</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Family and friends">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jughead's best friend is <a href="/wiki/Archie_Andrews_(comics)" class="mw-redirect" title="Archie Andrews (comics)">Archie Andrews</a>, despite their personality difference. Archie was the first person that Jughead met upon moving to Riverdale, and he is often dragged into Archie's schemes and antics. Jughead is usually the first one to bail Archie out of trouble (though some times he only makes things worse). Jughead, extremely loyal, is willing to do almost anything to help his friend, something that Archie occasionally takes for granted.
</p><p>Reggie is another one of Jughead's close friends, though his and Jughead's relationship is defined by their constant competition. Reggie never loses an opportunity to insult Jughead ("needle-nose" being his favorite nickname) and Jughead often responds with tricks to aggravate Reggie. Though it often appears they hate each other, and neither will admit otherwise, they really do care for one another. Jughead will even help Reggie escape being injured when <a href="/wiki/Moose_Mason" title="Moose Mason">Moose Mason</a> is angry with him.
</p><p>While  Betty is a great cook, which is one of the reasons that he likes to hang out with her, Jughead also provides a shoulder to cry on whenever Betty has trouble with Archie or Veronica. One of the few things Jughead does not like about Archie is how he takes Betty for granted. There have been a few stories where Jughead showed a sincere brother and sister type relationship with Betty. In the show <i><a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(2017_TV_series)" title="Riverdale (2017 TV series)">Riverdale</a></i>, Betty and Jughead are dating. Writers and fans of Archie Comics sometimes comment<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> that if they were to pair Jughead up with one of the girls, it would be Betty. In one comic, Jughead tells Betty that if he were to ever kiss a girl willingly, it would be her. 
</p><p>Jughead and <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Lodge" title="Veronica Lodge">Veronica Lodge</a> are constantly arguing. Veronica cannot stand his laid-back attitude witticisms, and Jughead enjoys teasing her and making her lose her temper with clever insults. Although at times, he relishes causing her to get angry because sometimes he sees her as an uncaring selfish snob. He once told Veronica he was "mad with passion" for her, and began popping up wherever she went, to get her off his back after she criticized him publicly. They were once cast together in a school play, which required them to kiss. Due to the way Jughead kissed her, she was caught up in the moment and fell in love with him for a while. However, Jughead managed to get her over her crush, with the help of a garlic and onion sandwich. <sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Jughead's other friends include <a href="/wiki/Dilton_Doiley" title="Dilton Doiley">Dilton Doiley</a> and <a href="/wiki/Moose_Mason" title="Moose Mason">Moose Mason</a>. Other than Dilton, Jughead probably gets along with Moose better than the other boys because of his non-confrontational attitude (and his lack of interest in Moose's girlfriend <a href="/wiki/Midge_Klump" title="Midge Klump">Midge</a>). Jughead’s nerdy ways and lack of interest in sports and girls probably account for how well he gets along with Dilton.
</p><p>Jughead's family includes his father, also named Forsythe, his mother Gladys, and in later comics, his younger sister Jellybean. He also has many eccentric relatives including slightly-addled Uncle Herman, or "Doc Jones", a daffy and slightly pompous <a href="/wiki/Inventor" title="Inventor">inventor</a> whose creations usually wreak havoc on Jughead and/or his friends and his lookalike younger cousin, Souphead. Other one-time relatives appear frequently.  Jughead also tells many stories of his ancestors, who prove to be quite as interesting as himself. A 1970s "Archie" cartoon episode featured Jughead's paternal grandparents—<i>both</i> of whom resemble Jughead.
</p><p>Another acquaintance of Jughead's is Trula Twyst, Riverdale High's budding pop psychologist, who is constantly trying to analyze Jughead to determine what makes him so odd. The two share a strange love/hate relationship that would have gone much smoother had they not met the way they did- Trula tricked Jughead into dating her in order to become more popular.
</p><p>In one story, Jughead claimed his great-grandmother was a <a href="/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States" title="Native Americans in the United States">Native American</a> "from the Pawtuxet tribe," despite the fact that the <a href="/wiki/Patuxet" title="Patuxet">Patuxet</a> were killed off by 1622.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> In another story from the same era, he claimed his great-great-grandfather was "an <a href="/wiki/Eskimo" title="Eskimo">eskimo</a>," who was "the only cop north of Nome...'Ol' Bluenose Jones' they called him!" <sup id="cite_ref-Giant_Box_of_Comics_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Giant_Box_of_Comics-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> It is likely that neither of these claims are meant to be taken seriously. In both stories he also used his claims to make fun of Veronica, which suggests more that his comments were meant as jokes, since Jughead has a history of making up fibs to tease Veronica and other opponents. 
</p><p>In the "Mad Magazine" universe, Jughead's doppelgänger is nicknamed Bottleneck. Bottleneck wears a beanie that resembles a broken bottleneck. His best friend (and fellow juvenile delinquent) is Starchie.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Miniseries">Miniseries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Miniseries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Alternate_universes_in_Archie_Comics#Jughead&#39;s_Time_Police" title="Alternate universes in Archie Comics"><i>Jughead's Time Police</i></a> was a series that began in 1990 featuring Jughead as a hero of the 29th century and a member of the Time Police, an organization that ensures history to remain the same for the future's sake. In this series, the beanie gives Jughead the ability to time travel by thinking. With his supervisor, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#January_McAndrews" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Marshal January McAndrews</a>, Jughead repairs disturbances in the past.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Other spin-offs include <a href="/wiki/Alternate_universes_in_Archie_Comics#Jughead&#39;s_Diner" title="Alternate universes in Archie Comics"><i>Jughead's Diner</i></a> in 1990, where he ran a <a href="/wiki/Diner" title="Diner">diner</a> with an eclectic cast of patrons; and <i>Jughead's Fantasy</i>, resulting from <i>Jughead's Folly</i>, lasted for three issues and featured Jughead's dreams of various <a href="/wiki/Alter-ego" class="mw-redirect" title="Alter-ego">alter-egos</a>, including "Sir Jugalot", "Peter Goon--Private Eye", and "Son of Hercules".<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Jughead also featured in <a href="/wiki/Alternate_universes_in_Archie_Comics#Explorers_of_the_Unknown" title="Alternate universes in Archie Comics"><i>Explorers of the Unknown</i></a>, playing Squint, a daredevil escape artist.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sexuality">Sexuality</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Sexuality">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jughead is known for his disinterest in romantic relationships with girls, setting him apart from Archie and Reggie. Jughead's philosophy on romantic relationships, gained from observing Archie's romantic entanglements, is that dating complicates a guy's life and deprives him of cash that could be used to buy burgers. This often attracts girls rather than repels them. His most ardent admirer is <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Muggs" title="Ethel Muggs">Ethel Muggs</a>, an awkward but very friendly girl who chases after Jughead at every opportunity, despite Jughead's constant and blunt refusals.  Recent story-lines have shown a decrease in her obsession with him, and even show her dating other guys, surprisingly making Jughead jealous. Jughead secretly enjoys the attention, although he claims he puts up with Ethel's company only if she cooks for him. He has shown romantic interest in her on rare occasions, and eventually fell in love with her in the Life With Archie: The Married Life series where the "Archie Marries Veronica" stories ended with Jughead and Ethel's wedding.
</p><p>In 2016, Jughead's orientation was confirmed to be <a href="/wiki/Asexuality" title="Asexuality">asexual</a><sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> in the stories of Chip Zdarsky (and later Ryan North and Mark Waid) for the <i><a href="/wiki/Jughead_(comic_book)" title="Jughead (comic book)">Jughead</a></i> comics as part of the <i><a href="/wiki/New_Riverdale" title="New Riverdale">New Riverdale</a></i> line.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Zdarsky said of his run on the book that "the next writer could make him discover girls or boys or both and that's totally fine. There have been iterations of Jughead over the decades where he HAS been interested in girls, so there's room to play around if someone was inclined. For me though, I like an asexual Jughead."<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> He later tweeted that he viewed Jughead as "ace and probably demi-romantic, but for the purposes of his teen years, aro."<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Both <i>Riverdale</i> fans in the asexual community<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> and Cole Sprouse,<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> who portrays Jughead in The CW's <i>Riverdale</i>, have complained of asexual erasure in mainstream television and expressed desire to see the character's asexuality explored. However, Sprouse later noted that the Jughead Zdarsky created is the only asexual version so far. At the same time, he said that Jughead is aromantic in the classic Archie stories, "a different thing [from asexuality] but deserves attention as well."<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>A group of girls formed the UGAJ (United Girls Against Jughead) in an effort to get him interested in romance<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>, using methods such as computers<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> or food,<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> though ultimately failing.
</p><p>For his first forty years or so, Jughead often claimed girls were despicable, and even as late as the 1990s.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (March 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> A shift in societal attitudes has caused later writers and fans to try different ways of explaining this, such as past heartbreak from childhood romance, bad impressions from Veronica and Ethel, and most recently in the 2015 reboot, <a href="/wiki/Asexuality" title="Asexuality">asexuality</a>. There has never been one consistent canon explanation for Jughead's avoidance of girls and dating.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>  In one story, Jughead declares, "I don't hate girls—I just love food more!"<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>  In the story "Phood Phobia", Archie and <a href="/wiki/Dilton_Doiley" title="Dilton Doiley">Dilton Doiley</a> discover a hidden layer to this: it turns out that Jughead is actually nervous around girls, and he turns to food for comfort.  When confronted with this, Jughead is stunned; finding out that girls are the reason he enjoys food so much makes him lose his appetite.  In another story, when Jughead saw that <a href="/wiki/Miss_Grundy" title="Miss Grundy">Miss Grundy</a> was upset on her birthday, he rallied the other students to cheer her up.
</p><p>In "A Lass From The Past", written by Nate Butler and first appearing in <i>Jughead</i> #5 (April 1988), Jughead explains the reason he does not chase after girls is because of a childhood heartbreak, sustained before he moved to <a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(Archie_Comics)" title="Riverdale (Archie Comics)">Riverdale</a>.  Little Jughead befriended Joani and they had a <a href="/wiki/Puppy_love" title="Puppy love">puppy love</a> relationship.  Jughead's family moved to Riverdale and he had to leave Joani behind.  Determined to not endure heartbreak again, he swore off girls, keeping little Joani's picture in his wallet as a reminder.  That changed when Debbie moved to town (in "Jughead's Journal," written by Rod Ollerenshaw and also first appearing in Jughead #5), and Jughead began to overcome his old heartbreak as they started dating.  However, Joani suddenly visits town.  Now a young woman, she tells Jughead that she never forgot him and still has a crush on him, and they share their first kiss.  Before Jughead decides whether he wants to pursue a relationship with Joani, she leaves him, but with a promise that they will be together again. The comic even had Jughead say, "Sometimes, life is just more interesting than burgers and shakes."<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>  For a while, Debbie and Joani formed Jughead's own love triangle, but fans did not like it, so both girls disappeared and Jughead reverted to his classic independent lifestyle.  A few years later, a girl named <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#Trula_Twyst" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Trula Twyst</a> appeared. She tends to drive Jughead crazy with her ability to predict his next moves, and they developed a <a href="/wiki/Love-hate_relationship" class="mw-redirect" title="Love-hate relationship">love-hate relationship</a>.
</p><p>Another thread that has run through stories in recent years involves a psychic experience he is supposed to have had, caused by an odd pin he wears on his hat.  In the early 1990s, Jughead had three girlfriends: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#Debbie_and_Joani" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Debbie</a>, <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#Debbie_and_Joani" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Joani</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Minor_characters_in_Archie_Comics#January_McAndrews" class="mw-redirect" title="Minor characters in Archie Comics">January McAndrews</a>.  The latter is Archie's descendant in the future, who starred with Jughead in <a href="/wiki/Alternate_universes_in_Archie_Comics#Jughead&#39;s_Time_Police" title="Alternate universes in Archie Comics">Jughead's Time Police</a>, a short-lived spinoff comic about their adventures traveling through time.
</p><p>Archie Comics trademarked the term 'Bughead', the name created by fans of the relationship between Betty and Jughead in both comics and the CW Riverdale. Betty and Jughead are canon, romantically, so far only in the 'Riverdale' universe, though Archie Comics has introduced their sleuthing relationship and subsequent ship name (#bughead) into their run of Riverdale tie-in comics.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Musical_interests">Musical interests</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Musical interests">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Jughead was the <a href="/wiki/Drummer" title="Drummer">drummer</a> for <a href="/wiki/The_Archies" title="The Archies">The Archies</a>. In one four-part story, which included many flashbacks of Jughead's life, Archie had commented that the reason he'd chosen the position of drummer was that he was too introverted to play at the front of the stage. Another reason was that he could put food in his drums to eat while he played.
</p><p>He has also professed a love for <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> music, once detailed in an issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Jughead_Magazine" class="mw-redirect" title="Jughead Magazine">Jughead Magazine</a></i> where he develops an obsession with an obscure <a href="/wiki/Jazz" title="Jazz">jazz</a> <a href="/wiki/Drum" title="Drum">drummer</a> named "Crazy" Willie Jim. After collecting his records, Jughead finally met Jim, who was now very old, ailing and reclusive, playing on a street corner. Jughead convinced Jim that he was trustworthy and they became fast friends. Jim even played with The Archies at one of their gigs. Jim died very soon after, so Jughead sat in with Jim's friends on the corner to play one last, mournful version of "<a href="/wiki/St._James_Infirmary_Blues" title="St. James Infirmary Blues">St. James Infirmary Blues</a>".
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Special_abilities">Special abilities</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Special abilities">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In spite of his reputation as slow and lazy, Jughead is frequently shown to be extremely intelligent, and often surprises his skeptical friends (notably <a href="/wiki/Reggie_Mantle" title="Reggie Mantle">Reggie Mantle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Lodge" title="Veronica Lodge">Veronica Lodge</a>) with his vast knowledge on a wide variety of subjects, including history, <a href="/wiki/Shakespeare" class="mw-redirect" title="Shakespeare">Shakespeare</a>, sports, and science. In several issues, Jughead has demonstrated that his IQ is well above average. He is an extremely good student, which exasperates his teachers when he sometimes pays them no attention.
</p><p>His intelligence varies from story to story. In many cases, he has trouble keeping decent grades, but was once given an award for being the best student in the school. Once, in <a href="/wiki/Little_Archie" title="Little Archie">Little Archie</a>, it was revealed that as a student, Jughead was second only to <a href="/wiki/Dilton_Doiley" title="Dilton Doiley">Dilton Doiley</a>. His intelligence is displayed by his sharp wit, the occasional deep insight, and the odd chance he gets to upstage or outsmart <a href="/wiki/Reggie_Mantle" title="Reggie Mantle">Reggie Mantle</a>. In one issue, <a href="/wiki/Mr._Weatherbee" title="Mr. Weatherbee">Mr. Weatherbee</a> tries to cut down on Jughead's food consumption, only to realize that Jughead's brain stops functioning without massive amounts of food. Professor <a href="/wiki/Flutesnoot" class="mw-redirect" title="Flutesnoot">Flutesnoot</a> implies that Jughead's brain burns all the calories, which keeps him thin.
</p><p>Jughead is also a talented artist, and refers to his works as his "Dipsy Doodles", which feature in one-page comic strips. Often, what he paints comes to life or becomes a real, three-dimensional object. Another recurring gag, featured in the 1960s, was "Professor Jughead", where he would wear a gown and mortarboard and lecture to his fellow teenagers about subjects he considered relevant. However, the content of the lesson was often nonsensical and useless in real life. <i>Professor Jughead's Loony Laws</i> was a strip in which he presented various <a href="/wiki/Dumb_laws" title="Dumb laws">dumb laws</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> One strip explores the fact that Jughead is seldom drawn with his eyes open by noting that Jughead has the unusual ability to "see" with his eyes completely shut, and not see with them open.
</p><p>In his spare time, he enjoys playing <a href="/wiki/Video_games" class="mw-redirect" title="Video games">video games</a>, reading <a href="/wiki/Comic_books" class="mw-redirect" title="Comic books">comic books</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Skateboarding" title="Skateboarding">skateboarding</a>. It was also noted in one issue that he has an interest in Japanese <a href="/wiki/Anime" title="Anime">anime</a> and <a href="/wiki/Manga" title="Manga">manga</a> as well, with titles such as <a href="/wiki/Sailor_Moon" title="Sailor Moon">Sailor Lunar</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dragon_Ball_Z" title="Dragon Ball Z">Dragon Tall 33</a>, <a href="/wiki/Robotech" title="Robotech">Tech Robo</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Magic_Knight_Rayearth" title="Magic Knight Rayearth">Tragic Knight Sayearth</a>. In the late 1980s, Jughead became obsessed with the skateboarding subculture, and his interests and taste in fashion were revamped to fit this. A number of short-lived characters were added to the <i>Jughead</i> comics, including his paraplegic African-American crush <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#Anita_Chavita" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Anita</a>; blind rival for Ethel's affections <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#Jeffrey" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Jeff</a> and his guide dog Spike; and heavy metal band <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#The_Potholes" title="List of Archie Comics characters">The Potholes</a>. However, this makeover only lasted for a few years.
</p><p>Jughead rarely takes an interest in sports, but has a few unexpected athletic talents. These are often side effects of his other activities. For example, he is a very fast runner due to his constant evasion of <a href="/wiki/Ethel_Muggs" title="Ethel Muggs">Ethel</a> and his determination to be at the front of the lunchline every day. Coach Kleats often tries to recruit him for various school teams, usually without longterm success. Over the years, he has been seen as a basketball player, baseball pitcher, martial artist, swimmer, dancer and gymnast.
</p><p>Many stories revolve around other characters making comments about Jughead's appetite. One story has Dilton Doiley making calculations to scientifically prove that it's impossible for Jughead to eat the way that he does, and even convinces Pop Tate to cook everything Jughead ate earlier to prove it. When the entire gang isn't paying attention, Jughead ate all the food and ended the story by saying that if they didn't believe it they could make the food and he'd eat it again. Another story has Jughead going against a guy from another group in an eating contest. The rival gang sends a girl to feed Jughead to fill him up the night before in an effort to cheat but he manages to win anyway. In still another, Archie expresses concern over Jughead's eating and lack of exercise, which prompts Reggie to show off lifting a single crate of Pop's bottles over his head. Jughead helps Pop by effortlessly lifting three crates at once and carrying them to the back room, stunning Archie and Reggie. Unnerving them even more, he rips out a stool that Pop said he could take for a souvenir (he was replacing his counter stools later that day) with one hand again with no effort. In that story, he says he eats to get strong, cracking up Archie and Reggie, but they change their mind when they see he's very strong, and even take up eating in an effort to get strong, with no success.
</p><p>Often, when Jughead attempts to join a team, he ends up with an undesirable menial task, such as water-boy or equipment manager. However, he often takes part in less strenuous school activities, such as the school newspaper, <i>the Blue and Gold</i>. His reputation as a food critic and his unbiased views of the school teams make him a valuable member of the staff. In some stories, Jughead has been shown to have supernatural abilities that are never seen again. These include controlling weather, giving the evil-eye, learning the skills presented in any book he reads, and predicting the future.
</p><p>In the "<a href="/wiki/Alternate_universes_in_Archie_Comics#Super_Teens" title="Alternate universes in Archie Comics">Super Teens</a>" stories (where several of the main <i>Archie</i> characters are secretly <a href="/wiki/Superheroes" class="mw-redirect" title="Superheroes">superheroes</a>), Jughead could become <b>Captain Hero</b>, gaining a caped costume and more muscular physique (varying from story to story), but retaining his crown-shaped cap. Captain Hero appeared when Jughead recites the magic incantation (similar to <a href="/wiki/Green_Lantern" title="Green Lantern">Green Lantern</a>'s oath):
</p><p><i>Teeny weeny magic beanie pointing towards the sky; give me muscle, power, vigor - form a super guy!</i>
</p><p>Captain Hero often seemed to possess just the right powers for the problem at hand. These were never completely defined, but he maintained certain "stock" superhuman powers, such as flight, enhanced strength and resistance to injury. Early on, Captain Hero was often the most serious and competent of the Super Teens, and the others would defer to his leadership, in contrast to Jughead's perceived status as Archie's sidekick and a generally lazy individual.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Hot_Dog">Hot Dog</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Hot Dog">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:202px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Jughd.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/6/6c/Jughd.jpg" width="200" height="155" class="thumbimage" data-file-width="200" data-file-height="155" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Jughd.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Jughead and Hot Dog</div></div></div>
<p>Hot Dog is a long-haired <a href="/wiki/Mixed-breed_dog" class="mw-redirect" title="Mixed-breed dog">mutt</a> who resembles an <a href="/wiki/Old_English_Sheepdog" title="Old English Sheepdog">Old English Sheepdog</a>. He belongs to Jughead, although when he first appeared in <i>Pep Comics</i> #224 (December 1968), he belonged to Archie. Hot Dog switched owners frequently in his early appearances, but was eventually given a permanent home at Jughead's.
</p><p>Hot Dog usually thinks like a <a href="/wiki/Human" title="Human">human</a>, in that his thoughts are presented in <a href="/wiki/Thought_bubble" class="mw-redirect" title="Thought bubble">thought bubbles</a>. He is lazy, constantly hungry, and, much like Jughead, has a dislike for <a href="/wiki/Reggie_Mantle" title="Reggie Mantle">Reggie Mantle</a>.
</p><p>Hot Dog is usually considered a member of <a href="/wiki/List_of_Archie_Comics_characters#Main_characters" title="List of Archie Comics characters">Archie's Gang</a>. He tends to be attracted to <a href="/wiki/Veronica_Lodge" title="Veronica Lodge">Veronica Lodge</a>’s pedigreed dogs, and even had a litter of puppies with one of them, a poodle named Lucretia.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (December 2017)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> In the 1970s series <i><a href="/wiki/Sabrina_the_Teenage_Witch" title="Sabrina the Teenage Witch">Sabrina the Teenage Witch</a></i>, Hot Dog has a <a href="/wiki/Sidekick" title="Sidekick">sidekick</a>, Chili Dog, who is smaller and red-haired. A <a href="/wiki/Running_gag" title="Running gag">running gag</a> is the two dogs' "battles" with Sabrina's cat <a href="/wiki/Salem_Saberhagen" title="Salem Saberhagen">Salem Saberhagen</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Alternate_universes_in_Archie_Comics#Hot_Dog" title="Alternate universes in Archie Comics">alternate universe limited series <i>Jughead's Pal, Hot Dog</i></a>, when Jughead's family objects to Hot Dog living indoors because he is covered in dirt, <a href="/wiki/Dilton_Doiley" title="Dilton Doiley">Dilton Doiley</a> builds Hot Dog a <a href="/wiki/Doghouse" title="Doghouse">doghouse</a> full of whimsical inventions. Hot Dog's sidekick in that short-lived series is a chihuahua named Pablito.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In the <a href="/wiki/Filmation" title="Filmation">Filmation</a> animated series <i><a href="/wiki/The_Archie_Show" title="The Archie Show">The Archie Show</a></i>, Hot Dog is the mascot for <a href="/wiki/The_Archies" title="The Archies">The Archies</a> and is often portrayed as pretending to "conduct" the band.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Other_versions">Other versions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Other versions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Afterlife_with_Archie"><i>Afterlife with Archie</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Afterlife with Archie">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jughead appears in the first issue of <i><a href="/wiki/Afterlife_with_Archie" title="Afterlife with Archie">Afterlife with Archie</a></i> before being bitten by a resurrected Hot Dog, which transforms him into a zombie. He is referred to as "Patient Zero" in terms of being the first one affected. However, the eighth issue reveals that Jughead is a ghost and his soul is no longer in his body; whatever is controlling his corpse isn't him.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Jughead:_The_Hunger"><i>Jughead: The Hunger</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Jughead: The Hunger">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In this <a href="/wiki/Archie_Horror" title="Archie Horror">Archie Horror</a> spin-off, Jughead is a werewolf responsible for a number of murders in Riverdale and becomes known as "The Riverdale Ripper." He and Archie realize he's a werewolf after he murders Dilton during a full moon. The next day, Betty reveals that he comes from a long-line of <a href="/wiki/Lycanthropy" class="mw-redirect" title="Lycanthropy">lycanthropy</a> dating all the way back to medieval England and that her ancestors have always been there to stop them. Not wanting Betty to murder his friend, Archie suggests going to the botanical gardens to cure Jughead with Wolfsbane. This works for a couple of months, but after it wears off and Jughead murders Reggie in his wolf state, he leaves town with Hot Dog as he knows Betty wouldn't give him a second chance.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Life_with_Archie:_The_Married_Life"><i>Life with Archie: The Married Life</i></span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Life with Archie: The Married Life">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Jughead appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Life_with_Archie#Volume_2_(The_Married_Life)" title="Life with Archie">Life with Archie: The Married Life</a></i> beginning in 2010. In this series, Jughead has taken over Pop's from Pop Tate and has renamed it Jughead's, which later spanned a franchise in each respective universe.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> He ended up abandoning the franchise to operate the original shoppe in Riverdale. In this series, he ends up marrying <a href="/wiki/Midge_Klump" title="Midge Klump">Midge Klump</a> in one universe, and Ethel in the other.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="In_other_media_2">In other media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=16" title="Edit section: In other media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Animated">Animated</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Animated">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<ul><li>Jughead appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_Archie_Show" title="The Archie Show">The Archie Show</a></i>, a 1968 cartoon series produced by <a href="/wiki/Filmation" title="Filmation">Filmation</a>. He also appeared in the various spin-offs produced in the same format. He was voiced by <a href="/wiki/Howard_Morris" title="Howard Morris">Howard Morris</a>.</li>
<li>Jughead appeared in the animated video for <i><a href="/wiki/Sugar,_Sugar" title="Sugar, Sugar">Sugar,_Sugar</a></i>, by <i>The Archies</i>, as the band's drummer.</li>
<li>Jughead was featured in a Filmation-animated segment for <i><a href="/wiki/Sesame_Street" title="Sesame Street">Sesame Street</a></i> spotlighting the letter J.</li>
<li>A short-lived show in the 1970s, <i><a href="/wiki/Archie%27s_TV_Funnies" title="Archie&#39;s TV Funnies">Archie's TV Funnies</a></i>, featured other comic strip characters, such as <a href="/wiki/Broom-Hilda" title="Broom-Hilda">Broom Hilda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Smokey_Stover" title="Smokey Stover">Smokey Stover</a>. Archie introduced each educational short, like a lesson on the importance of bathing from Nancy and Sluggo.</li>
<li>Another show, <i><a href="/wiki/The_U.S._of_Archie" title="The U.S. of Archie">The U.S. of Archie</a></i>, depicted Archie and the gang as themselves in different historical eras. The goal was to teach history.</li>
<li>Jughead  appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Archies" title="The New Archies">The New Archies</a></i>, a 1987 re-imagining of Archie and the gang. Jughead was portrayed as a pre-teen in junior high. He was voiced by Michael Fantini.</li>
<li>Jughead appeared in <i><a href="/wiki/Archie%27s_Weird_Mysteries" title="Archie&#39;s Weird Mysteries">Archie's Weird Mysteries</a></i>, voiced by Chris Lundquist.</li>
<li>In <i><a href="/wiki/The_Simpsons" title="The Simpsons">The Simpsons</a></i>, Jughead, Moose, Archie, and Reggie made a cameo beating up <a href="/wiki/Homer_Simpson" title="Homer Simpson">Homer Simpson</a> in "<a href="/wiki/Sideshow_Bob_Roberts" title="Sideshow Bob Roberts">Sideshow Bob Roberts</a>".</li></ul>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Live-action">Live-action</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Jughead_Jones&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Live-action">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:132px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg/130px-Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg" width="130" height="172" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg/195px-Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg/260px-Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1482" data-file-height="1956" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Cole_Sprouse_by_Gage_Skidmore.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Cole_Sprouse" title="Cole Sprouse">Cole Sprouse</a> plays Jughead on <i><a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(2017_TV_series)" title="Riverdale (2017 TV series)">Riverdale</a></i>.</div></div></div>
<ul><li>Derrel Maury<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> played Jughead in "Archie", a 1976 episode of the <i>ABC Saturday Comedy Special</i>; this was intended to be the <a href="/wiki/Television_pilot" title="Television pilot">pilot</a> for a new series, which never materialized.<sup id="cite_ref-Archie76_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archie76-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> He reprised this role in the 1978 <a href="/wiki/Television_film" title="Television film">TV-movie</a> <i>The Archie Situation Comedy Musical Variety Show</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-Archie78_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Archie78-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Jughead appears in <i><a href="/wiki/Archie:_To_Riverdale_and_Back_Again" title="Archie: To Riverdale and Back Again">Archie: To Riverdale and Back Again</a></i>, a 1990 TV movie which was broadcast on <a href="/wiki/National_Broadcasting_Company" class="mw-redirect" title="National Broadcasting Company">NBC</a>, portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Sam_Whipple" title="Sam Whipple">Sam Whipple</a>. Set fifteen years after his graduation from high school, the movie depicts Jughead as a psychiatrist who owns a successful private practice outside of Riverdale.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> He is also divorced and raising his young son Jordon on his own, who Archie jokingly refers to as Jughead Junior.</li>
<li>Jughead is a main character in <i><a href="/wiki/Riverdale_(2017_TV_series)" title="Riverdale (2017 TV series)">Riverdale</a></i>, a drama series on <a href="/wiki/The_CW" title="The CW">The CW</a>, where he is portrayed by <a href="/wiki/Cole_Sprouse" title="Cole Sprouse">Cole Sprouse</a>. This version of Jughead is markedly different from the comic character, major differences including include an initial strained relationship with Archie (which is reconciled) and a darker, moodier demeanor. In the show, Jughead wears an ordinary dark gray knitted hat with points and pins, but appears in a dream sequence wearing his distinctive crown cap and "S" sweatshirt. His mother and sister are mentioned but absent, having left due to his father's drinking. His father, F.P., is leader of the Southside Serpents, and Jughead, choosing not to live with him, is homeless. In the first season finale, F.P. goes to prison for a short time, and the Serpents offer Jughead one of their jackets, essentially making him an honorary member. In the second season, he undergoes an initiation that includes a brutal beating to become a full-fledged member. Over time, he becomes a de facto leader of the teenagers in the gang, and in the second season finale, after he attempts to save the Serpents from a fight with a rival gang that badly outnumbers them by trading himself, F.P. retires and names him as his successor, to the acclamation of the gang. Jughead's first act as Serpent King is to induct Cheryl Blossom into the Serpents.</li></ul>
