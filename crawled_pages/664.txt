<p>The <b>geography of Scotland</b> is varied, from rural lowlands to unspoilt uplands, and from large cities to sparsely inhabited islands. Located in <a href="/wiki/Northern_Europe" title="Northern Europe">Northern Europe</a>, Scotland comprises the northern one third of the island of Great Britain as well as 790 surrounding islands encompassing the major archipelagoes of the <a href="/wiki/Shetland_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Shetland Islands">Shetland Islands</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orkney_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Orkney Islands">Orkney Islands</a> and the Inner and Outer <a href="/wiki/Hebrides" title="Hebrides">Hebrides</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-third_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-third-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Scotland's <a href="/wiki/Anglo-Scottish_border" title="Anglo-Scottish border">only land border</a> is with England, which runs for 60 miles (97&#160;km)  in a northeasterly direction from the <a href="/wiki/Solway_Firth" title="Solway Firth">Solway Firth</a> in the west to the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> on the east coast.<sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Separated by the <a href="/wiki/North_Channel_(British_Isles)" class="mw-redirect" title="North Channel (British Isles)">North Channel</a>, the island of Ireland lies 13 miles (21&#160;km) from Mull of <a href="/wiki/Kintyre" title="Kintyre">Kintyre</a> on the Scottish mainland.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> Norway is located 190 miles (310&#160;km) to the northeast of Scotland across the North Sea. The <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>, which fringes the coastline of western and northern Scotland and its islands, influences the <a href="/wiki/Temperate" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate">temperate</a>, maritime climate of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Scotland contains the majority of mountainous terrain in the UK. The topography of Scotland is distinguished by the <a href="/wiki/Highland_Boundary_Fault" title="Highland Boundary Fault">Highland Boundary Fault</a>&#160;&#8211;&#32;a <a href="/wiki/Geologic_fault" class="mw-redirect" title="Geologic fault">geological rock fracture</a>&#160;&#8211;&#32;which traverses the Scottish mainland from <a href="/wiki/Helensburgh" title="Helensburgh">Helensburgh</a> to <a href="/wiki/Stonehaven" title="Stonehaven">Stonehaven</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Boundary_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Boundary-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The faultline separates two distinctively different physiographic regions; namely the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Highlands" title="Scottish Highlands">Highlands</a> to the north and west and the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Lowlands" title="Scottish Lowlands">Lowlands</a> to the south and east.<sup id="cite_ref-Fault_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fault-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The more rugged Highland region contains the majority of Scotland's mountainous terrain, including the highest peak, <a href="/wiki/Ben_Nevis" title="Ben Nevis">Ben Nevis</a>.
</p><p>Lowland areas, in the southern part of Scotland, are flatter and home to most of the population, especially the narrow waist of land between the <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Clyde" title="Firth of Clyde">Firth of Clyde</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Forth" title="Firth of Forth">Firth of Forth</a> known as the <a href="/wiki/Central_Lowlands" title="Central Lowlands">Central Belt</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Fault_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fault-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> is the largest city in Scotland, although <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a> is the <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a> and political centre of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-settlements_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-settlements-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> While the Lowlands are less elevated, upland and mountainous terrain is located across the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Uplands" title="Southern Uplands">Southern Uplands</a>.
</p><p>An abundance of natural resources such as <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a>, <a href="/wiki/Iron" title="Iron">iron</a> and <a href="/wiki/Zinc" title="Zinc">zinc</a> contributed significantly to the industrial growth of Scotland during the 19th and early 20th centuries.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup> Today, <a href="/wiki/Energy" title="Energy">energy</a> is a major component of Scotland's economy.<sup id="cite_ref-energy_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-energy-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Whilst Scotland is the largest producer of <a href="/wiki/North_Sea_oil" title="North Sea oil">petroleum</a> in the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>, the production potential of <a href="/wiki/Renewable_energy_in_Scotland" title="Renewable energy in Scotland">renewable energy</a> has emerged as an important economic and environmental issue in recent years.<sup id="cite_ref-energy_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-energy-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Geology_and_geomorphology">Geology and geomorphology</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Geology and geomorphology">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Geology_of_Scotland" title="Geology of Scotland">Geology of Scotland</a></div>
<p>The land area of Scotland is 30,981 square miles (80,240&#160;km<sup>2</sup>), 32% of the area of the United Kingdom (UK). The mainland of Scotland has 6,160 miles (9,910&#160;km) of coastline.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Geomorphology" title="Geomorphology">geomorphology</a> of Scotland was formed by the action of <a href="/wiki/Tectonic_plate" class="mw-redirect" title="Tectonic plate">tectonic plates</a>, and subsequent erosion arising from <a href="/wiki/Glaciation" class="mw-redirect" title="Glaciation">glaciation</a>. The major division of Scotland is the <a href="/wiki/Highland_Boundary_Fault" title="Highland Boundary Fault">Highland Boundary Fault</a>, which separates the land into 'highland' to the north and west, and 'lowland' to the south and east. The <a href="/wiki/Scottish_highlands" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish highlands">Highlands of Scotland</a> are largely mountainous, and form the highest ground in the UK: they are bisected by the <a href="/wiki/Great_Glen" title="Great Glen">Great Glen</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Grampian_Mountains_(Scotland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grampian Mountains (Scotland)">Grampian Mountains</a> to the southeast and the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Highlands" title="Northwest Highlands">Northwest Highlands</a>. The <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Lowlands" title="Scottish Lowlands">Scottish Lowlands</a> can be further subdivided into the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Uplands" title="Southern Uplands">Southern Uplands</a>, an area of rolling farmland and high <a href="/wiki/Moorland" title="Moorland">moorland</a>, and the lowland farmland of the <a href="/wiki/Central_Belt" title="Central Belt">Central Belt</a> and eastern Scotland.
</p><p>Scotland has an incomparable variety of <a href="/wiki/Geology" title="Geology">geology</a> for an area of its size.
</p><p>The oldest rocks of Scotland are the <a href="/wiki/Lewisian_complex" title="Lewisian complex">Lewisian gneisses</a>, which were formed in the <a href="/wiki/Precambrian" title="Precambrian">Precambrian</a> period, up to 3 billion years ago. They are among the oldest rocks in the world. During the Precambrian, the <a href="/wiki/Torridonian_sandstone" class="mw-redirect" title="Torridonian sandstone">Torridonian sandstones</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Moine_Supergroup" title="Moine Supergroup">Moine</a> were also laid down. Further <a href="/wiki/Sedimentary" class="mw-redirect" title="Sedimentary">sedimentary</a> deposits were formed through the <a href="/wiki/Cambrian" title="Cambrian">Cambrian</a> period, some of which <a href="/wiki/Metamorphic_rock" title="Metamorphic rock">metamorphosed</a> into the <a href="/wiki/Dalradian" title="Dalradian">Dalradian</a> series. The area which would become Scotland was at this time close to the south pole..
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Silurian" title="Silurian">Silurian</a> period (439-409 Ma), the area which became Scotland was part of the continent of <a href="/wiki/Laurentia" title="Laurentia">Laurentia</a>. Across the <a href="/wiki/Iapetus_Ocean" title="Iapetus Ocean">Iapetus</a> ocean to the south, was the continent of <a href="/wiki/Baltica" title="Baltica">Baltica</a>. The two continents gradually collided, joining Scotland to the area which would become England and Europe. This event is known as the <a href="/wiki/Caledonian_orogeny" title="Caledonian orogeny">Caledonian Orogeny</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Highland_Boundary_Fault" title="Highland Boundary Fault">Highland Boundary Fault</a> marks this stitching together of continents. Silurian rocks form the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Uplands" title="Southern Uplands">Southern Uplands</a> of Scotland, which was pushed up from the seabed during the collision. The highlands were also pushed up as a result of this collision, and may have been as high as the modern-day <a href="/wiki/Alps" title="Alps">Alps</a> at this time. The <a href="/wiki/Old_Red_Sandstone" title="Old Red Sandstone">Old Red Sandstones</a> were laid down in low-lying areas during this period. <a href="/wiki/Volcano" title="Volcano">Volcanic activity</a> occurred across Scotland as a result of the collision of the <a href="/wiki/Tectonic_plate" class="mw-redirect" title="Tectonic plate">tectonic plates</a>, with volcanoes in southern Scotland, and <a href="/wiki/Magma_chamber" title="Magma chamber">magma chambers</a> in the north, which today form the <a href="/wiki/Granite" title="Granite">granite</a> mountains such as the <a href="/wiki/Cairngorms" title="Cairngorms">Cairngorms</a>.
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/Carboniferous" title="Carboniferous">Carboniferous</a> period (363-290 Ma), Scotland lay close to the equator. Several changes in sea level occurred during this time. The <a href="/wiki/Coal" title="Coal">coal</a> deposits of <a href="/wiki/Lanarkshire" title="Lanarkshire">Lanarkshire</a>, and further sedimentary deposits, date from this time. More volcanic activity formed <a href="/wiki/Arthur%27s_Seat" title="Arthur&#39;s Seat">Arthur's Seat</a> in <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>, among other hills. By the <a href="/wiki/Triassic" title="Triassic">Triassic</a>, Scotland was a desert, the origin of large <a href="/wiki/Sandstone" title="Sandstone">sandstone</a> outcrops of the southwest. Although large deposits of <a href="/wiki/Cretaceous" title="Cretaceous">Cretaceous</a> rocks would have been laid down over Scotland, these have not survived erosion, as have the <a href="/wiki/Chalk" title="Chalk">chalks</a> of England.
</p><p>By the <a href="/wiki/Tertiary" title="Tertiary">Tertiary</a> period, the tectonic plates were again moving, separating into modern-day <a href="/wiki/North_America" title="North America">North America</a> and Europe with the creation of the <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a>. The split occurred to the west of Scotland, leaving a chain of former volcanic sites through the <a href="/wiki/Hebrides" title="Hebrides">Hebrides</a>, including <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Skye" class="mw-redirect" title="Isle of Skye">Skye</a> and <a href="/wiki/St_Kilda,_Scotland" title="St Kilda, Scotland">St. Kilda</a>. This was the last period of rock formation in Scotland.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Physical_geography">Physical geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Physical geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Extreme_points">Extreme points</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Extreme points">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Scotland_from_space.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Scotland_from_space.png/220px-Scotland_from_space.png" width="220" height="267" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Scotland_from_space.png/330px-Scotland_from_space.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Scotland_from_space.png/440px-Scotland_from_space.png 2x" data-file-width="751" data-file-height="910" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Scotland_from_space.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Scotland viewed from space</div></div></div>
<p>The extreme points of the Scottish mainland are:
</p>
<ul><li>North: Easter Head, <a href="/wiki/Dunnet_Head" title="Dunnet Head">Dunnet Head</a>, <a href="/wiki/Caithness" title="Caithness">Caithness</a></li>
<li>East: <a href="/wiki/Keith_Inch" title="Keith Inch">Keith Inch</a>, <a href="/wiki/Peterhead" title="Peterhead">Peterhead</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire_(traditional)" class="mw-redirect" title="Aberdeenshire (traditional)">Aberdeenshire</a></li>
<li>South: <a href="/wiki/Mull_of_Galloway" title="Mull of Galloway">Mull of Galloway</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dumfries_and_Galloway" title="Dumfries and Galloway">Dumfries and Galloway</a></li>
<li>West: <a href="/wiki/Corrachadh_M%C3%B2r" title="Corrachadh Mòr">Corrachadh Mòr</a>, <a href="/wiki/Ardnamurchan" title="Ardnamurchan">Ardnamurchan</a> (headland), <a href="/wiki/Lochaber" title="Lochaber">Lochaber</a></li></ul>
<p>It is often yet incorrectly stated that <a href="/wiki/John_o%27_Groats" title="John o&#39; Groats">John o' Groats</a> is the most northerly point of mainland Scotland. The pre-Union phrase "John o' Groats to <a href="/wiki/Maidenkirk" title="Maidenkirk">Maidenkirk</a>" was the Scottish equivalent of the British <a href="/wiki/Land%27s_End_to_John_o%27_Groats" title="Land&#39;s End to John o&#39; Groats">Land's End to John o' Groats</a>.
</p><p>The extreme points of Scotland, including outlying islands, are:
</p>
<ul><li>North: <a href="/wiki/Out_Stack" title="Out Stack">Out Stack</a>, north of <a href="/wiki/Unst" title="Unst">Unst</a>, <a href="/wiki/Shetland_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Shetland Islands">Shetland Islands</a></li>
<li>East: <a href="/wiki/Bound_Skerry" title="Bound Skerry">Bound Skerry</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outer_Skerries" class="mw-redirect" title="Outer Skerries">Out Skerries</a>, Shetland Islands</li>
<li>South: Mull of Galloway, Dumfries and Galloway</li>
<li>West: Either <a href="/wiki/Rockall" title="Rockall">Rockall</a> (annexed in 1972 to the former <a href="/wiki/Inverness-shire" title="Inverness-shire">Inverness-shire</a>), the international status of which is disputed, or <a href="/wiki/Soay,_St_Kilda" title="Soay, St Kilda">Soay</a>, <a href="/wiki/St_Kilda,_Scotland" title="St Kilda, Scotland">St. Kilda</a>, <a href="/wiki/Outer_Hebrides" title="Outer Hebrides">Western Isles</a></li></ul>
<p>The geographical <a href="/wiki/Centre_of_Scotland" title="Centre of Scotland">centre of Scotland</a> lies a few miles from the village of <a href="/wiki/Newtonmore" title="Newtonmore">Newtonmore</a> in <a href="/wiki/Badenoch" title="Badenoch">Badenoch</a>, far to the north of the modern population heartlands.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> The geographical centre of the mainland alone lies near the peak of <a href="/wiki/Schiehallion" title="Schiehallion">Schiehallion</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Land_use">Land use</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Land use">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The total land area of Scotland is 8,023,947 hectares. Grasses and rough grazing account for 67% of the land area, forest and woodland 17%, urban development 8%, crops and fallow 7%, and other agricultural land accounts for 2%.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span id="Topography.2C_mountains_and_hills"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Topography,_mountains_and_hills">Topography, mountains and hills</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Topography, mountains and hills">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:BenNevis2005.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/BenNevis2005.jpg/220px-BenNevis2005.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/BenNevis2005.jpg/330px-BenNevis2005.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/BenNevis2005.jpg/440px-BenNevis2005.jpg 2x" data-file-width="2048" data-file-height="1536" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:BenNevis2005.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Ben_Nevis" title="Ben Nevis">Ben Nevis</a> is the highest peak in Great Britain.</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Mountains_and_hills_of_Scotland" title="Mountains and hills of Scotland">Mountains and hills of Scotland</a></div>
<p>Scotland contains the most mountainous terrain in Great Britain. Much of the highest uplands lie to the north and west of the Highland Boundary Fault in the <a href="/wiki/Northwest_Highlands" title="Northwest Highlands">Northwest Highlands</a> and <a href="/wiki/Grampian_Mountains" title="Grampian Mountains">Grampian</a> ranges. The jagged <a href="/wiki/Cuillin" title="Cuillin">Cuillin</a>, on the Isle of Skye, represents a major mountain range that is not located on the Scottish mainland. Located at the western end of the Grampian Mountains, at an altitude of 1,345&#160;m (4,413&#160;ft), <a href="/wiki/Ben_Nevis" title="Ben Nevis">Ben Nevis</a> is the highest mountain in Scotland and Great Britain. <a href="/wiki/Ben_Macdui" title="Ben Macdui">Ben Macdui</a> and <a href="/wiki/Braeriach" title="Braeriach">Braeriach</a> are, respectively, the second and third tallest peaks in Scotland. Both are located in the <a href="/wiki/Cairngorms" title="Cairngorms">Cairngorms</a>, a mountain range in the eastern Highlands which is the highest upland area in Britain.
</p><p>In the south of Scotland lie the <a href="/wiki/Southern_Uplands" title="Southern Uplands">Southern Uplands</a>, a vast, rolling mountain chain that is less rugged and more forested than the Highlands. Shorter than the Highlands, the tallest mountain in the range is <a href="/wiki/Merrick_(Galloway)" title="Merrick (Galloway)">Merrick</a>, at 843 m (2,766&#160;ft).
</p><p>There are ranges of gentler, rolling hills even in the central lowlands of Scotland. Such ranges include the <a href="/wiki/Ochil_Hills" title="Ochil Hills">Ochil Hills</a>, near <a href="/wiki/Stirling" title="Stirling">Stirling</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Campsie_Fells" title="Campsie Fells">Campsie Fells</a> outside <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Lomond_Hills" title="Lomond Hills">Lomond Hills</a> in <a href="/wiki/Fife" title="Fife">Fife</a>.
</p><p>Mountains in Scotland are <a href="/wiki/Hill_lists_in_the_British_Isles#Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Hill lists in the British Isles">categorised</a> by their height. Peaks over 3,000&#160;ft (914.4&#160;m) are known as <a href="/wiki/Munro" title="Munro">Munros</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Munros_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Munros-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> There are 282 Munros in Scotland, all within the Highlands.<sup id="cite_ref-Munros_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Munros-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Corbetts" class="mw-redirect" title="Corbetts">Corbetts</a> are peaks with an altitude of between 2,500 and 3,000&#160;ft (762.0 and 914.4&#160;m), with a <a href="/wiki/Topographic_prominence" title="Topographic prominence">relative height</a> of at least 500&#160;ft (152.4&#160;m) .<sup id="cite_ref-Munros_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Munros-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> The classification of peaks in Scotland is kept under periodic review by the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Mountaineering_Club" title="Scottish Mountaineering Club">Scottish Mountaineering Club</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Munros_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Munros-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Scottish_eez.PNG" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/63/Scottish_eez.PNG/220px-Scottish_eez.PNG" width="220" height="163" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/63/Scottish_eez.PNG/330px-Scottish_eez.PNG 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/6/63/Scottish_eez.PNG/440px-Scottish_eez.PNG 2x" data-file-width="1141" data-file-height="845" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Scottish_eez.PNG" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>30A map of British Isles EEZs and surrounding nations. Internal UK borders are represented by thin lines.Scotland's EEZ is highlighted in blue.</div></div></div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Coastline">Coastline</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Coastline">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg/220px-Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg/330px-Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg/440px-Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Traigh_Sheilebost.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Large parts of the Scottish coastline are dune pasture, such as here at Traigh Seilebost on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Harris" class="mw-redirect" title="Isle of Harris">Isle of Harris</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>Mainland Scotland has 6,160 miles (9,910&#160;km) of coastline. Including the numerous islands, this increases to some 10,250 miles (16,500&#160;km). The west coast in particular is heavily indented, with long promontories separated by <a href="/wiki/Fjord" title="Fjord">fjordlike</a> <a href="/wiki/Sea_loch" class="mw-redirect" title="Sea loch">sea lochs</a>. The east coast is more regular, with a series of large estuarine inlets, or <a href="/wiki/Firth" title="Firth">firths</a>, and long sandy beaches, for example at <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a>.  Much of the Scottish coastline consists of a <a href="/wiki/Machair_(geography)" class="mw-redirect" title="Machair (geography)">machair formation</a>, a dune pasture land formed as sea levels subsided.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The east coast has several significant <a href="/wiki/Estuaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Estuaries">estuaries</a> and other <a href="/wiki/Nature_reserve" title="Nature reserve">nature reserves</a> including the <a href="/wiki/Ythan_Estuary" title="Ythan Estuary">Ythan Estuary</a> and <a href="/wiki/Fowlsheugh" title="Fowlsheugh">Fowlsheugh</a>, both of which have been designated as <a href="/wiki/Important_Bird_Area" title="Important Bird Area">Important Bird Areas</a>.
</p><p>Firths of Scotland include the <a href="/wiki/Solway_Firth" title="Solway Firth">Solway Firth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Clyde" title="Firth of Clyde">Firth of Clyde</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Lorne" class="mw-redirect" title="Firth of Lorne">Firth of Lorne</a> on the west coast, and the <a href="/wiki/Cromarty_Firth" title="Cromarty Firth">Cromarty Firth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Moray_Firth" title="Moray Firth">Moray Firth</a>, <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Tay" title="Firth of Tay">Firth of Tay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Forth" title="Firth of Forth">Firth of Forth</a> on the east coast. The <a href="/wiki/Pentland_Firth" title="Pentland Firth">Pentland Firth</a> is not an inlet, but the <a href="/wiki/Strait" title="Strait">strait</a> that separates the <a href="/wiki/Orkney_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Orkney Isles">Orkney Isles</a> from the mainland.
</p><p>Major sea lochs include <a href="/wiki/Loch_Fyne" title="Loch Fyne">Loch Fyne</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loch_Long" title="Loch Long">Loch Long</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loch_Ryan" title="Loch Ryan">Loch Ryan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loch_Linnhe" title="Loch Linnhe">Loch Linnhe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loch_Torridon" title="Loch Torridon">Loch Torridon</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loch_Ewe" title="Loch Ewe">Loch Ewe</a>, and on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Lewis" title="Isle of Lewis">Isle of Lewis</a>, <a href="/wiki/Loch_Seaforth" title="Loch Seaforth">Loch Seaforth</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Islands">Islands</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Islands">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_islands_of_Scotland" title="List of islands of Scotland">List of islands of Scotland</a></div>
<p>Scotland has some 790 islands, most of which are located off the northern and western coast of the country.<sup id="cite_ref-islands_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-islands-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The <a href="/wiki/Northern_Isles" title="Northern Isles">northern</a> and <a href="/wiki/Western_Isles" class="mw-redirect" title="Western Isles">western</a> islands of Scotland can be found in three main groups: <a href="/wiki/Shetland_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Shetland Islands">Shetland</a>, <a href="/wiki/Orkney_Islands" class="mw-redirect" title="Orkney Islands">Orkney</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Hebrides" title="Hebrides">Hebrides</a> which can be divided into the <a href="/wiki/Inner_Hebrides" title="Inner Hebrides">Inner Hebrides</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Hebrides" title="Outer Hebrides">Outer Hebrides</a>. Shetland and Orkney, together with <a href="/wiki/Fair_Isle" title="Fair Isle">Fair Isle</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stroma,_Scotland" title="Stroma, Scotland">Stroma</a> are referred to as the <a href="/wiki/Northern_Isles" title="Northern Isles">Northern Isles</a>. With a total land area of 860 square miles (2,200&#160;km<sup>2</sup>) <a href="/wiki/Lewis_and_Harris" title="Lewis and Harris">Lewis and Harris</a> (referred to as two islands but actually just one) is the largest island surrounding Scotland.
</p><p>Many of these offshore islands are swept by strong tides, and the <a href="/wiki/Gulf_of_Corryvreckan" title="Gulf of Corryvreckan">Corryvreckan tide race</a> between <a href="/wiki/Scarba" title="Scarba">Scarba</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jura,_Scotland" title="Jura, Scotland">Jura</a> is one of the largest <a href="/wiki/Whirlpool" title="Whirlpool">whirlpools</a> in the world. Other strong tides are to be found in the <a href="/wiki/Pentland_Firth" title="Pentland Firth">Pentland Firth</a> between mainland Scotland and <a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orkney</a>, and the <i>Grey Dog</i> between Scarba and <a href="/wiki/Lunga,_Firth_of_Lorn" class="mw-redirect" title="Lunga, Firth of Lorn">Lunga</a>. There are also numerous clusters of islands in the <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Forth" title="Firth of Forth">Firth of Forth</a> and the <a href="/wiki/Firth_of_Clyde" title="Firth of Clyde">Firth of Clyde</a> and in freshwater lochs such as Loch Lomond and <a href="/wiki/Loch_Maree" title="Loch Maree">Loch Maree</a>. Outlying islands include <a href="/wiki/St_Kilda,_Scotland" title="St Kilda, Scotland">St Kilda</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rockall" title="Rockall">Rockall</a> the status of which is disputed.
</p><p>Scotland's islands have a varied topography. <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Mull" class="mw-redirect" title="Isle of Mull">Mull</a>, <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Skye" class="mw-redirect" title="Isle of Skye">Skye</a> and <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Arran" title="Isle of Arran">Arran</a> are noted for their mountainous terrain, whilst <a href="/wiki/Tiree" title="Tiree">Tiree</a>, <a href="/wiki/Coll" title="Coll">Coll</a> and most of the Shetland group are flat or low lying. Striking topographical differences can be seen within island groups themselves; in Orkney, the Island of <a href="/wiki/Hoy" title="Hoy">Hoy</a> is hillier and more rugged than surrounding islands and Harris is distinctive in being more mountainous than the islands of <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Lewis" title="Isle of Lewis">Lewis</a>, <a href="/wiki/North_Uist" title="North Uist">North Uist</a>, <a href="/wiki/South_Uist" title="South Uist">South Uist</a> and <a href="/wiki/Barra" title="Barra">Barra</a>, in the Outer Hebrides. <a href="/wiki/Islay" title="Islay">Islay</a>'s terrain is gentle and rolling; however, its neighbor <a href="/wiki/Jura_(Scotland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Jura (Scotland)">Jura</a> is mountainous and rugged. <a href="/wiki/R%C3%B9m" title="Rùm">Rùm</a> and <a href="/wiki/Eigg" title="Eigg">Eigg</a> display jagged terrain; nearby <a href="/wiki/Canna,_Scotland" title="Canna, Scotland">Canna</a> and <a href="/wiki/Muck,_Scotland" title="Muck, Scotland">Muck</a> are flatter.
</p>

<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Loch_Shin.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Loch_Shin.jpg/220px-Loch_Shin.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Loch_Shin.jpg/330px-Loch_Shin.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Loch_Shin.jpg/440px-Loch_Shin.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Loch_Shin.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div><a href="/wiki/Loch_Shin" title="Loch Shin">Loch Shin</a> is one of many freshwater bodies in Scotland.</div></div></div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Freshwater" class="mw-redirect" title="Freshwater">Freshwater</a> bodies in Scotland are known as <a href="/wiki/Loch" title="Loch">lochs</a>, with the exception of the <a href="/wiki/Lake_of_Menteith" title="Lake of Menteith">Lake of Menteith</a> and one or two man-made "lakes". 90% of the standing fresh water volume of Great Britain lies within Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Loch_Lomond" title="Loch Lomond">Loch Lomond</a> is the largest freshwater body in Britain by area, although with a capacity of 1.78 cubic miles (7.4&#160;km<sup>3</sup>) <a href="/wiki/Loch_Ness" title="Loch Ness">Loch Ness</a> is the most voluminous. The water in Loch Ness is nearly double that of all the lakes of England and Wales combined, and is by volume the largest lake in the British Isles.<sup id="cite_ref-SNH_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SNH-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ness_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ness-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Distances_to_other_countries">Distances to other countries</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Distances to other countries">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Scotland's only land border is with England, and runs for 60 miles (97&#160;km) between the basin of the <a href="/wiki/River_Tweed" title="River Tweed">River Tweed</a> on the east coast and the <a href="/wiki/Solway_Firth" title="Solway Firth">Solway Firth</a> in the west. The <a href="/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean" title="Atlantic Ocean">Atlantic Ocean</a> borders the west coast and the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a> is to the east. The island of Ireland lies only 19 miles (31&#160;km) from the southwestern peninsula of <a href="/wiki/Kintyre" title="Kintyre">Kintyre</a>;<sup id="cite_ref-Atlas_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Atlas-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> Norway is 190 miles (310&#160;km) to the east; the <a href="/wiki/Faroes" class="mw-redirect" title="Faroes">Faroes</a>, 170 miles (270&#160;km) to the north; and <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a>, 460 miles (740&#160;km) to the northwest.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Climate">Climate</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Climate">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Climate_of_Scotland" title="Climate of Scotland">Climate of Scotland</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Edinburgh_climate_graph.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Edinburgh_climate_graph.png/220px-Edinburgh_climate_graph.png" width="220" height="120" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Edinburgh_climate_graph.png/330px-Edinburgh_climate_graph.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Edinburgh_climate_graph.png/440px-Edinburgh_climate_graph.png 2x" data-file-width="646" data-file-height="352" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Edinburgh_climate_graph.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>Climate graph for <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>. Mild temperatures and evenly distributed rainfall, throughout the year, are characteristic of Scotland's <a href="/wiki/Temperate" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate">temperate</a>, <a href="/wiki/Maritime_climate" class="mw-redirect" title="Maritime climate">maritime climate</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>The climate of Scotland is <a href="/wiki/Temperate" class="mw-redirect" title="Temperate">temperate</a> and very changeable, but rarely extreme.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Scotland is warmed by the <a href="/wiki/North_Atlantic_current" class="mw-redirect" title="North Atlantic current">North Atlantic Drift</a> and given the northerly location of the country, experiences much milder conditions than areas on similar latitudes, such as <a href="/wiki/Labrador" title="Labrador">Labrador</a> in Canada&#8212;where <a href="/wiki/Icebergs" class="mw-redirect" title="Icebergs">icebergs</a> are a common feature in winter.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Average temperatures are lower than in the rest of Great Britain, with the coldest ever UK temperature of −27.2&#160;<a href="/wiki/Celsius" title="Celsius">°C</a> (−17.0&#160;<a href="/wiki/Fahrenheit" title="Fahrenheit">°F</a>) recorded at <a href="/wiki/Braemar" title="Braemar">Braemar</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Grampian_Mountains_(Scotland)" class="mw-redirect" title="Grampian Mountains (Scotland)">Grampian Mountains</a>, on 10 January 1982 and also at <a href="/wiki/Altnaharra" title="Altnaharra">Altnaharra</a>, <a href="/wiki/Highland_(council_area)" title="Highland (council area)">Highland</a>, on 30 December 1995.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Winter maxima average 5.0 to 5.7&#160;°C (41.0 to 42.3&#160;°F), with summer maxima averaging 14.9 to 16.9&#160;°C (58.8 to 62.4&#160;°F).<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandaverages_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandaverages-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Western coastal areas of Scotland are warmer than the east and inland areas, due to the influence of the Atlantic currents, and the colder surface temperatures of the <a href="/wiki/North_Sea" title="North Sea">North Sea</a>; however, during rare hot spells, the west is cooler than the east.<sup id="cite_ref-meantemperatures_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-meantemperatures-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> The highest temperature recorded was 32.9&#160;°C (91.2&#160;°F) at <a href="/wiki/Greycrook" title="Greycrook">Greycrook</a> in the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Borders" title="Scottish Borders">Scottish Borders</a> on 9 August 2003.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Areas such as <a href="/wiki/Inverewe_Garden" title="Inverewe Garden">Inverewe Garden</a> in the northwest and the <a href="/wiki/Logan_Botanic_Garden" title="Logan Botanic Garden">Logan Botanic Garden</a> in the southwest have warm enough microclimates to support palm trees.
</p><p>Rainfall totals vary widely across Scotland&#8212;the western highlands of Scotland are one of the wettest places in the UK with annual rainfall up to 4,577&#160;mm (180.2&#160;in).<sup id="cite_ref-rainfall_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> Due to the mountainous topography of the western Highlands, this type of precipitation is <a href="/wiki/Orographic" class="mw-redirect" title="Orographic">orographic</a> in nature, with the warm, wet air forced to rise on contact with the mountainous coast, where it consequently, cools and <a href="/wiki/Condensation" title="Condensation">condenses</a>, forming clouds.<sup id="cite_ref-orographic_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-orographic-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> In comparison, much of eastern Scotland receives less than 870&#160;mm (34.3&#160;in) annually; lying in the <a href="/wiki/Rain_shadow" title="Rain shadow">rain shadow</a> of the western uplands.<sup id="cite_ref-rainfall_21-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rainfall-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The town of <a href="/wiki/Dunbar" title="Dunbar">Dunbar</a>, east of Edinburgh, has an annual rainfall of merely 560.18&#160;mm (22.05&#160;in), less than <a href="/wiki/Barcelona" title="Barcelona">Barcelona</a>. Snowfall is less common in the lowlands, but becomes more common with altitude. Parts of the Highlands have an average of 36 to 60 snow days per year,<sup id="cite_ref-snow_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snow-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> while some western coastal areas have between 0 and 6 days with snow a year.<sup id="cite_ref-snow_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-snow-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Scotland's snowiest spot is the <a href="/wiki/Cairngorms" title="Cairngorms">Cairngorm</a> mountain range.
</p><p>The Hebridean island of <a href="/wiki/Tiree" title="Tiree">Tiree</a> received a total of 329 hours of sunshine in May 1946 and again in May 1975, the highest number of sunshine hours ever recorded in one month in Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> On the <a href="/wiki/Solstice" title="Solstice">longest day of the year</a> there is no complete darkness over the northern isles of Scotland. <a href="/wiki/Lerwick" title="Lerwick">Lerwick</a>, in Shetland, has four hours more daylight at midsummer than <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, although this is reversed in midwinter.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>  Annual average sunshine totals vary from as little as 711&#8211;1140 hours in the Highlands and the north-west up to 1471&#8211;1540 hours on the extreme eastern and south-western coasts.<sup id="cite_ref-sunshine_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-sunshine-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In common with the rest of the British Isles, wind prevails from the south-west, bringing warm, wet and unstable air from the Atlantic.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The windiest areas of Scotland are in the north and west, with parts of the Outer Hebrides,  Orkney and Shetland experiencing over 30 days with gales per year.<sup id="cite_ref-scotlandclimate_4-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-scotlandclimate-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Vigorous Atlantic <a href="/wiki/Low_pressure_area" class="mw-redirect" title="Low pressure area">depressions</a>, also known as <a href="/wiki/European_windstorm" title="European windstorm">European windstorms</a>, are a common feature of the autumn and winter in Scotland.<sup id="cite_ref-depressions_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-depressions-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Human_geography">Human geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Human geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Demography_of_Scotland" title="Demography of Scotland">Demography of Scotland</a></div>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Crofting_Township.jpg" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Crofting_Township.jpg/220px-Crofting_Township.jpg" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Crofting_Township.jpg/330px-Crofting_Township.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Crofting_Township.jpg/440px-Crofting_Township.jpg 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="480" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Crofting_Township.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>In northern and western areas, many people live in small crofting <a href="/wiki/Township_(Scotland)" title="Township (Scotland)">townships</a> such as here on the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Skye" class="mw-redirect" title="Isle of Skye">Isle of Skye</a>.</div></div></div>
<p>According to the <a href="/wiki/General_Register_Office_for_Scotland" title="General Register Office for Scotland">General Register Office for Scotland</a>, the total population of Scotland stood at 5,168,500 in June 2008, an increase of 2.1% since the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom_Census_2001" class="mw-redirect" title="United Kingdom Census 2001">census</a> of April 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-Population_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Scotland's share of the United Kingdom population has been declining in recent years and stands at just over 8.5% due to differential rates of growth in the <a href="/wiki/Home_nations" class="mw-redirect" title="Home nations">home nations</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-decline_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-decline-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup> However an increasing <a href="/wiki/Birth_rate" title="Birth rate">birth rate</a><sup id="cite_ref-births_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-births-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> and higher levels of inward migration to Scotland have reversed the decline and contributed to the recent population growth.<sup id="cite_ref-Population_26-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Compared with the rest of Europe, Scotland has a low <a href="/wiki/Population_density" title="Population density">population density</a> at 65 people per square kilometre.<sup id="cite_ref-Population2_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population2-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> However Scotland is a highly <a href="/wiki/Urban_area" title="Urban area">urbanised</a> country, with 82% of the population living in settlements of 3,000 people or more.<sup id="cite_ref-Population3_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup> As a result, the majority of the population live in the <a href="/wiki/Central_Belt" title="Central Belt">Central Lowlands</a> of Scotland, surrounding the chief cities of <a href="/wiki/Glasgow" title="Glasgow">Glasgow</a> and <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Population4_31-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population4-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> Other concentrations of population include the northeast coast of Scotland - principally surrounding the city of <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a> and its environs - and around <a href="/wiki/Inverness" title="Inverness">Inverness</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Population4_31-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population4-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> With a population density of 8 people per square kilometre, the <a href="/wiki/Scottish_Highlands" title="Scottish Highlands">Highlands</a> are the most sparsely populated part of the country, and one of the most sparsely populated areas in Europe. In these areas, the population is scattered in villages, small towns and isolated farmsteads or <a href="/wiki/Croft_(land)" title="Croft (land)">crofts</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Population3_30-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population3-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nearly 100 of Scotland's islands are inhabited, the most populous being <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Lewis" title="Isle of Lewis">Lewis</a> with 16,782 people resident in 2001, primarily concentrated in <a href="/wiki/Stornoway,_Outer_Hebrides" class="mw-redirect" title="Stornoway, Outer Hebrides">Stornoway</a>, the only <a href="/wiki/Burgh" title="Burgh">burgh</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Outer_Hebrides" title="Outer Hebrides">Outer Hebrides</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Population5_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Other island populations range down to very low levels on certain small isles.<sup id="cite_ref-Population5_32-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Between 1991 and 2001, the total number of people living on Scotland's islands fell by 3%.<sup id="cite_ref-Population5_32-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> Conversely, islands such as <a href="/wiki/Tiree" title="Tiree">Tiree</a>, Skye and <a href="/wiki/Eigg" title="Eigg">Eigg</a> experienced increases in their respective populations over the same decade.<sup id="cite_ref-Population5_32-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population5-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>There are seven <a href="/wiki/City_status_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="City status in the United Kingdom">cities</a> in Scotland; Glasgow, Edinburgh, <a href="/wiki/Aberdeen" title="Aberdeen">Aberdeen</a>, <a href="/wiki/Dundee" title="Dundee">Dundee</a>, <a href="/wiki/Inverness" title="Inverness">Inverness</a>, <a href="/wiki/Perth,_Scotland" title="Perth, Scotland">Perth</a> and <a href="/wiki/Stirling" title="Stirling">Stirling</a>. The 2001 census identified Glasgow as being the largest city in Scotland, with a total population of 629,501, while the Scottish <a href="/wiki/Capital_city" title="Capital city">capital</a>, Edinburgh had a population of 448,624, in the same year.<sup id="cite_ref-Population6_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population6-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Between 1991 and 2001, the populations of Edinburgh and Stirling grew by 2.9% and 6.5% respectively.<sup id="cite_ref-Population6_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population6-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Inverness experienced population growth of over 10% during the same period.<sup id="cite_ref-Inverness_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Inverness-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> At the same time, Glasgow, Dundee and Aberdeen all witnessed population decline.<sup id="cite_ref-Population6_33-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population6-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> Aside from the cities, the greatest intra-census population growth was experienced in the local authorities of <a href="/wiki/West_Lothian" title="West Lothian">West Lothian</a>, <a href="/wiki/East_Lothian" title="East Lothian">East Lothian</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aberdeenshire" title="Aberdeenshire">Aberdeenshire</a> and <a href="/wiki/Perth_and_Kinross" title="Perth and Kinross">Perth and Kinross</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Population6_33-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population6-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> The Western Isles saw a 9.8% decrease in population between 1991 and 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-Population6_33-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Population6-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_geography">Political geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Political geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Scotland_map.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Scotland_map.png/220px-Scotland_map.png" width="220" height="323" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/48/Scotland_map.png 1.5x" data-file-width="253" data-file-height="372" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Scotland_map.png" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The territorial extent of Scotland has been relatively unchanged since the late 15th Century.</div></div></div>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Politics_of_Scotland" title="Politics of Scotland">Politics of Scotland</a></div>
<p>The territorial extent of Scotland is generally that established by the 1237 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_York" title="Treaty of York">Treaty of York</a> between Scotland and England<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and the 1266 <a href="/wiki/Treaty_of_Perth" title="Treaty of Perth">Treaty of Perth</a> between Scotland and Norway.<sup id="cite_ref-Mackie_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Mackie-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Exceptions include: the <a href="/wiki/Isle_of_Man" title="Isle of Man">Isle of Man</a>, which having been lost to England in the 14th century is now a <a href="/wiki/Crown_dependency" class="mw-redirect" title="Crown dependency">crown dependency</a> outside of the United Kingdom, the acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Orkney" title="Orkney">Orkney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Shetland" title="Shetland">Shetland</a> from Norway in 1472,<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> and the permanent recovery of <a href="/wiki/Berwick_upon_Tweed" class="mw-redirect" title="Berwick upon Tweed">Berwick</a> by England in 1482.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> Originally an independent country, Scotland joined with England to form the <a href="/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain" title="Kingdom of Great Britain">Kingdom of Great Britain</a> in 1707 with the <a href="/wiki/Act_of_Union_1707" class="mw-redirect" title="Act of Union 1707">Acts of Union</a>.
</p><p>Between 1889 and 1975 Scotland was divided into <a href="/wiki/Burgh" title="Burgh">burghs</a> and <a href="/wiki/Counties_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Counties of Scotland">counties</a>, which were replaced by <a href="/wiki/Local_government_areas_of_Scotland_1973_to_1996" class="mw-redirect" title="Local government areas of Scotland 1973 to 1996">regions and districts</a>. Since 1996, for the purposes of <a href="/wiki/Local_government_of_Scotland" class="mw-redirect" title="Local government of Scotland">local government</a>, Scotland has been divided into 32 <a href="/wiki/Subdivisions_of_Scotland" title="Subdivisions of Scotland">council areas</a>.
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Rockall" title="Rockall">Rockall</a>, a small and uninhabitable rocky islet in the North Atlantic,  was annexed by the UK in 1955 and later declared part of Scotland by the <a href="/wiki/Island_of_Rockall_Act_1972" title="Island of Rockall Act 1972">Island of Rockall Act 1972</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> However, the legality of this claim is disputed by the Republic of Ireland, <a href="/wiki/Denmark" title="Denmark">Denmark</a> and <a href="/wiki/Iceland" title="Iceland">Iceland</a> and it is probably unenforceable in international law.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As one of the constituent countries of the United Kingdom, Scotland is represented by Members of Parliament at the <a href="/wiki/Parliament_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Parliament of the United Kingdom">Parliament of the United Kingdom</a> at <a href="/wiki/Palace_of_Westminster" title="Palace of Westminster">Westminster</a>, <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>. In 1997 a <a href="/wiki/Referendum" title="Referendum">referendum</a> was held, and the people of Scotland voted for the establishment of a <a href="/wiki/Devolution" title="Devolution">devolved</a> Scottish Parliament in Edinburgh. The new parliament has the power to govern the country on Scotland-specific matters and has a limited power to vary <a href="/wiki/Income_tax" title="Income tax">income tax</a>. The United Kingdom Parliament retains responsibility for Scotland's <a href="/wiki/Defense_(military)" class="mw-redirect" title="Defense (military)">defence</a>, <a href="/wiki/International_relations" title="International relations">international relations</a> and certain other areas. A <a href="/wiki/Scottish_independence_referendum" class="mw-redirect" title="Scottish independence referendum">Scottish independence referendum</a> was held in September 2014, at which time independence was rejected.
</p><p>The latest Scottish parliament election results for major parties:
Scottish National Party- 63 MSPs
Scottish Conservatives- 31 MSPs
Scottish Labour- 24 MSPs
Scottish Greens- 6 5 MSPs
Scottish Liberal Democrats- 5 MSPs
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Economic_geography">Economic geography</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Geography_of_Scotland&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Economic geography">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Economy_of_Scotland" title="Economy of Scotland">Economy of Scotland</a></div>
<p>The <a href="/wiki/Gross_domestic_product" title="Gross domestic product">gross domestic product</a> (GDP) of Scotland in 2006 is estimated to have been £124 billion,<sup id="cite_ref-SES_2008_A1.8a_43-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-SES_2008_A1.8a-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> resulting a per capita GDP of approximately £24,000. Major industries include <a href="/wiki/Banking" class="mw-redirect" title="Banking">banking</a> and financial services, <a href="/wiki/Steel" title="Steel">steel</a>, transport equipment, <a href="/wiki/Petroleum" title="Petroleum">oil</a> and <a href="/wiki/Natural_gas" title="Natural gas">gas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Whisky" title="Whisky">whisky</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Tourism" title="Tourism">tourism</a>.
</p>
