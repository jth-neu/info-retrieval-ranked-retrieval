<h1 id="firstHeading" class="firstHeading" lang="en">BFI Top 100 British films</h1>			<div id="bodyContent" class="mw-body-content">
				<div id="siteSub" class="noprint">From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia</div>				<div id="contentSub"></div>
				<div id="jump-to-nav"></div>				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#mw-head">Jump to navigation</a>
				<a class="mw-jump-link" href="#p-search">Jump to search</a>
				<div id="mw-content-text" lang="en" dir="ltr" class="mw-content-ltr"><div class="mw-parser-output"><p>In 1999, the <a href="/wiki/British_Film_Institute" title="British Film Institute">British Film Institute</a> surveyed 1,000 people from the world of British film and television to produce the BFI 100 list of the greatest <a href="/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_Kingdom" title="Cinema of the United Kingdom">British films</a> of the 20th century. Voters were asked to choose up to 100 films that were 'culturally British'.<sup id="cite_ref-BFI_(1999)_1-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BFI_(1999)-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> The list also includes two non-British films, namely <a href="/wiki/My_Left_Foot" title="My Left Foot">My Left Foot</a> and <a href="/wiki/The_Commitments" class="mw-disambig" title="The Commitments">The Commitments</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="List_breakdown">List breakdown</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BFI_Top_100_British_films&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: List breakdown">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>The 1960s led the pack with 26 films of merit for the decade.  Four films made the list from each of the years 1949, 1963, and 1971.  The earliest film selected was <i><a href="/wiki/The_39_Steps_(1935_film)" title="The 39 Steps (1935 film)">The 39 Steps</a></i> (1935). Only two other 1930s films made the list, and after 1935, the only years not represented are 1936–1937, 1940–1941, 1950, 1956, 1958, 1972 and 1974–78.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/David_Lean" title="David Lean">David Lean</a>, with seven films, is the most represented director in the list, followed by <a href="/wiki/Michael_Powell" title="Michael Powell">Michael Powell</a> (five films, of which four were collaborations with <a href="/wiki/Emeric_Pressburger" title="Emeric Pressburger">Emeric Pressburger</a>). <a href="/wiki/Powell_and_Pressburger" title="Powell and Pressburger">Powell and Pressburger</a> ("The Archers") and <a href="/wiki/John_Schlesinger" title="John Schlesinger">John Schlesinger</a> had four films each, while <a href="/wiki/Alexander_Mackendrick" title="Alexander Mackendrick">Alexander Mackendrick</a> and <a href="/wiki/Tony_Richardson" title="Tony Richardson">Tony Richardson</a> each had three. Seven of the films were produced by <a href="/wiki/Ealing_Studios" title="Ealing Studios">Ealing Studios</a> during the years 1949–55.</li>
<li>The most represented actor is <a href="/wiki/Alec_Guinness" title="Alec Guinness">Alec Guinness</a> with nine films of merit, although three appearances were in supporting roles. <a href="/wiki/Michael_Caine" title="Michael Caine">Michael Caine</a> is the most represented living actor, starring in seven films.</li>
<li><a href="/wiki/Julie_Christie" title="Julie Christie">Julie Christie</a> is the most represented actress, with six films on the list.</li>
<li>All of the directors in the list are male.</li></ul>
