<p><b>LeapFish.com</b> was a <a href="/wiki/Search_aggregator" title="Search aggregator">search aggregator</a> that retrieved results from other portals and search engines, including <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> and <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a>, and also search engines of <a href="/wiki/Blog" title="Blog">blogs</a>, <a href="/wiki/Video" title="Video">videos</a> etc.  It was a registered trademark of <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dotnext&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dotnext (page does not exist)">Dotnext</a> Inc, launched on 3 November 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Mission_and_product_concept">Mission and product concept</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LeapFish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Mission and product concept">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dotnext&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dotnext (page does not exist)">Dotnext</a> Inc incarnation of Leapfish based in <a href="/wiki/Pleasanton,_California" title="Pleasanton, California">Pleasanton</a> focused on selling advertising via a <a href="/wiki/Telemarketing" title="Telemarketing">telemarketing</a> team consisting of 80 salespersons<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> that leveraged what it called a single experience for both searching and sharing traditional and real-time content.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LeapFish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The LeapFish.com domain was originally home to a domain name appraisal service created by Jeremy Harris in 2006; this was purchased in 2008 and rebranded as a Meta Search Engine.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Leapfish (the company) launched in November 2008<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> and incorporated the top three search engines (<a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!" title="Yahoo!">Yahoo!</a>, <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Live Search</a>) as well as other tools like <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, <a href="/wiki/Amazon.com" class="mw-redirect" title="Amazon.com">Amazon</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Yahoo!_Answers" title="Yahoo! Answers">Yahoo! Answers</a>.
</p><p>Leapfish launched its last version in November 2009<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2013)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>. The update included features such as traditional and real-time search, a customizable homepage, interactive widgets, and <a href="/wiki/Social_media" title="Social media">social media</a> integration.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>As of February 12, 2012, the leapfish.com domain has been for sale.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Features">Features</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LeapFish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Features">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div style="width:52px"><img alt="Ambox scales.svg" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Ambox_scales.svg/40px-Ambox_scales.svg.png" width="40" height="40" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Ambox_scales.svg/60px-Ambox_scales.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5c/Ambox_scales.svg/80px-Ambox_scales.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="620" data-file-height="620" /></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>has been nominated to be checked for its <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Neutral_point_of_view" title="Wikipedia:Neutral point of view">neutrality</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Discussion of this nomination can be found on the <a href="/wiki/Talk:LeapFish" title="Talk:LeapFish">talk page</a>.</span>  <small><i>(August 2010)</i></small><small class="hide-when-compact"><i> (<a href="/wiki/Help:Maintenance_template_removal" title="Help:Maintenance template removal">Learn how and when to remove this template message</a>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<ul><li><a href="/wiki/Real-time_search" class="mw-redirect" title="Real-time search">Real-time search</a><sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Meta-search<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> – LeapFish retrieved its search results from other portals and combined them on one page.  Results from Google, Yahoo, and <a href="/wiki/Bing_(search_engine)" title="Bing (search engine)">Bing</a> were displayed on the page as well as images, video, news, Yahoo! answers, shopping, publications and blogs.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Customizable home page – LeapFish offered a customizable homepage to its users, similar to iGoogle.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Share Bar – Outgoing links from LeapFish search results would display a "share bar" at the bottom of the page that allowed users to link the page to social sharing sites.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Profile pages – LeapFish gave users space to display social information such as Tweets, YouTube Videos, Blog Posts and Flickr images.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Widgets - Provided non-webpage results (such as multimedia or news articles) in form of widgets.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>LeapFish offered affiliate applications for website owners.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Advertising">Advertising</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LeapFish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Advertising">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The main LeapFish advertising programs allowed business owners to display a permanent advertisement on the top of the search results page. The ad space was granted by purchasing a keyword of the advertiser's choice.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> The space was sold via aggressive telemarketing<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> with the promise that the space could be sold on at a potentially increased price at a later date.
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Domain_appraisal">Domain appraisal</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LeapFish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Domain appraisal">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Prior to its purchase by <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dotnext&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dotnext (page does not exist)">Dotnext</a> Inc, LeapFish<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> was home to a domain name appraisal service. In addition to its search tools, the new LeapFish provided domain appraisals complemented with a scoring system.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> In addition to appraisal values, LeapFish provided domain information such as Traffic Rankings and Unique Visitors from Compete.com.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversy">Controversy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LeapFish&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Controversy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The <a href="/w/index.php?title=Dotnext&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Dotnext (page does not exist)">Dotnext</a> Inc incarnation of leapfish.com had a mixed reception from users of sitepoint forums, from bloggers and other regular web users.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 3, 2009, the online blog <a href="/wiki/TechCrunch" title="TechCrunch">TechCrunch</a> posted evidence of LeapFish sales representatives intentionally abusing <a href="/wiki/Google" title="Google">Google</a>'s pay-per-click model against potential customers.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/w/index.php?title=Ben_Behrouzi&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Ben Behrouzi (page does not exist)">Ben Behrouzi</a>, the CEO of LeapFish's parent company, DotNext, confirmed the sales representative's actions, but announced that the representative was no longer employed by the company.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The company has also been accused of <a href="/wiki/Astroturfing" title="Astroturfing">astroturfing</a><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> and spamming.
</p><p>As opposed to other search engines, such as Google or <a href="/wiki/Yahoo" class="mw-redirect" title="Yahoo">Yahoo</a> which appear to favour web-based sales, Leapfish keywords are largely sold via an aggressive <a href="/wiki/Telemarketing" title="Telemarketing">telemarketing</a> operation<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup> which has not been without friction with the online community.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> The telemarketing team consists of 80 sales persons.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/Better_Business_Bureau" title="Better Business Bureau">Better Business Bureau</a> rated LeapFish "F" (its lowest possible rating) after receiving 20 separate complaints (some of which remain unresolved).<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> The Better Business Bureau recorded eight separate complaints about sales practices.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
